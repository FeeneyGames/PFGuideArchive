Nephril's Extended Beginners Guide to Pathfinder

(and to a lesser extent preparation for Pathfinder Society)

This is my guide to help people acclimate to Pathfinder.  It will also help you get ready for Pathinder society.  It is lengthy but I have put it into chapters and sections so that you can locate what you are looking for.  This covers a lot of basic information as well as some in depth coverage of different game mechanics.  All of this information is available on the website for standard rules I have just made the wording a little more common and put it into categories I believe people will be more likely to use.

        Nothing in this is law.  The rules of pathfinder are fluid they change to fit the game as needed.  From first edition to 5th there was a ton of errata.  I would always suggest that in case of an argument refer back to the srd (www.d20pfsrd.com) or the official forums (www.paizo.com) there is a wealth of information here.

Chapter 1.  Getting Started

        This chapter's purpose is to help you get ready to play the game.  It covers a variety of points that are important to understand before you ever roll your first attack dice.  This section may read a little slower than the rest but that does not demote its importance at all.  I hope you can take some good information from this Chapter.

Section 1.  Game Expectations

        This section explains what players should be capable of during game play.  Most will sound like common sense.  But still they should be stated.

  1. Show up on time with everything you will need to play.  Your character sheet, dice, pencil, drinks, snacks if necessary, and anything else you usually need to play your character.  A spell caster will need access to the book there spells came out of (or preferably spell cards).
  2. Please make sure to be clean and free of strong odors.  There is nothing worse than sitting next to someone with a strong body odor for 3+ hours or talking to someone with overwhelming breath.
  3. Keep outside influence to a minimum.  Phone calls, text messages, smoke breaks, and anything else that detracts your attention from game play should be kept to a minimum or if possible completely avoided.
  4. Know how your character works.  And if necessary have a print off covering your feats and abilities for quick reference.  Or you can have a glossary that notes the book and page to reference each ability or ruling.  If you have a grapple focused character make sure you understand how grappling works and so on.
  5. Maintain your character between games.  If you level up or lose an important item or anything make sure that your character sheet shows that.  If you level up specifically make all modifications to your sheet prior to coming back to the game the following session.  This can be time consuming depending on the class and no one should have to sit and watch you do that while they are waiting to play.
  6. If it is not on your character sheet or an inventory list you don’t have it or you can’t do it. I have heard casters claim to have not chosen a spell last level so they will just take whatever spell it is they need right then. This is far from OK  If you show up with an incomplete sheet your character is incomplete and should not be allowed to gain the new abilities until the next scenario comes up.  Pick your spells, feats, skills, languages, and any other class specific choices before you play the character.
  7. Let your Dungeon Master know what you are doing.  He needs to know if you are taking a new feat or a new class this will help him be better prepared to handle situations as they pop up.
  8. Keep out of character talk to a minimum during game play.  This is to help keep people in the game realm it helps promote role play and expedites the adventure allowing you to cover more ground each session.
  9. During other players turn keep your mouth shut.  Its not your turn.  Unless you have a readied action that is activating right then or unless you have an immediate action you should not be talking during someone else turn.  This above all else is rude and destructive to an efficient game.
  10. As soon as your turn is over start thinking about what you are going to do next turn.  As other players take their actions formulate any changes that need to be made.  When your turn starts you should not be trying to locate your champion then deciding what you should do.
  11. Watch the board.  If you are playing a game you need to pay attention.  Everything that happens could affect you.  If you don’t know what’s going on you're probably not paying attention.  
  12. Use game speak.  If you are wanting to hit someone with your sword when they move up to you say “i am readying an action to strike the first enemy that approaches me.”. You should never say “i draw my weapon” be precise “i am drawing my falchion” chances are you have more than one weapon and it should never be the job of the DM or other players to decipher or guess what you did.  If you say “i am charging monster x” but what you are really doing is moving into position then attacking as a standard action to activate an ability you need to make the differentiation, say “i am going to move up to base monster x then as a standard action I will be attacking him while activating cleave to strike monster y.”
  13. player knowledge vs character knowledge.  You may have read the bestiary you may know every monster type by heart and their associated weaknesses and strengths.  Until you make a knowledge check in game while looking at the monster or until a friend does so and then tells you there is absolutely no reason for your character to know about that and you should play accordingly. Invest in knowledge skills if you think its that important.
  14. Play your character.  Not everyone is a role player (though I wish they were)  but if your character has a charisma of 7 with no social skills don’t try to talk your way out of situations for the party.  Being a charismatic person should not give you character any special power.  If your int is below 10 don’t pretend to know things you shouldn’t  Also if your int is 7 8 9 you are not mentally handicapped.  Don’t make bad plays just because you say your character is too stupid to know better.
  15. Your character has limitation.  A ranger cannot use handle animal to make a wild animal into a loyal combat trained pet.  There are steps to this process.  Don’t try to expand your abilities further than they were intended.
  16. Be creative.  I love creativity please try something new.   But don’t try to add extra power to your character just for the sake of breaking them.  Using the spell web to glue yourself into place as a boat thrashes violently in the waves is much different than claiming you can make a ring of true strike permanent because you have a crafting feat.
  17. Err on the side of fairness when you read a rule or ability.  Rules as written are subject to interpretation.  If you read it as super powerful your probably reading it differently than it was intended.
  18. Don’t berate other players for how they play there characters out of game.  If they do something stupid berate them in game and face the consequences.  If your party healer does something stupid and you tell him hes an idiot and almost got you all killed.  Don’t be surprised he prefers to back up other players instead of you.  Keep it on the field.
  19. Don’t play someone else character for them.  You can attempt to suggest something but your word isn’t law and  you should not be upset if they go with a different plan.
  20. NEVER threaten another player.  Your character may threaten there character from time to time (this should be kept to a minimum too though)  but a player or DM should never threaten outside of game.  If this happens the game will likely die soon unless the problem is solved quickly.
  21. If you don’t want to play anymore of if you can't play anymore tell the group and leave. Don’t show up and be unhappy, don’t make excuses and just start skipping.  You can be replaced and it is poor form to be unreliable.
  22. If you are not going to make it to session notify the DM (and all players if at all possible) as soon as you know.  If you miss a week because of a family reunion that’s fine.  If you tell one player  (or worse no one at all) the night before that is not OK  
  23. If you cannot consistently make it to games tell the DM and players and offer to resign if necessary.  You can't expect everyone to wait on you.
  24. If the DM makes a ruling you don’t like voice your opinion 1 time.  If he sticks to it quietly look for backing proof to your argument if you can do so without disrupting the game.  If you can't do that research it during down time between sessions then bring the proof to game.  If he wants it to be a new table rule that is within his power hopefully its fair.  If it isn’t and you can't stand it find a new game.  Having a power struggle with a DM will never go in your favor.  Even if he gives you your way next time you get in a bad situation he will not be nice to you.  So live to fight another day so to speak.
  25. Be friendly not confrontational, go to every game with the intention of having fun.  
  26. Stay until the game is done.  Don’t rush people to finish the game.  Keep track of time and calmly let the DM know the time.  If you have to leave at a specific time just keep him apprised every 15 minutes not every turn.
  27. If you are ever asked to leave a game by the group (not just one player) just leave. Chances are there is a problem that will be difficult to fix during game play  Not everyone belongs at every table.  Just try to be the nice guy and chances are no one will ever ask you to leave.  Do not throw a fit do not storm out just leave you probably were not having fun anyways.
  28. Ask before bringing friends to a game.  Role play can be embarrassing to some players and they might feel less inclined to do so with an audience.  And your company should do his best to follow all of the before mentioned table expectations.
  29. Respect the place you are playing in and the owner.  If you are playing in a shop adhere strictly to their play rules.  Do not become a pest they are doing you a favor.  If you are playing at a friends house clean up after yourself and do anything they ask you too.  Use coasters, flush toilets, keep the noise down if they need you to, park where they ask you too.  Pretty much if they ask you to do something to make them hosting easier do it unless its a big inconvenience then let them know about the problem.
  30. Be considerate, be nice, its a game have fun, this game takes some work if you have downtime work then do it.  Be ready to play don’t bring down the others.
  31. Electronic Devices can prove useful for quickly looking up information but should be used for game related purposes and not detract your attention from what is going on at the table.
  32. Understand the demeanour of the game you are playing in.  a more casual game will be more forgiving of small trespasses while a more serious power based game may require more from its players.

Section 2. Game Terminology

        This game is full of terms you may not be familiar with I will do my best to cover the majority of them here.

  1. Saving throws are set values  that are composed of a combination of stat modifiers and the base saves given by your class plus any any random modifiers from abilities or feats.
  2. BAB is your Base Attack Bonus.  This is a big chunk of what decides if you hit or not.  You add this number to your strength modifier for melee attacks and to your dexterity for ranged attacks and most spell casts that target someone with a roll.
  3. Hit dice refers to the amount of hit points you get per level.  In society play each class gets ½ of their hit dice rounded up every level and max at first level.  Others may have you roll or other variations.
  4. "Level refers to many things. Character level refers to your overall amount of levels. If you are a fighter 1 barbarian 7 and someone asks what level you are you can simplify I am an 8th level character. Spell level refers to how difficult a spell is to cast. Caster level refers to how many class levels you have in the class that casts a spell.
  5. DC is short for Difficulty Class.  This refers to a target number you are trying to hit with a bonus from a source and a d20 roll.
  6. SR is Spell Resistance.  Some characters will be naturally resistant to be targeted by spells.  The spell resistance is a dc that you have to beat in order to effect them with your spell.
  7. AC is short for Armor Class and is comprised of all the different defenses you have added together.
  8. Touch ac refers to how easily you can be touched by another character.
  9. Flat footed means you are not able to dodge like normal but your armor is still taken into account.
  10. CL or caster level shows your effective level for casting a spell or using an ability.
  11. CR is Challenge Rating,and this is used to describe how hard a challenge will be for a character, whether it be a monster a trap or any other test for the character.
  12. Fort is short for fortitude and is governed by your constitution this comes into use to battle poisons and other things that affect your immune or digestive systems usually.
  13. Will is short for willpower and this is governed by your wisdom.  This is the resiliency of your mind and your ability to defend against mental attacks.
  14. Ref is short for reflex and is governed by Dexterity this is usually used to show your ability to get out of the way of something.
  15. A Check is a term used when you attempt to do something requiring a D20 roll. Sometimes other dice can be used but D20 is the default dice for most things.

Section 3. Pitfalls of Pathfinder.

  1. grappling is still a beast of an ability and its very common for high damage animals and monsters to have improved grab which means after they deal their damage to you then can initiate grapples as free actions further ruining your day.  Trying to beat them at their own game is possible but requires some focus and big physical stats.  Having a way to deal with this is much easier.  The spell grease gives you a +10 to escape artist to escape a grapple, freedom of movement means you can slip right out.  Without magic your options come down to escape or fight.  You will generally be better off “tanking” the grappler by staying in the grapple and dealing damage with a one handed weapon while your party attack it from outside the grapple.
  2. Traps.  Its not every game that someone takes on the trap finder responsibility. Hopefully someone can at least find them then disarming them is a matter of controlled tripping or magical means.  Find the trap try to identify it then pick the best suited person to trip it.  Just blundering through blindly and hoping for the best result can result in many dead party members.
  3. Water combat.  Drowning takes longer than most people would think.  You are given a pool of 2 rounds/ point of constitution.  Each round you take a full round action or a standard action reduces that time by an additional round.  After the time expends you start making relatively easy constitution checks (dc 10) that get +1 harder each subsequent round. Instead of trying to swim to the surface finish your fight calmly then get some breath. As a spell caster I would strongly suggest swimming if it is at all possible to avoid.
  4. Swallow whole. It is annoying and comes from grapple. However most monsters do much less damage while you're inside than when you're on the outside. Carry a dagger, since you cannot use large weapons while inside, and make them pay for swallowing you.
  5. Flying bad guys can be difficult to deal with if you are not prepared.  Though if you are prepared it will usually be a breeze.  The ability to fly increases there CR quite a bit.  If you get on even footing with them you will find they are usually easier than other monsters of their CR to defeat.
  6. Incorporeal monsters are much like flying monsters.  If you are not ready for them they can lead to a total party wipe pretty quickly.  But with a ghost touch weapon you can quickly dispatch appropriate CR monsters, Alternatively a magic weapon can deal half damage and “magic missile” is a low level spell capable of hitting the incorporeal making them less of a hinderance.
  7. Traps can prove annoying.  They are time consuming to deal with and depending on the trap can have very powerful negative effects.  Spike pit trap can kill a low level tank where an explosive trap could deal damage to an entire party requiring the consumption of party resources to recover from.  If no one has the trapfinding ability then magic traps can prove even more problematic.  Detect magic and dispel magic can help a party quickly get past magical traps in this situation but again this consumes a 3rd level spell that could have been used to debuff an enemy or counter another spell.
  8. Invisibility.  Oddly enough this comes into play quite often.  You can counter this magically (glitterdust, or invisibility purge), mundane means exist (sack of flour, or other dust) the most dangerous thing about invisibility is that it makes all party members flat footed. A rogue with invisibility (or greater invisibility) can pick and choose who he wants to execute in a party with massive sneak attack dice.  Being ready to handle invisibility is something every party should come prepared for.
  9. Illusions.  Though not as deadly when viewed alone they can be used to hide all manner of terrible thing.  A massive spike trap with an illusory wall over the top makes for a death trap with almost no save.  The first person in the party will generally fall and become stuck inside the trap unless played carefully.  One means of doing this is to learn from the blind.  Take a quarterstaff and use it to tap test all surfaces.  The  party must walk slower than normal to utilize this technique (most DMs agree some may not require a movement speed decrease) but it could save a lot of game time and party resources. And at the low cost of a mundane staff you couldn’t have a cheaper solution.
  10. Darkness.  Its nice when the whole party has dark vision but this is not a realistic expectation for early play.  But having light sources for when fighting breaks out and being able to just follow along when you are blind allows you to function adequately. The range of your attacks dictates the vision you need.  Obviously the spell light will be insufficient for an archer but a melee character could deal with only seeing 30 feet.  The rules for counteracting darkness sound confusing.  To simplify.  Higher level spells nullify lower level ones.  Darkness beats light, daylight beats darkness, deeper darkness beats daylight.  To beat deeper darkness you need to posses heighten spell (or a heightened magical spell item) and create a fifth level light spell.  Continual flame on a stone that has been heightened to 5th level is a reusable source to defeat deepening darkness.
  11. Damage reductions can be a pain.  At higher levels a lot of creatures have them. Having a way to bypass them is very important.  Blanket DR (shown DR/-) is something that you just have to swing through to deal damage.  But most other types have a bypass method. A knowledge check can help you identify these in monsters.  And its usually what I ask to know first.
  12. Sometimes its better just to walk away.  Not every fight must be fought.  And there is no shame in retreating.  
  13. The biggest problem I have is a self imposed on players.  I call it “Scooby Dooing”  this happens in several situations.  The cocky fighter feels he can handle anything and wants some 1 on 1 time with the DM.  The rogue/mage/stealther greatly over values there skills and feels they could adequately scout ahead solo.  Any player who gets to a high enough level they feel untouchable and wants to test their character only to fall prey to an encounter meant for the party (and usually a DM that has run out of pity for people who do stupid things)  stay together, fight as one, stay alive.
  14. Poisons and disease.  These suck.  High saves can help you so much here but anyone can roll poorly and fall to these attacks.  Antitoxins and antiplauge are low level ways to fight back.  For only 50 GP each you get a +5 bonus on the next saving throw.  At higher levels spells can be used to instantly cure these poisons and diseases.  Scrolls of these make for a great on demand item.
  15. Ability drain and damage.  This kills people.  Constitution drain can kill you while STR and DEX drains can paralyze or leave you incapable of moving.  Charisma and wisdom just limits your abilities.  Intelligence drain can leave you a blithering idiot.  Level drain imparts a big penalty across the board.  A level 4 spell (death ward) makes you immune to much of this and gives you saves vs others.  When hunting wights or other enemies that deal most of their killing blows through these means it is a good idea to have a scroll (or spell caster) with this available making the fight much easier.
  16. Swarms.  These guys are immune to tons of things and have DR for other things.  Having an alchemist fire or a spellcaster with burning hands is a reliable way to handle them low level.  At higher level use the same kind of things just larger caliber

Chapter 2.  Character Creation

        Now that you have a general understanding of what to expect from your game

Section 1.  Core Class Overview (Strengths and Weaknesses)

  1. Barbarian.  Strength is obviously damage and life.  These guys are the quintessential meat shield.  Almost all of these weaknesses can be covered with with rage powers.  Making there true weakness that without rage up they can be susceptible to a lot more than they would normally during combat.  Making surprise rounds especially dangerous for them.  All around this is a very strong class that multi-classes well but truly shines when taken to level 20.
  2. Bard.  My Main Problem with the bard could also be considered there strength.  they do a lot of things all at once but they really shine when they specialize it is easy to be a half character with the bard.  Meaning you could kinda do everything ok while never excelling at anything. No one buffs a party more profoundly than a bard. Bardic music your key ability is a scaling powerful buff to your entire party.  With a large pool of skill points and charisma as a casting stat these guys make a great party face and double as a very effective skill monkey.  A class that should require a lot of role play since you will have to talk to the DM during all the social encounters.  The spell list that is given to the bard is varied and includes a host of arcane spells (primarily buff, enchantment, and illusion.) as well as many bard only spells and several healing spells. Not strong enough to really be a primary healer but definitely useful.  This class is also a spontaneous caster which is a huge boon for versatility.  Use magic device is very nice for someone who primarily uses charisma and has the skill points to keep this maxed. The primary shortfall even when in a party is that you are very squishy.  Limited on armor because of arcane spell failure, d8 hit dice are not bad but do pose a very risky early game where a stray arrow could possibly crit and end you for several levels.  Not a class for a new player.
  3. Cleric.  Versatile as a prepared caster could be this class excels at healing while being fully capable of being in combat.  Divine casters do not suffer from arcane spell failure so strap on a shield and Medium armor and wade into battle with everyone else.  It is recommended to have at least one divine caster per well rounded team.  And the cleric unlike most other classes is allowed to pull from the entirety of divine spells in creation every day.  No spell book and no predefined limited spell allotment.  You can prepare any spell that you have sufficient level to cast and a slot to prepare it in.  saves for this class are amazing as you have a strong save for both fortitude and willpower.  On top of that you are able to prioritize both stats that help these making you very resilient to the save or lose brands of spell.  The domains also give a lot of diversity and some extremely powerful abilities.  This class can see some strong multiclass options with good saves armor proficiency the ability to use all divine magic items (wands and so forth) and a lot of power at level one with domain powers.
  4. Druid.  Very similar to a natural variety of cleric has the same spell casting set up with a different spell list also possessing 2 strong saves which are both very important vs save or lose type spells.  The ability that really sets the druid apart is his ability to assume to shape of several different types of creatures.  Starting out with just animals and eventually gaining the ability to change form into elemental  This ability scales with level for duration and increases what you can change into and how many times you can change.  This ability makes you very flexible and with a feat you can even cast in the assumed form.  The druid can pick between an animal companion or a domain (like the cleric but limited) both of which can be useful.  My suggestion would be animal companion almost every time (very few exceptions).  the druid can wear armor while casting as he is a Divine caster and does not suffer arcane spell failure he can wear light or medium armor but druids have a special restriction that stops them from wearing any metallic armors.  Wood, Leather, Dragonhide, and several other special materials are still viable options.
  5. Fighter.  Here is a class for experienced players and new players alike.  With so many feats and the ability to exchange some feats at high level they allow you to make some mistakes leveling up or optimize for the level.  With this class you will be getting a new feat every level and you are given access to a list of “super combat feats” that are fighter only.  D10 hit dice full armor and shield proficiency coupled with martial weapon proficiency give lots of variation in builds and types of fighters.  Capable of some of the highest damage in the game
  6. Monk.  This class has a lot going on for it.  It really shines when paired with the plethora of archetypes out there.  Maneuver master monks, qi gong monks, ki mystics, and zen archers.  The primary ability with the monk is there flurry of blows making them very effective at dealing out damage if they can stand still.  They are ¾ BAB but when they flurry they get to use their class level instead of there BAB making them equivalent to full BAB during full attacks.  Limited on weapons you can use your fists gain additional damage and abilities as you progress.  From DR bypass to additional attacks your fists can prove an exceedingly difficult weapon.  And an amulet of mighty fists allows you to give weapon enchantments to your natural attacks so you can keep up with everyone else.  Getting to add your wisdom to ac is nice but it is at the cost of severely limiting your armor (none).  The monk will quickly become one of the fastest and tankiest characters in the group.  There saves are incredible the only class with 3 good saves. They are a a D8 hit dice so make sure to play it safe for a few levels until you can take a few hits.  Late game with a large wisdom item and some bracers of armor you will sport an ac equivalent to the medium armored fighters while still moving 60 feet a round.  You get a class ability every level and bonus feats pretty often which you can take without meeting prerequisites AMAZING.  Probably not a class for a real beginner but in the right party anyone could play one of these guys.
  7. Paladin.  One of my favorite classes. The major down fall with these guys is that most other players and DMs will have something to say about how you play the mandatory lawful good character.  An exceedingly frustrating event for a role player is to be told “your character wouldn’t/couldn’t do that.”  the class itself is full of win.  Good fort and will saves again make you much more resistant to save or lose spells.  At 2nd level you get to add your charisma to your saves which for 5 points on a buy can be a 14 that’s +2 to all saves.  You get immunity to fear which is nice and comes in handy.  Additionally you get a divine spell list which has lots of nice spells some of which only you can use. The lay on hands with the attached mercies can prove extremely powerful.  Being able to heal yourself as a swift action (or with a second level paladin spell an immediate) makes you resistant to a lot of burst damage.  This coupled with heavy armor proficiency and d10 hit dice means you can stand to walk a little further away from your healer than the early level fighter or barbarian.  The spell list also allows you to use wands of cure light wounds without a check which can come in handy for out of combat healing.  You eventually get an animal companion that has to be a horse (or horse like) or you can choose to get a nice active to boost your weapon damage.  Smite evil is an ability you get early on that just gets better and better this increases damage, to hit, and can allow you to bypass some DRs.  A final note about paladins is that they don’t have to be melee.  It is completely possible to play a ranged smiting paladin and lay down some serious DPR (damage per round).
  8. Ranger.  Half fighter half druid and half rogue this is 6/4s of a character very nice. Your saves should be pretty good.  Assuming you start play with some wisdom (your casting attribute) and your strong fort and reflex saves you should have very well rounded saves even early game.  Full BAB with a specialized combat style allows you to excel at any kind of martial combat you want.  Being a divine caster you can wear medium armor and you don’t have to worry about spell failure.  You also have some spells that are on very limited lists.  Two that I really like are “Lead Blades” and “Gravity Bow”  both of these spells increase the damage dice of your weapons.  Coupling this with vital strikes can make you an amazingly devastating skirmisher.  Some of the class features from Ranger are situational but depending on the campaign you are running you can pick the race you want to fight and the terrain you want to spend most of your time in.  the large skill list and amount of ranks you get per level allow you to be a decent skill monkey.  Your animal companion can act as a meat shield, mount, or combat buddy.  Having an animal companion to get flanking for you or to provoke AoO so you can escape can make your life much easier.  And unlike a familiar if your animal companion dies you just find a new one and train it no cost.
  9. Rogues have lots of “shortcomings” unfortunately.  They would be such a fun class to play they have some amazing abilities with rogue talents but it all comes back to 5 things in my opinion. Number 1 biggest problem they are a martial class and have ¾ BAB progression with no ability like a monk to pretend to be full BAB. Number 2 they have a D8 hit dice with no class ac buffs and only light armor proficiency.  Number 3 bad fort and will saves these are the two most important saves in the game and you have to go 3 levels before getting a base of any kind.  4 again fighter type but bad weapon proficiency choices.  5  you are the most qualified to be a scout cause of your high skills that everyone will want you to lead the party looking for traps.  However that puts you in a dangerous position.  Now for some good things.  You get more skills than anyone else in the game and you have an amazing skill list that includes perception disable device and use magic device.  Sneak attack dice can add a huge boost to damage if you find yourself in advantageous situations.  Rogue talents are much better than feats for a rogue and you get them at every even level.  These are amazing and allow for lots of customization and specialization.  In pathfinder you are now allowed to critically hit (and therefore sneak attack) a lot of creatures that you couldn’t in the past.  Trap sense gives you a bonus on saves vs traps but its a bonus to your already best save meaning when you set off magic traps later in game with fort or will saves you just end up failing those saves.  Improved uncanny dodge and improved evasion allow you to avoid some negative secondary effects as well as stops you from getting flanked anymore.   Finally you can do all of your sneak attack damage at 30 feet with a ranged weapon (60 with rogue talent).  This opens up a lot of opportunities for blitz attacks however it makes it much more difficult to qualify for sneak attack dice.
  10. Sorcerers.  Spontaneous arcane casters.  This is like the impatient younger brother of the wizard.  With only one good save this class was designed to avoid close combat and requires some thought to play.  Generally the squishiest member of the party with the lowest hit dice, armor, and saves you will be an easy target for bad guys.  But with a good team and some planning you can become nearly untouchable.  The true power of this class comes from its spontaneity but your limited selection of spells requires you to put a lot more thought into your level up than a wizard.  Possessing only a handful of spells you can cast whenever you like is excellent when combined with multiple use spells or spells with lots of utility.  Bloodlines give a new flavor to the class each one possessing new abilities and skills.  These range from free pseudo metamagic feats to increased damage or defense.  There are a lot of variance in the bloodlines.  A few to consider are arcane because it allows you to get a familiar or arcane bond just like a wizard, sylvan (fey wildblood) allows you to get a an animal companion just like a druid, elemental is great for evokers because it allows you to change all your energy types to one you select,  with draconic you pick an element and whenever you cast an energy spell with that descriptor you get 1 more damage per dice rolled.  Others give additional powers this was just a few for example.  The class also gets additional bonus feats at level 7 and ever 6 levels thereafter based off your bloodline.  Arcane is (in my opinion) a much stronger type of magic than divine  with so many powerful spells the shortcomings of this class can be quite appealing.  When playing this class always watch for ranged opponents and when you can spend some time and effort into defensive strategies or spells.
  11. Wizard.  The most powerful caster in the game.  Capable of learning and preparing any arcane spell in existence.  This class is extremely squishy and relies on thought to keep it alive.  In many ways its just like the sorcerer before this.  The primary differences is that this class gets no bloodline.  Instead they get a school of magic they specialize in. these schools each come with abilities and when choosing a school you must choose 2 restricted schools.  In pathfinder unlike 3.5 you are still allowed to cast from these restricted schools but to cast a spell you must use up 2 spell slots to prepare it.  The wizard is also a prepared spell caster unlike the sorcerer.  Each day he sits down with his spell book and puts together a list of spells he thinks he will need for the day.  If you know what you are doing that day this can be a simple task but when adventuring you never know what you will need so again it comes in handy to prepare spells that have a variety of uses and other spells you know you will need every day.  As a wizard you are allowed to leave spell slots open then you can just prepare the spells later in the day by taking another hour and assigning a spell he knows to the open slot.  So out of combat spells like teleport or magnificent mansion don’t usually have to be picked first thing in the morning.  Being intelligence based casters you will end up with additional languages and skill points making you capable of performing multiple roles.  Knowledge skills, spellcraft, and appraise are all nice skills for a wizard to pick up if he can spend the points.

Section 2. General build

  1. Avoid Min/ Max Tactics.  By this I mean avoid creating a one trick pony of a character.  Focus is nice but not at the risk of leaving yourself excessively weak in other areas. 
  2. Balance and focus build a stronger champion than a character who pours all his resources into a single trick.  For instance a fighter who wants nothing more than to trip everyone will fill much less useful when fighting flying creatures.  A fire mage will feel exceedingly weak when fighting fire immune or resistant creatures.
  3. Multiclassing is easiest between classes with full BAB progressions because if nothing else at least you can still hit.  Multiclassing partial BAB progression classes is difficult because you want to take levels until the next drop off so for a ¾ BAB take all 4 levels instead of a one level dip unless you think the dead BAB level was worth the abilities gained.  Multiclassing causes you to lose out on your favored class bonuses and even 1 odd level causes you to lose your classes capstone ability.
  4. When multiclassing consider your save gain and loss.  If one more level in your current class would get you +1 to all saves it may be worth hanging around for that one more level.  If you multiclass fighter/barbarian your will save will remain extremely low for a very long time giving you a weak spot that is easy to  exploit.
  5. Don’t take weapon focus unless its a prerequisite to something else.  Leaving yourself open to use whatever weapon you want is a nice advantage.  And that +1 to attack could be a +2 to your worst save or any other number of things that you could use.
  6. Dropping any stat below 10 should be done with caution.  It is very easy for an entire party to use charisma as a dump stat leaving no one that can perform the social skills needed.  At low levels this is tough at higher levels this can be overcome with skill points, equipment, and spells.
  7. If your character has a weakness and you can't think of an efficient way to fix it let your compatriots know and do your best to not get killed by them.  If you can't make a will save and you're a melee fighter type find some protection via gear or spell.  If you are a wizard and can't grapple to save your life keep distance and have a grease spell up your sleeve.
  8. Be able to do damage at close and afar without provoking attacks of opportunity.  If I see a ranger without a melee weapon or a barbarian without a bow by second level I see a huge problem with the character.
  9. You may start out a sword fighter with your end game being the most powerful archer ever.  You don't have to start play practicing the combat style you want to end with. Being an archer is very feat intensive whereas for the low cost of 1 feat (power attack) you can greatly increase a means of combat that you will most likely spend a lot of time using.
  10. Consider your race for more than the stat bonuses and flavor.  Gnomes and halflings get bonuses to attack and stealth but penalties to CMD.  Dwarfs excel at wearing armor (suffering no speed penalty) and get nice save bonuses, elves and half elves have some nice spell immunities, half orcs get falchion proficiency for free and some very nice racial alternates, humans get bonus feats and skills, past that different races get vision enhancements, move speed changes, and a host of other abilities.  Once you look at the other races outside the core the differences are even more vast.  Your race is just as important a choice as your class or weapon.  Take the time and go with one that complements your characters build.
  11. Favored class bonuses can add up to a nice power difference between you and another member of your class.

Section 3.  Seemingly Required feats.

  1. If you are going to focus with a bow then Precise shot is nearly a required early game feat allowing you to ignore the -4 attack penalty of shooting into contact.  Respectively at later levels Improved precise shot will prove just as useful attempting to counter snipe enemy ranged characters. Or pick off enemies from a hiding place.
  2. A two handed fighter without power attack is wasted.  The amount of damage scales so quickly that it becomes a large chunk of your base damage pool.  This is the difference between a poke and a death blow often times.
  3. Plan on summoning creatures the feat Augment summoning is an amazing feat that should be a metamagic feat but instead just blatantly adds the bonus to anything you summon without increasing the level of the spell.
  4. The difference between a rangers pet and a druids pet is usually a hit dice some skills possibly a feat and a BAB.  While all these don’t sound like a lot it can make a HUGE difference.  You spend one feat (boon companion, from Pathfinder Players Companion, Animal Archive) to bring your animal companion up to par with a druids and your battle buddy is going to be much stronger.  
  5. If you plan on specializing in a certain school of magic then spell focus is very helpful.  
  6. If you plan on casting spells that target enemies then Spell Penetration and greater spell penetration are very nice to have.
  7. Consider your goals as a spellcaster there is a metamagic feat that will make you much better at it.
  8. At higher levels Damage reduction can become an issue even for an archer with a nice enchanted bow.  Clustered shots has some prerequisites (that you probably have anyways)  but allows you to stack your damage then apply Damage reduction to it only once instead of to each shot as normal.
  9. For a character who plans on changing between multiple weapons (bow to sword or back again) then quick draw ensures you never miss out on an attack.
  10. Natural Spell allows you to cast spells while in wildshape.

Section 4.  Feats and Features

  1. if you have a feat or a class feature that you don’t use all the time be it a prerequisite feat you don’t often use or a class ability like Bravery (fighter class feature bonus vs fear saves) that doesn’t come up very often.  These can save your life when you least expect it.  Have these recorded in a way they pop up when you would need them.  Write a big bold B next to your will save to remind you of your bravery bonus.  Write Combat Expertise next to your ac.  You may never use it but there may be that one time when you're in dire need of the extra ac and it could save your life.  By recording these in places that you are already looking when you would need them they will remind you in case of emergency.
  2. Races have a number of features from energy resistance to movement speeds and attack bonuses that are easy to forget.  Fighting goblins and barely missed your last attack damn if only you remember you really hated goblins and have the hatred class feature for bonus to attacks.
  3. Don’t give up a class feature for an archetype or racial alternate without first considering the strength of the ability even if it would be seldom used consider how strong it is when you would need it.  2 perfect examples:  Elven immunities,  not used often but when you need them they are AMAZING immunity to a whole category of magic and a significant bonus to 2 others.  And Trapfinding for the rogue,  you can trade this for a host of cool things but at some point in time you may need this in which case having it will be a huge boon to your effectiveness
  4. Consider your leveling strength not just your end goal.  Playing a pure fighter with a million feats is fun you plan out all the super high level abilities you can get but you forget your saves suck and that you could probably use some extra hit points being the party tank.  Take some time to consider what your character is missing before just blindly following a feat chain.
  5. When looking at class abilities and feats consider how easy these will be to use in your group.  If you are in a rotating group like a convention you need to have abilities that are flexible.  If you are in a home group you can build your character with someone else in mind knowing they will be there to aid you.  A party with a monk that specializes in stunning fist would be a great place for a sneak attack focused dual wielding rogue. That same rogue however would be hard pressed anywhere else because of his big to hit penalties.
  6. Look at prerequisites in advance.  Create a plan for your build.  This is not set in stone it can change but its still a good idea to have an idea.  If you want to do a lot of combat maneuvers but started with an intelligence of 10 you are going to have problems since combat expertise is a requirement and requires int of 13.  

Chapter 3.  Combat

Section 1.  Action Economy

  1. In the order of importance actions are generally as follows:                                                  Full Round Action>Standard>Move>Immediate>Swift>Free.
  2. action economy is an easy way of saying use the most of your actions during your turn. When you look at your actions as a value instead of just a move or a standard you can see that making sure to utilize all of the actions available you can squeeze more power out of your character than someone who simply attacks and stands there.
  3. You get a free perception every round.  Try to use it without bogging down the game. Check an exit or double check something that you rolled poorly on earlier.
  4. Swift and immediate actions share the same place.  If you use one you cannot use the other.  If you do not have any abilities that allow you use immediate actions then consider what can be done as a swift action.  Many feats allow you to do something as a swift action.  Often times these are very useful feats.  Arcane armor training allows you to ignore 10% arcane spell failure.  Arcane strike adds a bonus to damage.  There are a lot of abilities and items that you can activate or direct as a swift action.  These can be a boon to your character and grant you more actions per turn than your opponent.
  5. Prior to having 2 attacks you will find yourself attacking then sitting there with a move action waiting.  Move actions are not limited to just moving.  Many spells allow you to direct an ongoing effect as a move action.  You could use a move action to gather additional information with another spot check.
  6. A trait called accelerated drinker can be a big boon to someone who plans on using potions.  Freeing up your standard action to instead use a move means you could drink a potion of enlarge then attack someone with your newly gained reach.
  7. The order you take your actions in matters.  Using a bow or casting a spell move out of melee range before taking your standard action to avoid attacks of opportunity.
  8. A 5 foot shift is a free action that you can take in any round that you do not take a move action to actually move.  Move actions could be directing a spell, drawing/sheathing a weapon, or standing up.  You are then allowed to move 5 feet away possibly to safety.
  9. A lot of feats affect a full round action.  Most modify the action and are not limiting to being combined.  Using Rapid fire and Manyshot both effect the same action and allow you to make the most of standing still.  In times like this it makes since to use a full attack action instead of skirmishing (moving then attacking or vice versa.)  while a champion that focuses on the Vital strike feat chain would be well suited to moving then attacking as a standard action.  Making the most of your actions is a combination of positioning and character ability.  Some people make a better turret than charger.
  10. Casters have the ability to summons monsters that in turn have there own round of actions.  This doubles (or more) the amount of actions you have a round.  Meaning that a caster has the opportunity to outplay other characters purely based on the fact they can do more each turn than anyone else.
  11. Rangers, druids, and sylvan sorcerers make use of powerful animal companions that have there own actions although they are limited to physical attacks and movement they still allow these characters an obvious advantage.
  12. Wizards, Summoners, and Arcane Sorcerers take this to the limits.  Possessing both the ability to cast summoned monster spells and a familiar they can abuse the action economy game.  An improved familiar with hands and the ability to speak (imps, mephits and others) can utilize your use magic device skill to activate wands, magic devices, read scrolls, and get stuff out of your bag for you.  These little guys are in a sense a half character and can prove to be very dangerous.

Section 2.  Combat Rounds

  1. Each round is 6 seconds take this into consideration when trying to convey a point to someone in your group during combat.
  2. Combat starts and usually a surprise round ensues  all relevant players should be given a perception check to see if they can also act during the surprise round.  Everyone is flat footed until they act.
  3. Next everyone rolls initiative.  Roll a d20 then add your dexterity plus additional modifiers to see what your placement will be obviously highest goes first.
  4. Some players may wish to delay their initiatives to go after other players.
  5. Each character takes their actions in order of initiative.  Anyone who has not yet acted is considered flat footed until they take their first action.
  6. Players can hold a standard, free, or move action to perform later during the turn under a set circumstance (ill hold an attack to shoot the spell caster when he tries to cast a spell) this does  move your initiative however.  

Section 3.  Attack Bonuses

  1. Flanking is the easiest bonus to get and give to friends.  You both get +2 to attack just for positioning.  This is the equivalent of 2 feats each or a buffing spell each.
  2. High ground.  While this is not an easy bonus to get as you must use a melee weapon and still be able to reach your opponent this +1 to attack is nevertheless an increase that you will have opportunity to use.
  3. Abilities.  This is the one most people remember to use.  Usually limited to a few times per day but some class abilities give a bonus to hitting certain opponents  It is a good idea to use these when the opportunity arises.  Saving everything up will leave you wasting abilities by the end of the day when the big fight never came.
  4. Racial bonuses.  Several races get bonuses to attack specific opponents.  This usually comes in the form of the hatred ability but there could be other ones out there.  Read your race it is more than just what you look like and your stat bonus.
  5. Applying a penalty to your opponents ac or DEX is even better than getting a bonus to your attack.  Because then everyone in your party gets that same bonus vs the opponent  Ways to do this include combat maneuvers, debuffing spells,  and application of conditions such as sickened or shaken.
  6. The biggest one that everyone forgets about is Aid Another.  This is a standard action that allows you to give your friend a +2 armor bonus or attack bonus.  If you are unable to strike a foe because of your low attack bonus with careful planning you could give one friend flanking while aiding another for a +2 bonus to two friends and in the case of you getting an Attack of opportunity you will also have a +2 to do this.
  7. Potions and scrolls are cheap ways to give a decent bonus to attack.  If you are low level a potion of bulls strength costs 300 gold but gives 2-3 DMG and +2 to hit. Or a 50 GP potion gives +1 to hit and saves vs fear.  Consumables are your friends.
  8. Charging can grant you a +2 to attack (with a -2 to ac in trade) but allows you only one attack.  If your enemy is going to hit you anyways and you only have 1 attack why not charge.

Section 4.  Defense

  1. Wielding a shield even if you do not plan to use it during the fight means you have a bonus to your armor class even if you are caught flat footed.
  2. Wielding a shield you are not proficient with means you take that shields check penalty to skill rolls and attack rolls.  I personally would trade a -2 to skill checks for +2 ac during a surprise round.  A masterwork heavy shield has a check penalty of -1 and a mithral or darkwood shield has a 0 penalty making these amazing marching items.
  3. Fighting Defensively gives you a +2 ac while taking a -4 to attacks.  This is a standard action that can be used while aiding another.
  4. Combat Expertise is a feat that allows you to sacrifice some of your attack in exchange for some armor.  It is also a prerequisite to a lot of other feats.  If you have this feat remember it there may be a time to use it.  When using this feat you must attack that round.  This can be added to fighting defensively.
  5. Total Defense is also a standard action that gives a +4 dodge bonus to ac however you cannot make any attacks that round even attacks of opportunity.
  6. There are several ways to increase your ac.  The obvious ones are wearing armor, carrying a shield, and having dexterity.  Increasing these is an easy way to increase your ac.  Magical bonuses to all of these is possible and effective.  But before you put that +2 on your armor or shield consider an amulet of natural armor.  This would stack with your current ac for cheaper.  Also rings of protection are fairly inexpensive early on and give the best ac bonus in the game (as it applies to all types of armor)  if you don’t wear a shield a wand of shield costs 2pp or 750 gold and gives a nice bonus (assuming you can use magic devices) or a wand of mage armor in the case you don’t wear armor again 2pp or 750 gold.
  7. Mages can wear armor.  You are bound by 2 things as a mage.  Proficiency and arcane spell failure.  These can be overcome pretty easily.  Proficiency only matters if the armor has a check penalty.  Finding armor with low check penalties that can be made masterwork (reduces that penalty by 1) or mithral (reduces by an additional  2) can open up the possibilities of armor to arcane casters.  Armored kilt has a +1 ac and a check and arcane spell failure of 0.  you could wear this and put a +1 magical on it then a number of named bonuses to work with your mage armor.  A mithral buckler has 0 check penalty and 0% spell failure meaning you could carry and use one without any problem.
  8. Saves are incredibly important in this game.  Using any save based stat as a dump is asking for disaster.  Where as for 5 points of your build a 14 gives you +2 to a save this is completely worth it.  Min/maxing is not the best way to build.
  9. Cheap items like cloaks of resistance or belt of +2 constitution give quick bonuses saves that stick around after spells and potions wear off.
  10. Hit points are the last line of defense for your character.  With this in mind make sure yours are close to full at all times.  At low level make sure they are full.  Toughness is a feat that gives you an immediate burst of hit points then 1 per level as you go on (20 total).  Bears endurance is a 2nd level spell that gives 2 hp per level, +2 to fort saves, +4 more hp before death, and a +2 on stabilizing rolls.
  11. Aid other as mentioned before can be used to grant a +2 ac bonus to a friendly unit.
  12. Stepping in between your friend and an enemy gives them a chance to escape.  The enemy now has you to worry about and cant simply chase your friend without leaving itself open to you.
  13. If you are being shot at from range simply dropping to the ground gives you a +4 ac bonus versus their shots forcing them to close distance.
  14. Disarming an opponent can drastically reduce their combat potential.
  15. Applying conditions that reduce your opponents ability to hit are even better than increasing your ac because it effectively increases your entire teams ac.  Shaken and sickened both reduce attack bonus and other checks by 2.  a powerful defensive move.
  16. Don't be there alone.  If you rush into combat alone you get a single attack or at most a pounce.  The enemy team gets full attacks against the only person there usually you. And if you charge you are at -2 ac.
  17. Don't be there at all.  Some fights can be skipped.  Or even be initiated from an unexpected angle.  If you see the enemy directly down the path why walk up to them and start fighting.  Instead keep a scout watching them while the others work through the trees to attack them from sides.  Using tactics can greatly decrease the damage you take.
  18. Have a ranged weapon.  I know this doesn’t seem like a defense idea.  But a round of shooting arrows into your enemy before he ever gets to you means you have less damage to do when he gets to you.  Meaning he has fewer rounds to hurt you with.
  19. Brace weapons are amazing for destroying chargers.  Dealing bonus damage like that can often times cripple a charger before they ever finish attacking you.
  20. Mobility is very important being faster than your opponent means they can't catch you. Being able to fly means they have limited ways to deal with you.  Being able to meld with stone means you have a safe escape and teleportation opens up so many options. Having a swim speed could mean the difference between dying and an easy encounter.
  21. Defense against combat maneuvers is also important.  Fly counters trip completely.  Fly 2 inches off the ground and you are immune to trip.  Spells like grease and later freedom of movement completely shut down grapplers.  A weapon cord is 1 sp and allows you to retrieve a disarmed weapon as a move action without provoking attacks of opportunity buy 10 of them and put them in your backpack.  A backup weapon has the same effect.  Get disarmed draw another identical sword.  Remember the improved and greater versions of combat maneuver feats also increase your defense as well as your offense.
  22. Gaining reach by any means rather it be increasing your size or using a reach weapon can allow you to avoid a lot of damage.

Section 5.  Damage

  1. Whetstone is a cheap and easy way to give you a +1 damage bonus on your first attack. Combine this with a few weapons and you can get that bonus for a few rounds by simply drawing a new weapon.
  2. Two handing a weapon gives you 1 ½ your strength modifier rounded down as bonus damage (instead of the normal 1x).  This can even be used on a one handed weapon.
  3. Power attack is the most common way to increase your damage.  When used 2 handed adds 3 damage instead of the normal 2.
  4. increasing your attack bonus increases your damage per round.  The more you hit the more damage you do.
  5. Increasing your strength by any means increases your damage.  For ever 2 points you get 1 dmg one handed or 1.5 2 handed while getting an attack bonus this is by far the most effective combat bonus for you.  A potion of bull's strength costs 300 gold and lasts 3 minutes.  For 3 minutes you get +2 to hit and +2 damage (one handed) +2 to hit and +3 damage (two handed) and +2 to your cmb and cmd.  That is a minimum of 3 feats and the bonus against cmb and cmd is amazing.
  6. Increasing your size magically also increases your damage.  With the strength bonus (a size bonus that stacks with enhancement bonuses), the damage dice increase with the weapon, and the reach for more attacks and attacks of opportunity this is one of the most powerful things a melee fighter could use.
  7. Positioning is very important when it comes to dealing damage.  Making sure you are in a place that allows you to take attacks of opportunity is a key way to increase your damage per round.  If you have an attack of opportunity left when your turn comes back around that means there was a chance last round you could have dealt more damage.
  8. Focusing an enemy as a group.  Each enemy does 100% of their total damage until they are 100% disabled.  Meaning if you and your party are damaging 4 different people and get them all to 75% you are still taking 400% damage.  Now if you had grouped and focused one person you would have 1 at 0% and 3 at 100% meaning you take only 300% damage.
  9. Attacks per round.  Early on you have 1 attack.  You take it charge or standard action it doesn’t matter its still one attack.  Later in game you get multiple attacks through bab increase or spells like haste.  Even if you have 5 attacks this round and you charge you are now back to 1 attack.  Finding ways to utilize your higher attacks per round will increase your damage.  If you are a hasted level 11 fighter with 4 attacks and a strength of 22 even if you specialize in great sword you are better off using your compound bow this round.  Because a single great sword at 2d6+16(9 str, 2 feat, 3 weapon enhance, +2 weapon training) is still not equal to 4 bow shots each at 1d8+9(+6 composite, +2 enhancement, +1 weapon training).  This also stops you from getting into the disadvantageous situation where you charged away from your party.  Now your enemy has to move up to you to attack and you can drop your bow pull out your sword and attack away.
  10. Don’t look at your damage on a per hit basis.  Sure it is rewarding to deal tons of damage per hit.  But if anther party member is hitting more often and more times than you chances are he will be doing significantly more damage than you over the entire fight.

Section 6.  Damage Reduction

  1. Having damage reduction or fighting against damage reduction can largely impact your choices for a fight.
  2. Using a bow specifically you have 2 options for damage reduction.  #1 have the appropriate arrow to bypass the dr.  blunt arrows, cold iron arrows, silver arrows all exist. #2 use a large compound bow or the feat clustered shots to just do enough damage to overcome the dr and deal damage still.
  3. As a melee fighter you have the same options as the ranged fighter though it is more realistic for a 2 handed fighter to deal large amounts of damage per hit.
  4. Enhancement bonuses on weapons bypass dr.  +1 bypasses magical, +3 silver and cold iron, +4 adamantine, and +5 alignment. This does not bypass slashing bludgeoning or piercing however.  And none bypass blanket dr as shown dr 5/-
  5. rushing the first +3 on your weapon before adding additional abilities proves very beneficial in these cases.
  6. Carrying several weapons can also help here.  A tactic referred to as golf bagging. Always having the right weapon for the job.  The easiest way to cover the most drs is with 2 weapons.  An alchemical silver morning star (simple weapon 1d8 damage, takes -1 damage when piercing cause of alchemical silver) and a slashing cold iron weapon of your choice.
  7. Some classes are given abilities to bypass drs.  The paladins smite comes to mind but there are others.  Keep track of these and make sure you use them when the time arises.
  8. there are some creatures with multiple Damage reductions.  it is important to understand that some of these require you to use 1 of the listed methods whereas other require you to use both.  A creature with DR 5/ piercing and silver requires you to use a piercing silver weapon to bypass the dr.  At the same time a creature with DR 5/Piercing or Slashing means you could use one or the other to bypass the Damage reduction of that creature.

  2. The Attack of opportunity is resolved before the action that provoked it is resolved.

Chapter 4.  The Finer Points

Section 1. Skills

  1. you cannot make a knowledge check until you have a rank in it.  So if its a class skill and you are not poor (in the way of skill points) invest 1 rank in each of them so you can at least attempt a check.
  2. If you have a skill on your class list that is trained only you should consider putting a point into it.  If the need ever arises getting a roll at 4+modifier might save the party some time in case the specialist fails.  You could always roll high.
  3. Use magic device is an amazing skill for almost any character.  At a certain level your wizards time will be better spent controlling the battlefield and he may not have time to enlarge you or cast bulls strength.  Potions get expensive so wands are very helpful to get low level buff spells off.  Also a use magic device character with a small scroll library can prove extremely powerful and an amazing utility.
  4. Perception is the highest called for check in the game.  It would be foolish to not have any points in it.
  5. Acrobatics can allow you to move through threatened squares without provoking attacks of opportunity.
  6. Diplomacy is very useful it can allow you to take shortcuts that were otherwise shut off.
  7. Intimidate can be used much like diplomacy and in addition can be used as an attack to demoralize your opponent applying the shaken effect a very nasty use of a skill.
  8. Fly climb ride stealth and swim are all important for characters that think they may ever use that mode of transportation.  Nothing like drowning or falling off a horse during combat to ruin your day.
  9. Sense motive comes in very handy during social situations but can also prove useful when fighting high level rogues as some of them specialize in bluffs called feints that you can completely negate with sense motive.
  10. Spellcraft is mandatory for most spell casters but to a point can be useful for others. Also if you are going to use magic device then spellcraft comes in handy.  Find a scroll on the floor spellcraft can identify it so you know what you are casting instead of activating blindly.
  11. Survival can allow you to feed yourself in the wilderness and build a fire.  This can also save you a Know direction spell from your caster since survival can get you out of the woods when you're lost.
  12. Some crafts and professions can come up in day to day campaign stuff.  If you have extra skill points a few points in a profession like sailor or a craft like alchemist could prove useful if your dm allows it.
  13. Appraise can help you find what you want and can also be used as a knowledge check.
  14. Disguise and bluff can be very useful for a charismatic player/character and then its only your creativity that can limit you.

Section 2. Combat Maneuvers

  1. combat maneuvers are much easier in pathfinder than in previous versions.  Now with the introduction of CMD (combat maneuver defense) and CMB (combat maneuver bonus) it is an easy system to tell if something succeeds or not and they eliminated almost all counter rolls required.
  2. Grapple is the ugliest out of the bunch mainly because lots of things do it.  And commonly large things do it while imposing a significant penalty.  However now in grapples you are free to attack your enemy with any one handed weapon or natural attack.  The penalties for being in a grapple are the same for each party involved.  You cannot move, you take -4 to dex (resulting in a -2 to ac) you take -2 on all attacks rolls and combat maneuvers except for those used to grapple or escape a grapple.  It is very difficult to cast a spell while in a grapple dc 10+grapplers cmb+spell level.  Making it nearly impossible to get a spell off.  This maneuver can be done in place of an attack.
  3. Trip allows you to give people the prone condition.  Or in other words you can make them fall down.  A lot of creatures are immune to this.  Those that can fly as well as those that don’t have a defined top or bottom make up most of the category.  Tripping someone opens up a lot of opportunities first off when they are prone they take a -4 penalty on all attack rolls and cannot use a ranged weapon (except a crossbow) you also get a -4 to your ac against melee and a +4 ac vs ranged attackers.  Additionally whoever you trip must stand up eventually and that provokes attacks of opportunity from all surrounding players.  This can prove a very brutal combo to set up the rest of your group.  This maneuver can be done in place of an attack.
  4. Disarm allows you to take someones weapon away.  The system has been very simplified.  There are no longer modifiers for weapon size or wielding it one or two handed.  If you do this unarmed (if you have the improved unarmed strike feat your hand is considered a weapon) you take a -4 penalty to the attempt.  Successfully disarming someone causes there weapon to fall into  a random adjacent square unless you disarm them with an unarmed strike then you can choose to end with the weapon in your hand.  If they pick up there weapon from the ground they provoke attacks of opportunity and it eats up there move action.  If you took away there only weapon they are now provoking attacks of opportunity every time they attack you with a fist (unless they have improved unarmed strike).  This can be done as an attack action.
  5. Sundering allows you to attack items and possibly destroy them.  Dealing damage to items is easy to figure out.  If you know the items hit points and hardness its easy to figure the damage out.  Hardness is reduced from total damage for each successful hit. This could reduce damage to 0.  once an item is down to less than 50% of its hit points it gains the broken condition.  For a weapon this means all attack and damage rolls are reduced by 2.  for armor and shields the ac is halved, if its a tool the skill check takes a minus 2 penalty.  If its a wand or staff it uses 2x as many charges to operate.  When you reduce the hit points to 0 or lower you can choose to destroy the item rendering it unusable or you can choose to leave it at 1 hit point with the broken condition.  This can be done as an attack action.
  6. Dirty Trick is a new maneuver added with pathfinder it is very useful but requires your DM to think a little bit.  Depending on the enemy you can fight you can apply a number of different conditions i.e. Blinded, Dazzled, Deafened, Entangled, Shaken, Sickened. Only one of these can be applied per attack.  Dirty Trick can be done as a standard action.
  7. Taking the improved combat maneuver feat allows you to perform these maneuvers without provoking attacks of opportunity.  This also gives you a +2 to your cmd and cmb for that specific maneuver type.  The greater combat maneuver feat gives an additional +2 to your cmd and cmb and gives a bonus effect.

Section 3. Spells

  1. the biggest drawbacks to spells are the attacks of opportunity they provoke when used, a lot of them give the target a saving throw, a lot are subject to spell resistance or immunity, and as far as a source of damage they usually fall short of a skilled fighter.
  2. Be smart about your location when casting.  Casting defensively is a great backup plan but its better to just not be in the way.  In addition to attacks of opportunity you must also consider npcs holding action to disrupt your spell casting and enemy spell casters prepared to counter spell you.  Keep your eyes open.
  3. Focus on spells that have no save or that even on a save have a strong effect.  Spells like glitterdust are amazing at low levels cause you could blind a target but even at higher levels when you can see invisibility (by the spell) and no one else can you can still use glitterdust to make them visible.  Much lower level spell than dispel magic or invisibility purge this saves you valuable resources.
  4. Know what spells you cast are subject to spell resistance and make knowledge checks before attacking to see if your target has strengths versus your spells.
  5. I’m going to stress the importance of scrolls here again.  If you are a prepared spell caster you may not always need protection from energy.  If you are spontaneous you might not have taken it at all.  Having scrolls to use to fill in the gaps makes you a better caster.
  6. Abuse your actions.  I will go into more detail later in about action economy but specifically for casters some spells can be controlled as a move action while using your standard action to cast a different spell.  If you are not using your move action your probably missing something.
  7. Summoned spells may take a full round to cast but after that you have a constant source of damage or utility.
  8. Make sure you can direct and if possible communicate with your summoned creatures. Speaking Terran and summoning earth elemental is much more powerful than just summoning earth elemental
  9. Many feats benefit spells.  Rather it be from increasing the dc, changing the spells limits, or making it easier to cast.  If you are going to be a spellcaster taking feats to increase your effectiveness would only make sense.
  10. Augment summoning gives a massive boost to summoned creatures.  More life more damage and better to hit.
  11. Spell focus increases the dc of a school of spell by one.  This can prove very useful if you strongly focus on one school of magic.
  12. Metamagic feats possess the most strength for casters.  These feats allow you to manipulate spells in ways that can drastically increase their power.  Extending a buff or maximizing a summoned monster spell could drastically increase the outcome of a fight.
  13. When summoning monsters through spells you can use a higher level spell to summon monsters from a lower table and getting 1d4+1 of them.
  14. Some low level spells can prove exceedingly more powerful just by increasing the save dc.  Since the save dc is equal to your casting modifier plus the spell level and add in any additional modifiers.  By using heighten magic to increase the level of the spell you can increase the dc of the spell.
  15. Feats like spell penetration and greater spell penetration can help a caster who is going to be focusing on offensive spells.
  16. Combine spells to a more dramatic effect.  A simple web spell could keep someone entangled reducing their dexterity by 4 making it that much easier to hit them with area of effect spells (you effectively reduce their reflex save by by 2.)  or using spells to increase your own casting stat thereby further raising your save dcs.
  17. Buffing your friends is your best way to sustain damage in a fight.  A simple haste on your friendly archer gives him an additional attack at his highest bab every round.  With him acting as a turret he should be full attacking constantly.  The same spell on your charging friend gives him 30 feet of speed the same extra attack once he gets to full attack range making him a blaze on the battlefield.  They both also get +1 ac and +1 to attack.  For 1 round per caster level.  Since you have to be 5th level to cast the spell that means that on an archer you are doing 5(1d8+composite bow bonus+enhancement bonus+dmg feats) all of these with a possibility to crit.  On your melee guy the numbers change depending on there movement but this also gives a defensive bonus with retreat speed increase and armor increase.  If you are in a party of 4 at 5th level your one spell has to potential to grant 5 extra attacks to all party members.  The damage from this 3rd level spell is most likely going to far exceed the fireball you could have cast.
  18. Out of the magic schools I (personally) feel some schools are more powerful than others.  While it would seem otherwise I generally consider evocation among the weaker half of the schools.  Putting conjuration on top with abjuration, and transmutation tied for second.  Schools like illusion and necromancy are situationally very powerful. Divination is very useful and can save lives with vision enhancing spells or the odd scouting spell but is probably largely considered the weakest of the schools.  
  19. Considering clever ways to use spells can make them much more useful and keep them relevant later into game.
  20. If a spell creates difficult terrain as a secondary effect you have just simultaneously cut your enemies speed in half as well as shut down any charging attackers.  Beware these penalties apply to anyone moving through the area.
  21. Spells that obscure vision give you team a chance to gather your wits or make a quick escape.  In some cases (like darkness) you may even be able to see when your enemy cannot.
  22. Casting silence on a barbarian before he charges the enemy team means that the enemy spell caster is going to be hard pressed to get a spell off.
  23. Spells with verbal components require you to be able to speak to cast.  a deafened character has a 20% spell failure chance with such spells.
  24. Spells with somatic components require you to have at least one hand free.  in the case of casters with shield proficiency a light shield or buckler can allow you to continue casting spells without having to drop your shield.
  25. Something as simple as holding an action to attack someone while they are casting a spell can cause them to lose the spell.  Remember intelligent creatures you encounter may do the same to you.

Chapter 5.  Gearing

Section 1.  Bonuses, Stacking and Non-Stacking

  1. Unnamed bonuses (bonuses that are not listed as typed) always stack with one another.
  2. Dodge Bonuses stack always.
  3. Natural armor bonuses generally stack unless they specifically state they don’t
  4. Bonuses that are named i.e. enhancement, luck, competence, trait, racial, or any other that says it is a XXXX bonus will not stack with any other bonuses of that type.  Instead you get the higher of those of the two and ignore the second.
  5. Armor bonuses from different sources do not stack.  So mage armor and a breast plate and a bracer of protection do not stack.
  6. Deflection bonuses also do not stack with one another.
  7. Shield bonuses likewise will not stack even if you carry 2 shields and cast the shield spell.
  8. Items with stat bonuses generally give an enhancement bonus.  So until you get that belt of +4 strength then having a bulls strength potion around could prove very useful.  But once your belt has achieved +4 or higher you can probably hand the potion off to someone else or sell it for half its value.
  9. Remember the words temporary bonus and permanent bonus are subjective in this game. A belt of giant strength gives 4 strength as a temporary bonus for 24 hours then becoming a permanent bonus.  The value of that buff does not change only the title. This was originally introduced as a way to separate bonus types and make use of the new “temporary” boxes under your primary stats which allowed you to show modified stats by spells or abilities.
  10. Your bonuses from gear, feats, and class features all count towards feat and class prerequisites.  However if at any time the items lose there magic or the bonuses are removed for any reason you lose all benefits that were dependent on them.

Section 2.  Consumables

  1. Consumable.  It sucks spending money just to see it go away in the form of consumables but honestly this is where an experienced player will prove they are better.
  2. Fighting an enemy that does bleeding attacks a potion or wand of infernal healing stops bleeding every round heals one hit point every round and auto stabilizes you if you go below 0 hit points for 10 whole rounds.  Amazing.
  3. Enlarge person takes a full round to cast for your wizard.  But a potion of enlarge person takes one standard action to drink. (or a move action with accelerated drinker combat trait.)
  4. enemy takes to the skies follow them with a potion of fly.  Much less embarrassing than running back to the wizard and begging he let you play too.

  6. Monk tired of being a spell tax to your wizard buy a wand of mage armor for 2 pp or 750 gold.
  7. Rogues you truly shine here with umd as a class skill (or anyone who takes dangerously curious as a trait)  personal spell wands are amazing.  All of those spells the mage couldn’t share with you because they are personal only are now at your finger tips.
  8. Wizards why prepare spells you only occasionally use.  Instead design a scroll library that covers a lot of low level spells that are very situational that don’t scale amazingly with level.  Glitterdust makes a good scroll so does obscuring mist.  Elemental resistance spells even work well here.
  9. Per day use abilities should fall into this area.  Make sure that you keep track of the abilities you have.  If you consistency end your day with abilities unused start using them more freely.  Also know what it is they do.  The paladins smite ability gives bonus damage and attack,  but it also bypasses some drs which is often times overlooked. Many abilities are this way.

  1. the net is a weapon no one usually has proficiency in.  it applies the entangled condition on anyone you successfully hit with it and it has a cord that allows you to keep the capture person near you with.  This weapon also target your opponents TOUCH AC.  Meaning even without proficiency its not that hard to you.  Make that net masterwork then ghost touch and you are now playing ghost busters with the best of them.

Arrows are always fun at low levels.  If you want to bypass dr you have to carry silver and cold iron and blunt.  Alchemical silver is the only cheap way to buy arrows and they lose 1 damage.  Then you still have the problem of finding out if you need cold iron or silver.  In game this requires a knowledge check.  One you may not be able to make. However you can buy all cold iron arrows for 2g per 20 then apply a silver blanch to 10 arrows for just 5 gold.  So for 20 arrows that are cold iron and silver with 0 damage penalty it costs 12 gold.  Having 20 of these then the rest cold iron with a few extra blanchs around is a good idea.  Carrying 20 blunt arrows is also nice.  This is of course until your weapon has a +3 enhancement and no longer needs the special metal munitions.
  3. Oils can be made of any potion.  These can be applied topically instead of ingested. Which can greatly change the way they work in game.
  4. Oil of magic weapon is a nice cheap way to bypass magic dr until you have a true magical weapon.
  5. A spring loaded wrist sheath is 10 gold.  It allows you to equip a small weapon or a wand as a swift action.  If you are a 2 handed fighter and get grappled being able to get a small weapon out to strike with would be very nice.  This could also come in handy during a disarm or surprise round.
  6. A back up weapon is probably the most important thing you can buy.  If you specialize in long swords then losing your longsword would suck.  Having an extra one is very helpful.
  7. A pathfinders pouch is the perfect hiding place for items.  It is extra-dimensional space and it cannot be seen with detect magic.  For 1000 gold it could possibly save your life.
  8. If you ever plan on using a bow having bracers of falcons aim are an amazing item.
  9. A few abilities can be added to other magical abilities without increasing there enhancement cost.  For instance a ring can have sustenance or feather fall applied to it without the normal 1.5 cost. Comfort is an enhancement for armor that costs 5k.  It allows you to sleep in your armor no matter what the weight, it also acts as weather appropriate clothing (which usually equals a bonus to saving throws vs conditions) and reduces the armor check penalty by 1 more than it already was.
  10. Pearls of power come in very handy for mages obviously.  However if you’d like the party mage to cast fly on you every day or haste or any other number of spells this could be a strong investment for you.  This allows them to cast a spell and then regain it so they can use it again on someone else (or on you again)
  11. metamagic rods are awesome.  Extend is very cheap and usually helpful  A lesser extend costs 3k and doubles the duration of any spell of 3rd level or lower.  A healer that invests in a lesser metamagic rod of reach (3K) can heal people he normally couldn’t reach.  
  12. An armor check penalty sucks.  Most classes don’t reduce your armor check penalty as you increase in level.  There are ways to reduce it further though.  Masterwork reduces armor check penalty by 1, the comfort enchantment increases it by another 1, mithral and darkwood are automatically masterwork and it decreases the check penalty by 2. you could couple mithral armor with comfort to reduce the penalty by 3.  each of these are the equivalent of +1 to each str and dex skill check.
  13. The spell rusting grasp specifically targets iron weapons.  If you have a silversheen weapon or any other type that is not steel, iron, cold iron you are immune to a spell that can wreck a fighters day.
  14. Alchemists fire and flasks of acid are cheap ways to death with swarms.  Don’t always rely on your wizard to handle these situations.  If you want even cheaper get flasks of oil and a way to start a fire.
  15. Tanglefoot bags, smoke sticks, sunrods are all great non magical ways to deal with annoying situations.  Ranged attacker got you pinned down?? smokestik to the rescue. Want to know what is down the dark corridor or the deep well?? drop in a sunrod.
  16. Rope its your friend keep a length of it around knotted for a very low dc climb and if need be tie people up with it.  If its too foggy for your group to see one another tie the rope to each person and don’t risk being separated
  17. You can buy chains and manacles.  Come up to a double door that starts a huge encounter  slap a manacle on one side with the other ready to close the door and lock them together.  You now have a safe way to lock the door if you need to retreat back through it.
