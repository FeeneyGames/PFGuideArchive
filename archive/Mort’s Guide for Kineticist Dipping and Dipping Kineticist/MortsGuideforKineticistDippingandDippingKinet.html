This guide isn’t really meant to be a guide to the Kineticist itself. For that I would recommend N. Jolly's guide to the Pathfinder Kineticist. Rather, it is meant to be a guide for a niche I found unfilled by other guides. If you have any advice or comments, please feel free to correct me here.

First off, let me address why I believe a guide like this needs to exist. Unlike a true Spell using class the Kineticist is really rather meh to the concept of Caster Level. Most of your “Magic” is psychic powers that amplify the physical form bending the world to your will. This means that the Kineticist is a lot more flexible than a normal spell casting class being more of a marriage between Martial classes and Spell classes. Regardless, some dips are considered good for a Spell caster even with how much more they lose over the Kineticist. Ergo, there must be something good for the Kineticist.

Let’s start by talking about what you lose with dipping, so that we might know going into this guide what there is to gain back. You lose progression on your blast damage, 1 die for normal blasts and 2 die for composite per 2 levels. You miss out on Infusion Specialization every 3 levels. And finally, you also are missing a bunch of special abilities of various values thrown into the class. So at it’s base out of 2 lvls we are looking at trying to gain back a die of damage and two abilities or trading that damage die for a better hit chance (Full BAB) and a few good abilities.

For the reverse side, what another class can get from Kineticist, the answer for the most part is Elemental Defense. You never get to hold more Burn as a Kineticist, well, aside from stat point increases. Which means that in a dip your Elemental Defense is already okay and can be freely burned up to get whatever bonus you want. Note, this only works for characters with a naturally high Con. You also can nab a low level utility talent. Of particular note there are Water’s Slick and Aether’s Telekinetic Finesse, which is arguably the greatest thing ever if you are a Rogue.

Now, let’s look at the classes. For organization's sake the guild starts with full BAB Fighter like classes and moves away from them slowly.

The Fighter (Eldritch Guardian)

“I'm not good at the punchy punchy kicky kicky!”

Ah, the Fighter. Basic dip material, usually considered quite a strong dip option. However, the fighter’s weapon proficiencies are useless to you. The armor and shields are nice, but compared to other dips you only really grab 2 Bonus Feats in 2 lvls, and I don’t know that you even want to stay in Fighter that long. Because the Kineticist is rarely feat hungry there is almost nothing you can’t already do and if you are feat hungry for most builds Brawler just flat out makes for better dip material. Furthermore the archetypes are really just a question of what you want to replace Bravery with, Blackjack being a reasonable choice to help with Will saves. One exception to this rule is Corsair which gives you Cleave without having to met the qualifications for it. Worth? I don’t even really like Unbreakable. I mean, Samuel L. Jackson was great, but everything else was kinda meh.

Another exception is Eldritch Guardian. As far as options to get a Familiar from another class go this is the weakest. It is after a second level of Eldritch Guardian things get interesting. Now your pet has all your combat feats, all of them. With the Mauler Archetype and +1 lvl they can even grow big at will or with the Protector Archetype and +3 lvls trade damage with you or with the Figment Archetype they can die without it being a truly bad thing. Alternatively, with Boon Companion and Improved Familiar you can make for one killer Sprite. Or if you can get another Familiar lvl (Hint: Alchemist, Wizard, Rogue) you can nab Sprite’s upgraded version, Lyrakien Azata. Overall, you don’t get as much from Eldritch Guardian as true martial classes and there are better Familiar choices out there, but if you are taking some really awesome combat feats and want a pet to emulate them it’s really your only option. I would say that if you plan on using 3 lvls for a Familiar and no other dips this and Wizard will get you the best pet. But, this is far more expensive than spending 2-3 feats on a Familiar. This is only for if you really have a clear idea for your entire progression and want all your feats pulling double duty.

Fighters dipping Kineticist

The Fighter gains a lot more out of this deal than the Kineticist. Utility Talents can fill out a Fighter build quite nicely. Particularly having at will Grease and a nifty defensive talent to boot. If your fighter is a switch hitter this becomes a natural compliment as they are now never truly unarmed, though I would consider Kineticist abilities a backup plan rather than your main weapon.

Barbarian (Unchained Barbarian, Wild Rager, Armored Hulk, Mad Dog)

“HULK SMASH!!!”

The only thing to see here is Uncanny Dodge and a slightly better hit die over most dips. Unchained Barbarian is a bit better, it is nice that you get a flat attack hit chance and damage boost meaning that Dex. builds don’t suffer from Rage. But, it still doesn’t quite feel good enough. If you love Uncanny Dodge so much Shadowdancer is less painful as you can actually make use of those other class features. Do you really want a handful of Rage rounds a day? Wild Rager improves things drastically. It allows you to make a second attack while doing a Full-Attack action. Turn that frown upside down, you got an extra attack with Kinetic Blade! ...albeit while exposing yourself more. Your biggest regret is that Ninja do it too. And one can argue they are better, but Wild Ragers can do it unlimited times with better overall stats, health, and proficiencies. The catch? Potentially killing the rest of the party. Woops.

The Barbarian also brings up another point. Abandoning ranged bonuses for just having good melee can work well for a Kineticist, particularly Elemental Annihilator. If your GM rules that Kinetic Blade/Devastating Infusion doesn’t require concentration then Barbarians are a good fit. In this case I would consider playing an Armored Hulk or better yet, the Bloodrager version Steelblood and, well, hulking out. Rely on that armor and elemental melee weapon to see you through matches. For more brutish players this can possibly be better than Wild Rager, or at least less likely to murder your best friend. Dwarven, Duergar, and Oread players should especially consider it.

Special Note:

If it is Str. builds you desire then it would behove you to read through the Paladin, the Castellan Cavalier, and the Bloodrager as alternatives. For a short dip the Barbarian is arguably on the weaker side of choices. Alternatively, the section on the Elemental Ascetic Monk offers a very different build than the simpler Elemental Annihilator, but might still be a playstyle that appeals to you. If you wanna get some kick out of the Kineticist dropping the switch hitter and utility master titles in favor of brute power is perhaps the best option.

Finally, the Mad Dog archetype ditches Rage (Yay!) in favor of an Animal Companion. If you already grabbed a pet through Animal Ally and want an improved selection, I would read this as allowing you to pick a better pet with full progression. Though, if you are happy with a Wolf then this isn’t really all that useful to you. Furthermore, the Hunter exists. Your only edge on Mad Dog vs Hunter is +1 BAB. Don’t get me wrong, that’s a big deal. But is it worth all the front loaded side features Hunters get in 1 lvl?

Barbarian dipping Kineticist

You still have the great utility powers the Fighter can nab, but you can’t use them as effectively or need them. Honestly this is only blue or even above orange if you have high Con and can take a Kineticist with a great defensive ability. Pour your plentiful Burn from high Con into it at the start of the day and reap the rewards! Everything besides Elemental Defense is horrible, but Elemental Defense more than makes up for it. As a side note, ask your GM if Kinetic Fist would count as concentration. If not, that’s your Talent to go for as it gives your Natural Attacks a bonus die for absolutely free.

Paladin (Divine Hunter, Chosen One, Oath of Chastity, Iroran Paladin, Antipaladin)

“Paladin, Paladin, where do you roam?”

Finally, we end up with a reason for this guide to exist. Paladin comes with a built in Iron Will as well as a bunch of basic class features you aren’t getting otherwise. As Paladin I would treat the Divine Hunter as basically default. If you can take that archetype, do. Gets rid of the Heavy Armor in favor of Precise Shot. Unfortunately, the Oath of Chastity can’t mix with it, which sucks because it helps with your Will problem without needing Cha. Chosen One is a Lawful Good alternative to Eldritch Guardian, but only with Emissary Familiars and a less insane, but self contained and easier to make workable second level ability. If you are taking Paladin you did it all for the will, and an Emissary Familiar works for that, but prevents you from having Divine Hunter. As well as restricting your Familiar slightly, but not in a way that blocks Improved Familiar and if you want Improved Familiar I would insist you take Emissary Familiar anyways if you can. Paladin is a good dip for you already so Chosen One just feels like a bonus if you want a second lvl.

If you are Lawful Good I would take a single level of Divine Hunter almost every build just because it packages together full BAB, d10, Skills, Will, Precise Shot, Martial Weapons, Medium Armor, Detect Evil, and a once a day Smite. A Holy Guide archetype can even be tacked on to get a few more Class Skills for absolutely free! It’s hard to give up half a die of Blast damage, but this makes it an easier pill to swallow. It’s only a shame it’s only open to Lawful Good.

The only case where I wouldn’t take it is if I was going for Familiars as mentioned or a Str. build, an option well worth considering if you do a dip that gives you Heavy Armor. It pays to be the Chosen One if you have high Str. Speaking of Str., Dwarfs and Duergar also get access to the Stonelord archetype. Perhaps even an archetype good enough for 4 lvls to get it’s stance. Though, I wouldn’t go that far personally.

The Iroran Paladin makes a fair case for being an ally to the Elemental Ascetic. Personal Trail is much more effective as a dipping feature than Smite. And getting another kind of bonus to your AC is much appreciated. Compared to the base Kineticist the Elemental Ascetic really wants to be dipping into other classes, and this is a lethal weapon in that fight.

Antipaladins get a lower rating because of lack of archetypes and because Touch of Corruption is weaker than Lay on Hands, unless you happen to be undead or Dhampir.

Paladin dipping Kineticist

Have I mentioned lately that Elemental Defense can be good? It’s your only reason to dip Kineticist. It’s less valuable for you than the Barbarian, but if you want to live through a Nuclear bomb than a high Con Paladin with Kineticist splashed in would do the trick. If you have mega-high Con feel free to treat this as a green option.

Ranger

“Hi ho, Silver, away!”

Another Similar to Fighter Class. And again this is completely unsuitable. Extremely impractical. Basically inoperable. Your only light of hope is in “Aspect of the Beast.” At level two it’s among the feats you can pick with your Combat Style and Rageborn Skinwalker Natural Attack Kinetic Fist builds need claws... But this is completely outshone by any of the other archetypes or feats that can get you to natural attacks. It’s only real advantage being that most alternate paths are tied to things like Rage Rounds and Mutagens. Although the Draconic Bloodline for Bloodragers seems to be worded so that you just have claws constantly. Still, even at worse I would rather have Feral Mutagen or tell a werewolf to bite me than I would play a Ranger.

As far as Archetypes go, unless you know you will be facing a specific foe a lot (In which case pick the appropriate Archetype to counter them) then I would dumb Favored Enemy for either Freebooter or Guide. Falconer also gives you a very weak pet for basically nothing.

Ranger dipping Kineticist

I got nothing. Rangers are semi-spell casters that want to keep going with that. I can’t even really justify Elemental Defense as being worth it. It’s too different from your normal Ranger gameplan.

Cavalier (Castellan)

“Isn’t there a fiery knight upon a white steed?”

Anything Cavalier can do for you Animal Ally does better. If you want to upgrade your Animal Ally to any creature, pass on. Cavalier can’t do it until you reach lvl 4 in Beast Rider. Not worth it. Hunter and Mad Dog exist, go look at them. The only argument I am at all compelled by is starting life a Cavalier and then going Kineticist before grabbing Horse Master. And by “compelled by” I mean that ditching Cavalier ASAP sounds good. The core problem with Cavalier is that there are a certain number of features I want to see per level and it does not fill those out. Building a Cavalier that’s good is possible, but it requires intimate system knowledge and ignoring 90% of your options for the features worth not having enough at every level.

So, then we must talk about Cavaliers that are not riding a mount. Daring Champion is Swashbuckler without Deeds. This one actually isn’t terrible, just flatly worse than Swashbuckler. You want Deeds, not Tactician understand? I suppose if you can find a Teamwork feat worth using this is okay, but personally I just think it’s a shame you can’t force an ally to have a Betrayal feat. Why name them Betrayal feats and then prohibit us from forcing allies into it?! If you can find teamwork gold or play very closely with allies than this does get a bump up to maybe being a legitimate choice over Swashbuckler.

The Castellan is the only Cavalier that gives you enough features to be worth taking in a few levels. Castle Defender is excellent because you can MAKE cover or high ground if you so desire. Think of this as an alternative to Divine Hunter Paladin in the likely case you are not Lawful Good. Or, even if you are it’s an alternate way to get most of what you want from Paladin. The breakdown compared to Divine Hunter is that you keep Heavy Armor, loose the fantastic side features, swap Smite for Challenge, and trade away the free feat for Castle Defender, which if you play well should almost always be an easy condition to meet. This is arguably flatly if you are not a blaster and thus not using Precise Shot or if you needed Heavy Armor anyways. If you were looking to dip for proficiencies, this should be your gold standard. Sadly, you can’t trade away Tactician without being a Lashunta. And even then more of a downgrade with a free Combat Expertise. Oh well, at least Tactician works with racial Teamwork feats.

“Is it worth it for a second level fo-” No, Orders are less than feats and the ones that aren’t don’t synergize with Kineticist.

Cavalier dipping Kineticist

A surprising number of choices that are bad for Kineticist love the reverse. Cavalier is no exception. Thanks to Horse Master and Boon Companion you don’t have to worry about your pet. Spend a few levels in a good class, you know you want to.

Unless of course you are the fantastically flavorful and more potent Ghost Rider. Sadly, Ghost Rider cannot gain Horse Master or Boon Companion. This is a really big bummer for progression when you pick up Fire Kineticist and a conductive spiked chain. ...you were going to do that right?

Gunslinger (Gun Tank)

“Who’ll be the first to seize his gun? Who’ll be the last to see the ground?”

The Gunslinger is presented with the notably large caveat that you need a specific circumstance to make it work. That being that you need reliable access to advanced conductive firearms or an otherwise suitably powerful firearm with the conductive enchantment. This gives you access to a really sweat Kinetic blast gun. Yet, I can think of at least three good reasons for this not to be common in most campaigns. And even if they are you then have to get around the fact you need free hands for Gather Power, perhaps taking Quick Draw and either Gun Twirling or a strap so you can drop your gun and pick it back up as free actions. If magical advanced firearms happen to be candy though, this is an excellent way to bolster damage and open up some options. Though, personally I wouldn't bother taking this dip until you have such weapons already in hand.

Normally Gunslinger is a single level dip for firearms and then you are out. You have a few archetype choices to make though. Gun Tank is the most well rounded archetype giving you shields and armor proficiency. If for some reason you don't need the shields though, you can change what key ability Grit comes from. Siege Gunner keys it to Int. and Mysterious Stranger and Buccaneer key it to Cha. Buccaneer is regretfully a racial archetype, but happily is the easy to achieve Human race and comes with the added advantage of actually being worth a second level. You get to drink yourself under the table for bonuses and additional grit points keyed to your massive Con. So long as you aren't stuck on an island with Elizabeth Swann it's a reasonable number of additional bonuses. Plus, you get to RP being constantly drunk!

Oh, and you might want to check the Swashbuckler's section for Picaroon if that fits your concept better. Revolvers are not bad weapons.

Gunslinger dipping Kineticist

Gunslingers dipping into Kineticist just feels awkward to me. The key abilities, the Kinetic Blast is worthless, you want to be fighting from range, it just all feels a bit off to me in terms of synergy. Though you could argue on paper it’s just as good as for a Fighter, I would disagree strongly in practice. Different gameplans on this one.

Bloodrager (Steelblood)

“Death to all but metaaaaaaal!!!”

If Skald is the drummer, Bloodrager is the lead guitar. The Joan Jett. The Jack Black of Brutal Legends. The guy who’s ax is an axe. So, what does this mean for the humble Kineticist? It means frontloaded semi-Barbarian! Okay, so it’s not really that good, but it is metal. Points for that.

You might as well read everything said in the Barbarian section, it’s still all true for the Bloodrager. Steelblood is literally Armored Hulk, just on the Bloodrager frame which makes it better for a 1 lvl dip. To reiterate, for Str. builds this is a great way to put the Brutal into Brutal Legends. Just forget this Dex nonsense and prove you can do damage with the big boys! Man mode it! Ignore your low d8s and fifty points of Burn! Just punch it!

Ahem, final consideration is that all Bloodragers can get a Familiar with a buff in replacement of their 1 lvl Bloodline power. I don’t know why Oprah Winfrey was put in charge of Familiar Folio, but I’m not complaining. All said and done, this last trait raised Bloodrager up a color notch for me.

Bloodrager dipping Kineticist

Same as Barbarian dipping Kineticist, but with a downgrade because you are loosing your Sorcerous progression.

Brawler

“In the clearing stands a boxer, and a fighter by his trade.”

Brawler laughs at people dipping 2 lvl Fighter. If you have a feat intensive build and are dipping all for the two feats in 2 lvls Brawler should be your go to. You just get… more. Ignoring the Int 13 requirement on a lot of feats can let you optimize your stats more, and you get spare features just cause.

Yet, he isn’t quite perfect. This should be a match made in heaven. Really. But… argh. The reason I am so upset on this is that Brawlers are a natural fit for a Kineticist, as anyone who watched Avatar should know. You just need to mix the classes together. At the start this is a simple process, both you and the Brawler want the same items, equipment, and theme more or less. You are light flexible attackers. Both of you have an emphasis on either debuffing or insane Full Attacks. You can even use Martial Flexibility at the end of a Ride the Blast to switch between your ranged and melee forms.

The problem arises when you try to hit something. The rules are… not well defined on this point. Kinetic Fist is lackluster meaning you definitely want Kinetic Blade, but Kinetic Blade maybe cannot be used with Brawler's Flurry. Honestly, I would show this one to my GM and let them decide. It’s a bunch of weird interactions that mean a Kinetic Blade does count for Weapon Finesse, but apparently not for the Close Fighter Weapon Group. Even though it doesn’t call out the formed blade as not counting for the weapon it emulates’ group it also doesn’t say it can count.

Anyways, if it doesn’t count, as I believe most would read the rules, the Brawler is a more muted choice. Suffice to say that Brawler’s Flurry is amazing for mixing them. I’m not sold on the archetype that drops Brawler’s Flurry, Strangler, either. You can certainly build a Grappling Kineticist as they have some tools, but those tools are rated poorly alone for a reason. If you are going Brawler you really just want Feats and Martial Flexibility, but only a single level of Martial Flexibility is pretty eh. You can still use it as a move action can be slipped in with a Ride the Blast to close in, but it’s just giving you two feats at the cost of move actions. There is no reason not to grab Exemplar just so it’s slightly better, or Shield Champion for shields as weapons. Furthermore, a second lvl of Brawler is just barely better than a lvl in Fighter.

As much as it pains me to give up Martial Flexibility for a Kineticist, you do have two great archetypes options. Mutagenic Mauler does exactly what it says on the tin, and that might be good enough. Mutagens are really good, and will last you for quite a long time. Though, personally I see the flexibility of combat feats as being more versatile. And you get more uses albeit for a much shorter period. It trades away what I love about being a Brawling Kineticist for a burst of raw damage output and hit chance. Some will argue that’s strictly better and I can’t totally disagree. Snakebite Striker similarly gives up flexing for Sneak Attack. Since you are giving up damage die for multiclassing, getting full BAB and still getting additional damage dice is compelling. But, it does progress rather slowly. If you really want Sneak Attack this is your only way to nab it without loosing BAB. It also qualifies you for Accomplished Sneak Attacker.

If Kinetic Blade does count for Brawler’s Flurry, as I would definitely rule, Brawler suddenly becomes much, much better. The ability to flurry with Kinetic Blade and Kinetic Whip is fantastic and there’s no reason not to have Kinetic Fist for your offhands. This is good to the point where it is almost not really a dip so much as a mixed build where you want ranks of each class. At first Brawler is almost as greedy as Kineticist on being a purist to stack damage. However, if you are mixing your bags of damage in the same way the two can compliment and build on each other. For example, an ice Kineticist can use Chilling Infusion to trigger Medusa’s Wrath. Brawler can use Martial Flexibility to mix between close ranged and long ranged mode. The same sort of interactions that are great with Kinetic Blade become possible if Kinetic Fist becomes, well, not garbage tier. Basically, we are one ruling, Kinetic Blade Trait, or Feat/Wild Talent improvement to Kinetic Fist away from a fantastic class combo.

In this second case, treat Mutagenic Mauler and Snakebite Striker as blue too. Mutagenic Mauler does become slightly weaker with more levels and spread out encounters, but Snakebite Strike can seriously bring the pain.

Wild Child also gives you an animal companion. For free if you take only a single level. Can’t argue with that.

Brawler dipping Kinetics

If Kinetic Blade does count for Brawler’s Flurry, then see immediately above. Otherwise, the Brawler can still make out with Grease or any other useful trick and Elemental Defense. Though, there certainly comes a point with Brawler at which you can argue you have enough features, either around 12th lvl or 16th lvl, I can’t argue about throwing the rest of your levels away for the bonuses. A short dip is probably for the best. At least, until you can combo Kineticist with Brawler’s Flurry effectively.

Special Note:

I see a lot of potential for a Brawler and Kineticist Gestalt built. Even without a Brawler’s Flurry fix you can use Kinetic Fist with full progression and have access to a simply incredible list of ranged and melee options. If I was running an Avatar game I would probably make a Gestalt game with benders being Kineticists locked into the Element of their nation. (With a few minor changes like lighting being a Fire thing, and Earth having access to Fire as a secondary for Magma benders.) In that game a straight Brawler would be the ultimate in flexibility as Martial Flexibility is incredibly useful once on a Switch Hitter. Snakebite Strikers or Mutagenic Maulers are both quite good too in their own ways.

Other classes of note for Gestalt games would be Paladins, Rogues, and Barbarians. Though the latter only if your GM allows Kinetic Blade when raging. Actually… with Elemental Defense maybe just straight up Barbarian even without Kinetic Blade.

Slayer

Bounty Hunter has a lot of flavor? Deliverer can get you a Diety’s weapon without losing BAB? Um, ya that’s all I got. Slayer isn’t terribly helpful for you. It doesn’t let you do something new you want to do. Pass.

Slayers Dipping Kineticist

Basically, the same as Rogue dipping Kineticist only less extreme. (See: here) You get a lot of nifty little side tricks that can help you out. Aether Kineticist is virtually required.

Swashbuckler (Picaroon)

“Ho! Ha-ha! Guard! Turn! Parry! Dodge! Spin! Ha! Thrust!”

It’s easy to assume this is an instant no. But, there is no Brawler style failing of word choice here. You see, depending on which element you use your Devastating Infusion, Kinetic Blade, and Kinetic Whip infusions ARE Light or One-Handed Piercing Weapons. Thus, they qualify perfectly well for Swashbuckler Finesse so long as you stick to the right element. And as always you want additional attacks when in melee with these weapons such as from Opportune Parry and Riposte. This is where an easily overlooked part of the Kinetic Blade comes into play, it ends at the end of your turn meaning that unless you are carrying a backup sword or dagger in hand you lose the class abilities when it isn’t your turn. The Kinetic Whip on the other hand doesn’t, and explicitly says you threaten areas around you with your normal whip damage. Similarly, Elemental Annihilator has Ever-Present Threat.

If you want to play a Flaming Jack who Fights with Panache this is the way to do it. It’s a minor dip option, but you get bountiful features for a single level, +1 BAB, Swashbuckler’s Finesse, and Deeds. As archetypes go, Mysterious Avenger can give a bit more validity to cheating in Kinetic Whip, but is mostly a flavor thing. Whirling Dervish is, again, an RP choice. I would virtually never take more than a level as Duelist does that better. Just so you don’t think it’s all good by the colors here, a second level is red.

For archetypes you really only have the Picaroon, but they are a really good dipping archetype. For only the cost of Opportune Parry and Riposte you get a buffet of effective feats for gunslinging. As much as I talk up the deed, that's a mighty fine deal. Compared to Gun Tank you don't get shield and armor proficiencies, but you do have bucklers and this is a safer dip that, depending on your elements, can leave you with still having Finesse. It's possibly good enough to dip into without having a conductive revolver, and that's always a good thing to say about access to an otherwise situational plan. The sword and gun style is perfect for using a free offhand for Kinetic Blades or more exotic blasts. Staring down both a Kinetic Blade and Conductive Revolver in the same round ought to give people a scare. The sole caveat is that piercing damage is explicitly physical and simple blasts are only on Earth and Aether, and you need an energy blast to use Conductive weapons, so make sure to mix your pools.

Swashbucker dipping Kineticist

Okay, maybe I am overvaluing this one a bit, but I find the idea of a Swashbuckler saying “But I am not left handed.” and then summoning a Kinetic Blade in their right hand hillarious. You never have to worry about losing your weapon again! Actually, that’s the worst consideration unless you are going for a mix build. The compelling argument is pretty much everything said about Fighter dipping Kineticist.

Druid (Goliath Druid, Leshy Warden, Treesinger)

“Nature will rise against you!”

Error, file not found. Did you mean Hunter?

Okay, okay, humor aside. The main reason to take Druid is to improve an Animal Ally you picked up. For these purposes Hunter is better. Especially because of Druid’s pickiness with what they wear. The only advantage over Hunter is the ability to have far more options in Archetypes.

Goliath Druid is the poor man’s, or maybe the man that just wants to go indoors sometimes, Mammoth Rider. If you really want to be insane, you can even dip into both and have yourself a far, far too big pet. I’m sure somewhere out there is a Kineticist who can exploit this… If nothing else it’s a very, very big wall between your blasts’ target and you. Meanwhile, Treesinger is simply are you an Elf? Or count as Elf? Do you want plants? If yes, this is your only choice.

Leshy Warden, one of the many Familiar Folio Archetypes. Back in my day we all had Skill Focus and a Eldrich side. But, even then not Leshy as an option. If you want a Leshy as a Familiar this is your only choice. They are pretty good, kinda like already having Improved Familiar rolled in, so I rate this relatively high even though Druids still suck.

Druids dipping Kineticist

Druids and Kineticists aren’t friends. Druids have plenty of their own inherent magic, they don’t need your’s.

Monk: Qinggong Monk (Unchained Monk, Master of Many Styles)

“MORTAL KOMBAT!!!”

I haven’t ever really got stuck in as Monk before. And as such please forgive me if this section is ill informed. To start with though, if at any point in this guide I say Monk assume Qinggong Monk. That’s just how Monks work nowaday. Moving on, even though at higher levels Unchained Monk is flatly better, for a 2 lvl dip the core difference between Unchained and Monk is +1 BAB vs Will saves and archetypes. Let’s go ahead and say I am unimpressed by the Unchained Monk.

In terms of gathering feats the Monk is okay, your short list of selections suck pretty bad, but quantity still beats out over Fighter’s quality. Archetypes such as Far Strike, Flowing Monk, Hamatulatsu Master, Maneuver Master, and Master of Many Styles open up feats you were going to nab anyways and that’s always good. Special shoutout to Master of Many Styles for getting you style feats without prerequisites and ditching Flurry of Blows. Always a good archetype to look at and the one I would focus my attention on. Still, I feel like most builds might prefer the Brawler.

The Elemental Ascetic Monk

We can’t really talk about the Monk without talking about the Elemental Ascetic. Your main reason for looking into dipping Monk is because you want to play an Elemental Ascetic. I truly believe that on it’s own Elemental Ascetic is a weak archetype, but it was clearly intended to be mixed with the Monk (Or other Monkish archetypes like Iroran Paladin). It’s own description calling out how it mixes with Monk for the purposes of AC Bonus. The question then becomes one of how good this marriage actually is.

Well, to start with Sensei is the first archetype most people would look at for your Monk side. Getting almost all your abilities keyed to Wisdom does allow you to pour everything into Wisdom. This is more of a dipping way, 2 lvls, of building the Elemental Ascetic Monk. I’m unconvinced. I still need Str. for damage and it just doesn’t feel like I am being paid back all the features I’m missing here. Also, there is no clause saying you can’t still use Kinetic Blade and Kinetic Whip and you are not getting Wis. to hit or damage on those either. Just because you’re focused on your Full BAB Flurry of Blows doesn’t mean that’s what you should only be doing. Maybe if you got Wis. to hit on your Kinetic Blade and then after that maybe if you got Wis. to Damage from Elemental Ascetic this would be a good plan. But, as weird as it is neither of those happen to be true. Frankly, until an errata changes that I’ll take the advice of Treantmonk's Guide to Monks and focus on Str. with Wis. second. Honestly, though it pains me to say it, Dex. should be like the fourth stat you even consider. (Oh look, Oread finally made it out of orange range.)

Special Note:

Conductive Guided weapons maybe can get you Wis. to Hit and Damage so you can dump Str. But, the wording on Elemental Ascetic and Kinetic Blade is a nightmare for this. Have fun trying to figure that one out and causing your GM to murder their family you sick Min-Maxer. Even in this world Sensei becomes redundant.

Overall, I doubt in Sensei and believe more in a 4th lvl Hamatulatsu Master of Many Styles. (Qinggong Monk should be assumed.) The Hamatulatsu Master gives those extra attacks that always sound good to me with how scaling damage works for the Kineticist. I hate that the first is tied to Kinetic Fist rather than other kinetic weapons, but Hamatulatsu Master’s third Ki ability can also work for when you are breaking out the Kinetic Whip. Drunken Master is an alternate option for Drunken Ki. Master of Many Styles shouldn’t need an introduction. Style feats can be amazing and mixing them more so.

The other option is Unchained Monk. This is less of a dip option and more of a blend option. You want a Style Strike, specifically you want Hammerblow, Elbow Strike, or Shattering Punch. Hammerblow is best for just flat out getting more damage in there, Elbow Strike makes that extra damage non-lethal with the advantage of getting modifiers and disadvantage of being harder to hit. Good way to try and capture someone. Shattering Punch is for when everything being thrown at you resists your element. Fire Kineticists should focus on Shattering Punch hard (Or maybe just not be Elemental Ascetic Monks at all). You also want Ki Powers, of note are Empty Body and lvl 6’s Elemental Fury which can puedo pay you back for all the damage dice you are losing. Your goal more or less is to use Style Strike to BRING THE PAIN BROTHER.

Unchained Rogue (Carnivalist, Ninja)

“Well, I love to one shot mages. I live to two shot priest.”

If you weren’t planning on relying on Extended Range the Rogue can be a surprisingly good companion to the Kineticist. Going back to our list of things to look for, the Rogue does give an extra die of damage, albeit with the necessity of qualifying for Sneak Attack. Unfortunately, that’s a lot more situational. As far as reliable ways to get Sneak Attack goes, that’s one of your main considerations when looking at this dip. Ifrit can be a good way of getting Sneak Attacks in, but they like Snakebite Striker a lot better. Just remember, if you can reliably get Sneak Attack Rogues are amazing! Er, well the Unchained Rogue is anyways. If you are playing with Chained Rogue... Ick.

The key difference between the two Rogues is nabbing Finesse Training. You were going to take this feat anyways. Might as well have it for free. Using the joy of Archetypes, you may now pick out whatever you want to replace trapfinding with. If you are not playing with Archetypes Unchained Rogue and Chained Rogue become much worse.

Unfortunately, now you have to find these archetype gems in the rough. The truth is that most of them are not really gems and you honestly need to have a very solid build idea or an out of combat use good enough to justify the choice. In general Acrobat allows you a buff to a lot of different skills, some of which are useful for you. After that the Thug and Rake both like demoralizing their opponents at the cost of Sneak Attack, but neither gives you anything to reliably Sneak Attack and these traits are behind the curve. Swindlers make for interesting companions to the Kineticist, as they have both a strange form of combat bonus and pair well with an Aether Kineticist who wants to be stealing a lot. Unfortunately, they give up Evasion, one of the better class features.

There is of course the Carnivalist, with Unchained Rogue you can get both Weapon Finesse and a Familiar in the same dip. The Alchemist’s Homunculist is better in terms of a single level dip, but it locks you into a single Familiar choice unless you take Improved Familiar. This choice is still really strong if you want a specific basic friend or 2nd lvl. Especially since after that second lvl your Familiar now has your Sneak Attack. And there are no lines saying your new flanking buddy can’t have Sneak Attack increased from Accomplished Sneak Attacker or another class… I believe the options are Snakebite Striker, Alchemist’s Vivisectionist, Pathfinder Field Agent, Sleepless Detective, Master Spy, Assassin, Deep Sea Pirate, and Greensting Slayer. Not that I am suggesting most of these, the colors by the ratings are specifically for Kineticist Rogues that get reliable sneak damage. For a pure Rogue the colors would be less drab, but at that point you aren’t playing Kineticist anymore and are dropping on BAB. On the other hand, lvl 1 Vivisectionist mixes with Homunculist and then Boon Companion for lvl 7 Improved Familiars, with +2d6 Sneak Attack! For three lvls that might be worth consideration if you truly want a strong pet without an insane sacrifice.

Ninja didn’t need to be Unchained. You get a Ki pool and Ninja Tricks. Ninja Tricks vary in quality, but I would consider the better ones that require Ki to activate *cough*Shadow Clone

*cough* a fair investment. The Ki pool itself is incredible. The ability that sets Ninja apart is being able to make an extra strike at your greatest power for only 1 Ki. With a Kinetic Blade or Kinetic Whip Full Attack this is insanely strong. This synergy makes for a very strong contender in any build.

Unfortunately, if you do this please remember you don’t have the Rogue Talent feature and thus can’t take Extra Rogue Talent for more Ninja Tricks. Unless of course you take Rogue Talent > Ninja Trick > [whatever you wanted]. Ya, it's stupid, but rules are rules.

For Aether Kineticists Rogue gets a bump up in rating. If you want to build a Kineticist to be tricksy, then Rogues are the tricks master. How many lvls you take depends on the features you want.

Of special consideration for the Rogue is a Kitsune with the legendary lvl 4 Scout Ninja. (There are other choices so long as you include Scout, but we are going for maximum damage!) Mixed with a lvl 1 Divine Hunter (Or any full BAB class) and your lvl 13 feat can be spent on Vulpine Pounce, in other words you can have a full round attack charge with your Kinetic Blade that gains a bite and Sneak Attack +2d6 on all attacks. Or you can drop Ninja for Unchained Kitsune Trickster/Pirate/Thug/whatever to nab Weapon Finesse and Dex to Damage on your bite. Yes, this is starting to read more like a basic Rogue guide. But, I honestly like the thought of this, admittedly, intense dip because you are trading 5 lvls for throwing a lot of different kinds of damage die off a charge action. Now, let’s see what it does with the Kineticist in mind.

Making it a charge action gives you the chance to Gather Power at a safe distance and then unleash a Composite Blast and throw on a 1 point substance infusion or use Flash Infusion or leave a point free and get a 2 point substance infusion. One combo is Burning Infusion and Blue Flame Blast… a touch attack setting them on Fire and making your Bite more likely to hit. And why not Bite with Kinetic Fist for free and throw on another 1 point substance infusion.

By comparison at lvl 13 the pure Kineticist can do the same trick using Ride the Blast and Quicken Metakineticist, but you are spending your standing still round just to get the Quicken Metakineticist. If you want to then strike at point blank with a Composite Blast you are going to be hurting yourself. Without spending burn, but with spending a point of Ki, the damage comes down to 11 die thrice + a bite with an additional 4 die vs throwing 7 die and then two hits of 8 die, and assuming the same Bite they also get 3 die off that. For a total of 37 die vs 26 die (Not counting the bite’s own damage). While the purist have an additional rank of Infusion Specification and higher tier choices they are also well behind on their Hit Rolls and without near as many feats. Our multiclasser has gained effectively 5+ important feats and +1 BAB while outpacing their brethren.

Rogues aren’t for every build, but when they are they can be nasty when they are! Pick with causion.

Rogue dipping Kineticist

Do Rogues like having at will powers like ranged fine manipulation or invisibility? Why, yes, as it turns out they do! After having taken a sniff of Aether Kineticist it becomes a hard drug to pass up. The change in gameplan makes it easier for a Rogue to gather up levels of Kineticist than the reverse. You could even argue for a half and half build with an emphasis on doing Roguish things as opposed to Kineticist type of things. Best of all for those of you trying to charm your way to the top before backstabbing you are always armed at will! Say goodbye to having to Sleight of Hand and Quick Draw. Which is good because every level of Kineticist will be hampering your skill points.

Special Note:

Some people see Rogues as weak for lacking in “Combat” capabilities. I would argue that the strength of a good Rogue player is mixing the “Out of Combat” abilities with the “Combat” abilities to position themselves well. Certain Kineticists bare similar abilities and grant lots of “Out of Combat” utility. But, you need a really shrewd mind and willingness to focus on the less mechanically driven side of RolePlaying to be able to make it work. If this sounds difficult or unrealistic in your games, then Rogue Kineticists make a very poor choice as their purist combat abilities do not realistically overlap that much. For by the numbers players, this is a hard red. For those of you navigating games of intrigue and backstabbing, this is fantastic.

Alchemist: Vivisectionist (Homoculist, Winged Marauder)

“Cause I was Jekyll Jekyll Hyde, Jekyll Hyde Hyde Jekyll.”

Let’s be frank here, you are not taking the Alchemist. It’s unfair to rate it, as it doesn’t even enter into consideration. You are taking Alchemist Archetypes. Specifically, you are playing a Vivisectionist. You are almost like a Rogue Alternate class. But with the main gimmick being Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde. In a pinch a well chosen Mutagen can easily save your life. As far as your Sneak Attack goes you can consult the Rouge for tips. Next, you get to make a few minor spells.

A second lvl in Alchemist might be wise merely to give you access to Extra Discoveries. It depends on your build, but let me note that Discoveries such as Extra Arm, or even Tentacles, can be quite powerful if well thought out. The later is a free target for Kinetic Fist after all.

Now, let’s talk about Vivisectionist Archetypes, because quite happily this Rogue Alternate class came with plenty you can mix and match. The ones that stood out to me were Internal Alchemist which dumps a useless feat for the ability to be really, really good at holding your breath. Hey, Water Kineticists can actually make really good use of that! Or anyone who makes someone with Suffocate mad. The second is Oenopion Researcher which gives you about half a discovery in exchange for Poison Use, maybe worth it.

Third we have Homunculist, where you construct a new BFF Familiar. It kinda sucks that you have to get a Homunculus. But as far as single level dips for a Familiar go, this is the best you will get. It throws the most amount of stuff on your character sheet, and some might even be useful. Either way, now you have enough for Boon Companion and Improved Familiar to end up with something like the fantastic Sprite. It’s a low investment way to get a Familiar, but if you want additional lvls it’s not a class to stick with.

The final one is Goblin only, but Goblins are already so good at being Kineticists that’s hardly a problem. Winged Marauder makes you lose access to Mutagens, but it’s all good since they can be a discovery. The Animal Ally you surely got before this dip gets upgraded into being one of two fantastic flying mounts. Hunter

“Nyla! Who's my little, mmm, shnuffly, wuffly?”

Animal Ally allows anyone to get an animal companion. That’s good, but your choices of pet are limited. One cure to this is Monstrous Mount/Monstrous Companion, and if you like one of those Mounts that is probably the best answer. I happen to think that small folk can be perfectly happy with a Worg and bigger folk a Wolf. However, if that’s not your cup of tea nabbing Animal Ally and then Hunter opens up options. That’s all this single level dip is good for. You grabbed it because Druid’s proficiencies read more like the Miranda rights and you want an upgrade for Fido. Did you want a Giant Wasp mount? How about a T-Rex? Dire Bat? It’s all opened up to you. Really, rating this dip is hard because who can put a price on companionship?

Unfortunately, for a dip that you take for options it has a rather large lack of options itself. Your choice is basically binary, Scarab Stalker Hunter or Packmaster. Both are almost equal and will depend on your pet, but I give the edge to Packmaster for having a minute of Dex or Con bonus a day and the ability to divide up levels while losing nothing. If your GM blinks and misses that your buff only lasts a minute than Primal Companion Hunter can be good. Otherwise having an awesome companion for exactly a minute is horrible. Verminous Hunter gives more options, but not options I happen to like.

If you are looking for a companion upgrade, and are not a Goblin, then this is a solid dip giving you enough features to pay you back. The questions are how does it stack up against Mad Dog Barbarian and Wild Child Brawler? Against Barbarian, Mad Dog is +1 BAB. Hunter is, well, literally everything else. As good as BAB is, Hunter just gives so many assorted features including passive buffs to the pet. Not worth Mad Dog in my opinion. Wild Child stacks up better, giving us that +1 BAB and Martial Flexibility. There is literally no drawback to Wild Child on a single level dip, hard to argue with. The two are about equal and will come down to personal preference.

Special Note:

So, wait, why do I want an animal companion? Well, there’s a few answers to this. Not the least of which is that Kinetic Blasts are a standard action. If you only Gather Power as a Full Round Action and don’t take the additional move next turn you end up with a sorta free floating move action. Especially if you already have Supercharge. This leaves you with a free move action during your next turn, mounts are an excellent way to use this. Fire and Aether Kinticists do have an alternate move action they can make, but the other three elements don’t really. An animal companion is basically a constant mount palling around with some major perks. Though, if you are good friends with the party wizard and they don’t mind loaning out their Familiar (Or for certain sorcerers a legit animal companion) Form of the Dragon is a potent spell. The Spell-Sharing Collar and a Familiar of your own can also grant this effect.

All in all, this tactic is very build centric and depends on what you want to do. It’s hard to really judge something as optimal or not when it is so heavily based on what the end game goal is. Self-sufficiency, party synergy, maximum X, maximum Y, so choices like this are presented based on how good they are at the goal of a build that would use them. Not what an ideal Kineticist would be since that’s so subject to interpretation.

There is also an alternative I missed when looking at this class at first. That is to ignore an animal companion and just have the Animal Focus applied to yourself. The Feral Hunter is the best way to achieve this goal. Although generally weaker, certain builds might prefer the Verminous Hunter archetype for different Animal Foci. Of course, if you do that then you need to get your weak level 1 animal companion killed to keep the buff, you monster.

Prestige Classes:

In Pathfinder, Prestige Classes are kinda a joke when you consider the name. It implies they are somehow better, but they were nerfed hard in the change over from 3.5e. In many ways they are a relic, and one I wish was better preserved. Martial Classes get the short end of the stick because Pathfinder has so much that depends on class progression and keeps forgetting the words, “this prestige class counts as levels in X.” I mean, come on guys you worked this out for Brawler. And magic users can have +1 Spellcaster level. Yet, Shadowdancer which relies on ROGUE levels for talents via RaW has no Rogue lvls?

As if it wasn’t bad enough Pathfinder is plagued with completely useless Martial Prestige Classes. I really wish a resurgence of good Prestige Classes was used to improve Martials. Furthermore, you are hurt by most classes not being written with the Kineticist in mind.  Usually things concerning weapons or spells exclude you. It kinda shocks me that wasn’t addressed in Kineticist’s core description for the sake of elemental Affinities if nothing else! As such, you don’t have many Prestige classes to consider, but the ones you do are very interesting…

Duelist

“Hello, my name is Inigo Montoya. You killed my father, prepare to die.”

The Duelist is a case study in what happens when rules never explicitly meant for each other are paired perfectly together. I doubt Paizo was even thinking of Duelist at all when this class was made. While for most classes you end up not being able to take them, the Duelist is special. Same as with Swashbuckler your Devastating Infusion, Kinetic Blade, and Kinetic Whip infusions are definitely Light or One-Handed Piercing Weapons. So long as you remain vigilant about the fact Kinetic Blade ends at the end of your turn Duelist can be a natural marriage. Just be aware you do lose Canny Defense when not wielding a backup weapon that also fits the qualifications.

You can now play a Duelist taking advantage of AoO with a freaking Kinetic Whip or an Elemental Annihilator that absolutely annihilates. This does however limit you to Piercing Damage blasts. Meaning you need either Earth or Aether, though Composite Blasts opens up Water as a choice. Because Aetheric Boost is a weaker Composite choice I would focus on Earth and either stay a purist or mix it with Air. Unless of course you honestly think you can get away with Water’s heavy burn cost. Another option is that you can wait till lvl 15 to nab Duelist levels and get three Elements so you can have Earth, Air, and Water (All the composite Piercing damage!) or Double Water and Aether/Earth or any other element of choice so long as you have Aether and/or Earth and a composite. Even if you don’t wait that long, since you don’t meet the requirements of Duelist until after Expanded Element you can pick which of the Elements in your build to be your main freely.

Remember though, even with the damage die you are losing from playing Duelist your Kinetic Whip’s surprising damage is your main edge. For this reason, I would only take Duelist up to lvl 5. Yet, a Duelist armed with such a mighty weapon makes for a scary concept. You just need to pay close attention to make sure you qualify for everything and don’t get squished. Furthermore, Golbin is already a bright blue racial choice for Kineticists and Duelists, but for you they are practically perfect!

Special Note:

If you want to Prestige into Duelist you are playing a character that’s primarily Duelist, and just happened to start as a Kineticist. As such I recommend reading a Duelist guide first such as this one. Some things change, but others stay the same. Daggers and Starknifes are flatly inferior to your Kinetic Blasts. Single weapon style is just as bad as always and alternate forms of getting in addition attacks are still a good idea for Parry, if not just in general. Kinetic Fist gives a distinct boon to any unarmed or natural attack styles you might choose to go for. Anything that lets you swing more with your Kinetic Whip or Devastating Infusion *cough*Crane Style*cough* is a major consideration.

“You are the dancing queen, young and sweet, only 17 ya ya.”

You just can choose to be Invisible as a standard action. Red Mantis Assassin

Evangelist

“The power of ___ compels you!”

Evangelists are insanely above the power curve, as far as Pathfinder Prestige classes go at least. For the price of daily obedience you get a class with the feature Align Class, after second level you continue to get all features of whatever class you were before. Eh?! What’s the catch? Well, for others the catch is that you get the Hit Dice, base attack bonus, saving throw bonuses, and skill ranks of the prestige class. However, with the Kineticist your class features are everything; the Hit Dice is the same; BAB only costs you at higher levels; you lack slightly in Fort, which you shouldn’t want for anyways; and you miss out on a single level of class features.

Other than that, it is all good. You get more skill points, two class skills of whatever you want, access to Deific Obedience abilities sooner. (You can find Deific Obediences here and a guide here.) Other than that, the bonus in this PrC are very tame until lvl 10, but you don’t have to be awesome when you are getting the ability to get all of your Kineticist features for virtually nothing. Once you hit lvl 10 though, watch out. You get to go Super Saiyan with Spiritual Form. Your form can have a +4 boost to any one stat you pick and you can have an extra feature on top of it. You might not ever get to become the Avatar with Omnikinesis, but you can become something far greater before your capstone point.

Oh, and remember our earlier Kinetic Fist build with Natural Attacks? Your form can easily grant you a rare Natural Attack you otherwise would find impossible to pick up. Does it matter at this point who you worship so long as you worship someone? Yes, you can get even more silly bonuses!

Another option is to use Evangelist to progress another PrC. Would I recommend this? Not particularly. It is a good option for Red Mantis Assassin, but every other PrC is hard to justify 10 lvls of.

Pathfinder Field Agent

“Heh, Disguised Toast.”

The Pathfinder Field Agent is perhaps not a class I would consider for Pathfinder Society Play and is almost not worth mentioning at all. Almost. The reason it rates a mention is that if you want a just Rogue Talents this is the only way I could find out of a single level. You get access to only a single talent, but that qualifies you for Extra Rogue Talent It could be worse. The drunk over there is now a pirate.

Mammoth Rider

“Look at my horse, my horse is amazing.”


Thanks to the wonders of the Animal Ally feat, anyone can be a Mammoth Rider. There is always something nice to be said about a single level dip that allows you to do something otherwise impossible. In this case it would be asking if you would like that supersized. Compared to the Hunter and Druid we have already looked at the Mammoth Rider is Full BAB, and while your options are more limited you get it in Huge with an appropriate stat bonus. This is hardly a PrC to stick with, but it gives something unique for your Kineticist to wade into battle on. It’s a good way to ensure you have a meat shield to charge up for those massive blows. For ranged and defensive Kineticists, it’s better than the Hunter as you have quite a bit more to go through to get to the Kineticist. For melee Kineticists, you will have trouble reaching your target.

Storm Kindler

“Thunderbolts and lightning, very very frightening.”

And you’re sure that Kineticist doesn’t count for +1 Caster level here? Positive? You’re sure the Kineticist can’t become the embodiment of the storm? We’re positive? They make great Duelist, but not Brawlers or Masters of the Storms? A-are you… but… please…

Sigh, Paizo really needs to rework this Prestige Class. Being the master of the storm is an awesome concept for prestige. But this is actually quite meh and if anything Kineticists should be able to do this and gander bonuses more on par with their power level. Hopefuly as material comes out for Kineticists they will get a good Stormcaster to prove the master of the wind and wave.

Pain Taster

“Let the pain speak to me.”

OKAY, this is what I get instead?! Is this humor Pathfinder? Yes, yes the last one was painful I get it.

Sigh, okay. So, this isn’t one you want to stick in for long. But you don’t need to since if you choose to enter it with a Will save it gives you a Con. bonus. And even if you don’t gamble on your horrible Will the ability to flail yourself for more stat bonuses. Yaaaaay- I think. Stat bonuses are always good, and front loaded ones are a great way to get in and then very swiftly out of a PrC to never speak of this again. The catch? You might die. Have fun with that. Your average Kineticist is more likely to end up six feet under for the hubris of the Will roll than nailing that Con. bonus.

Stalwart Defender

“You shall not pass!”

Okay, so this is basically a Barbarian, but for Gandalf roleplaying. I’m… surprisingly happier with this than Barbarians. You have your Fort save split into Will, an AC Bonus, and can freely stand still gathering power for those mighty blasts of your’s. Which, unlike with Rage, you can now unleash. The Con. buff even gives you a higher concentration bonus! I don’t know that I am convinced by the Defensive Powers or class as a whole. It feels more like a single level to me. So long as you have Medium Armor and the feats met it is giving you almost every proficiency there is, an AC bonus, +1 BAB, and even a minor amount of Will.

Funny enough, I think I like this as a follow up to taking Barbarian or Bloodrager. Essentially the same gameplan as the Str. build mentioned in the Barbarian section, but you take get Extra Rage to shake off Fatigue and a second lvl of Stalwart Defender for the same. Now you don’t really need Armored Hulk or Steelblood anymore. Instead your only archetype considerations are trading away Fast Movement without passing on Medium Armor.  That leaves you with the Primal Hunter, the Drunken Rager, or Blood Conduit archetypes. None of them are particularly useful, Primal Hunter is only there for edge cases. But, if you can stomach an extra level being lost and some risk Wild Rager enters into consideration! In which case Drunken Brute would be your only real option for ditching Fast Movement.

I would say that it is definitely useful to have multiple forms of “stances” that you can switch between and compliment a Kineticist’s key stats and timing based gameplay. Just imagine an Earth Kineticist burying in and throwing massive rocks before switching to rage mode to tear you to pieces. It’s a compelling tactic.

In conclusion

When I was writing this guide the standouts for me were anything that let me throw more die with Kinetic Blade. Then Paladin for just being a solid stat block. As much as I prefer Dex., there does seem to be a compelling case for Str. builds. Duelist was the major surprise. Rouge taught us that Kitsune are clearly the best race for Kineticists… Okay, I think Kitsune are the best race for everything so that doesn’t count. Small Race’s ability to ride mounts indoors and just generally pick up a size bonus to everything Kineticist loves with no downsides definitely makes them a fantastic pick. Don’t forget that Tiefling, Aasimar, and Skinwalkers can be small. That really opens up your options if you want to be tiny, even though the best are arguably Goblins and Gnomes. Goblins and Gnomes. Both are super brilliant. But you woul- ♪♫

I spoke a lot about Familiars, so let’s recap. After all this the best way to get a Familiar is probably… to worship Calistria. If not that though, I do legitimately think all the options are worth consideration. Which one fits naturally into either concept or build will probably depend a lot on what you want to do. The worst of the choices is definitely Fighter, but if you want to be spamming dual-purpose feats every 2 lvls, then dipping Fighter can be legitimate. I do rate having a pet relatively highly though for their sheer utility.

Of course, some characters still don’t like dipping. If you want higher level abilities than- well, you should probably be an Evangelist anyways, but other than that it’s good be pure. Most of the builds I would recommend would only shave a few levels off of Kineticist for proficiencies and hit bonuses and such. You should definitely not take this as a guide telling you to take all the things, merely the strongest option for 1-3 lvls worth of gear with rare exceptions such as the Kitsune pouncing build.

Hopefully as time goes on the Kineticist will see more love, and become better as a class because of it. More options means more cool stuff and combos. I would like to especially see more for the Kineticist in PrCs. Like, maybe a Mystic Theurge equivalent for, say, a class that gives you a Phantom. Perhaps one that’s a mount as well. As well as perhaps a “Stormlord” for turning the heavens against your foes. I would also like a Fire Kineticist thing to allow Kinetic Blades to be wielded. Mostly for Ifrit benefit. It’s very weird edge wording that Kinetic Blades are never considered to be wielded.
