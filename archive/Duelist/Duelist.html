Oterisk’s guide to the Duelist

-or How To Be a Fighter Without Being Big and Stupid

“If I ever kill you, you’ll be awake, you’ll be facing me, and you’ll be armed.”- Mal Reynolds

If you take pride with being able to take down an opponent with intelligence and grace instead of imitating the brute beasts that have plagued civilization since the beginning of time, perhaps you could learn a thing or two from a Duelist.  We are the epitome of civilized society with a sense of honor many have lost.  

The Duelist is a fun fantasy trope with some good flavor.  It has been a long favorite of lots of people trying to make it work.  Well, that’s what this guide is for.  This being my second guide for a Prestige Class, It came a little easier.

Overview--

The Duelist is almost exclusively a combat option with a unique flavor and ability.  There are many ways to build one, so you may find some that are vastly different from each other between different weapons and styles.  The damage output of the Duelist is lower than other classes to an extent, but their defenses are pretty good.  Many of the highest AC builds use the Duelist to get there.  In fact, if you look at this as a defensively built fighter, you can do very well.  This guide expects you to stick with Duelist until you run out of levels for it, for most of the abilities get better the deeper you go.

As per the progenitor Treantmonk, this guide will use a 4 color coded scheme to demonstrate the usefulness of various options.

Fighting styles

You mean there is a choice?  Yes.  

Finesse or Strength

Just because you have Weapon Finesse, doesn’t mean you have to use it.  The finesse fighter is stuck with a few more feats to make them a higher damage creature, but a more balanced or higher Strength fighter is not a terrible plan.  Most of this guide was written with Finesse in mind, but I will try to point out a few areas where Strength builds will do well.

Single weapon style.- Orange

This is the style on which the Duelist seems to be based and it works, but barely.  There are other options than this one.  If you are simply taking the Duelist for flavor, this is a good way to go but if you are looking for optimization, and you wouldn’t be if you were not reading this guide, look further.

Two Weapon Fighting or TWF- Blue/ Green

Leaving one hand open doesn’t mean you can’t use other options.  Weapons like the Blade Boot and Spiked armor and Thorn Bracer allow for TWF without filling your off hand.  The best part about it is they are piercing weapons which can be used for Precise Strike as well as being Light weapons so they can be Finessed.  Problem with Spiked Armor is that it does limit your AC capability later on,

Here’s the real trick: there is some heated debate over whether one could use a weapon in their off hand for their parry attack.  Make sure you talk it over with your GM so you can be on the same page.  The short of it is some believe that electing not to take an attack with TWF on your off hand so you can parry is still attacking with your off-hand and so you wouldn’t get precise strike.  Others believe that if you don’t attack, you can still do precise strike.  I lean toward the latter with this because to me, not attacking is not attacking, and duelists in the past often carried parrying daggers.  Also, the Deflect Arrows ability at 9th level says you do not need to have a hand free.  I think this specifically speaks to the writers intent, assuming you would have a parrying dagger in your off hand, but that is my speculation.  As always, talk with your GM about what they expect so you don’t get to 9th or 11th level and get in a fight about it then.

Natural Attack Style- Grange

Claws and bite leave your opposite hand empty and allow you to get some extra attacks in.  A savage or feral Duelist can use their claw and bite for their parry attacks for fun.  I like this because the idea of parrying with your teeth sounds kind of epic.  The problems with this is the -5 you get on your natural attacks when using a manufactured weapon and the difficulty of enchanting natural attacks.  Multiattack would help if your GM allows Monster Feats, but otherwise there are better options.  This is a better option if you go with a Strength build.

Unarmed- Green

With a feat called Hamalatsu or the Snake Style, you can do piercing damage with an Unarmed Strike.  This works well for the Duelist because of the bonus damage for both hands while keeping the other hands free.  The only thing that doesn’t make this blue is that it is more expensive to enchant and the low crit range of Unarmed Strikes.

Thrown Weapons- Green/Blue

Daggers and Starknives for the win here.  The versatility of the weapons allow for melee or distance attacks, and could mean the difference between a round where you get an attack and a round when you don’t.  A case can easily be made that you still get Precise Strike damage with thrown weapons that can be used in melee and so your damage can stay competitive.  Possibly with Quickdraw, Rapid Shot, and TWF, you could get lots of attacks that all benefit from precise strike.  This is basically saying that you alternate hands and each hand is free as soon as it releases its dagger or starknife.  Still, that’s up to your GM on how they want to handle it.

Pistols and Crossbows- Red/Blue

The rules seem to imply that these weapons that can be used in one hand and are piercing weapons do not qualify for the bonuses afforded to the Duelist.  One handed weapons and ranged weapons are in different categories.  I mention this option specifically because your GM might think otherwise.  Don’t try to fool them into believing this is Kosher, but if it fits your groups play style, it might be a load of fun.

Shielded?- Red

Some people think that shield spikes would work for this, but because of the shield, you don’t get precise strike or canny defense if you use a shield, so it’s not that great of a choice.  At all.  Forget it.

Maneuver Specialist- Blue

Various manuevers work well with the concept of Duelist, and can help you gain the upper hand.  The problems with such a build is are that you are going to have to dump a couple extra feats into the mix to make them viable.  And those would be feats that might be of even greater value in the long run.  On the other hand, feats like Fury’s Fall allow you to double dip your Dexterity into tripping, Lore Warden gives you extra bonuses to maneuvers, and Manuever Master allows you a free Maneuver in any full attack.   You could actually be really good at it.  Not to mention every attack after as successful trip is at a +4 bonus... If you are going this route, I recommend the Flying Talon.

Class Features:

Weapon and Armor Proficiency- Red

If you spent 8 levels in monk or other class that doesn’t give you full access to martial weapons to get to duelist, this is situationally good.  If you waited that long to get to the weapon you will use for the rest of your career, I hope you started your campaign after you got your first level in Duelist.  

Canny Defense- Green

I like this ability and I do recommend that you make use of it for the most part, the problem is that it stacks with your Dexterity bonus to determine your maximum dexterity bonuses for armor.  This could be a problem once you get greater access to stat boosting items, but anytime you can add another stat to your AC, its good.  If you took levels of Monk, you have three stats adding to your AC.  This is why I say there are good defensive options.

Precise Strike- Blue

A straight damage bump for each level that multiplies on critical hits.  Since you likely don’t have much strength, this is nice.  Bread and Butter.

Improved Reaction- Blue

No one should complain about bonuses to Initiative.  That they stack makes them fantastic.

Parry- Orange

Giving up attacks for possible defense is not really a good option unless you dump feats into Snake style or something similar that allows return strikes.  Once you get Riposte, it starts looking like a better option.

Enhanced Mobility- Green

It’s not why I took the class, but I won’t say no.  This is great for closing in on large or bigger opponents.  Between this and mobility you get a +8 to your AC against attacks of opportunity from movement.  No reason to be upset about that.  It also removes most of your excuses to get into superior spots on the battlefield to maximize your capabilities.  Get that +2 for flanking.  Get that +1 for higher ground.  More bonuses to hit mean more hits and more damage.  

Combat Reflexes-  Orange

 Its a nice feat to have that you don’t have to buy.  Unless you already did.  Then it isn’t that good at all.  Maybe your GM will allow you to retrain...

Riposte- Blue

Now we are talking, this makes the Parry a worthwhile action.  A chance for them to miss and you to hit them instead?  Yes please.  Too bad it doesn’t stack with things like Crane Wing.  Look below to the feat section for a more thorough explanation.  

Acrobatic Charge- Green

Pretty situational but somewhat useful, this ability is not one that people take the class for.  It is worth two feats though, so that is something.

Elaborate Defense- Blue

Fighting defensively with the Crane style feats, a level 9 Duelist has a -1 to attack rolls and a +6 to AC while getting one negated melee attack per round.  It seems like a reasonable choice  to me.

Deflect arrows- Green

Another nice feat to add to the pile.  They are really stacking up here!

No Retreat- Orange

I suppose it depends on your GM’s style a little, but it beats spending a couple feats on Step Up and Strike when you basically get it for free.

Crippling Critical- Blue

There are some nice benefits to this when you get a critical.  It is much more versatile than some of the critical feats you can get and it can stack with them.  I hope you brought your keen weapon with you today.  

Races:

Dwarf- Grange

The Con bonus and the Wis bonus are good for a Monk build, and the penalty to Charisma isn’t important.  Add to this the darkvision and the bonus to saves and you have yourself a workable Duelist.  

Elf- Blue

Bonus to Dex and Int makes this a great option.  The Negative to Con hurts a little, but with your superior defenses and AC you should be fine.  Not to mention this race has automatic familiarity with Rapiers.  A great option!

Gnome- Red

No bonus to either Dex or Int, this is not a good option.

Half Elf- Blue

Variable bonus and a free exotic weapon feat?  Not a bad start for anyone going Duelist.  The two preferred classes allow you to mix and match even more for a solid choice.

Half Orc- Green

Darkvision and Ferocity are cool, but the lack of a feat edges this race back toward green.  They should like green anyway.  That’s a little racism for you.

Halfling- Green

Strength isn’t that important and neither is Charisma, but the bonus to dex is nice.  The added bonus of being small and getting the additional bonus to hit is nice as is the bonus to saves.  If you have the Halflings of Golarion guide, the jinx might be a fun way to go as well, but not necessary.  A decent choice.

Human- Blue

Humans make good with nearly every class with their variable bonus, extra feat and skill points.  That’s all I have to say about that.

Monster races-

Aasimar- Orange, Nothing good nothing bad.  I suppose it would do in a pinch, don’t choose it if you are rarely offered monster races.

Catfolk- Green, the bonus to Dex is nice and the wisdom doesn’t hurt you if you don’t go monk.  

Changeling- Green, the bonus to wisdom for a monk build and the natural attacks, this could definitely be workable.

Dhampir- Grange for a bonus to dex and um, darkvision?  Oh and they die when your friends try to heal you for the first time.  Well maybe it isn’t that bad, but it also isn’t the best option.

Drow- Not as good as an elf, slightly better than Dhampir.  I’m going green.

Drow, Noble- Remember when I told you not to make one of these a Dragon Disciple?  Make it a Duelist instead.  

Duergar- Same as a Dwarf.  Their enlarge person only hurts you, the immunities are nice though.  Invisibility can be somewhat useful.  I can’t help feeling this isn’t the prestige class for this race.

Fetchling- Same adjustments as a catfolk, same color.  Sure theres a trick you can do with the light, but its situational.  Not up to blue standards.  Green.

Gillman- Orange.  See Aasimar

Goblin- Blue, really blue.  I wanna make one now...

Grippli- Blue if you make a monk, Green if you don’t.  Being small helps with your to hit, and strength is not terribly necessary.  The bonus net proficiency is pretty awesome too.  You also don’t take a hit to your movement rate, which is also nice.

Hobgoblin- Makes a stout duelist, Blue

Ifrit- I wanna make them a firey color, but they are green.

Kitsune- I say Green, but only because you can go with Eldrich heritage with a Cha bonus.

Kobold- Of the few melee classes a Kobold could do, a Duelist is one.  However it doesn’t mean they are great at it.  I wouldn’t mind seeing it though.  Grange

Merfolk- I can see a duelist Merman with a trident, but only in the sea.  Anywhere else it doesn’t work.

Nagaji- Red.  No bonus to Dex, - to Int.  

Orc- Don’t waste a +4 str bonus on a class that doesn’t need it.  Red

Oread- Not a terrible choice.  But not a great one either.  Orange

Ratfolk- Reepicheep FTW!  Blue.

Samsaran- Bonus to Int and Wisdom, this makes a decent Monk build.  Green

Strix-  Bonus to dex and you can fly.  Blue.

Suli- NO.  Go play another class if you want to be this race, there are a fair amount of options

Svirfneblin- I could see this easily- Green

Sylph- Same bonuses to stats as an elf.  Yes.  Blue.

Tengu-  Blue.  Automatically proficient with sword like weapons, a natural attack, bonuses to Dexterity and Wisdom.  You can make almost any build with this race.  

Tiefling- blue in the base form.  Other options aren’t as good I don’t think.

Undine- A decent monk build, Bleen for that.

Vanara- Blue for not having their dump stat in Con

Vishkanya- Green for Dex and free poison.  Can’t do monk well though.

Wayang- Blue, gets a bonus to your primary stats, just don’t make a monk out of them.

You are looking at a 6-8 level build just to get to the Duelist, so here are the options.

Alchemist- Blue 4 Surprisingly good, even really surprisingly good.   Intelligence is used by the Alchemist so it synergizes with Duelist quite well.  Mutagen can amp your Dexterity score and give you a cool natural armor bonus.  If you are looking for a natural attack fighting style, your mutagen can also supply you with a claw and a bite.  Alchemical Allocation can be terribly useful for reusing potions, and some of the discoveries can be quite useful.  On top of that, you can get low level buffs and heals.  

The Vivisectionist is a good way to go because of sneak attack, but I could also see an alchemist with smoke bombs and blindfighting since concealment doesn’t affect critical hits and therefore doesn’t affect your precise strike.

Flavor wise, it isn’t bad.  You can just make him a bit of a drinking duelist, and flavor all of his extracts with Alcohol.  Drinking and Duelists go way back, if you don’t believe me, ask Cayden Calien.

Barbarian 3 or 6- Since this class is all about the Dexterity and Intelligence, Urban barbarian is really the only archetype that synergizes well with finesse.  But it does do that pretty well.  And getting uncanny dodge is a nice boon as well.  The fact that Urban Barbarian and Invulnerable Rager stack, well now that’s just cake. Another decent option is to go with Titan Mauler.  After you get Jotun Grip, you can wield all sorts of two handed weapons as one handed weapons, including reach weapons, but I’m pretty sure that most GM’s would turn their noses at still getting Precise Strike with them while raging, or at least Canny Defense.  The Titan mauler would be a pure strength build, but if you wanted some kind of noble savage, you might be able to make it work.     -Blue

Bard- 8 Bards do not multi-class well.  They don’t prestige class well either, unless the prestige class offers a continuance of some of their abilities.  The Duelist does not.  The Dervish Dancer and the Dawnflower Dervish Archetypes could work well as Duelists, but because of spell progression, more performance rounds, faster performances, and the particular spell lists and the fact you have to invest in Charisma as well make this a difficult switch.  It isn’t as if these archetypes are good on their own, I just can’t think of a good reason to leave that class and become a duelist.  -Orange

Cavalier / Samurai- 3,4,6 The challenge is nice, but the bonus feats don’t really work with the flavor.  The Mount is nice too, but there isn’t really a synergism there either.  If you can find enough feats, Classically duelists don’t do as much with teamwork.  If you do it for the mount, the Horse Master feat is a necessity.  A Sword Saint is a decent Archetype for entrance into duelist, but the rest of the powers of this class don’t.  Could it work, yes.  But you have better options.  - Green

Cleric- Where you can take 8 levels of cleric and get your greater domain powers before jumping into Duelist, I don’t recommend this.  Some of the greatest benefits of the Clerical abilities lies in their spells and the ability to cast in armor.  This is just a bad option- Red

Druid- Animals do not do one-handed piercing weapons and claws are slashing.  No.  -Red

Fighter- Blue.2-6 This class is a great fit for Duelists, you get tons of extra feats, a wider selection of feats, Weapon Training, Armor Training, and the availability of such archetypes as Weapon Master and Lore Warden.  The latter of those two being fantastic for any maneuver focused build.  Also, if you are into style feats, the Unarmed fighter isn’t a bad choice either.

Gunslinger.  If you can get your GM to admit that a pistol is a one handed piercing weapon, this could actually be a really exciting choice.  The language of the rules seems to imply that they are ranged weapons wielded in one hand, and so there might be legitimate reasons to deny this.  In history, Duelists often used pistols, so I could see this being a fun way to go.  Depending on the ruling this one could go either way from Blue to Red.

Inquisitor- There are some good options here, If you go with the Inquisitor as an entrance to Duelist you can get yourself a few pretty awesome options.  Judgement, Monster Lore, Cunning Initiative, Bane and Solo Tactics.  8 levels of Inquisitor also gain you 3rd level spells that you can use for buffing and healing.  Would it be better to continue with Inquisitor?  Probably, but if you want to do something new, a duelist built with Inquisitor levels can be fun.  - Green

Magus- 4-8 a good magus build emphasizes Dexterity and Intelligence.  This makes good synergy for a duelist.  Their low level spells can be very useful, and the ability to keep fighting while casting them can make an amazing Duelist.  Would they be better off staying magus?  Probably, but that doesn’t mean you can’t make the switch.  - Orange

Monk-2,4,8 A dagger can be flurried after 6 levels of Sohei as the thrown weapons group has a lot of Precise weapons, or the Siangham with any build.  Even the monk options that give up flurry are good options.  Master of Many Styles, and Sensei.  Two levels of the Many Styles archetype is all you really need, and you can take 5 levels of fighter for some really good synergy.  Get Snake and Crane styles.  One or two levels of Maneuver Master is also exceptional, especially when combined with Lore Master Fighter.  Either 4 or 8 levels of other archetypes are good, and four combine well with Weapon Master Fighter for a 8th level entry into Duelist.  Sohei is amazing if your GM allows you to flurry with one weapon, otherwise it’s not that great.  -Blue

Oracle-1, 8 Not that great unless you take the Battle Mystery.  Once you get to seventh level, one feat: Extra Revelation gets you the improved version of any Maneuver.  But Charisma isn’t one of your useful options.  It gets pretty MAD, but it wouldn’t be a terrible option since you still get 4th level spontaneous spells, which can come in handy.   Grange.

Paladin-  Most Paladins don’t do well with Dexterity or Intelligence, and would do rather well to stay paladins.  Let them.  On the other hand, the concept of a noble duelist is compelling, and 4 levels of paladin can get you to Oath of vengeance and allow you some slight healing.  You have better options, but this can work.  -Green

Ranger- Favored Enemy, Bonus combat feats, and full BAB might make this a viable option.  A half orc with the toothy trait and aspect of the beast might make a very interesting Duelist.  The Guide Archetype also looks good and the extra skill points don’t hurt.  There are better options, but if you are multiclassing anyway, a dip into Ranger could be useful.  You can use the two handed style to gain power attack with a strength under 13, but that’s only useful if you don’t have access to Pirahna Strike.  -Green

Rogue / Ninja - These skill monkeys lose skill points and sneak attack for a more stand up front line role.  It doesn’t really work that well.  That being said, a 4 level dip isn’t too bad to give you access to some of the Rogue talents / Ninja tricks that could be useful.  Getting Uncanny Dodge is a good idea as well, to keep your Canny Defense bonuses in a surprise round if you roll bad on initiative.  Dagger Duelists with a dip into Knife master are cool.   To be honest, a 3-4 level dip into duelist with an otherwise straight rogue build might be a better option, as many of the early bonuses can really help out.  If you do a three level dip after a one level dip into shadow dancer, you don’t lose any BAB at all.- Grange

Sorcerer- All options are not good.  Want sorcerer flavor?  Go Eldrich Heritage.  - Red

Summoner- 4  Can you make a Synthesist with 4 natural piercing attacks and a knife in one hand?  Yes, but you might as well just keep going with your Synthesist build for more evolutions and ability.  This is not the place for you.  The one thing that would make this viable is getting a wand monkey for buffs.  4 levels is all that is necessary for that, and then they give the shield bonus when they are within reach.  

Witch- Some of the Hexes might be useful, and cackling during a fight might be a lot of fun, but you are pretty limited here.  Best to stay away.  -Red

Wizard- A magus is a better option in every conceivable way when moving toward Duelist.  The Scrollmaster Archetype could possibly be orange, but EXPENSIVE.  -Red

Feats:

You are going to find that you have many more options than you have feats.  I recommend sticking to one or two options and running with those.  This is one of the reasons being a fighter is such a blue option, those extra feats can be quite golden.  

Agile Maneuvers- Red.  This isn’t very useful because you will probably do all of your Maneuvers with your weapon, and since you are automatically going to have Weapon Finesse, this feat would be worthless to you.  

Blind-Fight-  Not a bad option if you have the feats to spare.  But it might be better to get goggles of darkvision or a potion of See Invisibility for emergencies.  As an entry to Moonlight Stalker, it’s not that bad.  Having concealment and a bonus to hit can be really nice.  I prefer my duelists to be a bit more honorable than that, but nothing says they have to be.  Grange

Combat Expertise- If you don’t go with maneuvers, it might still be a good idea since it does stack with fighting defensively and becoming untouchable is sometimes preferable to hitting more often.  I know that some will cry blasphemy at such a statement, but we need to remember that we are part of a team, and having options is better than being a one trick pony most of the time. Green

        Improved Dirty Trick, Disarm, Feint, Reposition, Steal, Trip- Maneuvers can be a mixed bag.  They require some feats to be effective and you may find yourself a bit hungry because a good trip build can can be feat intensive in and of itself.  You will find that fighting things on the ground is a bit easier.  If you go this route, make sure to get Fury’s Fall, and double your Dex bonus to trip, and then hit them while they are down.

Combat Reflexes- Orange  I like it and would rate it higher, but you get it for free later on.  It kind of makes it rough until then, but you should manage.  If your GM allows you to retrain it when you get it, it might be a decent idea to get it earlier, otherwise just wait.

Critical Focus- not a bad feat, but I wouldn’t bother unless you have a weapon with an 18-20 crit range.  Oh, you do?  Then I would recommend it.  It is nice to get access to all those critical feats as well.

Deadly Aim- Great for Dagger Duelists, pretty meh for anyone else.  Although it’s not a bad way to increase damage if you have to do ranged combat.

Dodge- Um, Necessary to take duelist, but isn’t bad.

        Mobility- Also Necessary, helps you get into position.

        Underfoot- A really strong option for small Duelists, +4 to acrobatics checks when moving through threatened squares and an additional +2 to AC.  That stacks up to +10 to ac against attacks of opportunity when moving through threatened areas that stacks with all the rest of your AC bonuses.  

Exotic Weapon Proficiency- If you have the feats to spare, or you just want to play a half elf, this is a good way to get some good exotic flavor in with your Duelist, as well as getting some cool options.  It isn’t necessary, but it can be fun.  - Green

        Net and Trident- Despite what it says, you don’t have to use a Trident with it.  It also frees up your off hand for precise strike.  It isn’t optimal, but it is an option.

Horse Master- your best bet if you want a mount for your duelist.  4 levels of cavalier, and 6 ranks of ride, and you are set.

Improved Critical- a fine feat, but one that can be duplicated through the keen weapon enhancement.  It depends on how much money you have to be honest.  Feats are always expensive.

Improved Initiative- Going first is kind of nice, so you can prepare your defenses.  Otherwise it might be tough on you.

Improved Unarmed Strike- A fine plan for a duelist, I have seen quite a few throw a kick or an elbow in some pretty epic fights.  Of course, the best way to get this is through a level or two of monk, and as the gateway to the style feats, this is a real fine option otherwise.

        Hamatula Strike- One way to get piercing damage through unarmed strikes, Snake style is better.

        Boar Style- thematically works with snake style’s piercing damage, and bleed is nice, but I wouldn’t bother unless you get Master of Many Styles.

Crane Style- Defensively, this is the cream of the crop.  You can do this and do your duelist parry and riposte at the same time, which is awesome.  Just remember, you can get an attack of opportunity with the deflection from crane style, and an attack of opportunity from the parry from your Duelist class.  But you don’t get two attacks of opportunity from the same defensive move.  But with this you can get out of two attacks in the same round.   That’s nice.

        Kirin Style- Turn Intelligence into damage?  Sounds great.  If only it didn’t take three feats and three rounds to do it.  It might be a fun concept build though, and since you are defensively minded, it could be fun.

        Janni Style- Once you get tempest, you can use it to stack bonuses to a trip or something.  If you really wanted to focus on trip, this isn’t a bad plan.

        Snake Style- Piercing damage through unarmed strikes, retaliatory strikes, this is good.

        Snapping Turtle- You may not get a shield bonus otherwise, Don’t know if it is worth it.

Iron Will- Your will save won’t be great, it wouldn’t be a bad idea to give it a little boost.  

Lunge- not a bad feat at all, gives you much greater range and ability to full attack, but you have the mobility to get in on the big guys without as much of a fuss, so it may not be entirely necessary.

Nimble moves- I like this feat, because I seem to always run into rough terrain.  If you don’t, I wouldn’t bother.

Point Blank Shot- If you are going Dagger Duelist for ranged attacks, this is a feat tax to get the really good stuff like Rapid Shot and Precise Shot.  Although +1 to hit and damage within 30 feet is pretty awesome too.  If only you didn’t have to enchant all of your thrown daggers...

Power Attack- A good plan for some extra damage, although Piranha strike is a better bet if you are using a light weapon and not a rapier or other one-handed piercing weapon, so you don’t need a 13 Str in that case.  But alternatively, using it as a prerequisite for some of the Assault Feats might actually be a fine idea as well.  Dazing assault being one of my favorites.

Quick Draw- an absolute necessity for thrown weapons, otherwise, just keep it out and don’t get disarmed.

Step Up- Not a bad feat, but you do get something similar to this later, and doubling up on it may not be worth your while, even when considering this as an entry feat for the further ones down the chain.  Although putting them all together might make you pretty darn tenacious, there are other feats that would be better to pursue.

Two Weapon Fighting- I would recommend this for sure, at least to not slow down the rest of your attacks and parry with your offhand.  Check out my section above on two weapon fighting for more details.

Weapon Finesse- Absolutely necessary.

        Dervish Dance- the earliest entry to Dex for damage, works for Duelists, a good one.

Weapon Focus- If you have an extra feat, the +1 to hit will always be useful.

        Weapon Specialization- Taking more than 4 levels of fighter makes this available, and since you are low on damage, it isn’t a bad idea.

        Dazzling Display- I wouldn’t bother, it’s not like it isn’t fun to scare everyone, it’s just that it might be easier for someone who focuses on Strength and Charisma to do it.  Although Shatter Defenses and Deadly Stroke are so much fun.

        

Weapons-

Unarmed Strike + Hamatulatsu or Snake Style - Green

Just because your other hand is empty doesn’t mean you can’t use it.  Any Duelist should seriously think about picking this up for a pinch or when you run out of weapons.  The problem with this is that you can’t really use this with a weapon in your other hand, so in order to get your damage you have to invest entirely into unarmed attacks.  It does go a bit against raw to think that you can do piercing damage with a kick or a knee or a headbutt, but if you don’t mind it, you can have your cheesecake and eat it too.  I think there are better options though, but you can mix this in with any style of Duelist pretty well.

Dagger - Blue

The best part of this weapon is versatility.  Not only does it have a decent crit range, but it can also be thrown if you have difficulty closing into melee.  Duelists abilities still work with piercing Melee weapons that can be thrown weapons oddly enough, so it can be a really great choice.  With this you can actually pick up rapid shot and deadly aim and have some fun from a distance.

Spiked Gauntlet- Red

This fails on both flavor and mechanical reasons.  Don’t do it.

Blade Boot- Blue

A sneaky way to get TWF and leave your second hand free.  Good for evil or chaotic duelists.

Gladius- Orange

A short sword for people who like Gladiators.  Mechanically similar to a short sword, but you won’t find as many.  I wouldn’t bother.

Pick, Light- Green

Its the x4 modifier that makes this a cool weapon.  Your extra damage for Precise Strike multiples on a crit, so it really becomes a fantastic fun thing.  It’s a very different weapon stylistically from your normal duelists, so it gives them a distinctive flair.

Spiked Armor- Orange

Another way to get TWF without filling your off hand.  Not as cool as the Blade Boot though.  The problem lies in later levels when armor might not be a good idea because of max dex with most armors.  Oddly enough, it can be finessed.

Star Knife- Green

The x3 modifier is kinda cool, but less than optimal.  But the weapon itself reeks of flavor.  The fact that it can be thrown well is also a boon, and the distinctive weapon makes it good fun.

Short Sword- Green

better damage dice than daggers, but less versatile.  

Rapier- Blue

This weapon is what the class was built for.  High Crit range one handed weapon.  You can’t really go wrong.

Scimitar- UberBlue

Dear Scimitar,

With Dervish Dance, all your dex are not only bonuses to hit, but to damage as well.  Because you are the only weapon that can do this it actually makes you the best weapon of all if I have the feats to spare.  Sure all the other weapons can have the agile quality, but that gets increasingly more expensive the higher the enchantment gets.  Not everyone loves you, but I can’t say no...

Aldori Dueling Sword- Red

In order to get this and still enter duelist at level 7, you have to use all of your feats and be a human or half elf for the extra feat and convince your GM to let you take one of your prerequisite feats the same level you enter Duelist.  It’s crazy.  It’s mechanics aren’t that bad, but all those feats aren’t really worth it for me.

Flying Talon- Blue

Reach and special weapon properties make this a good choice. This is actually the only weapon that gives a +2 to the trip maneuver.  It also seems to give a melee reach of 10 feet as well as being able to be thrown.  If it had a better critical, I would recommend it for every build.

Butterfly Knife- Orange

You spend a feat to get a +2 to hide a dagger on your body.  You don’t find many of these in the world that you can just pick up.  Not worth it.

Katar- Red

Sure they look cool, but mechanically identical to the light pick and they take a feat to use.  Pass

Quadrens- Orange

As cool as a dagger, but with bleed damage on a critical hit.  This isn’t that good at higher levels and is probably not worth the exotic feat.

Thorn Bracer- Blue

Probably the best option for TWF if you have the feats to spend.  Dual wielding Thorn Bracers would be a cool visual.  They would be similar to the unarmed style mentioned earlier, but would be easier to enchant.  Having both hands free to throw daggers would be cool too.  If they had a good Crit I would recommend them for anyone.

Wakizashi- Blue

As good a crit range as the rapier with the ability to do Slashing damage too.  Not a terrible choice, especially if you get it for free.

Strength options:  Under construction

Morningstar- Not the best option, but it is the best damaging simple piercing weapon.  It will do in a pinch, or when you are fighting skeletons.

Short Spear- a pretty inferior weapon, but with one cool benefit.  It can be thrown.  

Sword Cane- Red

Only good for sneaking into places that don’t allow weapons, bad crit.  Also doesn’t work with weapon finesse.  Very situational, but cool flavor though.

Trident- Slightly higher damage but just as useful as the spear.  Is it just me or should this have a x3 critical?

Klar- Since this functions as a shield and as a weapon, you will have to have a good chat with your GM about the synergy of this one.  I generally think that since it functions as a shield, you can’t precise strike with it, which kind of ruins it for me.

Pick, Heavy- Got to love that x4 crit.  The light pick is a bit cooler because you can finesse it, but this is a solid choice for the strength build.

Scizore- A really cool weapon, doesn’t count as a shield, but grants a shield bonus.  It might actually be a fine secondary weapon for that very reason.  Since it isn’t considered a shield, you also might not be able to do things like attack with it and defend with it in the same round with Improved Shield Bash, and you may not be able to magically enhance the Shield bonus either.  Make sure you talk it over with your GM if you go this route.  In the case that it would be treated by the same rules as the Klar, it might not be that great other than the pretty awesome damage dice it does.  

Shotel- Exotic weapon feat for pretty much nothing: a complete waste.  That is unless you find some amazing intelligent dragon slaying disrupting defending + 12 or something like that.  Then maybe.

Sibat- I really don’t see it being much better than a spear, and certainly not good enough to be green, but I could see it being useful against spellcasters or easily grappled foes.

Armor

Armor is a decent option in several cases: when you have low stats, you have a good flavor reason for Silken Ceremonial, or when you have access to Celestial armors.  Excepting the eastern light armors and the bracers, the best Celestial armors can be maxed out after you get less restricted access to stat enhancements.  It is a pretty good and inexpensive way to keep your AC up at lower levels for sure.  Eventually, you will probably want to trade it out.  If you are using Armor for Armor spikes and TWF, you might want to invest in a magical Thorn Bracer instead.  Of course, all this is out if you want to be a monk, which is a blue choice when it comes to defence.  Mostly because they can still use bracers, which aren’t a bad plan to begin with.

If you go the armored route, it may actually be more beneficial for you to not put a whole lot of money into amping your stats because you will run out of beneficial space eventually.  Just think, the difference between a +4 Int headband and a +6 Int headband can get you a set of Padded +4 and buy a couple of potions.  Make sure you figure out your exact bonuses to maximize your specific potential.  

Armored Kilt- This isn’t a bad way to get started if you are pulling more of a tank role.  If your stats are low or you have a good access to movement boosting items, you can use this to augment your AC for cheap.  Also, the concept of spiked Armored Kilts... yeah.  That’s all I have to say.  After you start taking levels of duelist, this will become obsolete pretty quick.

Padded- Cheap, but you get what you paid for.  This armor does have one advantage over quilted, it’s 95 gp cheaper.  

Quilted Cloth- virtually identical to padded only it costs more.  

Leather-  Not a bad starter set of armor, it might last quite a while.  

Haramaki- Some people have a gripe about asian weapons always getting better statistics than any other culture’s weapons.  This is the armor version.  Both the Haramaki and Silken Ceremonial armors have no Max Dex adjustment, and so both are great options.  

Silken Ceremonial- This here is pretty much the same thing mechanically as the Haramaki only looks more awesome.  

Studded Leather- Also possibly a good starter if you don’t have a maxed out Dex.  Won’t last as long as leather due to lack of Max Dex allowance.

Mithril Chain Shirt- Same Max Dex as Leather, but two better on the armor bonus.  Once you can afford it, this is great.  Later on, it might cause some trouble though.

Celestial Armor- Max Dex of 8, Armor of 6.  This is a pretty sweet armor, but at high levels it can actually still cramp you a little.  Armor training and Sash of the War Champion can help, but if you start with a high point buy, or you get into the Inherent bonuses from wishes, you can outgrow this, easy.

Celestial Plate Armor- Max Dex of 6, Armor of 9.  Better for defense, but since Dex is also your to hit stat, it actually limits you pretty good there.  A first level Urban Barbarian can have a dex bonus of +7, the window of when you can afford this and the time it stops being useful is pretty narrow.  

Celestial Armor Enchantment?  This may be an option if you can have some armor custom made (and you might have to actually be good).  This is something you need to talk to your GM about, because there is some debate on whether or not you can mess with Special armors or apply them to other kinds of armors.  There is also debate on whether or not you can make these out of Mithril.  I’m for both, but your GM may not be, and that’s where the buck stops.  

Bracers- Slightly more expensive than Leather +5, but you don’t have to worry about that annoying Max Dex penalty.  But no armor spikes, no enhancements that cost gold instead of a +? bonus and it costs more.  But it stacks with the Monk’s wisdom bonus, which is nice, and maybe you can find a nice GM that will let you put flat GP enhancements on them.  Maybe.

Magical Equipment

Gloves of Dueling- An essential if you have levels of fighter.  At higher levels you get deflect arrows anyway, and a static +2 to hit and damage is absolutely a good choice.

Sash of the War Champion- chest slot, so that’s nice, cheap, and boosts your Max Dex (if you have Armor Training and bravery bonuses by 1.  I wish there was a greater version of this.  If you go straight fighter and don’t give up your armor training, this is a real good option.  Celestial Padded with this can get you a +12 Max Dex, Which should do the trick until pretty high levels.  

Weapon enhancements-

+’s- Nothing’s wrong with more pluses, but they shouldn’t be your first priority.  Of course they should at least be your third priority.  Agile, Dueling, and +’s in that order, because you are going to need +’s to overcome certain DRs.  Of course, if you just make a new sword out of Adamantine, that’s not a bad plan either.  

Bane- If you know what you are fighting, its a good plan, Otherwise, it doesn’t matter that much.  Dagger duelists might like a couple of +1 bane weapons for creatures they fight a lot because they are cheap and do some nice extra damage.

Defending- If you need more AC (and I sure hope you don’t) this is a good option later on after you get higher +’s

Elemental Damage- Not a bad plan for extra hurt for the baddies, but I would only make it an priority if you were fighting a lot of things susceptible to a particular element, like if you were in “Legacy of Fire” or something.

Ghost Touch- A backup weapon with this is better than half damage for incorporeal.  Pick one up if you can.

Keen- Saves you a feat, costs some money.  Not a bad plan for the builds that don’t have the feats to spare.

Throwing- possibly good for a Dagger Duelist, make sure you use it on your favorite dagger.  If there were a way to throw the same weapon multiple times in a round, I would go with that.

Alignment- A better option than Bane, because it covers more ground.  Having both is pretty awesome too.

Elemental bursts- Not a big fan of these, they seem not quite worth the money.

Wounding- For a +2 bonus, I would expect something more.  Although if you have a two weapons with this, that bleed damage could definitely add up.

Dancing- a cool option if you have a backup, make sure you talk with your GM about whether or not the weapon gets Precise Strike, can Power Attack, etc after you loose it.  With a +4 cost, I would have to say yes, but it might be a bit fuzzy RAW.

Dueling- I like the one not from the field guide, +4 to Initiative is pretty sweet.

Agile- If you didn’t go Dervish Dance, this is your ticket to inflicting pain.  Enjoy
