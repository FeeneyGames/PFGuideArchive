Frequently Asked Questions

So, what is this?

        Hello, friend! This is a compendium/optimization guide for Pathfinder feats.

Pathfinder? What’s that?

        I’m glad you asked! It’s a pen-and-paper roleplaying game based on Dungeons and Dragons, 3.5 edition. You can find all the rules completely legally and free here and here.

Why would you rate feats?

        Well, there are an extraordinary number of them (well over 100), and they are of greatly varying value, from worthless (or even detrimental) to incredibly overpowered. Beyond that, making a character (whether a player character or an NPC) is a time-consuming process. This guide is meant to give GMs and players a quick reference as to whether a feat is worthwhile.

I don’t agree with your rating of a feat!

        It’s completely possible I’m wrong. You can comment right here on the Document by selecting the relevant text you disagree with, right clicking, and selecting “Comment.” I’ll also be posting a thread on Giant in the Playground for discussion.

What are these “Homebrew” notes?

        Some feats are badly broken, whether being strictly inferior to another feat, or game-breakingly powerful. Sometimes I’ll suggest a quick fix of my own devising for the adventurous. If you try out these homebrew fixes, please let me know how they worked out. If they’re bad, I don’t want to leave them up, and if they work well, it’ll stroke my ego.

How will you be rating these feats?

        You really know the right questions to ask, anonymous stranger! My rating system will be as follows:

Excellent***** - An amazing feat. Often the foundation of entire builds. So good, any build can make use of it; or, possibly, a requirement for a class or build  to work properly.

Very Good**** - A solid feat. You’re going to have a hard time going wrong with a feat like this.

Good*** - A nice feat. It may only be useful if you build around it, it might only provide a small (but important) bonus, or it might be a situational feat for a quite common situation.

Fair** - A decent feat. It’s certainly not bad, per se, it’s just there’s usually something more important to pick up. It might be useful for a niche build or worthless outside of a narrow set of circumstances.

Poor* - A bad feat. Don’t take it. Try to convince others not to take it. It’s probably directly outclassed by another feat, and there are plenty of better choices out there. A wasted feat slot.

Magical Feats and Where to Find Them

 

        Feats are one of the most important ways to define who your character is and what your character does - slightly less important than your choice of class, and rather more important than race in terms of actual game mechanics.

        There are a variety of ways to acquire feats:

  • First and foremost, you gain a feat every odd level (including first level). This means that at level 20, every character will have a minimum of 10 feats. This may seem like a lot when you’re first starting out, but soon, you’ll be struggling to put everything you want into one build.
  • Your choice of race can grant a feat. Humans, for instance, gain one bonus feat of their choice at first level, while Half-Elves gain Skill Focus for a skill of their choice. Races that grant bonus feats are almost always a solid choice.
  • Some classes grant bonus feats, such as the Fighter, Monk, and Sorcerer. Classes that grant bonus feats almost always place additional restrictions on what feats can be chosen, but may also ignore the usual restrictions placed on feats (such as with a Ranger’s combat style feats).
  • Some class features can be used to temporarily pick up feats, such as the War Domain Weapon Master ability.
  • Very rarely, some magic items may grant the benefit of a feat.

        Some good dips for feats:

  • Monk 2 - Grants two feats off of a limited list as bonus feats, ignoring the usual prerequisites. In addition, you snag Improved Unarmed Strike and Stunning Fist. Make sure to check the monk archetypes for goodies, as they often have different lists of bonus feats. Master of Many Styles stands out in this regard.
  • Fighter 2 - Two combat bonus feats. Fairly useful for quickly burning through prerequisites for a feat.
  • Unbreakable Fighter 1 - Endurance and Diehard in one level. Probably the only way I’d ever recommend getting Diehard.

Core Rulebook

        -Acrobatic* - Boosting your Acrobatics check can be good for avoid attacks of opportunity, but if you’re really worried about that, take Skill Focus (Acrobatics).

        -Acrobatic Steps** - Not terrible, but usually Nimble Steps covers most of what you would need it for (i.e. 5 foot step in difficult terrain).

        -Agile Maneuvers** - (Com) - It’s a pretty decent feat for Dexterity-heavy builds. The main problem is that Weapon Finesse allows you to use Dex for CMB for trip, disarm, and sunder, and trip is probably the best combat maneuver (with disarm being not half-bad either). If you want to do a Dex-heavy grapple build, this is a must.

        -Alertness** - Perception is the most rolled skill in the game. Still probably not enough to take this feat, but if you are forced to take one of the +2/+2 feats, this is probably the top choice.

        -Alignment Channel* - It has a tiny spark of usefulness if the campaign you’re in is focused heavily on one type of outsider (i.e. you’re in the World Wound fighting evil outsiders 24/7), but you’re still probably better off just using spells.

        -Animal Affinity* - The +2/+2 feats are just bad.

        -Arcane Armor Training* / Arcane Armor Mastery* - (Com) - These are kind of cool in theory, but your swift action is just too important to waste on these, especially for a caster. If you’re in a very low level campaign, you’re sure you won’t need your swift action, and you’re already getting armor proficiency from another source...it’s still probably not worth it. Mage armor exists for a reason.

        -Arcane Strike** - (Com) - I’m probably overvaluing it, but for a core-only melee Bard, I could see this being neat. The full casters shouldn’t be swinging a weapon, and the Magus has much better things to do with its swift actions.

        -Armor Proficiency (Light, Medium, Heavy)** - Your class probably provides as good armor as you can benefit from, with two main exceptions: The cleric and psionic classes. The cleric’s heavy armor proficiency was stripped from it in Pathfinder, but you can easily replace it with this feat. Manifesting isn’t limited by armor, and most psionic classes don’t have any class features interrupted by armor, so add an extra star for them.

        -Athletic* - It’s bad, even for a +2/+2 feat. In a world full of magic where there are plenty of ways to fly, teleport, walk on walls, or breathe underwater, being able to swim and climb well is a little underwhelming.

        -Augment Summoning**** - Summoning is already a powerful strategy, so summoning creatures that are more accurate, hit harder, and are tougher to kill is a nice bonus.

        -Bleeding Critical*, Blinding Critical***, Deafening Critical**, Exhausting Critical**, Tiring Critical**, - (CRT), (Com) -

        -Blind-Fight*** - (Com) - Pretty helpful. Concealment is one of the best defenses, so anything that makes it easier to penetrate those defenses is useful.

        -Brew Potion** - (IC) - Though generally considered the worst of the item creation feats, it still is moderately useful - though mostly just if you get it for free, like the Alchemist does.

        -Catch Off-Guard** - (Com) - Very situational. Can occasionally be useful for a Rogue/Ninja with a reliable disarming option, but in reality, you’re rarely going to get to use this feat.

        -Channel Smite ** - (Com) - The main problem for positive-channeling Clerics is that it’s only going to work on undead - and you’ve already got plenty of ways to deal with undead. Add a star if you channel negative energy, you’ll have plenty of targets to channel against.

        -Cleave** - (Com) - One of the very few area of effect abilities for melee, but sadly saddled with an AC penalty and the requirement that both targets be adjacent to each other (and not simply within reach).

        -Combat Casting*** - It’s worth looking at for any spellcaster. If you’ve got a feat open, it’s worth considering, especially with the changes to concentration in Pathfinder.

        -Combat Expertise** - (Com) - It’s a prerequisite for an enormous number of feats, including some of the best combat feats in core (such as Improved Trip), but it’s not that great in and of itself. If your GM will consider 3rd party material, consider taking Martial Power from Path of War, as it can function as Combat Expertise.

        -Combat Reflexes*** - (Com) - By itself, it might be only two stars, but the more you put into it, the more you get out of it. Try to pick up things like reach and things that grant you attacks of opportunity to really let this feat shine.

        -Command Undead**** - The potential to have your cleric level in HD worth of minions makes this a very tempting offer.

Advanced Player’s Guide

        -Additional Traits*** - Depends heavily on how you use it. A trait is approximately equal to half a feat, but can obviously be worth more or less. Traits are a whole ‘nother mess, but I will confidently point you to Dragonnamedrake’s excellent Guide to Traits.

        -Allied Spellcaster** - (TW) - It’s not worthless, but, like most teamwork feats, it depends heavily on your allies. This feat encourages you and your allies to prepare multiple copies of the same spell, which is generally the opposite of what a balanced party wants (i.e. normally, each character should be bringing something different to the table). It’s much better for NPC spellcasters, as they’re likely to be 90-100% identical, so their prepared spells will be the same.

        -Arcane Blast** - It’s more damage than you’d normally get out of a 1st level spell slot, but you can’t even pick this up until you’re 10th level. By then, you should have plenty of 2-5th level spells to use without needing to dig that far back for a measly 3d6 damage. If you’re burning higher level spell slots, you’re crazy - just prepare scorching ray for multiple 4d6 rays. It only gets worse the higher up you go.

        -Arcane Shield* - By the time you get this, you should have a constant deflection bonus to AC (such as from a ring of protection). There are so many better defensive spells, heck, almost every spell ever printed is better than this, and you’re burning it and your immediate action for a minor bonus to AC for a very short duration. If you’ll allow me one Treebeard quote, “A wizard should know better!”

        -Arcane Talent* - There are plenty of nifty and fun uses for this feat, but then you turn level 2. A level 0 wand is only 375g for fifty uses.

        -Aspect of the Beast*** - Limited in who can take it - basically, if you’re not a Druid, Ranger, or lycanthrope, you’re probably out, but it’s a feat for two claws. Probably only natural weapon-focused Rangers are only ever going to take it.

        -Bashing Finish**** - (Com) - It can’t come online until level 11, but it’s one of the few bright spots for sword-and-board fighters. A free shield bash with every critical hit is nice, especially when there’s no limits on it - it doesn’t require an action, it can happen any number of times per round, and it can trigger itself if the shield bash crits. If you’re going sword-and-board, you have to take this.

        -Bloody Assault* - (Com) - If it wasn’t such an absurd penalty, it might be passable, but -5? No, **** you, Paizo. Bleed effects are already hamstrung from the get-go, there’s no need to make them laughably bad. Homebrew: Change the first sentence to read “Whenever you use your Power Attack feat, your melee attacks inflict 1d4 points of bleed damage, in addition to the normal damage dealt by the weapon. This effect can stack an additional time for every 4 points of base attack bonus you have, to a maximum of 6d4 bleed damage at base attack bonus +20.”

        -Bodyguard*** - (Com) - I used to think this feat was worthless until I read a post on a martial tanking guide explaining how to use it. The short answer: Combine it with something that boosts your aid other bonus (such as the Helpful trait), and you’ve got a clever little way of significantly boosting an ally’s AC.

        -Bouncing Spell**** - (MM) - Re-casting a failed spell with a swift action is both excellent action economy and good resource management, especially when it only increases the spell’s level by 1. A top-tier metamagic choice.

        -Breadth of Experience** - Making Knowledge checks untrained is decent, and bonuses to a bunch of fairly useful skills is cool. Still not worth it for most builds, but you can definitely do worse.

        -Bull Rush Strike**, Disarming Strike**, Repositioning Strike**, Sundering Strike*, and Tripping Strike*** - (Com) - You can only apply one of them to any given critical strike, and, as usual, trip is the all-round best combat maneuver. The others are still situationally useful, but you’re almost always going to get the most out of tripping. Especially cool with these feats: You get to add bonuses to critical confirmation to a quasi-CMB check.

        -Charge Through*** - (Com) - A difficult-to-find effect with a nice bit of action-economy to it. Especially useful if your GM (or players) like putting weak enemies or summoned creatures to block charges.

        -Childlike** - It’s riding the 1-2 star line.Taking ten on skill checks can be very useful, but it’s a very rigidly defined set of circumstances.

        -Spider Step*** and Cloud Step**** - They come online a little late, but you can’t deny their usefulness. Permanent quasi-flight, quasi-water-walking, and quasi-wall-walking is hard to beat; I honestly think these should just be class features for the Monk. Note that these feats actually give a minor purpose to the Monk’s slow fall.

        -Cockatrice Strike* - (Com) - This is like the definition of a bad feat. You can’t pick it up until your base attack bonus is +16, but the main class that would actually use it (the Monk), never actually reaches base attack bonus +16. [Errata eventually dropped the requirement to a base attack bonus of +14; it still comes way too late and is completely useless]. It requires several feats, it requires a full-round action (for only a single attack at a minimum of level 16 14) against a foe with a limited number of conditions. That single attack has to critically hit, and, after all that, they still get a saving throw. For one last kick in the knee, it’s a supernatural polymorph affect, so it won’t work in an antimagic field and some creatures will be immune to it (that aren’t already immune to petrification or Fortitude saves). I would definitely nominate this for “Worst Feat of the Advanced Player’s Guide,” and it’s in the running for “Worst Paizo Feat Ever.”

        -Combat Patrol** - (Com) - It’s certainly not unusable, but losing your full round action hurts, not to mention the fact that your movement provokes attacks of opportunity. Can be a little bit of fun at high levels when you’re getting +15 to +20 feet of reach.

        -Cooperative Crafting* - Skip. It’s just too much of an investment for the minor bonus it provides. Homebrew: Change it from a feat to a trait.

        -Coordinated Defense** - (Com), (TW) - The only reason you should consider this is if you’re playing an Inquisitor or Cavalier who’s getting a teamwork feat anyway. As a player, your opponents will quite often be larger than you, so the +4 bonus should occasionally come online, but, as usual for teamwork feats, needing to stand right beside each other hurts.

        -Coordinated Maneuvers* - (Com), (TW) - A +2 bonus to all combat maneuvers is great. Too bad you could just flank for a +2 bonus and not need to spend a feat (and not need to stand adjacent to each other, and also render your foe flat-footed to remove Dexterity and Dodge bonuses from their CMD). It’s not that much worse than Coordinated Defense, but the fact that you can just use better positioning and tactics for the same or better bonus is hilarious.

        -Cosmopolitan** - It’s more or less as good as making mediocre decisions with Additional Traits. Can be nice if you want to be the party face or are missing an important Knowledge skill. I actually like it quite a bit, but it is definitely a very situational feat.

        -Covering Defense* - (Com) - Bodyguard is so much better. Even Saving Shield (another poor feat) doesn’t use up your standard action. I could see an NPC Warrior defending a high priority target using this. Maybe. If he didn’t have anything better to do.

        -Crippling Critical** - (Com) - Fairly unique effect (against flying creatures, anyway), and a save only reduces the duration. Unfortunately, it requires a successful critical hit, so it’s not dependable.

        -Crossbow Mastery*** (Com) - If you want to use a Heavy Crossbow, you’re going to need this. Unfortunately, you’re still going to sink a lot of feats into it - in addition to the holy quadrangle of Point Blank Shot/Precise Shot/Rapid Shot/Deadly Aim, you need Rapid Reload and this. If you don’t have any bonus feats, you’re looking at a minimum of level 11 until you’re really online, but even a Human Fighter can’t have all these online until level 4.

        -Dastardly Finish* - Com - To take this feat, you’re probably going to need 9 levels in Rogue. You know what a 9th level Rogue can’t do? Stun someone or make them cower in fear. Sure, a party member could help, but a coup-de-grace is a full-round action, and if you’re already ready to make a full attack against a stunned target, they’re probably dead anyway.

        -Dazing Assault** - (Com) - The save isn’t great, the penalty to attack rolls is terrible, but at base attack bonus +11, you’ve got three attempts on a full attack action (four with a haste-effect). Sure, they might make 1 save, even 2, but they just have to fail 1 save for your gambit to work. Additionally, not too many creatures are immune to daze (mostly just Fortitude-save immune creatures like undead and constructs).

        -Dazing Spell* - (MM) - You might daze a foe for 6 rounds or more, but you’re most likely not going to affect anything. This feat is broken when it does work, and worthless when it doesn’t work. I’d advise players to stay away from it and GMs to ban it.

-Deep Drinker*** -  Only applies to one specific Monk archetype (Drunken Master), but for that archetype, it’s great.

-Deepsight* - Maybe I’m being a little harsh. If you’re a ranged attacker that’s planning on fighting in darkness 90% of the time, it could be good (i.e. a drow archer). Otherwise, this is a skip.

-Disrupting Shot* - (Com) - Requires 6 levels in Fighter for a questionable benefit. I feel like it has potential for NPCs, but players generally should be full attacking (3 attacks with Rapid Shot at this level). Really, just readying an attack to hit a spellcaster should be fine when needed, and you don’t have to be within 30 feet to do it.

-Disruptive Spell** - (MM) - It’s a handy little trick to keep around, especially when it only increases the spell level by 1. It’s not something to rely on, but even if you just negate one powerful spell with it, it may have done its work.

-Diviner’s Delving* - Ah, Spell Focus (Divination); it is perhaps only eclipsed by Spell Focus (Abjuration) as the worst Spell Focus choice. The effect of the actual feat is pretty piddly as well. Homebrew: Change it from a feat to a trait.

-Dreadful Carnage*** - (Com) - Comes online a little late, but it’s still a cool effect, both by fluff and crunch.

-Duck and Cover** - (TW) - Both effects of the feat are decent. Still don’t take it unless you’re forced to take a teamwork feat.

-Eagle Eyes* - Why, Paizo, why. Just take Skill Focus (Perception) - it’ll give you a +3 bonus all the time, and improve to +6 eventually.

-Eclectic** - There are two ways of interpreting this feat. The first interpretation says that because the feat specifies a hit point or skill point, those are the only options you can choose, and this seems to be RAW. If this is the case, this feat is junk, being strictly worse than Toughness. The second interpretation says that mentioning the +1 hit point and +1 skill point are just mentioning the normal options for favored class bonuses, and alternate favored class bonuses are still available. Under the second interpretation, there’s actually a reason to take this feat.

-Ectoplasmic Spell** - (MM) - Kind of the definition of “situational.” If you’re fighting a lot of incorporeal creatures, this is great. If not, it’s worthless.

-Eldritch Claws** - (Com) - It’s an option for natural weapon builds. At BAB +6, you should already be overcoming DR/magic with an item, but silver still might be a problem.

-Elemental Fist** - (Com) - I have a soft spot in my heart for this feat, but it really isn’t useful unless Ultimate Combat and style feats are on the table.

-Elemental Focus***/ Greater Elemental Spell Focus*** - For an elemental-themed caster, these are especially good. Stacks with Spell Focus and Greater Spell Focus.

-Elemental Spell*** - (MM) - Nifty way to get around resistances and immunities.

-Elven Accuracy**** - (Com) - Probably the best reason to be an elven archer. Rerolling miss chance every time you miss is pretty great.

-Enforcer**** - (Com) - It’s a core feat in many Intimidate builds. Improved Unarmed Strike is probably the easiest way to get it working in Core+APG, while Bludgeoner is great if Ultimate Combat is on the table.

-Expanded Arcana***** - Incredibly good for who it’s meant for (Sorcerers and Oracles). Proper use can vastly improve your flexibility. Almost every time you take a level of one of these classes, check this feat to see if it’ll be better.

-Extra Bombs**** - One major alchemist build is the bomb alchemist, and it’s fairly easy to run out of bombs if you don’t ration them well. This can help keep rationing to a minimum.

-Extra Discovery**** - There are plenty of great Alchemist discoveries. Always worth checking.

-Extra Hex**** - Hexes are the real unique class feature of the Witch, and there are plenty of good ones to go around, especially at low levels. Eventually, you’ll get to the point where you have all of them you need and want, but it’ll take a while.

-Extra Rage Power**** - Once again, rage powers are a key class feature of the Barbarian, and you’ll probably find yourself wanting more than the class gives you baseline.

-Extra Revelation*** - I find Oracle revelations to be slightly less important than some equivalent class features, as they are often only able to be used a very limited number of times per day, often for only a relatively small effect. Still, there are some good revelations, and you may way to get several online quickly for a build.

-Extra Rogue Talent*** - There are a lot of bad Rogue talents, so choose very wisely when taking this. Once you get to Advanced Rogue Talents, you’ll have more good choices.

-Fast Drinker*** - Very high Constitution requirement, and, once again, it’s only for one specific Monk archetype, but if you’re already a Drunken Master, this is a great feat for you.

-Fast Healer* - Requires Endurance and Diehard, neither of which are great, for a crappy payoff. Homebrew: Change prerequisites to just Con 13. Improve healing to your full Constitution modifier or half your HD, whichever is higher.

-Favored Defense*** - Only half? Still not terrible, but dependent on your DM throwing your favored enemies at you constantly until instant enemy comes online. If instant enemy is off the table (for example, if you traded away your spellcasting), drop this by a star.

-Fight On* - Only 1/day, costs you your immediate action, and only grants temporary hit points equal to your Con mod. Homebrew: The amount of temporary hit points is equal to your Con modifier times your HD. You can use this feat an additional time per day when your base attack bonus reaches +6 and every +6 thereafter.

-Focused Shot* - (Com) - Standard action, only one attack? Nope. You’re almost always going to be better off with Rapid Shot, or even Vital Strike. Homebrew: You must declare you are using this feat before making a ranged attack roll. If you hit, you add your Intelligence bonus to the damage roll for every successful ranged attack you make against the target that round, including the first attack. If you miss, that use of this feat is wasted. You can use this feat once per day, and an additional time per day when your base attack bonus reaches +4 and every 4 thereafter.

-Focused Spell** - (MM) - Not a bad little feat, there are just better ones out there.

-Following Step*** / Step Up and Strike*** - (Com) - Step Up is one of the biggest buffs melee received in Pathfinder. These add a little icing on the cake, but they’re not always worth spending two feats on. They do depend a bit on your GM/players’ strategies - if they never remember to 5 foot step away, the feats are pointless. In a strategic game where everyone is very mobile, these can counter a little bit of mobility and give you a little action economy fun.

-Furious Focus** - (Com) - Better at low levels, but falls off hard at level 6 and beyond.

-Gang Up** - (Com) - Very nice for gangs of NPC Rogues, but very difficult to actually get going for a player. Homebrew: Look up the 3.5 stance Island of Blades from the Tome of Battle. It’s under Shadow Hand here. That’s exactly how it should work.

-Gnome Trickster* - You can have lots of fun with mage hand and prestidigitation. Dip a casting class and get them at will or pick up a wand of each for 375g a piece.

-Go Unnoticed** - It’s got a tiny niche for helping you avoid combat before it starts.

-Improved Blind-Fight*** / Greater Blind-Fight*** - (Com) - Miss chance reduction is good. You’d probably be putting the points in Perception anyway. If you’ve got a few feat slots open, give these some thought, especially if your GM uses miss chance as much as I do (which is almost every boss fight). It’s a minor kick in the knee about blink though. So my level 15 feat can’t get past the spell the Wizard got at level 5?

-Improved Dirty Trick** / Greater Dirty Trick*** - (Com) - Improved Dirty Trick by itself isn’t really worth it, but the Greater version brings a huge boon: Removing your dirty tricks costs a standard action instead of a move action. If you’re planning to annoy your foes with dirty tricks, you need to have the Greater version.

-Improved Drag*** / Greater Drag*** - (Com) - I generally find drag to be the second best combat maneuver (after trip), as it allows you to pull an enemy from an area where it’s safe to an area where you’re safe. Greater Drag allows you to pull foes through all of your allies, giving them all attacks of opportunity and possibly setting up a flank. It also builds from Power Attack, which basically every two-handed weapon melee character should have.

-Improved Reposition** / Greater Reposition** - (Com) - I’ve never really seen the point of reposition, but I’d be happy for someone to explain. There’s a little more finesse to where you can get your opponent to move to, but...it mostly doesn’t seem worth the trouble.

-Shield Specialization* / Greater Shield Specialization* - (Com) - Only gives a tiny benefit to one particular shield after a laundry list of prerequisites. To add insult to injury, the benefit of the Greater version is easily replaced with a cheap item. Homebrew: Make Shield Specialization a trait. Cut Greater Shield Specialization.

-Improved Steal* / Greater Steal* - (Com) - Steal is just a poor combat maneuver.

-Groundling* - When was the last time you spoke with a gopher?

-Heroic Defiance** - The prerequisites are awful, and 1/day sucks, but it’s not completely unsalvageable. If you dipped Unbreakable Fighter for Endurance and Diehard, consider snagging this for a Pathfinder-style mini-Iron Heart Surge.

-Heroic Recovery*** - Like its big brother, Heroic Defiance, there are lots of limitations, but it can be quite useful if you already have Endurance and Diehard for some reason.

-Ki Throw*** / Improved Ki Throw*** - (Com) - Knocking your opponent prone is good. Knocking your opponent prone and then dropping them into the worst position possible is better. Knocking your opponent prone, then throwing them into another opponent (preferably making a noise like two coconuts colliding, or bowling a strike), who drops prone is awesome. They’re fun and useful feats for a Monk specializing in maneuvers.

-Second Chance** / Improved Second Chance* - (Com) - Second Chance is good from precisely BAB +6 (when it becomes available) to BAB +11 (when you get a third attack), because if you fail your first attack, you can use your iterative to reroll your first attack (instead of taking one at the normal -5). Improved Second Chance comes online at exactly BAB +11, when it becomes worthless.

-Improved Share Spells** - Cool, but the restrictions are brutal. You’d most want this for buffing a melee animal companion or eidolon, but you’d have to stay immobile beside them to keep it active. Add a star or 2 for a mounted build where you’d constantly be in contact with your companion.

-Sidestep** / Improved Sidestep** - (Com) - The real problem with these two is that you have to remain in their reach - it’s not actually helping you get to safety, or even engage well. It can conceivably help against creatures with long reach, and it can help you get into a flanking position, but don’t expect to use it to get away. There are often better things to do with your immediate action, though. Homebrew: Remove the restriction that you must stay within your foe’s reach (perhaps for the improved version)?

-Improved Stonecunning* - What in the world was Paizo smoking. Alertness and Skill Focus (Perception) are better every single time.

-Intensified Spell***** - (MM) - It’s the feat for the Magus. It drops down to about 3-4 stars for most other casters, however. Still, it’s certainly more bang for your buck for getting damage out of low-level spell slots than Arcane Blast.

-In Harm’s Way**** - (Com) - Pretty nice for a Bodyguard build (which is a prerequisite for it). If you’re already going for a Bodyguard build, this is a nice bit of icing on the cake.

-Iron Guts* - Take Great Fortitude.

-Keen Scent*** - Scent is a great ability, both in and out of combat.

-Leaf Singer* - The range of your bardic music abilities is not important 90% of the time; inspire courage (your most important performance) isn’t even affected by distance beyond needing to be seen/heard. Homebrew: Turn it into a trait.

-Light Step** - It’s not actually that bad of a feat, but the prerequisites are awful. The big draw is that it works in all natural environments, not just forests, swamps, mountains, etc. as these things normally go.

-Lingering Performance*** - Great action economy for the Bard, allowing it to stop playing for a little bit to do something else.

-Lingering Spell*** - (MM) - Interesting little crowd control/defensive effect.

-Lookout**** - (Com), (TW) - One of the best teamwork feats. More actions is always better. Getting a full-round action in a surprise round is especially good.

-Low Profile* - (Com) - Nine times out of ten, just get Dodge.

-Luck Halfling*** - Only once per day, but it doesn’t cost any action, and it’s a great ability.

-Minor Spell Expertise** / Major Spell Expertise*** - An additional 1st level spell a couple times per day isn’t that exciting. A spell of up to 5th level a couple more times per day is worth a second glance. Two extra castings of wall of iron or freedom of movement could be a life saver, even if it can’t come online until 17.

-Master Alchemist** - It’s riding the 1-2 star border, but it’s a great feat if you’re going for poisons. Unfortunately, poisons are usually not a good area to specialize in.

-Merciful Spell** - (MM) - Another super-situational metamagic feat.

-Missile Shield*** - (Com) - Mostly dependent on your GM/players’ preferences, but it’s cool when it works. It can screw up Gunslingers a bit, who are notorious for their damage output.

-Mounted Shield** - (Com) - Are you mounted? Do you use a shield? Boy, do I have a halfway decent feat for you!

-Mounted Skirmisher***** - (Com) - Mounted pounce. You can’t get it until level 14 (or level 10 for a mounted combat style Ranger), but it’s basically the holy grail of most mounted builds.

-Outflank*** - (Com), (TW) - Depends on your team composition (like all teamwork feats), but this one is pretty decent even without being forced to take teamwork feats (through Inquisitor, Cavalier, etc.). Two characters with crit-fishing builds can put out some pretty mean damage with this feat.

-Paired Opportunist*** - (Com), (TW) - It needs a way of generating attacks of opportunity (like Greater Drag), but it can definitely work.

-Parry Spell*** - Counterspelling isn’t the worst strategy, and this adds a little oomph to it.

-Parting Shot* - Stupidly high prerequisites for very little payoff.

-Pass for Human* - You can replicate this feat’s effects with roleplaying and a good, heavy travelling cloak. Homebrew: Make a trait.

-Perfect Strike** - (Com) - If it wasn’t for the stupidly restrictive weapon choices, this could be a good feat. Homebrew: Open it up to all weapons the monk can flurry with.

-Persistent Spell*** - (MM) - It’s certainly not a bad feat, but check it against Bouncing Spell.

-Point Blank Master**** - (Com) - You don’t provoke attacks of opportunity when attacking with a ranged weapon. Pretty sweet, even if you need Weapon Specialization.

-Practiced Tactician** - Tactician is not a particularly good ability, even if you can use it an additional time per day.

-Precise Strike*** - (Com), (TW) - Hey, it’s basically +1d6 sneak attack.

-Preferred Spell*** - Never prepare a certain spell again or avoid an increased casting time with that spell. Pretty fancy.

-Punishing Kick*** - (Com) - It’s a bull rush that doesn’t require a CMB check or a trip attempt based on a Fortitude save. It’s pretty awesome.

-Pushing Assault*** - (Com) - Lose some damage for a free bull rush.

-Racial Heritage - This has a lot of potential, but I don’t have a list prepared of what exactly you can do with it. The Advanced Race Guide would probably be the place to start.

-Raging Vitality***** - This is the feat for Barbarians. If you are a Barbarian and don’t take this feat, you are hamstringing yourself.

-Ray Shield** - (Com) - Could get messy if your shield gets disintegrated, but it can be a nice feat. Many rays are only 1/round, and many don’t affect objects (like enervation).

-Razortusk*** - It’s a bite. It’s a little bonus damage on a full attack.

-Reach Spell*** - (MM) - The big draw is getting to use touch spells at range.

-Rending Claws* - (Com) - Not the worst feat in the world, but it’s pretty bad. Homebrew: Make it work more similar to the rend special ability.

-Saving Shield** - (Com) - +2 shield bonus as an immediate action. Decent for a level 1-2 NPC warrior meatshield? At level 3+ Combat Reflexes+Bodyguard is probably going to be better. I guess if you have absolutely no use for your immediate action, it’s OK.

-Selective Spell**** - (MM) - Very useful for throwing down area of effect attacks with impunity. Wonderful even if it does come online a bit late.

-Shadow Strike**** - (Com) - Do you have sneak attack as a class feature? You need this feat - maybe not immediately, but eventually Homebrew: Allow sneak attacks against concealment baseline, instead of requiring this feat.

-Shared Insight* - There’s nothing here you would want. Homebrew: You instead grant a +2 insight bonus on Perception checks and attack rolls, but the feat can only be used once per day per point of Wisdom bonus you have.

-Sharp Senses* - Alertness and Skill Focus (Perception) strike again.

-Shield of Swings** - (Com) - Half damage for a +4 bonus to AC? Not a good trade, unless your foe misses every attack against you (which is a possibility, and the reason this isn’t rated 1 star). Homebrew: Instead of granting a +4 shield bonus to AC, it halves all damage taken (but not ability damage taken) for the next round.

-Shield Wall** - (Com), (TW) - Highly specific bonuses, probably not worth it for the most party.

-Shielded Caster* - (TW) - There’s a tiny niche for NPC bodyguards, but, boy, is it tiny.

-Sickening Spell* - (MM) - Increase the spell level by two for a measly -2 to a few types of rolls? Skip.

-Smash** - (Com) - There’s a little bit of usefulness in a true “kick in the door” campaign filled with locked doors, chests, and chains connected to iron portcullises. Homebrew: You ignore the first 5 points of hardness, period (even against constructs, on a sunder attack, etc.).

-Smell Fear** - Interesting. Very situational, but it’s kind of cool.

-Sociable* - Just use the aid other action.

-Spell Perfection***** - Metamagic reduction and more. This is better than many classes’ capstones, and you can get it as soon as level 15. How about quicken forever on a 5th level spell? That’s just the tip of the iceberg.

-Stabbing Shot*** - (Com) - Bull rush with no CMB check; sort of a poor-elf’s Point Blank Master. Remember that you can push them back with this feat, then take a 5 foot step for 10 feet of distance gained.

-Steel Soul**** - A +4 bonus on saves against all spells and spell-like abilities is huge. One of the best single sources of bonuses to saves this side of a cloak of resistance +5.

-Stone Faced* - I could be persuaded to bump it up to two stars, but Skill Focus (Bluff) is generally superior, and if you want a high Bluff character, consider a race that doesn’t have a penalty to Charisma.

-Stone Sense*** - Despite the super-short range, this is still a great feat for catching people sneaking up on you or invisible creatures.

-Stone Singer* - You’re a dwarf bard. YOU’RE A DWARF BARD. But seriously, all the same problems as Leaf Singer.

-Stunning Assault*** - Comes online late, but still pretty good. See Dazing Assault for a little more commentary.

-Summoner’s Call** - Decent, but enhancement bonuses are a dime a dozen (and don’t stack).

-Swap Places** - (Com), (TW) - I really want this feat to work, but there are so many limitations bogging it down for it to be very useful.

-Swift Aid* -  (Com) - Woooo. By 6th level, you should have something better to do with your swift action. Maybe for an NPC it could be decent? Homebrew: Change it from a +1 to half your normal bonus (this would make it decent for creatures with abilities like the Helpful trait that increase the bonus to aid other).

-Taunt** - I’ve never actually seen someone enforce the size penalties on Intimidate, but if they do, this is here. Homebrew: Change it to a trait.

-Team Up* - (Com) - You have a base attack bonus of +6. Just full attack.

-Teleport Tactician**** - (Com) - If you’re this far in the Disruptive feat tree, this is a great choice.

-Tenacious Transmutation*** - It’s a nice little feat for transmuters.

-Thundering Spell** - (MM) - Not worth 2 levels, but it’s better than Sickening Spell.

-Touch of Serenity*** - (Com) - Ah, the irony that this is a combat feat. It does grant a saving throw, but you’re trading your attack action for severe restrictions on their full round action. Attack them with Touch of Serenity until they can’t counter-attack, then let loose. Difficult prerequisites, though.

-Trick Riding*** - (Com) - Pretty worthless abilities, and then - WHAM! An additional use of Mounted Combat per round!

-Under and Over** - (Com) - Decent, but it requires your foe to try to grapple you and fail.

-Underfoot** - Not that great on its own, but can work well with a Panther Style build if Ultimate Combat is available.

-Vermin Heart** - Situational, but can be useful, especially if your primary foe in the campaign is built around a single vermin (such as Drow and spiders).

-War Singer* - If there are a dozen combatants on the battlefield, everyone is going to fall asleep in real life before the battle is over. Steal the rest of the pizza and consider it a job well done.

-Well-Prepared*** - Flavorful and fun. Certainly not gamebreaking, but it can get you out of several tight spots.

Optional Rule System: Hero Points

-Blood of Heroes** - Depends heavily on how quickly your GM is handing out hero points.

-Hero’s Fortune**** - If you can take this when the rest of the campaign isn’t using hero points, take it: Extra actions are awesome. If you already have hero points, knock a star off - it’s still useful, but not incredible.

-Luck of Heroes** - Again, depends heavily on how frequently you can gain hero points outside of leveling up (and how fast you’re leveling). A ring of heroes is probably the better way of acquiring this ability if you really want it.

Bonus: The Worst of the Worst

        In no particular order:

  • Athletic: As stated previously, it’s terrible even for a +2/+2 feat. Climb and Swim are two of the easiest skills to replace, with the abundance of methods of obtaining a climb speed, swim speed, water breathing, flying, water-walking, wall-walking, air-walking, levitation, burrowing, teleportation, plane jumping, and other useful methods of getting from one place to another (usually without even requiring a skill check).
  • Cockatrice Strike: Comes online way too late, single attack at a full-round action, only against targets that are already disabled, has to critical hit, and allows a save. Awful through and through.
  • Improved Stonecunning and Sharp Senses: Nothing says pitiful like “I really envy the guy with Alertness.”
  • Groundling: Unless the final boss is a mole…
  • Monkey Lunge: Special thanks to a reader for suggesting this one. To be fair, it's not quite 100% useless. Reach is fairly hard to come by, and it seems to stack with other sources of reach (reach weapons, size increases), so if you're doing a pure AoO build, it could conceivably be slightly above abysmal Edit: Nope, the bonus to your reach only lasts until the end of your turn; unless you have a way to attack an opponent as a swift action (maybe through Mythic), this feat is utterly useless. Spending a standard action to smack someone will basically always be better. Another kick in the nuts is that you can’t even get Lunge (its prerequisite) until BAB +6. If you don’t have any bonus feats, you couldn’t even pick it up until level 9! Homebrew: Cut the phrase “As a standard action,” and change “until the end of your turn” to “until your next turn” (as the standard Lunge feat). Boom: Now it’s a fine little pickup for those with the Lunge feat. If you’re really worried about balance, maybe make it a swift action so it’s consuming some kind of resource.
