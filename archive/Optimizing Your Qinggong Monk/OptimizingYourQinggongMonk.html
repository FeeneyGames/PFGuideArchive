Optimizing your Qinggong Monk By jb 200

The Qinggong Monk is a monk archetype from Ultimate Magic.  The Archetype allows you to switch out one or more Monk class features for new ki powers.  These ki powers are broken down into 3 categories 1) feats, 2) spell-like abilities and 3) standard monk class features.  This has significantly opened up the ability to customize your monk no matter what archetype (see below) and has really revitalized the Monk as a viable class in the view of many players.  However, with so many choices it can be hard to choose what power to take, so I have decided to write up this guide to help players to, if not optimize, then at least make an informed decision about their Qinggong Monk choices.

Discussion on this guide can be found Here.

One issue that may come up is the compatibility of the QM with other archetypes.  Archetypes are considered to be compatible (i.e. can be taken by the same character) if they do not replace or alter the same class feature.  Now, one school of thought says that the QM “alters” every ability that could be replaced.  If your DM ascribes to this reading then of the 24 monk archetypes (including racial ones from ARG) the QM is compatible with all of 1: the Sensei.  Everything else replaces one or more of the abilities listed as replaceable.  The Master of Many Styles comes close but blows it b/c both replace Perfect Self.  

Another reading of the Qinggong Monk entry is that each replacement is optional, meaning that if a Monk doesn’t have slow fall, then you just skip that choice.  This reading allows any Monk to cherry-pick the powers to replace, allowing the QM to stack with almost every archetype. The only thing you need is the ki pool (this kicks out only Martial Artists).  If the archetype in question doesn’t have High Jump or Diamond Soul, well you just skip that power and move on.  Ask your DM his or her reading of this section. This guide assumes the second reading, but even if it’s the first, the QM may still be a good choice.  

Update: So it’s official, the second interpretation (if you don’t have it skip it) has been endorsed by the design team (FAQ).  The only caveat is that if your archetype dumps high jump, you can’t select high jump at a later level.

Ki Powers

As I said above, at levels 4, 5, 7, 11-13, 15, 17 (x2), 19, and 20 a Qinggong Monk gives up a standard monk class feature in favor of a new ki power. I am going to break down the ki powers by level comparing them to each other and what you give up to get it. I’m not going to do the level 20 powers because no one gets there anyways.

Level 4 Powers: This list is used for both Slow Fall and High Jump

Slow Fall: Not a bad ability, scales with level but there are two drawbacks that hurt it. First, it’s just not that commonly used, and secondly you have to be next to something to slow your fall.  The new powers are a better choice.

High Jump: A borderline case, high jump definitely has some utility. If you are making an infiltrator monk, coupled with fast movement, getting that +20 acrobatics means you can easily jump over a city wall or other obstacle.

New Options:

Acrobatic Steps: Get to skip nimble moves and helps keep fast movement useful.  There are better choices on this list for ignoring difficult terrain.

Augury: A weak and vague divination with a fail chance even at high levels and even a successful casting may give you no response. Stay away!

Barkskin: As an (sp) it takes a standard action, as opposed to the swift +4 from normal ki ability.  However it lasts for 10 minutes per level, and since you have your neck slot taken up by AoMF, you won’t likely have a Natural Armor bonus from another source.   A solid choice to shore up the monk’s notoriously low AC.

Deny Death: Not bad. Not great, but not bad. You get to skip Endurance feat tax, and it takes no action and requires no ki.  I’m not a huge fan of the “this is only good if you almost die” abilities because you don’t want to put yourself in this position in the first place.  I would strongly suggest you take an offensive ability before this.  Since if the monster is dead it can’t kill you or stop your buddies from stabilizing you.

Feather Step: So you can take Acrobatic Steps and ignore difficult terrain for 15ft OR take this and ignore all difficult terrain for 10 min/level. Much better than Acrobatic Steps.  That said, I would still suggest an offensive ability first.

Hydraulic Push: A ranged bull rush attempt.  Problem is that a Monk wants to get closer to the enemy not farther, although, there is nothing in the spell description that says you have to bull rush away from you?

Ki Arrow: Make an unarmed attack at 100ft range.  Pretty nice. It starts out at about 6 damage, but it scales with your unarmed attack damage.  One of the best of the level 4 options.

Ki Stand: This is a little unclear b/c the feat says you need ki to use it, but the list says it takes 0 ki.  I would say just take the feat. It has no prerequisites you don’t already have.  Keep these choices for something you couldn’t already do.

Message: A cantrip!!!!  STAY AWAY!!

Power Attack: This is another feat you should just take.  You want to use it much more often than you have ki.  Good feat, bad ki power.

Quick Draw: If you don’t have a weapon in your hand and don’t have a move action to draw it, just use your empty hand!! I mean, you’re a monk for god sakes. No thanks.

Scorching Ray: Another A+ choice.  It’ll average 14 damage per ray, even better than ki arrow. Only drawback is that you need to make multiple rolls at higher levels (not a big deal) and unlike ki arrow it is subject to SR.

Slow Fall: Obviously, you’re not going to take this at level 4, but you could take it at level 5.  Taking one ability at 4 and then this instead of High Jump.  Seems like a bit of a waste though. Also, I’m not sure if it advances with level or not.

Throw Anything: How often are you going to need a thrown weapon (as opposed to using your great speed to close) and you don’t have a dart or shuriken handy? Very circumstantial, and that’s being generous.

True Strike: Guarantee your next hit, and I works with a spell touch attack.  Use this when you need to land that next blow, like you want to make sure that you don’t waste your stunning fist attempt. Not a bad choice, especially for a melee fighter instead of a wizard.

Level 6 Powers: With this group of powers you should notice something.  This is a list of 6th level powers, but you don’t get to your next drop until level 7 with Wholeness of Body.  There is another group of powers at level 8 and then 10, but you don’t get your next power until level 11! I know it doesn’t make sense to me either.

Wholeness of Body: A very small amount of healing for 2 ki.  An oftly steep price for all of 14 hps, less than the average for Cure Serious Wounds.  The same rule holds from above, a power that drops an enemy faster is better than one that keeps you up longer.

New Options:

Cloak of the Winds: So ranged attacks take a penalty and you can push around tiny creatures. Oh and it costs 2 ki.  Only use I can think of would be for swarms, since you can already get Deflect Arrows and Snatch Arrows.  Not worth it.

Gaseous Form: A decent utility spell for easy infiltration. Somewhat limited usefulness.

Heroic Recovery: Get a free second save v. effect that gives a Fort save.  It’s ok, but you’re already immune to poison and have the second best saves in the game behind the Paladin. Skip it.

Hydraulic Torrent: A slightly better version of hydraulic push.  Problem is that Huge or bigger creatures have CMDs so high that you’re just wasting your time. AND it costs 2 ki.

Remove Disease: First off, you’re immune to disease. Second, why not leave this to the Cleric/Witch/Druid/Paladin in the party.

Sidestep: A really bad idea.  Why? because you lose your 5ft step, and a monk who can’t make a full attack is a very sad panda.  Want to move during your opponent’s turn? Take the Step-up line of feats, they’re great for a Monk.

Snatch Arrows: You can get this as a feat for free at level 10.  Also, if you don’t have Deflect Arrows, it doesn’t work, you can’t use it.

Spring Attack: Same problems as Sidestep and Snatch Arrows.  You can already take this as a free feat without ki and you don’t really want it because you want a full attack action.

High Jump: You get to pick up a power you left in the dust 2 levels ago! After looking at this sad sack of choices you may be better off keeping Wholeness of Body, picking up this or going back to the level 4 list. Bleh!

Level 8 and 10 Powers: So these two sections are grouped together because you don’t get a new power at level 8, or 9 or 10, but 11.  If you couple this with the fact that all the level 6 powers stink, I’m starting to really be concerned about the Qinggong Monk.

Diamond Body:  Immunity to poison is ok.  It’s not bad but not that hard to give up either.

New Options:

Dragon’s Breath: Not a bad blast.  You get to choose the shape and element depending on what the circumstance calls for, and the save scales with level.  Bad news is that it costs 2 ki, it’s already at max damage when you get it and of course, spell resistance.

Gliding Steps: Avoid AoO from moving out of the first threatened square, or for an additional ki no AoO from movement at all.  This is only ok.  You won’t be leaving too many threatened squares, since Monks are so dependant on full attacks.  No AoO from moving period is a little better, since by level 11 there should be more Large or bigger monsters, and their reach means you have to provoke to close.  That said, your acrobatics should be pretty good and you can pick up Mobility, or save one ki and pump your AC for a round.

Neutralize Poison: As with remove disease, just leave this up to every spellcaster in the game (except wizard). Oh and it’s the most expensive ability to date at 3 ki.

Poison: So the good news, you can deliver touch attacks through unarmed strikes.  Bad news? One save ends the poison.  That said, 1d3 Con is some mean poison damage.  Also, for some reason I can’t fathom, this spell allows spell resistance.

Restoration: So you can heal yourself of almost every penalty in the game without paying the cost.  I assume that you can use both the cheap and expensive version.  A great choice.

Share Memory: I don’t think that you would ever use this to show one of your memories or show the target one if their memories.  But you can use it to read a target’s mind.  This can be decent and with a cost of 0 ki, you can do it all day long.  There are much better choices out there.  

Silk to Steel: An ok way to get a small shield bonus. Just invest in a wand of shield, even at caster level 1 it’ll last almost as long.

Spider Step: Better hope you didn’t give up slow fall, or this is useless. OR you could just take this as a normal feat.

Whirlwind Attack: In theory, feats like this and Great Cleave are great, allowing you to make many attacks in one round at full BAB.  Problem is that, with the rare exception, you will almost never be adjacent to more than 3 enemies at once.  Flurry will likely give you more attacks and at a higher BAB.

Discordant Blast: You get to do paltry damage, and again push enemies away from you, instead of toward you.  Also the CMB is based on Cha, your lowest stat.

Greater Bullrush: Give everyone a AoO, maybe.

Greater Disarm: Better than Bullrush b/c you can’t get this normally without the Combat Expertise feat tax. It’s not bad.

Greater Feint: Do monks feint?

Greater Sunder: Don’t forget, if you don’t have Improved you still provoke an AoO.

Improved Blind-Fight: A nice way to ignore concealment. It’s unclear if you don’t have blind-fight if you can reroll miss chance. If you can this bumps up to green.

Ki Leech: Wow, this is actually pretty nice. It has a long duration, so it might actually last you a couple fights and you get 1 ki back everytime you kill someone.  Pretty nice.

Lunge: Or just take the feat, since you qualify for it already.

Shadow Step: A medium range teleport effect.  I assume you need line of sight for the new location, maybe not.  Not a bad choice.

Spit Venom: Guaranteed blindness, and if they fail a save 1d2 Con each round with a scaling DC instead of just DC11.  No SR makes this a great choice.

Step-up and Strike: You get the last feat in a really nice feat chain, nice right? No, because it doesn’t actually give you the ability to follow, just if you can already follow with Step-up or Following Step then you can take an AoO. But if you already have Following Step, just take the feat.

Wind Stance: Not great but not terrible either.  You can still take the feat.

Level 12 Powers: Ok so this is where you start to hit some really nice abilities. Problem is, you don’t want to take them. Why? Because Abundant Step is a great ability, and with the Dimension feats from UC it’s even better.  You will take at least one at level 13 though.

Abundant Step: A really great ability for a monk, really you should almost never give it up.

Diamond Soul: SR is a double edged sword.  On the one hand you can avoid lots of spell damage, on the other hand you have to spend a standard action if your buddy wants to heal you.

New Options:

Abundant Step: I am quite happy this is on the list. Why? b/c there are several archetypes that give up Abundant Step but keep Diamond Soul, so trade one for the other.

Battlemind Link: Wow is this expensive! But it’s also pretty good. You get to double roll your attacks and take the best one.  And to top it off, it last for 1 min/level. Pretty nice.

Diamond Body: You just dumped this and the list is getting good.

Elemental Fist: I know I say this a lot, but you should just take this as a feat.

Improvised Weapon Mastery: You’re a Monk, you’re whole thing is using unarmed attacks. Why would you want to take a feat that you would only use if you are unarmed?

Ki Throw: You can take this as a bonus feat, also it doesn’t require combat expertise so you can take it as your level 7 feat after taking Improved Trip at 6.

Punishing Kick: If you hit, get a free Bull Rush or Trip w/o CMB roll (trip gets save).  You would probably be better off with Stunning Fist.  Not a terrible choice.

Shadow Walk: A long range travel spell with some error.  It’s ok, but there are better choices.

Level 14 Powers: Again you are picking level 14 powers with a level 15 slot.  I just don’t know why the powers are staggered like this?  

Quivering Palm: If you hit, free SoD save.  The Good: nice ability and the save scales with level.  The Bad: Fort save, your Wis isn’t going to be as high as a full caster and double saves are not nice.  

New Options:

Blood Crow Strike: Make a full attack at a distance.  This alone fixes one of the Monks biggest weaknesses. As spell damage it also avoids DR.  The downside is 1) fire resistance is common, 2) SR, 3) casting time is 1 round.

Cloud Step: As with spider step, you need to have slow fall to take this, also it’s pretty easy to get.

Cold Ice Strike: Swift action 15d6 cold damage. SR and a save, but great for action economy.

Diamond Soul: This might be better than quivering palm, or you could just not drop this at 13 and pick from the level 12 powers at 15.

Disarming Strike: Free disarm w/crit.  Nice since you don’t need Combat Expertise now and it specifically says no AoO, so it’s ok if you didn’t take Improve Disarm as a bonus feat.

Improved Ki Throw: In essence a double trip. Problem is that you have to have ki throw for this to work, since it specifically does not grant you the ki throw ability, just an extra option.  Also, you can take this as a bonus feat at 14.

Ki Shout: A decent single target blast. Sonic resistance is practically nonexistent, but still SR and Save.

Sonic Thrust: Hurl objects at target, if you have a boulder nearby it can reach up to 15d6 damage or 15 arrows for 15d4. No SR, so that is nice and no save.  However it is a straight up attack roll and Int and Cha are your dump stats.

Level 16 and 18 Powers: I’m grouping the last two together because the level 18 powers are just replacing abilities you dumped for the level 16 abilities.  It’s nice because you get up to 2 choices for level 17.

Timeless Body: Your character will almost never have to worry about natural aging penalties, and at this level there is likely someone in the party that can heal magical aging, if it ever comes up.

Tongue of the Sun and the Moon: You can speak with everyone. Shame tongues is a 3rd level spell.

Empty Body: Go ethereal.  It’s ok, but not world changing.

New Options:

Bleeding Critical: A nice feat, and bleed damage bypasses damage reduction.  That said, there may be better ways to use that 3 ki, or you may want to just take the feat.

Greater Blind-fight: A nice feat, still unclear if you can reroll miss chance if you don’t have regular Blind-Fight.

Improved Vital Strike: The vital strike line of feats is actually pretty nice for a Monk, since flurry is build around getting full attacks.  This is treated as 3 attacks instead of 1 for a standard action. Not bad really.

Lightning Stance: A good way to avoid that killing hit after a withdraw action.  That said, at this level your speed is so high that almost nothing will be able to keep up with a single move action, not to mention a double move.

Penetrating Strike: Fighter only feats are always nice.  You need weapon focus (unarmed) for this to work, but you likely have that anyways.  That said, the only DR you can’t already bypass is DR/Good.

Quivering Palm: A good trade off for any of the abilities.

Strangling Hair: Grapple with your hair and do Unarmed damage.  Some problems though: 1) Your normal grapple check will be higher, 2) again Int and Cha are your weak stats, 3) also your CMD is going to be a lot higher, 4) Oh, and SR.  That said it works independant of your actions, so not terrible.

Timeless Body: Didn’t get any better.

Tongue of the Sun and the Moon: This neither.
