Attacks of Opportunity: The Redheaded Stepchild of Pathfinder

Overview:

Have you ever been frustrated with the limited number of times you can hit enemies in a given turn? Feeling starved for standard actions? Maybe you’re just playing a reach build and wanted a few extra pointers for how to get ahead? This guide will provide a good starting point towards abusing the wonderful system called attacks of opportunity.

Want to see what happens when these attacks get taken to the maximum? I’m also providing some build options for entire builds centered around taking - and granting - attacks of opportunities. Did you know that one single member of a team, if properly built, can effectively give every single person on the team full use of attacks of opportunity with his allies only needing combat reflexes to get full benefit? Let’s take a look.

Color Guide:

Following the normal Treantmonk color scheme here

Orange is a decent choice

Blue is an amazing choice

The Setup

Roles:

The Tank

Wear medium or heavy armor, grab Stand Still or the Improved Trip line, and sit in front of your favorite squishy. When someone tries to hit your friend, you stop them in their tracks. Works well with Fighters, Brawlers, and special consideration for Stonelord paladins or Stalwart Defenders with the Bulwark stance power and Halting Blow. Prefers either Fauchard, Dorn-Dergar, or Meteor Hammer. This is one of the less feat-intensive lines.

The Dangerous Duo

Obtain an animal companion as a teamwork feat buddy. Requires either three levels of Hunter or the Horsemaster’s Saddle, which is fortunately only 12k gold. Alternately, convince one of your friends to pick up Outflank. Only one other person needs to have it. Consider a lance if you’re on horseback, otherwise you have free choice of weapon. Almost every kind of quadruped animal companion can technically wear the saddle and nothing says the rider has to be actually riding it for it to use the teamwork feats. Don’t try this with a familiar - they’re expensive to replace, don’t have as much HP, and don’t get bonus feats of their own.

As a side note, familiars and eidolons can also qualify for this, but it’s a little bit more difficult because of the necessity for feats and the saddle slot. However, familiars and eidolons are much more likely to have an extended critical range for Outflank, so its really up to you if you think they are worth it.

For unchained summoners, I recommend the Elemental subtype with the Quadruped base form for Mount (which makes sure the saddle works) and the movement speed versatility. Burrow, Fly, and 20ft are all very good options to help you flank faster and you can always give it arms and a weapon proficiency feat.

For people with familiars...the hardest part is probably going to be making sure your familiar doesn’t die too much, combined with finding one big enough to actually flank with you. Do remember that Evolved Familiar will allow you to put reach on one of your familiar’s attacks for flanking fun.

Dweomercat Cubs have DR, 5ft reach, and a saddle slot (with pounce for icing).

Nycar have regeneration and DR to keep them from dying, and a saddle slot but no natural reach.

Arbiters miss the reach again but have much better regeneration and technically come proficient with a short sword, which has a 19-20 crit range, but no saddle slot.

Shikigami have fast healing and DR, but they are more notable for the rule that, at DM discretion, you can trade out their natural feats for other feats.

Voidworms can cast blur on themselves and have fast healing and immunity to crit as well. Good movement, but no natural reach and saddle slot is unlikely.

The Crit Fiend

Dual wield 18-20s and go keen ASAP. I strongly recommend using the wakizashi with one or more levels of Swashbuckler - it is the only light piercing weapon with an 18-20 crit range and you can abuse opportune parry and riposte until your DM ragequits. Strength Rangers and Urban Barbarians may also apply. Please remember that at 8th level, you can sell your +1 keen weapons and get Improved Critical instead. Think of it as renting a keen sword for 4k until then.

As an addendum: you will not be the one doing all of the damage with this build. The damage comes when each crit you get provokes 3-4 attacks of opportunity from you and your friends.

The Two-Handed Beatstick

Hit things with a big sword. Hard. A lot. This one won’t be able to go as deep into the world of AoO as other builds due to feat requirements, but I can still help you get the most out of your 2d6+30 hits.

The Buffer

You aren’t the one making the attacks of opportunity. Maybe you love helping your friends beat up bad guys. Maybe someone needed to make skill checks around here. Maybe you just gave up on rolling the d20 because it hates you. Either way, your job in combat is to enable everyone else to punch the stuffing out of anything that stands in your way. Make sure that you talk to your party members in advance so that your martial friends can pick up Combat Reflexes.

Races:

Half-elves are a strong choice for both Multitalented, which lets you cross-class a little more freely, and the Ancestral Arms alternate trait that can let you pick up either the Fauchard or the Wakizashi - both strong choices - for free.

Humans are always a good choice. Always. Attacks of Opportunity are very feat-intensive. You can also consider replacing Skilled with One with the Multitude for extra bonuses to your AoOs.

Dwarves can be a good idea for their automatic proficiency with the Dorn-Dergar, another good weapon choice. They are slightly less compatible with the high-Charisma builds, though, and their lack of a Dexterity or Strength bonus can be painful. If you’re looking at the Stonelord archetype this goes to Blue.

Half-Orcs are useful if you are working on a tank, because they can have both ferocity and the Shaman’s Apprentice alternate racial trait, which can get you started on the Diehard feats earlier. Consider taking Sacred Tattoo and Fate’s Favored instead for bonus on saving throws.

Elves have a bonus to Dexterity, and can use the Elven Branched Spear for a bonus to some of their AoO’s. The -2 Constitution really hurts a frontliner, but the bonus to Intelligence can help going down the Combat Expertise route. Definitely consider the Fleet-footed alternate racial trait if you decide to go Elf.

Halflings and Gnomes both lose out by being small. Consult your DM as to whether Enlarge Person increases their reach or not, if it does then you can bump Halflings up to Green for their Dexterity, saving throws, and 30ft move speed (assuming Fleet of Foot).

Other Races:

Since there’s so many, I’ll give you an idea of what you want to look for. Bonus to Dexterity or Strength is almost a must, preferred dump stat is whatever mental stat you don’t need. Defensive racial traits and proficiency with a good exotic reach weapon also help.

Stats:

Dexterity is your primary stat. It controls initiative, number of attacks of opportunity, AC, and reflex saves. You will need it all. You probably want an 18-20. Minimum 14 for Str builds.

Strength is a secondary stat. If you’re going with a two-handed weapon build, it becomes a primary stat. You still want at least a 13 for Power Attack.

Constitution is hit points. Minimum 12, don’t ever dump it. You are a frontliner.

Intelligence...if you are going for Combat Expertise you need 13. Otherwise, dump it. If you want to dump it anyways, there are a few ways to conveniently ignore the 13 Int prerequisite anyways.

Wisdom is also situational. If you take levels of Hunter, you probably want at least a 14. Otherwise, don’t tank your will saves too hard.

Charisma is only useful if you take levels of Paladin, Swashbuckler, or Bloodrager, in which case you want a 14.

Classes

Paladin (Holy Tactician/Holy Guide): 3-6 levels

This is absolutely critical for a team-focused builds like the Buffer and the Crit Fiend. The real kicker is that at level 3, you gain the ability to share one of your teamwork feats - chosen by swift action - with all of your friends within 30 ft. Since this is a paladin aura, it has no time limit, no number of uses per day, just a bonus that only works when you aren’t flat footed. If you want it all the time, worship Torag and grab the Threatening Defender trait and now you have it constantly. Holy Guide adds a second teamwork feat at level 6 that you can grant in place of your smite.

Paladin (Stonelord):

An alternate paladin build for people who like tanking. One of the few paladin archetypes that barely cares about Charisma and also gains the amazing Defensive Stance, which can be augmented to increase the acrobatics check to avoid AoO’s and eventually halt them in their tracks as soon as they even enter your range. Comes with a pet rock for some mild teamwork shenanigans as well.

Cavalier:

Not surprisingly, Cavaliers make great AoO abusers and decent teammates. They get teamwork feats for free that they can share, although they’re limited use and less flexible than Holy Tactician. A few bonus feats help relieve the feat starvation and a mount with the Horsemaster’s Saddle makes a great teamwork partner. Charge-Overrun builds can get a lot of mileage out of Cavalier and it’s a pretty decent choice for a Holy Tactician’s second class. Special call out to the Strategist archetype, which allows for a longer-lasting preemptive sharing of teamwork feats for free.

Slayer (Vanguard):

Vanguard Slayers, like Cavaliers, get the tactician ability with a single free teamwork feat. However, they can always pick up new feats and gain more uses per day than the Cavalier. Slayers also get pretty decent access to ranger combat feats, prerequisite-free. Great for crit fishers. Initiative bonuses and inability to be surprised are just icing on the cake.

Hunter:

Hunters are a strong choice with their ability to cast buff spells and an animal companion that automatically shares all of their teamwork feats. This is the typical base for the dangerous duo build, and comes with some icing in the form of Animal Focus. Snake is available to most archetypes and gives a scaling bonus to and against attacks of opportunity - up to a +6 at level 15. Adding in bonuses from feats like Outflank (which you get for free) and Paired Opportunists, you and your companion can both have a +14 on your AoOs whenever you both threaten the same target. Delicious.

Inquisitor (Sacred Huntsmaster):

Very much like the hunters, this archetype of Inquisitor gets later access to Animal Focus, Hunter’s Tactics, all the teamwork feats of a normal Inquisitor, and a companion with no restrictions. The tradeoff is that instead of the early Animal Focus and a few other class features, Inquisitors instead get a single Domain and the extremely potent Bane ability (and a slightly different spell list). When you make several extra attacks per round, bane is pretty delicious and if you combine the Branded for Retribution feat with a team full of Paired Opportunists, you can get some sick damage. Unlike Hunters, who are restricted to some Neutral alignment and thus cannot also be Holy Tacticians, Inquisitors just need to be within 1 step of their deity. For domain choices, I suggest looking at Brewer’s Guide to Reach Clerics.

Inquisitor (Monster Tactician):

A very different build from the rest, you rely heavily on summoning creatures that benefit from your teamwork feats. I would be wary of using this because of the sheer amount of time and micromanagement your one turn will require. This build becomes even more feat-intensive than normal because you add summoning feats into the fray as well, though you do still get some free teamwork feats. Later on this can make for a sick ranged build.

Fighter:

Not surprisingly for a feat-intensive build, Fighters make a great choice both as a dip and as a full member. They can make decent tanks, especially if they enter the Stalwart Defender prestige class, or they can go for crit fiending with unparalleled access to critical feats. They also get access to Inquisitor’s Solo Tactics for you loner beatsticks out there using Weapon Training. Lore Warden makes a surprise entry for a 2 level dip when they get Combat Expertise, prerequisite free, in addition to their bonus feat. Other archetype contenders are the Dragoon if you happen to have a mount, Phalanx Fighter for using a shield with your two-handed reach weapon, and Polearm Master for...well, for being good at anything polearm. Unbreakable works as a 1 level dip for people who want access to the Diehard tree. Tactician gives you late and limited use of the Cavalier’s Tactician ability.

Swashbuckler (1+ levels):

Mostly useful for the dual-wielding critter, the swashbuckler gains parry and riposte with wakizashis, weapon finesse for free, and a relaxation of Intelligence requirements for Combat Expertise builds. And that’s just as a one-level dip, they also make excellent choices for a full build with bonus feats, bonus to initiative, and a smattering of nice defensive features. With the amount of bonuses specific to AoOs that you will get, you will be able to parry incoming criticals regularly to keep your skin intact. Keep your Cha decent for charmed life and panache. Mythic swashbucklers may also enjoy a combat maneuver build if they want to pick up Combat Trickery ability from the Trickster path.

Brawler:

The final class that ignores Intelligence, Brawlers are decent with most builds. Bonus feats are extremely welcome. Martial flexibility offers some utility for figuring out crazy situations, whereas the Mutagenic Mauler archetype can give you tons of strength and armor to hit everything that much harder and tank. Wild Child makes another choice for a deadly duo build, though getting teamwork feats onto your friend animal can be a bit of a pain. Two-weapon critters don’t gain quite as much from flurry because there are no weapons a brawler can flurry with that have the full 19-20 crit range, so feel free to trade that out for something else.

Brawler (Exemplar):

Special call-out for this archetype for buffer builds. Exemplar brawlers combo bardic performance with the Cavalier tactician ability for some pretty sweet party-wide buffs. Sadly they don’t gain access to masterpieces so they can only share one teamwork feat at a time, but they can flex into any teamwork feat that is also a combat feat.

Monk (3+ levels):

Preferably the Unchained kind for full BAB, this build will use a great number of feats and centers around using Ki Throw with improved maneuvers. Technically the feat path is open to other classes as well, but only monks are able to use it against creatures larger than themselves. Take a look at Flowing Monk, it gains the ability to make its victims flat-footed to make damn sure everyone’s attacks of opportunity land. If you really need your will saves for some reason, and you are mythic, you can consider maxing out Bluff and grabbing the Combat Trickery path ability for Tricksters instead of having full BAB.

Vigilante (Mounted Fury):  

Vigilantes can do pretty much anything you can possibly imagine as long as you pick the correct archetype. This is your preferred pick if you are a dangerous duo. Mounted Fury gets the most feats of any possible build in the entire game, including some talents which give you entire feat chains for free and a twofer deal on teamwork feats and a mount that shares them. You don’t get quite as much choice in animal companions as your Hunter friend, but you get 8 more feats to make up for it. Plus pseudo-pounce. Plus scaling extra damage on your two-weapon fighting.

Vigilante (as a tank):  

Did I mention that Vigilante does almost everything better than anyone else? Break out a reach weapon and prepare to break into Stalwart Defender. As long as you go Avenger, you get full BAB and as many feats as a fighter, plus some special benefits to some of those feats and a distinctly nice lack of prerequisites. With Vital Punishment, you can vital strike on an AoO once per round, including improved versions if you have them. With Shield of Fury, you can get Improved Shield Bash and skip TWF entirely if you wanted to Dorn Dergar with a shield. If you prefer to two-hand, you can grab Shield of Blades for extra AC from your Power Attacks. If you need Improved Unarmed Strike as a prerequisite for a style feat, grab Fist of the Avenger. Just in case, Shadow’s Speed gives extra movement speed to make sure you get into position faster. Use Combat Skill as many times as you need to get feats that aren’t free-with-icing already. The one and only downside is that you have no way of granting teamwork feats, so you can keep your AoOs to yourself and your 80ft reach.

Vigilante (combat maneuvers):  

So yeah...Vigilante. Someone’s beloved child at Paizo. Favored maneuver gives you any Improved maneuver you want, prerequisite free, with a circumstantial bonus. As many times as you want. Note that the nice-looking Brutal Maneuvers talent is actually a trap since it offers such heavy penalties. If you prefer the stalker specialization, you can pick up Leave an Opening for a guaranteed AoO any round that you actually hit something and the Sneaky Maneuver rogue talent to swift-action trip after booping them. It doesn’t matter if you grant teamwork feats because greater combat maneuvers make them provoke from everyone anyways.

Vigilante (crit fishing):  

Last one, I promise. Lethal Grace gives you some nice TWF support. Signature Weapon is a twofer deal on feats you already wanted. Vital Punishment, once again, gives you more damage on your inevitable AoOs. You can also go into the stalker specialization for a hit to your BAB but access to the Leave an Opening talent that gives you a free AoO, once per round per opponent hit. However, please note that stalkers do not get infinite free feats like avengers. This is probably the best way to do damage by yourself as a crit fisher. I still recommend a single-level dip in Swashbuckler to help your survivability with Opportune Parry and Riposte.

Bloodrager:

There are multiple bloodrager bloodlines that offer increased reach while raging. This is for the two-handed hitters who want a few more AoOs and are the one class that doesn’t require a reach weapon to regularly hit things 10 feet away. Also consider Arcane bloodline for tanking. Beware of feat starvation.

Barbarian:

Like bloodragers, usually the two-handed variant. Urban barbarians may apply to the dex-based builds, and mounted fury is an okay choice for deadly duos. Barbarians in particular are feat-starved and unlikely to gain the full benefits.

Bards and Skalds (1+):

Surprisingly, bards end up filling the same niche as Holy Tactician and Cavalier due exclusively to the masterpiece Battle Song of the People’s Revolt, which gives you the ability to grant a teamwork feat as a bardic performance (Thank you, Jodokai). They can try their hand at a crit build with Dervish Dancer and Arcane Strike despite the ¾ BAB or just go pure buffer. Duettist can also help the buffer by allowing early access to simultaneous bardic performances (usually a 4th level spell), and Totem Skalds can give some unique benefits. Technically speaking, you only need one level if you are willing to sacrifice a feat to grab People’s Revolt.

Clerics and Oracles:

You have many different ways to go here, none of which are exceptionally good. Reach Clerics and Battle Oracles can grab a reach weapon and beatstick it up. If you take one level of Bard or Skald, the Divine Expression feat allows you full performance rounds on People’s Revolt. The Community domain can let you hand out a teamwork feat to your melee friend. The variant negative energy channel for gods of Envy can force enemies to provoke if they fail their save. Battle Oracles can get free feats for one combat maneuver, very very slowly.

All of the Above????:

Technically speaking, several of the teamwork-sharing abilities stack. You can use a Holy Tactician’s Battle Presence alongside a Cavalier’s Tactician ability while you perform the Battle Song of the People’s Revolt and summon a bunch of monsters with teamwork feats from Monster Tactician. If you want to do this, make sure you compare it to other possible builds to help determine just how many levels of pure class you can sacrifice before the worth of another teamwork feat is not sufficient. You can maybe consider this for Gestalt purposes.

Reach Clerics, Battle Oracles, and anyone else using polearms:

I can guide you to good feats and traits to augment your build. You probably have a bunch of other ones you want already, but these will help fill any gaps you might have.

The Way it Works

The Basis:

You and your friend or animal companion each get the feat Outflank. If you have an animal companion, Pack Flanking makes this easier. You set up a flank with your friend or companion. Whenever you score a critical hit, your friend gets a free AoO. If you both get Paired Opportunists as well, then you both get to take the AoO.

But I’m Ranged:

I have just the feat for you. Target of Opportunity is a free attack that is extremely easy to trigger with the caveat that it is an immediate action instead of an attack of opportunity. Later on you can also take the Snap Shot/Improved Snap Shot feats to get in on Paired Opportunists anyways.

Making it Broken:

The main drawback of this is the required feat investment. You and your friend have now both spent 2 feats to get some extra AoOs occasionally. If you’re a pair of crit rogues, you’re already set, but for most parties this will be lackluster. That’s where the Paladin (Holy Tactician) and Cavalier classes come into play.

If you can use your Battle Presence or Tactician ability to give Paired Opportunists to everyone, then suddenly your critical hit turns into an AoO for your friend, just like before, but now everyone on your team who is threatening gets to take an AoO alongside him because of Paired Opportunists.

As a side note, any other method you have of making your enemy provoke an AoO, including Greater versions of maneuvers, Broken Wing Gambit, now will also grant the AoO to yourself and any team members that previously would have been left out.

The Ki Throw path:

This version instead abuses the Greater maneuvers that you can already use to make your enemy provoke by using multiples of them against the same enemy in a single round. It’s extremely feat intensive for exactly one player. The feats required are as follows:

Improved Unarmed Strike - bonus, Combat Expertise, Combat Reflexes - bonus (1-2), Improved Trip, Greater Trip, Ki Throw - bonus (10), Improved Bull Rush - bonus (6), Greater Bull Rush, Spinning Throw, Improved Ki Throw, Vicious Stomp

Because of the feat intensity, this build peaks around 11th level. It allows you to Greater Trip a target followed by a Greater Bull Rush that knocks another nearby opponent prone. Vicious Stomp and Greater Trip technically stack since one activates on a successful trip attempt and the other activates when an enemy falls prone. This should give up to 4 AoOs per turn, 1 of which is exclusive to you. Combine with the Style feats of your choice and enjoy.

Riptide Version:

Thanks to some new feats coming out, there’s another way to double down on combat maneuver-provoked AoOs. This is the Riptide build.

Feats: Dirty Fighting, Improved/Greater Trip, Power Attack, Improved/Greater Drag, Riptide Attack

Extra Feats for bonus: Vicious Stomp, Combat Reflexes

Attack of Opportunity Triggers

Enemy moving through your threatened range - This happens frequently with reach builds. Remember you can attack them in any one and only one of the threatened squares they pass through, except the one they entered. Talk to your DM, but I believe that if you AoO them with Paired Opportunists when they enter your allies’ threatened area, it provokes again if they continue their movement and provoke from your ally, resulting in multiple AoOs from each person on the same movement.

Drinking a Potion/Extract or using a Scroll - as far as I know this cannot be done defensively and always provokes. I hate potions of Cure Moderate anyways

Standing from Prone - this is why Greater Trip/Overrun are better than their other counterparts. Keep in mind a few builds don’t provoke when doing this.

Using a Ranged Weapon - alchemical bombs and items, crossbows, rays, whatever. If it’s ranged and they don’t have a build that lets them not provoke doing it (looking at Gunslinger and Point Blank Master), it provokes.

Casting a Spell Non-Defensively - unlikely to happen unless they don’t realize they’re in a threatened area (which is why Greater Invisibility is awesome). Still provokes.

Escaping from a Net - for those of you who like that kind of thing, I suggest proficiency and the Net Adept chain, as well as a ghost-touch net.

Picking Up or Retrieving an Item - an okay reason to use Disarm, also helps against the Wizard of Many Scrolls. Does not apply to wrist sheaths, weapons you are drawing, or spell component pouches.

Using an Untrained Combat Maneuver - most of the time this won’t matter, but against enemies with bad tactics feel free to murder them. Note that negative-channeling variant Envy clerics can force this unless your enemy makes a save.

Paired Opportunists - most of the following triggers only provide an AoO for some members of your team, rather than all. Paired Opportunists is the master feat that lets everyone who has it participate in the gang-up fun. Ask your DM about which interpretation he wants to use (strictly only ones that use the “provoke wording? Anything that lets you make an attack roll against your opponent on your opponent’s turn? Anything that uses the words “attack of opportunity” or uses up an AoO?)

Ideally, you want your DM to decide that anything that allows you to attack your opponent or use an AoO on their turn qualifies for Paired Opportunists, but if you have to choose - Snake Fang or Bodyguard? All of the others fall into both categories.

Using Greater Trip, Bull Rush, Reposition, Overrun, or Drag - some provoke from you and your friends, some only provoke from your friends.

Outflank or Seize the Moment with a Critical Hit - no, RAW these do not stack, Outflank requires you and your ally with this feat to be flanking, whereas with Seize the Moment you just have to both be threatening.

Vicious Stomp - for all you unarmed strikers out there. Ask your DM if he will let you use a natural attack instead if that’s your thing. Provokes from you alone, but on a very common trigger (opponent falling prone).

Bodyguard - falls under the “ask your DM” category. It does technically use an AoO to trigger but the actual action used is “aid another”. I personally wouldn’t allow it but your DM might.

Archon Diversion/Archon Justice - RAW this doesn’t work, since the language says your allies get to take an AoO instead of that your enemy provokes an AoO. Should be fairly easy to convince your DM that they both mean the same thing, and technically you get up to 2 AoOs from the same attack here, since Diversion gives your ally an AoO for your enemy having the chance to strike you and Justice gives all of your allies an AoO when the strike is successful.

Crane Riposte - a different style with the same RAW issue, this provokes from only you when you use your Crane Style successfully. Sweet and simple, but comes online later than Archon Style.

Snake Fang - the third style to choose from, requires you to use an unarmed strike to make your AoO and, depending on how you interpret it, can also grant two AoOs to all of your friends. Ask your DM.

Intrepid Rescuer - unless you are in a party of NPCs, this is probably too niche.

Opportune Parry and Riposte - Swashbucklers can use an AoO and make an attack roll, it’s not *exactly* RAW but it’s pretty close, especially if you Riposte as well. Ask your DM.

Fortuitous weapon enchantment - I heard you liked AoOs, so I gave you an AoO on your AoO so that your team can gang up murder your enemies while they’re already ganging up and murdering your enemies. You take the penalty, your friends do not. Not technically RAW, so ask your DM.

Feats

Individual Feats: 

Totem Beast - Do you have an animal companion? Does it have an animal focus? Snake gives a scaling bonus between +2 and +6 depending on your level and you don’t have to be a Hunter. Oh you already have a focus? Have another one! Bonuses to Strength or Dex help your companion stay relevant and AoO-happy.

Combat Reflexes - The absolute minimum for any AoO build. Period.

Bodyguard - if your DM rules that this works with Paired Opportunists, your enemies will suddenly stop living past the first round of combat. Otherwise, its nice but not necessary.

Combat Expertise - Not so useful on its own, but an important prerequisite for Improved and Greater Trip, and also for the Stalwart tanking build and Butterfly’s Sting.

Combat Patrol - How awesome this is depends entirely on what the rest of your party is doing with it. It’s decent for people using reach weapons, especially ones with full BAB. If the rest of your party is a group of archers and wizards taking cover behind you, this is stupid amazing. Stalwart Defenders will love this twice as much. If your party likes to charge in and hit things, probably not going to mean much.

Power Attack: A necessary prerequisite for drag, overrun, and bull rush. Also a pretty nice feat by itself for most builds.

Improved, Greater Trip - Send your enemies prone and have them provoke on the way down for another shot. Beware of things with lots of legs or flying things. One important note - once you have Greater Trip you can use your AoO to make a trip attempt and grant yourself and your friends a free extra AoO with it. Trip is the only combat maneuver that can both cause an AoO with its Greater version and be used in place of any melee attack.

Improved, Greater Overrun - an alternative to Trip for low-Int people with Power Attack. Still ends up with them prone and provoking. Look into getting this on your mount. If you really like overrunning with your mount, also look at Trample, Charge Through, Mounted Onslaught, and Vicious Stomp.

Improved, Greater Bull Rush/Drag - like Overrun in that it only requires Power Attack, but alters their position instead of knocking them prone. Not usually as good, although it still makes them provoke at Greater, Bull Rush becomes Green when used with Spinning Throw (see Ki Throw section of How It Works for details) and Drag becomes Green when used with Riptide Attack.

Improved, Greater Reposition - Like Bull Rush and Drag in outcome, but still requires Combat Expertise. Ew

Two-Weapon Fighting, Improved, Greater - if you’re playing a two-weapon character, you take the two-weapon feats. Str Rangers may also look at Double Slice. They get successively less useful as the chance to hit with that last shot goes down, keep in mind that a critical threat that isn’t a natural 20 still has to hit the enemy’s AC to be a threat.

Butterfly’s Sting - When your friend has Outflank (or a Scythe) and you don’t. Also required for the Seize the Moment feat. Requires Desna as your god.

Improved Critical - Crit fiends love it, everyone else probably just wants to buy the weapon enchant. Prerequisite for Seize the Moment.

Critical Focus, Critical feats - Again for the two-weapon crit build, add some extra debilitating effects and confirm even on a 2.

Boon Companion/Horse Master - Do you like having a pet that’s actually fully leveled? This helps. Horse Master notably always uses your character level whereas Boon Companion works on any kind of animal, but only covers 4 levels of multiclass.

Nature Soul/Animal Ally - Another odd way to get an animal companion, this is usually your worst option since you cannot have an existing animal and it requires two feats.

Intrepid Rescuer - an odd feat that requires worship of Kurgess. It gives you an extra AoO every time an enemy attacks an NPC or helpless ally. This is more of a situational feat for Martial Flexibility than anything else, unless for some reason you have a lot of NPCs around in combat.

Leadership - If your DM allows this feat, it is broken. Get a nice tanky follower that has teamwork-feat-sharing abilities and have him stand somewhere close to melee. This is a great way of doing an all-of-the-above build: share 3 teamwork feats with everyone and his brother without sacrificing a single class level.

Endurance/Diehard/Stalwart, Improved - For tanking builds only. It does also require Combat Expertise, but it will give you stupid high DR. Endurance and Diehard are only particularly useful in conjunction with Stalwart. Ignore this if you aren’t going tank.

Darting Viper/Dorn-Dergar Master - if you’re using the Dorn-Dergar as your reach weapon, Darting Viper can help you switch from reach to non-reach faster and DD Master can allow you to use a shield and one-hand it.

Riptide Attack: If you have both trip and drag feat chains, this can get pretty nice. Be careful of flying or otherwise untrippable foes.

Vicious Stomp: If you are able to use your weapon with one hand and have Improved Unarmed Strike, this is another way for you to get a free AoO after a successful maneuver.

Vital Strike, Improved, Greater: Exclusively for Vigilante, who can vital strike on an AoO. This works best for high-damage weapons like the Lucerine Hammer, Dorn Dergar, etc.

Style Feats:

Archon Style/Archon Diversion/Archon Justice - This might be passable for a tank option if it didn’t require a free hand, as it is you probably want to be two-handing or holding a shield in your off-hand instead. Archon Justice and Archon Diversion do trigger an AoO once per round, but it doesn’t necessarily make up for the rest of the chain. I’ve seen some good uses for it for Sacred Shield paladins whose primary goal is preventing allies from taking damage.

Crane Style/Crane Wing/Crane Riposte - This style suffers from the same free hand problem as Archon Style, with a slightly more selfish set of abilities. If you happen to be wielding one-handed somehow, this is good for tank builds. You may actually get more out of this than the Stalwart line, especially early on or if you’re not Intelligence-oriented. Sadly, Crane Wing got nerfed.

Snake Style/Snake Sidewind/Snake Fang - The middle feat is a bit on the boring side, and otherwise this is fairly similar to the Crane Style, except that it doesn’t require defensive fighting and is less likely to block a hit. Snake Fang gets bonus points for letting you hit a second time on your AoO, although technically this won’t stack with Paired Opportunists. It also, if you read closely, can be used to make an AoO multiple times per round. (Thanks, Scott!)

Panther Style/Panther Claws/Panther Parry - RAW they aren’t technically AoOs, but any leniency at all on your GM’s part for interpretation will work here. You get a number of free retaliatory attacks equal to up to your Wisdom modifier with the second feat of the chain and mess up your opponents’ attacks too with the third. Panther Claw effectiveness varies depending on the number of AoOs you expect your enemies to have. (Thanks, Gregory Connolly!)

Wolf Style/Wolf Trip/Wolf Savage - Wolf Style is an okay 1 feat dip for defenders with high strength, simulating the Stand Still feat at reach range. It doesn’t get super great unless you have high damage output or only attack things with slow movement speeds. Wolf Trip makes a nice addition to Greater Trip, and Wolf Savage is probably useless unless you are a Monk as well. I suppose it is possible to be a monk using a reach weapon to flurry, but that’s the only scenario in which all three of these feats would be useful.

Brute Style/Brute Stomp/Brute Assault - It won’t give you a lot, but if for some reason you happen to be doing unarmed strikes with reach this gives you a wider area to AoO. The next two feats are much more focused towards doing damage by yourself.

Teamwork Feats:

Outflank - A solid building block for anyone in melee, Hunters actually get this feat for free at second level. The bonus to flanking is worth on its own, but the AoO on an ally’s crit is what makes two-weapon crit fishing a viable option for these builds. If you’re using it in conjunction with Paired Opportunists, this feat becomes purple.

Seize the Moment - a late-game outflank for people who are bad at flanking. You lose out on the bonus to hit but gain the ability to AoO off a crit regardless of flank position.

Paired Opportunists - Most people just see this as “oh I get +4 to AoOs. Nice.” and stop reading there. That’s nice but that is ICING compared to the real part: whenever an ally gets an AoO, any other ally gets to also make an AoO. That means that any effects that grant one person an AoO now grant it to everyone. These effects include Outflank, the Fortuitous weapon enchantment, reach weapons, Unexpected Strike for Barbs and Stalwart Ds, and potentially opportune parry and ripostes. Consult your DM on that last one, though.

Target of Opportunity - If you don’t happen to need your swift action for anything, this allows a ranged attack that isn’t technically an AoO whenever someone else also makes a ranged attack. Easy to trigger in the right party, useless in the wrong party, and of ok usefulness when it happens.

Broken Wing Gambit/Wounded Paw Gambit - This has an annoying prerequisite and doesn’t work against intelligent targets very well. It also only works once per round, even if your opponent falls for it. The second feat is for ranged attacks only.

Pack Flanking - Are you a Hunter, Mounted Fury, Sacred Huntsman, and/or person with a Horsemaster’s Saddle? Congratulations, you are now always flanking. Enjoy your Outflank bonuses. Otherwise, you can’t benefit from this feat.

Blades Above and Below - This is similar to Pack Flanking in that it allows your allies to always flank. The problem is that it is now conditional on 1. You having an ally in a different size category and 2. Someone being the same size as your enemy. Don’t expect to use this against a Tarrasque anytime soon.

Harrying Partners - This is a godsend for Bodyguard abusers. Now the AC bonus you grant lasts for a whole round! Much less useful if your DM rules that Bodyguard as an action doesn’t trigger Paired Opportunists.

Lookout - a nice feat that’s pretty difficult to actually use. Even with a divination wizard or similar in the party, it’s usually not worth it for everyone in your party to sink a feat just to get into the surprise round. Tactician doesn’t really cut it unless you know for a fact you’re going to be running into an ambush in the next 18 seconds, but for the Holy Tactician this feat suddenly becomes Green - you can take it for your full team and switch it off as soon as initiative rolls. Bonus points if you have Uncanny Dodge or Defensive Strategist and can use it when you are also surprised.

Escape Route - Another niche teamwork feat, this one is useful to have against reach enemies or if everything goes to hell and your whole party needs to get out fast. Extremely convenient for mounted characters as you will never eat an AoO again.

Shake it Off - like Lookout, this is a feat you probably will only use outside of combat. It’s very convenient if you’re running into a lot of traps or environmental hazards. Uses in combat are fairly limited since the party is likely to be more scattered. If at least 3 people in your party get it, it’s about the same as all three of them getting Iron Will, Lightning Reflexes, and Great Fortitude - which is decent but very unexciting.

Traits

Fencer (Combat) - +1 to Attacks of Opportunity with swordlike weapons

Fight with the Flock (Race - Werebat) - a good reason to be Adopted, gives a +1 to AoOs whenever you are within 10 feet of two allies.

Finish the Fight (Race - Half-Orc)  - +1 to attack against anyone you already hit today. Good stuff.

Temple Guard (Regional) - wonderful because it’s not a combat trait, this is +1 to AoOs for polearms.

Bullied (Combat) - +1 to Attacks of Opportunity with unarmed strikes (thanks, Scott)

Defensive Strategist (Religion - Torag) - Uncanny Dodge in a trait. Useful for Holy Tacticians, whose teamwork aura doesn’t work while flat-footed.

Weapon Style (Faction) - Gives you proficiency with one monk weapon. If for some reason you only have proficiency with simple weapons, this is how you get a reach-trip weapon.

Opportunistic (Religion) - +1 to Attacks of Opportunity with whips, swords, and daggers.

Equipment

Weapons:

Meteor Hammer - A good choice for anyone, you can switch this between a reach weapon and a double weapon. You should be able to two-hand it either way, so just view it as reach that you can switch to not-reach when the enemy gets too close. Gives a few extra bonuses for AC and tripping.

Dorn-Dergar - Another reach weapon that can switch to non-reach, Dwarves are automatically proficient. It takes a bit longer to switch than the meteor hammer, but deals more damage. Can be used one-handed with a shield with a feat investment.

Elven Branched Spear - the really exciting parts are the +2 to AoOs and the potential to finesse it. A good choice for elves, who get it as a martial weapon.

Lance - Are you on a mount? Do you charge? Meet your best friend. Also martial rather than exotic, so easier to pick up.

Fauchard - a decent exotic reach weapon with an 18-20 crit range, this is the halfway point between a normal build and a crit fishing build.

Dwarven Longhammer - if you don’t like having backup plans for when people get close, this has a large damage die. Dwarves are again automatically proficient.

Double-Chained Kama - the highest damage monk reach weapon for you flurry people. Has a few nice extra traits.

Guisarme - another martial reach weapon, this one has trip as well. Probably the best for people with neither a mount nor an exotic proficiency.

Naginata - for samurai. I’m still not sure why you gave up tactician if you’re looking at this guide.

Wakizashi - the only weapon that is 18-20, light, and piercing. Recommended for swashbucklers doing crit builds for compatibility with panache. Also convenient for samurai, but I don’t know why you’d go samurai and give up tactician.

Kukri - if you’re doing a crit build without levels of swashbuckler, this one is still light and 18-20, plus doesn’t require exotic weapon proficiency.

Weapon Enchants: Usually it’s ok to go straight +1 to +5,, but these are worth consideration.

Answering - for swashbucklers with wakizashis. Put this on one and enjoy a +5 whenever you parry and riposte, which will be often.

Fortuitous - once per round, make a second AoO after the first with a penalty. Your friends with Paired Opportunists take no such penalty and the feat mostly negates it anyways. Cheap and will often straight-up double your damage between rounds. Drop this to Orange if your DM rules the second attack does not trigger Paired Opportunists.

Keen - you know why you want this already.

Menacing - if you have multiple allies (including your companion) who have Outflank, this is a great way to get even more bonuses to hit. Pack Flankers will love this.

Wondrous Items:  

Horsemaster’s Saddle - give your quadruped animal companion all of your teamwork feats for free. Absolute gold.

Sable Company Elite Saddle - a little more expensive, but works better for flying mounts. Still gives them all of your teamwork feats.

Ring of Tactical Precision - give one of your allies one of your teamwork feats on a limited basis. You need to recharge it every 24 hours, but it works.

Banner, Commanders - improves the Tactician feature immensely.

Vambraces of the Tactician - the Commander’s Banner for people who have a different banner. Not quite as nice, but occupies the little-used wrist slot.

Resplendant Uniform Cloak - Better than the Vambraces, cheaper than the Banner. Instead of increasing the duration of your Tactician, you get one extra use per day. I find that more useful.

Dire Collar - a nice way to give your animal friend reach and size once per day, pretty cheap too.

Juggernaut’s Pauldrons - at will enlarge person for all of your reach needs! Comes with some pretty snazzy benefits too, including ferocity and the ability to use Deadly Juggernaut (a 4th level spell!) as an immediate action 3 times per day. The price makes it a mid to late-game item, and being a shoulder slot hurts. Ask your DM if you could have Juggernaut’s Pantaloons or something instead.

Cloak of the Hedge Wizard (Transmutation) - another way of grabbing enlarge person 1/day. Still a shoulder slot, but a lot cheaper. Note that it costs the same as 50 battles with a potion, so consider those as well.

Other Items:

Potion of Enlarge Person or Longarm - Use with a spring-loaded wrist sheath at 50gp a pop. More reach is better reach, and statistically speaking if it takes you 10 battles to level up each time, and you use one every battle, it still only costs you 5k by level 10. Not a bad deal. Keep a longarm or two around in case of cramped space.

Wand of Enlarge Person - it’s quite a bit cheaper than the potion, but takes a full round and potentially a UMD check each use. I’d stick with potions.

Mythic Campaigns

Mythic Feats

Combat Reflexes - Also known as how to dump dex in an AoO build. This is probably most useful for the tanky builds and other particularly MAD ones.

Agile Maneuvers - if you are doing combat maneuvers, you probably need all the help you can get to make ‘em land.

Critical Focus - This one is really circumstantial. If your enemies are not going to be mythic, it’s awesome. If the bosses are still mythic, that means it probably stopped working when you need it most. If your DM hates that you have a crit build and gave everyone fortification then it goes back up to being kinda nice.

Dual Path - You’ll probably know if you need this. It lets you take two different mythic paths’ abilities. Note that Tricksters can already take a single path ability from someone else with Path Dabbling.

Improved Critical - Get a bit more damage out of each crit. This favors people with big weapons or big modifiers. If you are doing Butterfly’s Sting, your scythe-wielding friend will enjoy this.

Improved Drag/Trip/Bull Rush/Reposition/Overrun/Grapple - these are all cookie cutter copy-pastes of each other. A moderate bonus to combat maneuvers is nice. Grapple is suddenly blue because your enemy now provokes every single time they try to grapple you. Including when they try to regain control of the grapple you put them in. Grappling suddenly became a viable AoO technique!

Vital Strike  - for vigilantes with the Vital Punishment talent, or people that need their move action every round for something else.

Legendary Teamwork - If only this was listed as a teamwork feat so that you could tactician it! Still offers some nice bonuses to Paired Opportunists and Outflank.

Extra Path Ability - You can take a path ability that gives you an extra feat or a feat that gives you an extra path ability. I like to take this at tier 3 because you get a feat right as you unlock a new set of path abilites, but you can play around with it.

Mythic Paths

To save you from having to read massive walls of text, I am going to sort out preferred paths by build here.

Dangerous Duo: Guardian path for defenses and utility, Champion path to help with crits. If you happen to have a good domain, you can substitute Heirophant for Guardian.

Crit Fiend: Champion all the way. The Marshal path might help if you want to be more of a team player. If you have sneak attack, you might want to consider Trickster as well.

Tank: Guardian has everything you ever wanted. Marshal can offer some extra bonuses, including the broken Aura of Perserverance combo with a Deathless-casting Legendary Item.

Ki Throw: The bad news is that this just doesn’t get a lot of support anywhere. The Champion path can help a little and the Trickster path, surprisingly, also helps a lot IF you are willing to invest heavily in Bluff. You probably want to cherry pick Titan’s Bane, Ever Ready, and Combat Trickery or Maneuver Expert and just get a lot of Extra Mythic Feats and a sweet Legendary Item.

Buff Build: Marshal path will let you buff people beyond their wildest dreams. Also consider the Divine Source, Legendary Item, and Mythic Spellcasting universal path abilities.

Universal abilities

Unfortunately, most universal path abilities provide bonuses against non-mythic enemies. If you are in a mythic campaign, it’s a damn good bet that at least the bosses you fight will be mythic. That means that these abilities will be useless precisely when you need them the most.

Tier 1

Extra Mythic Feat - The exact opposite of Extra Path Ability but with similar uses.

Legendary Item - This is so stupidly broken. The spellcasting ability alone is worth its weight in gold because it allows you to snipe choice spells off of whatever spell list you so please. I particularly enjoy pulling wordspells like Accelerate, since they are buffs you won’t see from any of your normal party members. Metamagician can make it even sweeter. Undetectable can combo with Greater Invisibility to bypass truesight so you can keep your enemies flat-footed forever. Foe-biting is some nice extra damage.

Mythic Spellcasting - Varies heavily depending on what spell list you have and what spells you choose. There are other guides that cover this.

Tier 3

Divine Source - This varies in effectiveness depending on the domains you choose and how fast your mythic tier is increasing.

Enhanced Ability - An extra +2 to your main stat is nice, but hardly comparable to the other options you have at tier 3. There might be some niche uses for extremely SAD characters.

Champion abilities

Champion is a good go-to particularly for crit-fishing builds, as it has a bounty of bonuses for them. It doesn’t work quite as well for the tank or the dangerous duo.

Champion’s Strike

Fleet Charge - The most widely useful option, this gives you pseudo-pounce with a free extra attack that ignores DR.

Sudden Attack - This does not help with positioning nearly as much as Fleet Charge, but it does give you roll-twice-take-better. Therefore it makes you much more likely to crit with your 15-20 crit range.

Tier 1

Backlash - I want to like this because it gives you an AoO, but the trigger condition is downright awful. You have to 1. Get crit, 2. Survive the crit, and 3. Be able to hit the enemy that crit you. Having all of this happen is neither likely nor beneficial.

Clean Blade - Sickening critical is cool, and this is basically a stronger variant on that. Is it worth a path ability? Maybe.

Crusader - See Leadership feat for details. Note that many path abilities are worth more than a feat. The surge bonus isn’t worth a whole lot.

Ever Ready - Now this is the kind of bonus we like! Depending on your tier, this can replace Mythic Combat Reflexes with a scaling bonus on your AoO rolls as well.

Always a Chance - On a related note, you get lots of bonuses on AoOs and they use your max BAB. You probably CAN hit on a 1, if it didn’t automatically fail you. This is how you fix that, if the 5% chance to miss was killing you.

Impossible Speed - If you have trouble positioning due to your movement speed, this will help. Otherwise you probably have better things to do with your life.

Titan’s Bane - Later on in your career, you will probably be fighting big things. This helps you hit them and keeps them from hitting you as well.

Tier 3

Disabling Strike - This is downright awesome for a crit-fishing build. Your crits can do Con damage and completely disable your enemy’s limbs. Mythic enemies get a save but can still be affected. Ask your DM about disabling atypical limbs like noodly appendages.

Maneuver Expert - Spend mythic power to gain improved and greater insert-maneuver-here. This might be useful early on in the ki throw build, before you’re able to get all the feats you want. You’d have to retrain it later, though.

Maximized Critical - I don’t need to tell you why you want this. I can see a crazy Butterfly’s Sting-Mythic Vital Strike build in here.

Precision - Again for crit-fishing TWF builds. It won’t take away the -2/-2 for dual weilding but it will make your (example 11th level) BAB look like +9/+9/+9/+9/+4/+4 instead of +9/+9/+4/+4/-1/-1.

 

Tier 6

Critical Master - Much love for crit fiends here. Select it twice, never roll confirmation again. If you had Mythic Critical Focus, you can go retrain that now.

Sweeping Strike - This is mega-cleave for reach builds. I’d like to rate it higher but it has the typical cleave drawback that it only works when you are fighting a bunch of mooks you could probably kill already. It has no effect on boss battles unless your enemy is a summoner.

Guardian abilities

Not surprisingly, guardian abilities are particularly useful for tanky builds. They also have some nice utility and defensive abilities for the dangerous duo.

Guardian’s Call

Sudden Block - If you have free swift actions, this can save your allies’ butts. However, as a tank, you really shouldn’t be letting people target your allies anyways. It can work as a band-aid when shit hits the fan.

Absorb Blow - DR or damage reduction mostly on-demand. This still requires a swift action, so beware.

Tier 1

Adamantine Mind - There aren’t a lot of things that can disable a good tank. Most of the things you are still susceptible to are mind-affecting, though. This gives you bonuses against fear, compulsion, illusions, and all the other annoying things that keep you from doing what you want to do. The stun is a nice bonus.

Armored Might - The bonuses to your armor are fairly negligible compared to what your AC should be, but a little more never hurt.

Avenging Maneuver - I want to like this because it gives you an AoO, but the trigger condition is downright awful. You have to 1. Get crit, 2. Survive the crit, and 3. Be able to succeed at a combat maneuver against the enemy that crit you, and 4. Be in range to use a combat maneuver on them. This is even worse than Backlash.

Draw Fire - Just get an Arrow Catching shield. Smaller range, but it doesn’t eat your precious swift actions and is literally a +1 enchantment.

Ever Ready - Now this is the kind of bonus we like! Depending on your tier, this can replace Mythic Combat Reflexes with a scaling bonus on your AoO rolls as well. Just as good for Guardians as it was for Champions.

Irrepressible Soul - Unless you happen to be immune to fear, Adamantine Mind is probably better. Unlike Adamantine Mind, this requires mythic power and you have to have already failed your save once. It also gives you a reroll instead of a bonus, which after tier 4 is statistically worse. Plus it’s restricted on what it protects against.

Mythic Companion - Finally, something to help out the dangerous duo build. I believe this also qualifies them as mythic for the purposes of not getting auto-failed on abilities that target non-mythic creatures.

Pack Wild Shape - Pros: everyone in your party can now have a reach of 20 feet for AoO goodness. Cons: requires someone to be a druid, and that someone is probably not you. Also not very helpful in caster-heavy parties.

Retributive Reach - Yes please and thank you. 5 extra feat of threatened area is actually a massive increase to your threatened zone regardless of what your current reach is, because area = reach^2

Tier 3

Cage Enemy - This is absolutely glorious if you don’t need your swift action. It actually takes the best feature of the Stalwart Defender prestige class and flat-out gives it to you. Note that Stalwart Defenders can still enjoy their Bulwark and Unexpected Strike abilities.

Drive Back - Do you have Greater Bull Rush? This is nice. Like most cleave effects, it has the sad drawback that it has no effect on single-target boss fights.

Impervious Body/Impervious Companion - Again for the dangerous duo, being in melee can be bad for your health and your buddy’s. Your companion is a natural target since your teamwork feats won’t work if one or the other of you falls. This helps prevent that.

Turn the Tables - Much like Avenging Maneuver, this offers too little benefit and is too situational. When was the last time an enemy tried to use a combat maneuver on you, and failed? This turns green if you built to grapple since your opponents have to maneuver check you just to get out.

Tier 6

Companion Power - Your animal companion just got more reach. Duos everywhere rejoice.

Impassible - Some more bonuses to your favorite Tier 3 ability. If you have Greater Trip and your enemy is trippable, this can be pretty nice. It is still fairly lackluster compared to its parent ability.

Indomitable - Immunity to crippling conditions sounds great. Unfortunately, you only get to pick one. This would be viable if you could pick a number equal to your tier or even half your tier. If you can convince your DM to do that then the tier list for condition prevention is something like: confused, frightened, dazed, staggered, blinded, everything else.

Mythic Resolve - Spend mythic power to make clutch saves. This is a nice nat-1 protection, but you probably have better things to do with your life.

Heirophant abilities

This is mostly here for the occasional reach cleric or battle oracle that steps in on the AoO game.

Divine Surge

Inspired Spell - Cast any spell that you could possible know. The sheer utility of this ability makes it far and away the best choice.

Beast’s Fury - Fleet Charge for your animal companion. You still need to get in position yourself.

Tier 1

Mythic Companion - Just like the Guardian path ability. Give your companion a surge, and I believe this also qualifies them as mythic for the purposes of not getting auto-failed on abilities that target non-mythic creatures.

Pack Wild Shape - More Guardian path stuff. Same pros and cons.

Tier 3

Blessed Companion - Similar to Impervious Companion, except that it applies to only your animal companion and not yourself, but doesn’t require another path ability.

Domain Immunity - This is a very unique defensive ability. Try doing the Magic domain, which basically makes you immune to dispel magic, antimagic field, and a whole slew of other anti-caster stuff. Charm - Lust gives you immunity to most of the nasty compulsion spells. Darkness - Loss or Death - Undeath protects against enervation and energy drain. Madness protects against confusion, insanity, and variations on phantasmal killer.

Enduring Blessing - If you take it twice, it suddenly applies to all of the polymorph spells. I suggest Monstrous Physique IV so that you can maintain your armor and weapons while still gaining reach and some sick abilities.

Tier 6

Overcome Curse - Dual-cursed oracles will appreciate this. Even regular people will enjoy being immune to Greater Bestow Curse. Unfortunately it is pretty limited in scope.

Marshal abilities

This is wonderful for bards and skalds, and pretty decent for cavaliers and the occasional dual-path tank. You end up giving massive action economy and bonuses to everyone in your party. Endgame here is that you can round-1 give everyone within 30 feet of you 10 rounds with an extra attack, extra move action, immunity to fear death and unconsciousness, a ton of bonuses to whatever roll they please, and of course a teamwork feat.

Marshal’s Order

Advance - Fleet Charge for...everyone! Minus the attack, at least. Everyone gets to move into position at the cost of one of your swift actions. That is some mad action economy right there.

Decisive Strike - Fleet Charge for someone! This one includes the attack but is less useful because it affects fewer people.

Rally - Give everyone some extra luck on their next turn. This is great for clutch situations mid-fight.

Tier 1

Additional Order - Honestly all three orders have their uses. I would pick up Advance and Rally and leave Decisive Strike though.

Clarion Call - Usefulness depends on what your challenge/smite does, obviously. This can be anything from a CHA deflection bonus to something that only works on your mount.

Directed Assault - Another windfall for a crit fisherman. You can keep your crit and give it away too! This is like Butterfly’s Sting on steroids and since each crit provokes an AoO, it is a free attack for everyone as well.

Inspired Defense - This is a great buff for your bard with Inspire Courage. Unfortunately, if you are using Inspired Courage it usually means that you are not using Battle Song of the People’s Revolt, thus defeating the entire purpose of the teamwork feat build. If you have a way of performing two songs at the same time, this goes back up to green.

Lightning Performance - For low-level bards or ones who have more mythic power than available actions on the first round of combat.

Loyalty - See Leadership feat for details. Note that many other path abilities are worth more than a single feat. Champion’s Crusader ability is comparable but better.

Mounted Marshal - Pack Flanking with icing. If you expect to be charging a lot, this can be pretty nice. You might be able to swing some stupid damage from Spirited Charge/Mythic Vital Strike/Mounted Marshal/Butterfly’s Sting/Maximized Critical if you want to dual path. This is completely unrelated to AoO’s though.

Painful Gambit - Step 1: intentionally provoke from an enemy. Step 2: destroy them.

Perfect Aid - This is another bit of tank support for people who use their AoO on Bodyguard. You should be able to increase your buddy’s AC by a pretty decent chunk now.

Tactical Genius - A free teamwork feat and pseudo-Solo Tactics. Plus you can change it each morning. If you are looking for free feats with icing, this is one of the better icings.

Tier 3

Aura of Perseverance  - I usually view diehard-type abilities as a great way to get yourself murdered. However, this one can be combined with a particular mythic spell called Deathless to make you and your friends within 30ft a juggernaut zombie army. Teach it to your legendary weapon.

Dispel Fear  - This is fairly situational, but one thing that will definitely stop all of your friends from taking advantage of all the AoOs you give them is a mass fear effect. If you are using Aura of Perseverance+Deathless, this can also get them killed as they run away with -300 hit points. If you think you can wait, I’d recommend grabbing Words of Valor from Tier 6 instead.

Glorious Charge - This looks cool on paper but in practice it is about equivalent to the Advance order you got 3 tiers ago, except less malleable.

Word of Hope  - At first glance, this looks a lot like the Rally order. Then you realize that it applies once per round for multiple rounds, making it mostly better. The caveat is that it requires a standard(!) action instead of a swift. If you can hold off getting Rally until tier 3 this is probably worth it.

Tier 6

Inspiring Assault  - The multi-turn version of the Advance order, with the ability to attack instead of move. This one is slightly dependent on you at least attempting to attack or harm your enemy, though, and the haste clause can be problematic.

Unswerving Loyalty - The kind of immunity that we like. It might eat an immediate action but it provides flat immunity to the worst debuffs at the potential cost of a mythic power. No rolls, no chance to fail, just straight-up immunity.

Words of Valor - This is some more action economy lubricant. Multi-round version of Dispel Fear, mass heroism, and extra surges for everyone, all at the cost of a single standard action for you.

Trickster abilities

This path has some decent abilities for crit fishers and potentially combat maneuver builds. I would look at it more as a candidate for Dual Path than anything else, unless you have a whole lot of sneak attacks or somehow got a 15-20 crit range on a kinetic blade.

Trickster’s Attack

Fleet Charge - Just as good for tricksters as it is for everyone else. Enjoy your free pseudo-pounce.

Surprise Strike - More copy pasta from other paths.

Tier 1

Aim for the Eye - Much like Clean Blade from the Champion order, it looks pretty nice but has a lot of potential to just not be useful. Ranged sneak attacks are nice, but you shouldn’t be built for them.

Combat Trickery - You can pump the Bluff skill to death far easier than you can pump your CMB to death. The problem is that if you are going the Ki Throw build, the only build that really uses this, you just made yourself Cha-dependent as well. It is a nice way of getting cleave that still benefits you on single-target fights.

Deadly Dodge - This would be bonkers if it didn’t require mythic power. Since it does, I can only give it a green. Keep in mind that the attack just has to miss you somehow, not necessarily because of the dodge bonus. So displacement, opportune parry and riposte, etc all work for getting you that free AoO.

Down Like Dominoes - Cleave trip! Unfortunately you take a cumulative penalty for each person you cleave, you’d better hope they aren’t immune to trip, and it has no effect on single-target boss fights. Altogether that makes this a bit of a trap ability.

No One of Consequence - This gets an obligatory call-out just for the nondetection ability. As long as your abilities don’t depend on your allies being able to see you, this prevents invisibility spells from being penetrated most of the time.

Path Dabbling - This is equivalent to the best ability you could pick for any path at the tier you are currently at. I highly recommend Directed Assault or Inspiring Assault from Marshal, Retributive Reach or Cage Enemy from Guardian, and Ever Ready, Maximized Critical, or Precision from Champion. If your DM lets you take this more than once, absolutely do it.

Titan’s Bane - Just as good for you as it is for the Champions. Possibly a little better, if you happen to have sneak attack from somewhere.

Treacherous Critical - I want to like this, but it requires you to have two distinct lines of feats: the crit fishing set, which is already pretty demanding, and a combat maneuver set for either reposition or trip. Ask your DM how it combines with automatically confirmed crits if you have them.

Tier 3

Fickle Attack - How many damage dice do you roll on your turn? Take that answer and multiply it by 0.5. That is the amount of damage per round you gain from selecting this ability.

Mirror Dodge - Do you have abilities that trigger when an opponent misses? Spend power to trigger them more often. Honestly, unless you are running Snake Style this probably isn’t worth the power.

Tier 6

Class Mimic - It has some restrictions, so the usefulness of this really depends on what your friends have for their classes. Look at your friends’ auras, bloodline abilities, revelations, and martial abilities.

Precision Critical - If you have sneak attack, you might as well add it onto all of the criticals you will be getting.

Slayer’s Cyclone - As far as cleave abilities go, this one is pretty sweet. You can move between each attack, use your full BAB, and make a number of attacks equal to the number of enemies present plus one.  The restrictions are pretty easy to deal with. Its good enough to get it to half-green, which is pretty high for an ability that you can’t use on single target fights.

Unending Performance - At first glance you would think that you should use this ability for a bardic performance. However, a technical reading suggests that it also applies to buffs that are extremely strong and intended to only last one round. For instance, the Destructive Smite power from the Destruction domain gives a morale bonus to damage rolls equal to half the cleric’s level. The Orc bloodline’s first level power is even stronger, applying the same power to three types of rolls. Don’t have a domain or a bloodline? There’s a feat for that...

Sample Builds

Kelly the Crit-Fisher (Level 3, 6, 11)

Kelly is a half-elf with the Ancestral Arms trait to grant her proficiency with the wakizashi. She uses the Fencer trait to help her AoOs and has a friend with the Outflank feat and a scythe with a x4 critical. Her other trait is Defensive Strategist for her planned levels of Holy Tactician. She is on a 20 point buy and allocated her stats as follows:

Str 14, Dex 17, Con 14, Int 7, Wis 12, Cha 15

At level 3, Kelly has two levels of Lore Warden to give her access to Combat Expertise and fill in a few of her early core feats. Notice that she effectively has two feats per level by utilizing dip feats. She just took her first level of Swashbuckler, with a panache pool of 2 for parrying. Once per round, when she scores a critical hit, she can instead let her scythe friend take it for a huge amount of damage.

Feats: Combat Reflexes (1), Two Weapon Fighting (Fighter bonus), Combat Expertise (Lore Warden), Butterfly’s Sting (Fighter bonus), Weapon Finesse (Swashbuckler), and Weapon Focus (3).

At level 6, Kelly continues to fish around with various classes to complement her build. She takes 3 levels of the Holy Tactician class, grabbing some charisma-friendly goodies and Battle Presence. She doesn’t get as many feats, but she makes sure to take Outflank at level 5 with her friend. Her 4th level stat bonus goes to Dexterity, rounding it to 18. She uses Paired Opportunist with her friend to get extra attacks whenever her 3 attacks with keen wakizashis score a critical. She still passes off one critical per round to her friend since it doesn’t matter whose turn it happens on - the AoO occurs anyways.

New Feats: Outflank (5), Paired Opportunist (Holy Tactician)

At level 11, Kelly’s opening build is pretty much complete. She takes a short jaunt into the Mutagenic archetype of Brawler to give her bonuses to her dexterity and take Improved Critical at level 8 before heading back into Swashbuckler for the bonus initiative, AC, and extra save bonus once per day. Her level 8 stat bonus goes into Charisma to give her extra panache and saves. Also, her criticals that she keeps now make her enemies sickened and she almost always confirms. She trades out her old Keen wakizashis for an Answering one and a Fortuitous one.

New Feats: Improved TWF (7), Improved Critical (Brawler bonus), Critical Focus (9), Lookout (11), Sickening Critical (Swashbuckler bonus)

Including standard magical gear for her level, her AoO’s now strike at 11[BAB]+6[Dex]+1[weapon enchant]+5[AoO bonus]+1[Weapon Focus] for a total of +24. If she is using her answering weapon to parry and riposte, that instead becomes a +28 for a pretty decent chance to wash away an enemy critical.

David the Huntmaster (levels 3, 6, 11)

David the Human is primarily a Hunter, although he does take a jaunt into Cavalier for the Tactician ability and some nice Order stuff. He has a pet Allosaurus that functions as the party’s primary tank. He wields a Fauchard for the extra crit range for Outflank while staying a bit farther to the back so that enemies focus more on the heavily armored dinosaur. His stats are much more strength-focused than Kelly’s:
Str 17, Dex 14, Con 12, Int 13, Wis 14, Cha 8

At level 3, David is pure Hunter. He didn’t want to delay his floating teamwork feat at all, or his Hunter’s tactics, and is mostly focused on building a base for his animal companion to be a beast.

Feats: Combat Reflexes (1), Outflank (Hunter bonus), Pack Flanking (Hunter floating), Combat Expertise (3)

At level 6, David has a strong base and has taken his 2 level Cavalier dip pretty seriously. The Beast Rider archetype lets his Hunter levels stack with Cavalier for advancing his companion. He chooses the Order of the Dragon to help his allies, since he is almost always threatening and the aid another bonus helps him be viable outside combat. His 4th level of Hunter gives him access to second level Hunter spells as well. He puts his 4th level stat increase in Strength and starts looking closely at a Trip build. His Fauchard is now Fortuitous to give his pet an extra smash every time he crits on it, and a Keen Edge wand or friendly caster helps him do that more often.

Feats: Paired Opportunists (Tactician), Improved Trip (5)

At level 11, David is immensely enjoying his second animal focus and, in fact, likes it so much that he got his pet Allosaurus an extra focus to help out with the tanking. His pet’s focuses are usually Bull and Tiger to increase its main stats. All of his remaining levels go into Hunter and he has 3rd level spells already, which he can use with relative impunity due to Escape Route and reach. He got himself a Str/Dex belt to increase his number of AoOs and Greater Trip to take advantage of it. A Commander’s Banner lets him give all of his friends Paired Opportunists twice per day instead of once, and its more likely to last a full combat.

Feats: Totem Beast - Snake (7), Escape Route (Hunter bonus), Greater Trip (9), Critical Focus (11), Shake it Off (Hunter floating)
