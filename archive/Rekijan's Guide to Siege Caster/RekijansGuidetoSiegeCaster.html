Foreword

This is not the most optimal build to play, a straight up sorc/wiz/arcanist will be better. But this is about flavour, the satisfaction of blowing up BBEG’s and doing overkill damage. But I will still try and go over the most optimal way to build this demolition expert. Oh and since Siege engine rules are uncommon I will go over those as well.

Choosing your siege engine

Basics

Siege engines rules - d20pfsrd

So a few important cliff notes. You want to keep an eye out on the damage, the minimum number of crew members, the actions it takes to aim and load, if its direct or indirect, firearm based or not, and speed.

If you don’t have the minimum number of crew members the number of actions you need to aim are doubled. So keep your siege engine crewed well. In the table you can count up the number of actions needed to aim and reload. Each one is a full-round action. But each crewmember can make those.

So if you have 1 aim and 3 reload it takes 4 full-round actions to get it ready to fire. At level 10, due to ranks required, you can take a feat to reduce those actions to move actions for you and your crew (Master Siege Engineer). You still need to make the normal actions to fire (standard action). If you can do aim/reload cycles fast enough (enough crew members) you could full attack as well.

Direct siege engines use attack rolls, indirect use either BAB+int modifier or knowledge engineering. Indirect siege engines still get critical threats on a natural 20. Indirect siege engines also target a square instead of a creature.

Firearm siege engines follow normal firearm rules, like targeting touch in the first range increment, and misfires. Non-firearm based siege engines hit normal AC (or a square in case of an indirect siege engine), and suffer a mishap on a natural one, unless you have the Siege Engineer feat.

If it does not have a speed it can’t be moved and needs to be disassembled and reassembled. And that takes a lot of time. Move speeds are still low though, 10 or 15 feet, and take the minimum amount of crew members to all spend a move action on it.

For the purpose of this guide I am ruling out all indirect fire engines. For our purposes they are just not usable except for very specific campaigns. This is because at best their minimum range is 50ft. Most pathfinder combat is not designed for such large maps, the fighting is usually more in your face.

Light ballista 500gp

3d8 19-20/x2, 120ft direct non-firearm siege engine, 1 crew, 0 aim, 2 reload, speed 10ft.

At 500gp the cheapest siege engine. And even then pretty much out of your budget when starting at level 1. You can choose to use it for level 2, but with standard wealth you could also craft a cannon at level 3. And the ballista touches normal AC.

If you man it alone, it takes two full round actions to reload, then a standard action to shoot.

That means one shot every third round.

If you have one crew member. You can both load. On the next turn you shoot and your crew reloads. Next turn he does another load action and you can fire again.

So with one crew member you can shoot every other round.

With 2 crew members they can reload and you can shoot each round.

If you have the Master Siege Engineer feat you effectively half the time it takes to go through a cycle. Because that full round action becomes a move action, and you can do two move actions if you want to use your standard action for it. That means you only need one crew member in addition to yourself. The crew member will use 2 move actions to load while you use a standard action to fire.

Heavy ballista 800gp

4d8 19-20/x2, 180ft direct non-firearm siege engine, 3 crew, 2 aim, 3 reload, speed 0ft.

For only a d8 more you suddenly need to do 3 more actions? And it can’t be moved without reassembling it. Ammo is also 3 times the price of a light. All in all, not worth it.

Gate Breaker ballista 1200gp

6d8 19-20/x2, 100ft direct non-firearm siege engine, 5 crew, 3 aim, 5 reload, speed 0ft.

While 6d8 is nice and the crit multiplier is one more against objects, the amount of aim and reload actions is absurd.

Manticore’s tail 1000gp

3d8 19-20/x2, 60ft cone direct non-firearm siege engine, 3 crew, 0 aim, 4 reload, speed 10ft.

A special ballista in that it fires in a cone, ignoring concealment. That sounds nice but the cost of each shot is 120gp. For that money you can fire 12 light ballista bolts. And sadly we can’t switch out siege engines as easily as daggers. So unless a lot of combat is packed with a large amount of creatures this might be too situational. You are better off packing some AoE damage spells.

Firedrake and Firewyrm 4000gp/6000gp

Like the Manticore’s tail these are AoO siege engines. This one dealing 6d6 fire damage in a line or cone and setting enemies on fire. Also like the Manticore’s Tail the cost of ammunition just isn’t worth the trouble (200gp/400gp per shot).

Canon 6000gp

6d6 x4, 100ft direct firearm siege engine, 2 crew, 1 aim, 3 reload, speed 10ft.

If you are starting to doubt that there are any good siege engines left, well I saved the best for last.

The price tag is a bit steep, but if you craft it you only pay a third of that. If you include masterwork it comes down at 2100gp. Which is about the cost of a +1 normal weapon. But considering it does 6d6 damage you might as well consider it having the impact enchant instead, sort of. You can afford to craft this at level 3 at the earliest.

Since it is a firearm it targets touch AC. And a misfire value of 1, 20ft. If you are unfamiliar with that mechanic it means if you roll a natural 1 it gains the broken condition and on top of that, as long as it is broken it now misfires on a natural 1 to 5. If you misfire with a broken firearm it explodes, dealing its damage in 20ft (reflex save for half).

With 4 actions to cycle in between shots you can shoot every other round if you have 2 crew members on duty and you yourself are shooting. If you have 4 crew members instead you can shoot every round.

Again if you have the Master Siege Engineer feat you effectively half the actions needed. You need two crew members in addition to yourself to shoot once every round.

Cannon, fiend’s mouth 9000gp

8d6 x4, 150ft direct firearm siege engine, 3 crew, 1 aim, 3 reload, speed 0ft.

A bit more expensive than the normal cannon (ammo is also 15gp per shot more). It is a base 2d6 more. Which isn’t a lot but certainly is nice once you factor in vital strike and crits. The biggest downside over the cannon is that it has no speed and as such needs to be reassembled in order to move. Later this is less of an issue when you can permanency a shrink item spell on it. But early game it is a big deal.

Conclusion

If you want to, you can rock a light ballista at level 2, but if you can hold off to level 3 you can go straight to a cannon. Later on you can switch to a cannon fiend’s mouth once you can permanency a shrink item on it. If you want to you can also keep additional siege items fully loaded with shrink item. Fire them once in combat and then reload after combat and shrink them again.

Armor for your siege engine

Those machines are expensive so you might want to spend some coin protecting it. They all have some hardness and quite some hp but a low AC, because they have an effective dex modifier of -5. Because of this the best armor is usually the heaviest.

A full plate has an AC of 9 and a hardness of 10, and grants hp equal to its AC x5 (modified by size) with an additional bonus hp equal to the AC x 5. The base AC of a siege engine is 5+its size modifier. You add 9 for the armor, use the 10 hardness and calculate the hp based on its size. The price for the armor is for a creature of its size, so x2 for large, x4 for huge, x8 for gargantuan and x16 for colossal.

So let's take our cannons as an example

A cannon (large) has a base AC of 4, a hardness of 10 and 70hp. Adding the full plate armor on it gives it an AC of 13, hardness stays 10 and its hit points become (9*5)*2+(9*5)=125.

For the fiend’s mouth cannon (huge) we have a base AC of 3, a hardness of 10 and 140 hp. So the AC becomes 12 and the hardness stays the same and its hp becomes (9*5)*4+(9*5)=225.

Note that this is non-magical armor, if you want to enchant it (which is double price) you add 2 to the hardness and +10 to the armor hit points. This appears to be a flat increase so I don’t think it gets multiplied for siege engines.

Crew

So as you may have noticed your crew is instrumental to your success. There are several ways to do this each with their own advantages and disadvantages.

Party members

Probably the most reliable since they won’t die as fast as other options, but they generally want to do their own thing. Though some classes run on limited resources, if they have spent them (or want to reserve them) they might be able to help you out. But since that is unreliable it would be more to help pinch in, in case of another crew member going down for example.

Hirelings

The most straightforward method might be to hire some bodies. Trained hirelings have a minimum cost of 3sp per day. And you would need several of them. The downside is that they constantly cost money and die more easily the higher level you get. Not to mention the social implication of being a boss that keeps getting his employees killed. Talking about social implications there is always the options of buying slaves. But they cost more upfront so that might end up being more expensive.

Animate dead

Going beyond the social implications of owning slaves is raising undead to work for you. But generally that means being evil which might not be an option. If it is an option I recommend bloody skeletons simply because they last longer and creating undead has an initial monetary investment you want to protect.

Bonded creatures

Depending on the class you take you might have a bonded creature that can help you. They need to be at least medium size to be effective and have the motor skills required to operate it. An eidolon for example could work, but they don’t have some of the key spells required. Finding a way to make a familiar at least medium size could work though. And maybe your party has some companion creatures that could help you out.

Leadership

Basically unpaid hirelings but they at least level with you somewhat, especially the cohort. But chances are our charisma will be low(ish), we will be an adventure so we move around a lot, and people will die. While on paper I get why people outright ban this feat for being too powerful, but honestly after some play at high level games you will most likely end up with a negative leadership score due to all the death.

Unseen servants

This requires multiple spell slots, and due to their low strength won’t work for some siege weapons, but with a bit of an investment we can make that work. Since it is a low level it is also not a huge drain to recast them should they die.

Constructs

Another option is to build some constructs. Preferably you want them to be at least medium size because some siege engines are slower to load and aim when the crew is small or smaller. You also want to adjust your spell selection to include spells to repair them. Some might already fast healing, otherwise you can take rapid repair. And in any case you want make whole to repair them when they reach 0 hp and get destroyed.

Planar ally/binding

These provide some solid/durable bodies, but they do command the highest price. Since this build already imposes extra costs beyond the normal you might not be able to afford it. So you would have to be very creative in your bargaining with the outsider to offer services instead of gold

Summon monster spells

You would need high level castings to both give multiple creatures as well as creatures that can actually work a siege engine. I mean if you can convince your GM a celestial dire badger can work your cannon go for it, but I have some doubts. The duration also means it is not for extended use, but it could replace your long term crew if they go down in the middle of a fight.

Conclusion

Overall the safest bet, generally speaking is either undead if they are ok to be used in your campaign or otherwise unseen servants. I will focus on the latter for this build (enough other guides on using undead exist already).

Class/Archetype

First off to have the most bang for our buck, we will need a class that has the spell Named Bullet, which is basically only the sorc/wiz list. Inquisitors have it too but with their 6th spell level casting it impairs them too much. A witch has it and 9th level casting but misses other key spells. So we will look towards sorcerers, wizards, and arcanists.

Siege Mage, done. Right? Actually no. This is not the best class/archetype to pick. It just gives up too much for what it gives back. Without cantrips, and 3 opposition schools this just lacks a lot of early game. And early game is where you will struggle. If you start late game you can consider this, or retraining to this potentially but if we are looking for a 1-20 build there is a better way. It has higher siege engine damage due to Empower Siege Engine, but slightly worse spellcasting than the following variant.

Exploiter Wizard/Arcanist Both work. If you want the spell flexibility go arcanist, if you want higher level spells earlier go wizard. Since we will prestige into Eldritch Knight you wont get greater exploits or Magical Supremacy anyway and consume spells won’t be as useful as we can’t spare the charisma. In theory the exploiter archetype isn’t needed either, a vanilla wizard works too but an exploiter works a bit better at certain levels. And as such if you really wanted to you could also do a sorcerer.

Multiclassing

So for this build we will be doing some multiclassing for a bit of extra punch. First off we want to Variant Multiclassing (VMC) into Oracle. This is to qualify for Eldritch Knight (EK) without having to take a martial dip (like fighter) so we don’t lose too much spellcasting. You still lose one level from the first level of EK but can get that back with feats. I will discuss the benefits here, but this part is optional.

Benefits

  1. Higher BAB, means more chance to hit (even if it is against touch) and you can qualify for Vital Strike earlier and even get up to Improved Vital Strike.
  2. Bonus combat feats help offset the ones you lose from VMC
  3. Your final base saves will be more spread out (8/6/10 as opposed to 6/6/12).
  4. If you crit you get to cast a spell as a swift action (must target the same creature or include it in the area of effect).
  5. You get an oracle curse, and martial proficiency with all weapons

Downsides

  1. Strictly RAW you don’t get the two free spells per level as a wizard. Though most GM’s handwave this rule.
  2. Less ‘wizard bonus feats’ and exploits (as well as their scaling)
  3. Your will save is a bit lower.
  4. You get an oracle curse

So how does it even work? Well you take Oracle as your VMC and choose the battle mystery. At 3rd level you get a revelation from it, skilled at arms gives you martial proficiency with all weapons. So that qualifies you for that part of the EK. Then you just need to reach 3rd level casting. And it is why I also prefer exploiter wizard over the arcanist.

The curse you take is up to you, I prefer Deaf because who wouldn’t go deaf constantly firing a canon.

Feats

There are some feats that are just required to make this work well enough.

Extend Spell (Metamagic) - mostly for our crew of Unseen Servant. Starting as early as level 6 you have one up for 16 hours a day with a single cast. Since you need to sleep 8 hours anyway that is practically a full day. It can also be used for a lot of our other spells

Weapon proficiency with your siege weapon. This will probably be a cannon. Note: a siege mage can skip this because he gets proficiency with all siege weapons for free.

Siege Engineer (Combat) - Note a siege mage gets this for free. We want this mostly for the following feat. Note a mishap only matters with non-firearm siege weapons and we will want a cannon.

Master Siege Engineer (Combat) - Reduces the time of the actions that need to be performed. And a siege engine needs multiple actions per fire cycle. So reducing that to a move means your unseen servant crew can go through them twice as fast.

And then some feats are more or less optimal.

Vital Strike (Combat) and Improved Vital Strike (Combat) - The latter you can only qualify for if you multiclass (like EK). Since firing multiple times per round is a hassle increasing the already solid base damage of your weapon works great. A standard cannon goes from 6d6 to 12d6 to 18d6. And a Fiend’s Mouth Cannon goes from 8d6 to 16d6 to 24d6.

Favored Prestige Class and Prestigious Spellcaster - negate the spellcasting level you lose from your first level of EK, and allows you more hp or skill points to boot. This way you keep progressing as fast as  full wizard.

Deadly Aim (Combat) - Since we are targeting touch the loss of accuracy is ok later in the game. We can, at most, get a trade off of -4 to hit for +8 damage. And if we crit with a cannon that will be 32 extra damage. And we will crit a lot (with named bullet).

Craft Wondrous Item (Item Creation) - Siege engines and ammunition aren’t cheap. We can mitigate a fair bit by crafting ourselves. But still making up for it with this feat helps out more than normally.

Siege Commander (Combat) - Since we will be using unseen servants as crew they won’t be trained for that DC10 check. Giving them a +4 helps, and halving the time also helps. Not all siege engines need this, some can move, but the best for us (Fiend’s Mouth Cannon) does. The reason this isn’t in the must have? We can work around this with a casting of shrink item and permanency by the time we could otherwise switch this in.

Spirit Ally - This relieves some pressure of our casting of the spell. But only slightly so probably not worth the feat slot.

Depending on your build you have a few free feat slots to work around with as you see fit.

Spells

I am not going over all spells here. Just some that either complement well with the siege engine and some general gems. Because don’t forget, you are not just someone that fires a siege engine, you are also a 9th level caster. Summon monster is always nice for each level. Especially if you can summon multiple of a lower list to replace multiple unseen servants at once.

Level 0

Acid Splash - If you aren’t a siege mage you can probably pick up acid splash. With a 10gp acid flask you can make this a 1d3+1 damaging spell. Which is the most efficient damage spell starting at level 1 (except maybe disrupt undead). Later on liquid ice with ray of frost is fine too, but liquid ice is more expensive.

Mending - Doesn’t repair a siege engine fast, but it does so for free.

General

Detect magic, read magic, dancing lights/light, ghost sound, mage hand, message and prestidigitation.

Level 1

Abundant Ammunition - You want most ammunition in your bag of holding to reduce encumbrance. But one in a non-magical container so you can use this spell to reduce the cost of all that ammunition.

Unseen servant - You need it to crew if you want to fire the same siege engine more than once per combat. With extend spell these last for 2 hours per level. And if you have the potent magic exploit you can increase that by 4 hours. Be mindful that some siege engines require strength checks, and these have a strength score of only 2.

True Strike - If you do not go with Eldritch Knight you might want to incorporate this to guarantee a hit.

General

Protection from Evil (and others), shield, grease, infernal healing, mage armor, mount obscuring mist, snowball (pre UW), comprehend languages, detect secret doors, magic missile, color spray (low level only), darting duplicate, silent image, vanish, feather fall, magic weapon (until you get greater),

Level 2

Telekinetic Assembly - Before you can shrink item + permanency this can help you set up a siege engine so it doesn’t take hours. Still a minute to cast though, so not reliable once already in combat.

Make Whole - Heals faster than mending, but consumes spell slots. But unlike mending it can potentially fix destroyed magic items. So if you enchanted your siege engine you want this.

General

Resist Energy, Acid Arrow, Create Pit, See Invisibility, Invisibility, Mirror Image, Rope Trick.

Level 3

Clairaudience/Clairvoyance - Could be helpful as a spotter. Especially for indirect siege engines that go very far.

Magic Weapon, greater - Enchanting a siege engine costs double, so using this spell is a safer bet. Also lasts for hours. Only works for direct siege engines though.

Shrink Item - on its own not as efficient, but can help in a pinch until you get permanency.

General

Dispel Magic, Magic Circle against Evil (and others), Nondetection, Protection from Energy, Phantom Steed, Spiked Pit, Stinking Cloud, Tongues, Daylight, Fireball, Lightning bolt, Wind Wall, Major Image, Haste, Slow.

Level 4

Animate Dead - If casting evil spells and creating and commanding undead isn’t a problem in your campaign bloody skeletons make for a great crew.

Magic Siege Engine, Greater - If you want to use indirect siege engines magic weapon won’t work on them. But this one does.

Make Whole, Greater - Repairs more, only a standard action (so doable in combat) and can more easily repair destroyed magic items.

Named Bullet - this right here is a game changer. If you have its name or creature type (easy to know with your high INT score and having knowledge as a class skill) you never misfire, and any hit becomes a critical threat (still have to confirm). With firearm type siege engines confirming should be easy and they have big crit multipliers. Not only that you get bonus damage equal to your level. If the hit would have been a crit anyway that bonus damage even gets multiplied as well.

General

Acid Pit, Black Tentacles, Dimension Door, Solid Fog, Detect Scrying, Scrying, Wall of Fire, Greater Invisibility, Phantasmal Killer, Enervation.

Level 5

Energy Siege Shot - Only works on large siege engines and lasts for a nice duration (10 min/level) but the casting time is 10 minutes so you have to know in advance when to use it. The acid one makes your shot AoE (15ft) for half damage (reflex save halves again). Cold can entangle creatures and they take some cold damage. Electricity staggers. Fire causes things to catch on fire. Sonic can deafen (fort save negates) for an hour. Force just makes it force damage which could be helpful. Overall except for the acid it is not all that impressive for a 5th level spell.

Fabricate - Making new ammo takes quite some time, fabricate speeds that up significantly. Handy if you have time to spare.

Rapid Repair - If you want constructs as a crew this can heal them up quite nicely.

Permanency - Combined with shrink item makes moving even the bulkiest siege engine easy.

Prying Eyes - Scout to hit enemies from several hundred feet away.

General

Break enchantment, cloudkill, hungry pit, roaming pit, teleport, Wall of Stone, Sending, Echolocation, Overland Flight.

Special mention - Unseen crew - If your GM allows these crew members to work siege engines (loading and aiming) this changes a lot and it becomes a replacement for Unseen Servant. You could argue that loading a cannon is a skill a sailor would have. But it does seem a fair bit different from the tasks specifically called out and ‘other such duties’ is vague and thus up to your GM.

Level 6

Antimagic Field - Situational but hiding in a safe place while shooting your siege engine can be clutch. You will need crew that can operate in such a zone though

Named Bullet, greater - Only the extra damage is doubled. Which is nice on a normal hit and great on a natural crit (x4). Mostly not worth 2 spell levels higher, but if you don’t have enough 4th level slots for some reason and can spare some level 6’s it is still an upgrade.

Contingency - Maybe it should be in general because it's not specific to the build but it is just that good.

Energy siege shot greater - Can now target every size of siege engine, but at level 6 not be worth.

General

Wall of stone, True seeing, Chain Lightning, Cold Ice Strike, Sirocco, Permanent Image, Programmed Image, Shadow walk, disintegrate, stone to flesh.

Level 7

Arcane cannon - Not that great but it fits the flavor.

General

Create demiplane lesser (make sure you can get back to it), plane shift, Mage's Magnificent Mansion, greater teleport, control weather, limited wish.

Level 8

Prying Eyes, greater - Great scouting tool to spot from afar, with true seeing on top of it.

General

Dimensional lock, Mind blank, create demiplane, maze, stormbolt, clone, form of the dragon 3.

Level 9

Time stop - Setting up can be a real game changer, especially for this build.

General

Spellbane, Clashing Rocks, Create Demiplane Greater, Interplanetary Teleport, Resplendent Mansion, Teleportation Circle, Meteor Swarm, Astral Projection, Shapechange (see items), Wish.

Items

First of all not an actual item but casting permanency with shrink item on your siege engine costs 7500gp. Or if you can spend 660gp on spellcasting services and 15.000gp to instead permanency an animate objects on it. And you might want to spend some cash learning spells and scribing scrolls.

Weapons

You want to craft a  masterwork cannon and/or masterwork fiend’s mouth cannon. The first costs 2100gp and the second 3100gp. And you could consider a light ballista as an easy backup, to craft a masterwork version is 266gp 6sp and 7cp.

Enchanting a siege engine costs double the normal price. So don’t go overboard on it. You can cast greater magic weapon on it (lasts for hours). And while it only allows you to pierce magic damage reductions (this also means you get to hit incorporeal creatures), with the amount of damage a single shot does we don’t really care much for most DR anyway.

Ammo: A cannon is 30gp (10gp crafted), a fiend’s mouth variant is 45gp (15gp crafted) and a light ballista 10gp (3gp3sp3cp crafted) per shot.

For the cannons you can also consider some blast shots, which allows you to deal your damage in a 30ft cone instead. The cost is 30gp (10gp crafted) and doesn’t say it costs more or less depending on where it is used in. So funnily enough this is cheaper than regular ammo for the fiend’s mouth cannon. And maybe a bow.

You probably also want a backup melee weapon. With the VMC into oracle you are free to pick any martial weapon. Though I still prefer a simple dagger (cold iron or silver) in a spring loaded wrist sheath.

Armor

For the most part a casting of mage armor provides enough benefit. For 3gp you can pick up the eastern armor named haramaki which causes no arcane spell failure and +1 armor bonus without a maximum dex bonus. You can further enchant this, but need at least a +3 haramaki to break even with mage armor. Which then costs 9,153gp. In addition you could get an adamantine version for an extra 5k which grants DR1/-.

Shield

If you wanted to squeeze out some more AC I suppose you could do some tricks with a buckler or something.

Note: I am going over each slot and looking at what is best. You don’t necessarily need all of them.

Head

Cap of the free thinker - 12k, 1/day reroll a failed save against mind-affecting effect.

Headband

Headband of Vast Intelligence - You want to boost your Int like any other wizardly person.

Eyes

Eyes of the Eagle / Lens of Detection - One is 2.5k the other 3.5k. They both give a +5 on competence bonus on perception checks, the more expensive on also on survival when tracking. It's a bargain but you might not track a lot.

Goggles of Night - 12k for darkvision of 60ft can come in real handy. Sure you can cast light, but that also means announcing your presence.

Eyes of the Dragon - 110k, expensive but darkvision 120ft, as well as blindsense 60ft.

Goggles, Truesight - At 184,800gp the most expensive eyewear, but constant true seeing is very valuable.

Shoulders

Cloak of Resistance - The classic boost your saves is a classic for a reason.

Minor cloak of displacement; If you don’t need a cloak of resistance this is a nice alternative. For 24k gold you get a constant 20% miss chance on you.

Neck

Amulet of Natural Armor - Another staple. You won’t get an AC that stops every attack, but you can minimize the amount of iterative attacks that hit you.

Pendant of the Blood Scarab; 1k, when you threaten a critical automatically confirm it in exchange for 1d6 damage once a day. Considering our crits are huge it is tempting. But since we are hitting touch ac we are probably confirming rolls anyway.

Periapt of Health; Become immune to diseases for 7.5k. I feel like between the onset and the rarity of diseases it is not really worth it. But your mileage might vary.
Periapt of proof against poison; Expensive, 27k gold, but total immunity to poison

Hand of Glory; Once per day daylight and see invisibility, but that's not what you are paying 8k gold for. It's the ability to wear and benefit from a third ring.

Chest

Mantle of Faith - 76k, DR5/evil is nice though probably overcome easily at the time you can afford depending on your campaign.

Mantle of Spell Resistance - 90k is a bit expensive but SR21 is nice if you don’t want to pick it up as a spell.

Body

Kimono, Otherwordly - 67k, expensive but, a +4 to all caster level checks is huge. The once per day maze spell is also fantastic, and the +4 resistance bonus means you don’t need the cloak of resistance.

Belt

Belt of Incredible Dexterity, Belt of Mighty Constitution, or Belt of Physical Might. 

Since you don't really need Str covered you want Dex on your belt to boost your AC, Initiative, attack rolls as well as some skills. Alternatively you can go for Con or both.

Wrists

Bracers of Armor - If you want to up your AC this is your best bet. Though expensive they can grant more than mage armor (4 more) and an enchanted haramaki (2 more). Though the haramaki can offer DR1/- so it is whatever you prefer.

Bracers, Spellguard - 5k to gain a bonus on concentration checks to cast defensively. And 3/day reroll such a check.

Hands

Gloves of Arrow Snaring - 4k, Nothing major but you are operating at range. So negating some ranged attacks will probably come in handy,

Rings

Ring of Protection; A classic and best way to increase AC.

Ring of Feather Falling; 2.200gp automatic featherfall.

Ring of Sustenance; 2.500gp to not need food and drink and only sleep for 2 hours.

Ring of Freedom of Movement; 40k, continually under the effect of a freedom of movement spell.

Ring of Evasion; 25k for the evasion ability
Ring of Regeneration; 90k, heal 1 damage and 1 non-lethal damage per round. Immune to bleed. Grow back severed limbs.

Ring of Continuation; At 56k gold not cheap, but extends your most recent personal 10min/lvl spell to a 24 hour duration. Just be careful that casting another spell that qualifies overrides the first.

Spells to consider:

  • See Invisibility: Don’t let invisible creatures sneak up on you
  • Echolocation; Blindsight 40ft.
  • Shapechange: My favorite. Regeneration from giants, flight and other cool stuffs from dragons, sneak around as an animal, blast with breath weapons from both dragons and magical beasts. Switch as a free action once per round if needed

And many more.

Ring of Wizardry - Doubles your base amount of spells, but very expensive. Pearls of power are more gold efficient. The only advantage of this ring is that it doesn’t take an action to use.

Feet

Boots, Jaunt; 3/day as a move action move 15ft that doesn’t provoke. 7.2k

Boots of Striding and Springing; An increase in base land speed of 10ft. 5.5k

Slotless

Snapleaf; 750 one use item to get feather fall. If you don’t want to commit a ring slot for a ring of featherfall.

Pearl of Power - Extra spells per day are a great source to dump gold into.

Pouch, Pathfinder; Small bag of holding that doesn’t detect as magic to smuggle stuff.

Efficient Quiver; More storage for a ranged build.

Bag, Handy Haversack; A bag of holding of sorts for 2k but retrieving an item is a move action that doesn’t provoke.

Four-leaf Clover; 3/day (free action even when not your turn) a +2 luck bonus a single ability check, saving throw, or skill check.

Black Soul Shard; Ignore one negative level per week (12k).

Book, Tome of clear thought; Or any other variant of course to get an inherent bonus to stats if you have the money to spare.

Slotless - Ioun stones

Ioun Torch - 75gp for a light source. You have cantrips for it sure, but this way you can prepare another cantrip.

Tourmaline Sphere; For 1k you count your Con as 2 higher for negative hit points that cause death. The 800gp version gives you a +1 insight bonus on saves versus death effects.

Dusty Rose Prism; 5k gives a +1 insight bonus to AC. Cracked version is 500gp and gives a +1 competence bonus to initiative.

Pale Green Prism; For 30k you get a +1 competence bonus on saving throws, skills, ability checks, and attack rolls. Or 28k for a morale bonus instead. Or 4k for the competence bonus but only on saving throws and attack rolls.

Orange Prism; 30k for a +1 on caster level.

Pearly White Spindle; Like ring of regeneration but cheaper and not per round. Instead you regain 1 hp per hour for 3400 gp or 4 per hour for 18.000gp or per 10 minutes for 20.000gp.

Slotless - Rods

Metamagic Rod, Quicken; Even the lesser version is expensive at 35k. But 3/day you can quicken a spell allowing you to potentially cast 2 spells in a round or 1 and still do a full attack etc etc. Very powerful.

Any other metamagic rod might suit your need as well.

Mundane

Spring loaded wrist sheath; 5gp; Retrieve a small item as a swift action. Takes a full-round action to store. You can wear one on each arm.Can store stuff like a dagger or a wand, and I think a scroll or rod too. A dagger can be a cold iron one (4gp) or a silver one (22 gp). Helpful if you get swallowed whole, grappled or to pierce DR early on. Later on a wand or scroll in at least one of these.

Scroll case, spring loaded: Also 5gp, If your DM rules a scroll can’t go in a wrist sheath, you can always use this. In addition this isn’t limited to one per arm, only limited to what is reasonable.

Traveler's Any-Tool; 250gp Flexible item that can transform into a lot of common tools

Masterwork tools; 50gp for a +2 skill on any one skill check.

And for basic equipment I usually take:

A backpack (2gp), bedroll (1sp), blanket (5sp), 50ft hemp rope (1gp), belt pouch (1gp), waterskin (1gp), 4 trail rations (2gp), cold weather outfit (8gp), small tent (10gp). For a total of 29 gp and 6 sp for the bare essentials. But feel free to scour the items for flavorful additions.

Putting it all together

So at the start of each day you cast an extended mage armor and some extended unseen servants to get you through the day. Boosting the CL with your arcane reservoir if needed. If you are at the level, you cast greater magic weapon on your main siege engine. Ideally you have a permanent shrink item on it. If you know when the battle will take place you can precast (greater) Energy Siege Shot and (greater) Named Bullet. You have your crew of unseen servants to load and aim, and then you shoot with (Improved) Vital Strike and Deadly Aim. Unless you roll a natural 1 you will most likely hit, and through named bullet it will crit, confirming against touch AC will work most of the time too.

A cannon will do 6d6 base, +12d6 improved vital strike, +18d6 from the x4 critical multiplier + 32 from deadly aim (multiplied for the crit), 20 from greater magic weapon multiplied on a crit + 40 from greater named bullet.

For a total of 36d6+92 damage. Or 36d6+212 if it was a natural critical.

A fiend’s mouth cannon has a base of 8d6. Using the same calculations you get up to:

48d6+92 or 48d6+212.

For those last values it comes down to 140/260 minimum damage, 260/380 average damage and 380/500 maximum damage.

If you tough out the early game with siege mage, or start later with it the numbers look a bit different.

A cannon will do 6d6 base, +6d6 vital strike, +18d6 from the x4 critical multiplier + 24 from deadly aim (multiplied for the crit), 20 from greater magic weapon multiplied on a crit + 40 from greater named bullet, and if you want to go full on sacrifice a 9th level spell to get an additional 27 pre-crit and 108 post crit.

For a total of 30d6+192 damage. Or 30d6+312 if it was a natural critical.

A fiend’s mouth cannon has a base of 8d6. Using the same calculations you get up to:

40d6+192 or 40d6+312.

For those last values it comes down to 232/352 minimum damage, 332/452 average damage and 432/552 maximum damage.

Now you might think oh so siege mage is better. But let's not forget it's burning a 9th level spell to do so. And with 3 opposition schools it casts worse as a non-siege mage.

Adding on some animate object fun

Sadly we can’t get animate object on our spell list too. We can however ask our party cleric (if she can cast it, 6th level spell) or hire someone, Hiring a spellcasting service is CL*spell level*10gp. So at a minimum 660gp and then cast permanency yourself (another 15,000gp). If you do this you don’t need shrink item anymore of course (7500gp).

But the spell only allows us to do this on non-magical objects. So don’t enchant the armor if you want to do this.

One major benefit is that the siege engine can then move on its own, at a reasonable 30ft speed. And you can get some construction points based on size. To keep it alive I suggest the metal ability (2CP), so it gains some armor and increases its hardness to 10. If you have it equipped in full plate armor it will have a respectable AC of 25 (large) or 26 (huge). And then add either improved attack to make it deal damage as if one size category larger. And improved the critical multiplier by one with augment critical. Both are 1CP so for a large siege engine you need to choose one of the last two, but a huge one can have all three.

With these modifications lets recalculate the fiend’s mouth cannon.

8d6 base becomes 12d6. With improved vital strike you get +24d6. Together with a crit x5 is a total of 84d6. +40 from deadly aim, +25 from greater magic weapon, and +40/+200 from greater named bullet.

So 84d6+105/265 (min 189/349, avg 399/822, max 842/769)

Or as a siege mage.

72d6+410/570 (min 482/642, avg 662/559, max 609/1002)

In addition you could have an army of light ballista that reload themselves as long as you have the money of course. It takes two actions (full round or move depending on if you have the feat) for them to reload on their own. And then an action to fire. As large objects they would have 3CP. So increase their durability with armor and the metal construction trait. You have one left over to either bump up the weapon dice or crit multiplier or range.
