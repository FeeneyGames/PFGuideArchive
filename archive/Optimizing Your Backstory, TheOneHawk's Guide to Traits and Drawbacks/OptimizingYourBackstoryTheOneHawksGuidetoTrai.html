Optimizing your Backstory
TheOneHawk’s Guide to Traits and Drawbacks

Traits are designed to give slight mechanical bonuses to characters stemming from their experiences prior to the campaign beginning. They’re supposed to be backstory related, but they can also be very important in allowing your build to function optimally. Now I’m all for making mechanically poor choices to support the character’s personality, but I also like to have functional and useful characters and there’s a lot of mechanically poor traits. So I’m going to go through every single one of them and give a quick assessment so that you don’t have to. Grades will be done via colour coding, as popularized by Treantmonk, plus one extra colour because four isn’t enough for me. As a note, I tend to play with third party material, so occasionally a trait may make reference to a synergy with a class that isn’t from Paizo. If you don’t play with third party, just ignore that. Additionally, if you ever disagree with any of my rankings, or simply wish to ask a question, feel free to leave a comment. I’ll get back to you as soon as I am able.

If you want to discuss any aspect of the guide, I have a discussion thread on Giant in the Playground, right here.

Colour Explanations

Red: Bad trait. Unless you -really- want it for flavour, it’s not worth it. If you really want the fluff of the trait, you can probably just add that to your backstory and select something worthwhile instead.  

Orange: Situational trait. Some builds might find this worth selecting, but in general it’s not worth a second glance.

Green: Good trait. Always worth consideration, but not outstanding. You’re certainly not gimping yourself (not that traits are really important enough to do that anyways) but you’re probably leaving a bit of power on the table.

Blue: Great trait. Strong pick in almost any situation, does things that are difficult to get otherwise and is just straight up useful to nearly any build you can imagine.

Purple: Game-changing trait. This is not going to be a common colour, any time you see it you probably want to give selecting it serious consideration if it fits your build and concept at all.

Additionally, all traits have a link to Archives of Nethys if you click on the name of the trait. If Archives of Nethys does not have a trait on their site for whatever reason and it is available on D20PFSRD then a link to the SRD will be there instead.
