What a Cryptic Does 

A Cryptic is best described as a mix of an Alchemist and a Rogue with moderate damage but great versatility. A Cryptic can be a decent ranged damage dealer, secondary melee damage dealer (a la the Inquisitor or Monk), trapmonkey, party face, and scout, making it easy to fit into a party by adapting to the party’s needs. A Cryptic is approximately a tier 3 class - having a decent and useful option in a variety of situations, being very powerful in situations that appeal to its strengths, but generally lacking game-breaking and world-breaking power.

Vital Statistics

  • d8 HD/Average BAB - You’re not winning any HP competitions. You’re either a ranged damage dealer or a secondary melee combatant - don’t go running in to fights alone, but you should be able to take the hits you need to if you have to get in melee range
  • 4+INT skills - Painful on a skill-monkey class, but you’re going to be optimizing Intelligence like a fiend; if you don’t have at least 18 in INT starting out (thus gaining at least 8 skills/level, depending on race and favored class bonuses), you’re probably doing something wrong. Still, consider the Open Minded feat if you’re having trouble keeping up with the skills you want.
  • Good Reflex and Will - Good Reflex is nice for someone who likes to fiddle with traps all the time, and having a good Will save makes you the envy of so many Rogues.
  • ⅔ (as Psychic Warrior), INT-based manifesting - You’ve got some real gems on your power list, but you can burn through all of your PP for the day very quickly if you’re not careful. Your class features can get you through a lot of tight spots, but your manifesting is often a final “ace in the hole” for the worst situations.

Stats and Suggested Races

There are three main builds I consider when building a Cryptic.

  1. A ranged Cryptic build, optimizing around Disrupt Pattern (or firearms, with a Kaigun), built mainly like a vanilla Alchemist
  2. A Str-based Cryptic build, optimizing around a two-handed weapon or natural attacks, built mainly like an Inquisitor (requires Brutal Disruptor archetype or Pattern Breaker’s Gloves for natural attack builds)
  3. A Dex-heavy “TWF” build, optimizing around hitting as frequently as possible, built mainly like a Rogue (requires Brutal Disruptor archetype or Pattern Breaker’s Gloves)

Though there undoubtedly more potential builds out there, I would consider picking one of these and sticking with it, at least for your first Cryptic.

When building a ranged Cryptic, your go-to stat distribution should look like this:

INT>DEX>CON>WIS>CHA>STR

So when picking a race for your Cryptic, the primary feature you’re looking for is a good Int modifier; most other things are secondary. A good Dex helps with the ranged touch attacks you’ll inevitably be doing for disruptions, as well as initiative, AC, and quite a few helpful skills. Con is nice to sure up your D8 hit dice, and Str is good for some Brutal Disruptors, but a good Int modifier makes for an effective Cryptic. Small races fare better because of size bonuses to stealth and size bonus to hit (though obviously not as good at melee builds). A Wis of 13 is a nice bonus if you’ve got room in your build for Psionic Meditation, but don’t drop it under 10. Will saves are ugly if failed. Cha is OK if you’re going to be the party face, and this is the only build that might have room for it.

Anything that doesn’t give a bonus or penalty to Int is mediocre; you might have a great story to tell, you just won’t be optimized. Generally, anything with a penalty to Int is terrible.

A Str-based melee build is probably going to look more like this:

INT>STR>CON>DEX>WIS>CHA

        You’ve got an Int-to-damage class feature, and your manifesting and other class features still run on Int, but Str suddenly becomes a lot more important. Con and Dex are still vital (Con especially). You often are still going to want Psionic Meditation, but at this point, Wis might join Cha in becoming a dump stat - you’re just too MAD (multiple attribute dependant) otherwise.

        A Str-melee Cryptic build is similarly going to value the Elan, Human, Tiefling, Half-Orc, Half-Elf (consider trading Skill Focus for Martial Weapon Proficiency), and Maenad, but anything with a Con penalty becomes suspect or worse. Small races are generally are unfit for a Str-Cryptic build. Hobgoblins are surprisingly viable, as are Oreads (especially with alternate racial features), Half Giants, and the Psionics Unleashed Duergar (as opposed to the Paizo Duergar). Natural attack builds can pick up claws and bites from a variety of races; they’re great for low levels, but Bite of the Wolf/Claws of the Beast/Metamorphsis will eventually outstrip them in high levels of play. This build is especially nice for its feat-independence. Grab Power Attack at level 3 and you’re mostly set as far as melee feats, opening up things like Expanded Knowledge for juicy buffs from the Psion and Psychic Warrior power lists.

        A Dex-based melee build might optimize thusly:

INT=DEX>CON>STR=WIS>CHA

Int remains king, but not by much; high Dex modifier is crucial for picking up TWF feats and hitting with Weapon Finesse. Str can be dumped entirely if needed, but remember that your Int-to-damage is in addition to your Str bonus, rather than replacing it. If you don’t think you’re ever going to get an Agile weapon, but you’ve got a generous point buy or great die rolls, it can be advantageous to run a decent Str and skip weapon finesse. Wisdom carries the same caveat of “grab a 13 if you’re going to get Pionic Meditation, if not, eh.” This is the most painfully feat-heavy tree; Rogue Talents like Finesse Rogue help them get TWF builds going faster. Without dips for bonus feats, you’ll suffer at low levels if you try to dive into melee, making this build more attractive for campaigns starting at levels 3+.

Proficiencies

        -Light armor, simple weapons+rapier and shortbow. Nothing special, but note that armor does not interfere with manifesting, making Shield Proficiency or Medium Armor Proficiency potentially useful (but not always optimal)

Pattern Designs (Su)

        -Your powers are etched on your skin like tattoos - make sure to cover up - and your powers all have a unique visual display connected to your character. These mostly have RP implications, but be wary of who you manifest powers around.

Altered Defense (Su)

        -Minor defensive bonus for a few rounds a day, until it becomes permanent. AC is probably your best go-to defense early on, with the bonus on attack rolls decent for melee Cryptics. Not a game-changing ability, but it gives you something to do with your swift action at early levels.

Disrupt Pattern (Su)

        -This is going to be your bread and butter damage dealer for most of your career as a Cryptic. The INT-to-damage function is great in and of itself, rewarding you for optimizing INT even more than you already would have been for being an INT manifester and skillmonkey.
