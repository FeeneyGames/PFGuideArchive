Baggageboy's Mini Guide to Making the Most of UMD at Low Levels

UMD is often touted as being one of the premier skills. That is absolutely true, however, it's not easy to utilize at low levels. By low level I'm talking levels 1-3 where wealth is tight and it's difficult to have a very high skill bonus without an inordinate amount of investment. I’m not going to sugar coat it, UMD is tough at low levels, but there is a light at the end of the tunnel. Bear with me as I break down the difficulties because there ARE things you can do and it can be AWESOME!

First I’ll break down some high points from the rules, then discuss the minimum bonus, what I call the “sweet spot,” then list some good items. If you already feel like you understand UMD and are just here for a list of good items feel free to skip to the end.

So let's set the stage. Here's a link to the UMD rules. Read it. It’s the go to for questions of what you can and can’t do.

Major Takeaways: (Updates Coming!)

  • Barring special ability no take 10.
  • This means hitting our DCs with confidence can be hard at low levels.
  • Like most skills you don't automatically fail on a natural 1. However there is an added effect that if you do roll a nat 1 and fail, you can't use that item for 24 hours.
  • If you have something you need to rely on UMD to do consistently buy a backup device.
  • Activating an item blindly is hard at low levels. It's also dangerous as if you fail by 10 or more you cause a mishap, and the minimum skill modifier to avoid risking this is 15 (13 if we've activated that particular item before). We are not going to be doing much of this at level 1, but by level 2 or 3 we can try our hand.
  • Use a wand is a flat DC20.
  • This is great at higher levels as it's trivial with high skill bonuses, but that's not us.
  • We won't likely be using wands in active combat unless we get VERY desperate.
  • Using scrolls is a DC20+the scrolls caster level
  • Using scrolls is even harder than wands! Bummer
  • A lot of GM's wave this requirment, but technically you need to emulate an ability score to activate a scroll if yours isn't high enough. That means rolling twice. We want to avoid this if at all possible as it's another chance to fail, and we will probably be rolling a lot before we succeed.
  • Look for scrolls that key off of abilities you already have. If you have high intelligence for example pick items from the wizard, witch, or psychic spell lists. If you have high charisma we're talking about sorcerer, bard, oracle and summoner lists. It also means making your wisdom at least 12 will go a long ways to making certain items (cleric, druid, ranger) much easier to use.

Ok so what does this all mean? Basically it means that at low levels we are going to be limited to wands and low caster level scrolls due to both high DCs and low wealth.  Also we can't reliably activate anything in combat (barring special abilities). So what can we do then? We can still use UMD, it's just going to take a long time and a lot of rolls. So we target items with that in mind. Specifically items that provide buffs/utility with long duration (read 10+ minutes), or instantaneous effects. We want things that it doesn't matter how many rolls we have to make before they are active and that can last long enough to accomplish something even with a low caster level. And guess what? There’s actually a lot of items that fit this bill.

Getting to the sweet spot:

So we really want to get to at least a +15 bonus. At +15 we are never at risk for a mishap when activating an item blindly. For example, a bonus of +15 means that if we roll a nat 1 while attempting to activate an item  we'll have a result of 16 which means we missed the DC25 by 9 and so don't trigger a mishap. If we're working with an item that can be activates multiple times we can take advantage of the +2 for previously activating the item and so we only need a bonus of +13 after the first time we activate it.

I'm not going to do an exhaustive list of options you can use to get to this sweet spot. There's plenty of guides out there that can help with that, but I will list some of the basics to illustrate that it's not hard if you are serious about it. Let's assume you have an ability bonus of 3 (cha, or not if you use an ability to switch it,) UMD is a class skill, and you put a single rank into the skill (duh). That by itself gets you to +7. Throw in a trait bonus and skill focus and +11 is easy at first level. Add an ioun stone or similar item and a rank or two more and +13 is achievavble. That should be totally doable for most characters by level 3.

For fun here’s a sample rogue build at level 1:

Clever Wordplay (use int instead of cha)

Dangerously Curious (+1)

14 int (+2)

1 rank UMD (+1)

Skill focus UDM (+3)

Class skill (+3)

Total: +10

So he grabs the party’s CLW wand and tries to heal his barbarian buddy after a fight who’s pretty beat up. Each roll he has a 55% chance of succeeding, and a 45% chance of failing, with a 5% chance of bungling it so bad he can't try it again today.

Items that are good for low level UMDing:

Wands

1st level (CL1, 750gp)

  • Cure Light Wounds
    (Don't bother springing for higher level versions. In combat healing is rarely a good idea and the limitations on our abilities only make this worse. If you find one great, but don't buy one.)
  • Infernal Healing
    (Better than CLW usually if you can swallow the evil descriptor)
  • Heightened Awareness
  • Mount

(makes overland travel easier, as well as getting out your loot, plus disable device for dummies)

  • Keep Watch

(If you like to adventure in heavy armor and don’t want to worry about having to take it off to sleep this can be a literal lifesaver in the event that your GM likes to ambush you while sleeping, also great for evil parties)

  • Endure Elements

(If you’re in a game where this matters it probably matters frequently and a lot)
