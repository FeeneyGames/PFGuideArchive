Familiar Ratings Explained


Rating the familiars is a difficult task, as everyone has their own personal rating system.  I have put together what I look at for the ratings you will see below.

The primary qualities I looked at for All familiars are as follows.

  1. The familiars movement.  To prevent fliers from dominating this category they were only judged in this vs other fliers and were set with a 40’ speed with (average) maneuverability as their baseline.
    Special movement modes were given extra credit if commonly useful (ex. Earth glide)
  2. High Perception and additional sensory options such as Darkvision (DV), Blindsense (BS) or Tremorsense (TS) were favored, especially with a High Stealth for scout classifications.

Once the above things were factored in, we then looked at the lesser qualities below.

  1. AC improved ratings at the High Category, had no change at Base, but gets looked down on if poor.  The Familiar can be targeted unless it’s inside a familiar satchel at all times and never comes out.
  2. Strength was given a minor weighting in the case of fliers because it affects how much can be carried and still fly.
  3. Physical damage is mostly ignored because it's so low and even with some of the abilities available such as Poison, the DC is often so low it's nearly useless.

Improved Familiars (but not base familiars) were also judged on

  1. Do they have Hands & Can they Speak (thus make use of UMD)
  2. What spells and spell-like abilities they bring to the table and how often can they be used.

Base familiars (but not improved ones) were also judged on what bonuses they provided to their master.
        1st Tier: Bonus to Initiative, Fort & Will save boosters see the highest preference here.
        2nd Tier: Bonus to Reflex Saves & Perception checks make the second tier.
        3rd Tier: Bonus to any skill except Perception, or highly situational modifiers.
        If something is situational or of limited access, it gets knocked down 1 tier.

What is a Familiar?

A familiar is different for each person.  To some, they are nothing more than an extra ability or attack option.  For others, they can be as fully fleshed out as any well designed NPC.  Some DM’s target them heavily in combat because they dislike or are annoyed by them (which encourages those going for the passive benefits).  Other DMs can even twist a small tale around the familiar making it the star of the show for a night.  There is no right or wrong way to view a familiar because every situation, every party is different.

I personally cherish a well thought out story between the master and familiar and love seeing a familiar get played on my table.  My personal table uses the rule of if it has an intelligence score of 3 or higher, another player at the table gets to run it.  This usually ensures the familiar gets a bit more polish and shine and helps ensure that one player doesn’t hog the spotlight.  I believe that if you really want your familiar to shine, it’s not just the mechanics that will make it so, it’s the story that lives through it.

How do I get a Familiar?

There are many ways to acquire a familiar, the easiest being via a class choice, but here is a list of multiple classes, archetypes, class abilities and feats that can help you get a familiar of your own.  I have included several archetypes for classes that automatically give a familiar because the archetype provides a variation of the ability.  If the familiar is still the same for an archetype as the core class would be, it was not included below...

Feats available to “Anyone”
Feat:        Eldritch Heritage        Arcane bloodline
Feat:        Familiar Bond                This gets you a familiar, just lacking most abilities. Req: either Iron Will Feat or House of Green Mothers Pupil (magic trait).  Unless your class has a high Will save I favor Iron Will.

Imp. Familiar Bond:        The upgrade to Familiar bond, gives you all the goodies.
Feat        Hands Detachment        A bit feat heavy, but it can work for an unusual familiar.
Feat        Improved “EH”        Serpentine Bloodline
Feat:        Wasp Familiar                Must worship Calistra (or a deity of Trickery, Lust, and Revenge)

Feats (advanced) available: These take a bit more work than those above.
Feat:        Improved Familiar:        Replace your base familiar with an often stronger option.
Feat        Companion Figurine        Turn your figurine of wondrous power (Serpentine Owl, or Silver Raven) into something more like an animal companion or a familiar.  Other figurines seem to just be animal companion based.

Feat:        Vampiric Companion:        For a Vampire or Dhampir with 10 levels in a class granting a familiar (or animal companion).  Your familiar becomes undead, gains all vampiric (or dhampir) weaknesses, gains fast healing 5, and more depending on what type of vampire you are.  This will greatly extend the lifespan of a Protector familiar.  If you are a witch with Greater Gift of Consumption you can pass on lots of debilitating problems to your familiar instead of yourself.

Base Abilities of a Familiar.

*Or Now that I have a familiar, what does it do?*

So to begin let’s go over a refresher of the common familiar abilities and what they bring to the table.
*A familiar is treated as a normal creature of its own type, and use their own ability score modifiers for everything.  They do not share additional modifiers the master may have (such as temporary HP, a resistance bonus to saving throws, etc.)


1) Hit Dice: For the purposes of all effects related to HD, use the master’s character level or the familiar’s normal HD total whichever is higher.

2) Hit Points: The familiar has ½ of its masters maximum total HP (not including temp HP), rounded down.  This is regardless of its normal HD.

3) Attacks: Familiars use the master’s base attack bonus as calculated from all his classes (instead of their own), but they use their strength or dex mod (whichever is greater) to calculate their melee attack bonus with natural weapons.  The damage they inflict is as a normal creature of their kind.

4) Saving Throws: For all saves use either the Familiar’s base (Fort +2, Ref +2, Will +0) or the master’s (as calculated from all his classes) whichever is better.

5) Intelligence: A familiar’s intelligence is increased to a base of 5, +1 every odd wizard level the master has (6th at 1st level increasing to 15 by 19th level) This likely makes him smarter than most other members of your party.

6) Skills: For each skill in which either the master or familiar has ranks, use either the normal skill points for an animal of that type or the master’s skill ranks whichever is better. Regardless of a familiar’s total skill modifiers, some skills may remain beyond the familiars ability to use. Familiars treat Acrobatics, Climb, Fly, Perception, Stealth, and Swim as class skills.

7) Natural Armor: Every odd wizard level the familiar’s natural armor improves by 1 point.

8) You may change the Feat the familiar normally gained at 1st level from the default for something else it qualifies for.  There is a list of feats below that were specifically designed for familiars.

9) Improved Evasion: If the familiar makes a successful Reflex saving throw against an attack that normally deals ½ damage on a successful save they instead take no damage, on a failed saving throw they take only ½ damage instead of full.  If they are helpless or wearing medium or heavier armor they do not gain the benefits of this ability.  Mithril since it is treated as light for all cases except proficiency would be a good material for barding (or armor) for your familiar to help keep it safe.

10) Share Spells: This lets you hit your familiar with a wide variety of great defensive spells that you normally could only target yourself with enabling it to survive many more combat situations.  It also opens up options that would normally not affect creatures of the familiars type (usually magical beast).

11) Empathic Link:        This does not allow you to speak or communicate.  You “feel” an emotional state, you can tell when the other is scared, hungry, interested, aroused, frightened, etc. Simple basic emotional states and this might give you that moment's heads up that something is wrong, or tell you it found something it considers noteworthy.  One must be glad they are not limited to normal animal intelligence, so you will get less false positives as to something a base animal would be interested in or afraid of.

12) Alertness:        This bonus while nice is only available when your familiar is within arms reach, so good out of combat but not something to rely on at all times unless you just passively keep your familiar in a pouch and never do much else with it.

At 3rd: Deliver Touch Spells: This is where most people start tossing their familiar in combat.  You first must be in contact with your familiar and cast the spell, then it can be sent off to deliver it to the enemy.  Several things must be considered regarding this ability. 1) Using this requires your familiar to be in contact with you as you cast the spell.  2) Many familiars are tiny and thus must enter the enemy’s square (possibly provoking an AoO) to discharge the spell.  This makes selecting your target and timing crucial to minimize risk to your familiar.  If your familiar is small sized though this is much easier.

At 5th: Speak with Master: You and your familiar can now speak to each other as if you shared a common tongue.  Others still mostly rely on magical means to speak to a basic familiar unless it is a Raven or Thrush at which point it can speak a single language you already know that they might share.

At 7th: Speak with Animals of Kind: While this sounds great, your mileage will vary depending on your familiar choice and the DMs style & whims.  I find Birds are everywhere, Rats, Reptiles, and Cats are fairly common, amphibians, bats, weasels though are all fairly limited.


At 11th: Spell Resistance: Any chance to resist enemy spells is a good thing, and the wording of this ability is “that another spellcaster”, which means it doesn’t apply against spells you cast.


At 13th: Scry on Familiar 1x/day: When sending your familiar out as a spy this might be useful if through its empathic link it can signal you as to the best time to use it.  Otherwise, though it doesn’t seem to get much use.

Familiar Archetypes

Rules & Limitations

All archetypes swap out abilities that are common to base familiars and swap them out for new abilities tailored to a particular theme.  Some nonstandard familiars also alter or replace these features; such familiars can’t take familiar archetypes that alter or replace the same feature, just like two archetypes cannot be combined if they both alter or replace the same thing.  Where levels are referenced refer to the master’s level in the class that grants the familiar as a class feature.  Some familiars’ cannot take specific archetypes as listed below.

Archetypes: Figment & Soulbound: Elemental Whisper, Shaman Spirit Animals, Witch Familiars, and Familiars that grant spells can’t take these archetypes because the archetype is attached to patrons or spirits.  Also, the figment was written before the shaman class and according to the Designer should not work with the Shaman’s Spirit Guide. (source)

Archetype: Protector: A tumor familiar is already considered part of the masters body and thus cannot take this archetype. (sorry Alchemists.)

Familiars: Improved: They can not take any archetype that alters or replaces the variable bonus a familiar grants its master or speak with animals of its kind.  (A leshy warden’s leshy familiar does not have either of those abilities so doesn’t have abilities that stack with those familiar archetypes).


Familiars: Shapechanging: The Magical Child’s animal guide, the Imp, Quasit, and others must have the same archetype for each form and it must be legal for all those forms. (meaning if any form is an improved familiar, it can’t take archetypes that don’t stack with improved familiars.)

Familiar Archetypes Explained:

In this guide, some of the archetypes that can be combined will have their own entry explaining how to utilize the combination to its full extent.

        Ambassador:

        Green for a High Int but low Cha build as if you can spare the ranks it can be the party face, provided the people you are speaking to are fine with speaking with an animal.

Bluff, Diplomacy and Intimidate become class skills, and it gains the Persuasive feat instead of Alertness making it quite the social familiar.

Unlike a normal familiar the ambassador’s does not see a large boost to Intelligence, instead, its Charisma is increased to 6 and improves at the rate most familiars intelligence does.  Its intelligence is increased set to 6 when it becomes a familiar and remains there (or it retains its base Int if that was higher).  This can help if you use your familiar to intimidate regularly.

Special Req: The familiar able to speak at least 1 language either as being a Raven or Thrush, or an improved familiar.

Abilities Lost: Alertness, Intelligence Boost.

        Animal Exemplar.

This is the leader of the pack able to use Handle Animal as a class skill enabling it to lead via pushing other animals to do what needs to be done.  As you advance in levels it will gain a bevy of new abilities, unfortunately, limited to only working on animals of its own kind, such as Speak with Animals at 3rd level (instead of 7th), Influence Animals (at 7th) which works as per Druids Animal Empathy.  At 11th it can cast Charm Animal 3x/day and at 13th Dominate Animal 1x/day.

Special Req:        Can not be applied to Plant, Vermin, Improved, or any non-animal themed familiar.

Abilities Lost: Deliver Touch Spells, Speak with Animals of Kind, Spell Resistance, Scry on Familiar.

Arcane Amplifier

The secret of the arcane amplifier familiar was first discovered where winter witches used white-furred foxes or hares to strengthen their spells.

        Echo: 1x/day when delivering a touch spell an arcane amplifier can apply the Extend Spell Metamagic Feat to the spell.  They can use this ability an additional 1x/day at 8th and 13th levels.

        Reverberate: at 7th level, 1x/day when delivering a touch spell the amplifier can apply either the Empower Spell metamagic OR use Heighten Spell metamagic feat to increase its spell level by 2.  This can be used 2x/day starting 11th level.

        Resonate (su): At 13th level 1x/day when delivering a touch spell, an arcane amplifier can apply the Maximize spell metamagic feat to the spell.
        NOTE: An arcane amplifier can apply only one metamagic effect to a single casting of a spell using its echo, reverberate, or resonate abilities.

Abilities Lost: Alertness, Improved Evasion, Speak with animals of kind, Spell Resistance, Scry on Familiar

Familiar Archetypes Continued:

        Decoy:         This might work for someone who needs a distraction on a regular basis.

Your familiar is bait.  At first, it gains the Deceitful feat so it can bluff being wounded to draw someone (or something) to it.  At 5th level, it can start sounding just like you so may draw enemies away to check that out.  Once you hit 11th level though it can take on your form for several minutes at a time to really sell the “Look at me” bit.  This means your party needs to work with that tactic for it to be effective.

Abilities Lost: Alertness, Speak with Animals, SR

Associated Feat: Decoy’s Misdirection. - Reviewed down with other feats.

Egotist:        While the RP amuses me, overall I just don’t see it being that great.
        This archetype gains Intimidate as a class skill which it will often use to attempt and bully its “master” into doing what it thinks is best especially once it hits 3rd level and can speak with the “master” directly.  Once it reaches 5th level it can demand a touch spell be cast upon it, and by doing such its “master” can cast it on the familiar as if the spell had a range of Close as if using the Reach Spell Metamagic.  Once it hits 11th level it can scry on its “master” 1x/day.  In addition to all this it does not provide its “master” with the Alertness Feat or the Variable bonus provided to the master (such as a +4 bonus to Initiative of the Rabbit) instead it keeps those bonuses for itself.

Abilities Lost: Alertness, Masters Familiar Bonuses, Speak with master, Deliver Touch Spells, Scry on Familiar.

Soulbound:        So. give it skill focus a 1x/day spell, and make it expensive to replace. Pass.
        You implant a part of a creature's soul (possibly your own) into a familiar, this gives it Skill Focus as a bonus feat in a skill important to the creature the soul originally came from, it also grants a partially neutral alignment rather than the same as its master (unless the master donated his own soul).  As long as this focus remains intact it can be removed and bound to any other familiar (with the same cost and time of replacing a familiar).  Doing this will enable the new familiar to have all the memories and personality of the old one.  The focus itself has a hardness of 8 with 12 hp and a break DC of 20, although it cannot be attacked unless surgically removed from the familiar.  Creating a new soul focus if the old one is destroyed costs just as much as replacing a familiar (in addition to having to pay to replace the familiar anyway).  At 8th level the familiar gains one of the following spell-like abilities based on its alignment.  CN: Rage.  LN: Suggestion, Neutral: Deep Slumber, NE: Inflict Serious Wounds, NG: Heroism, no matter which ability is chosen it is cast 1x/day at a caster level 3 lower than the masters.  At 13th level the reduction of the caster level is removed.

Valet:                The Valet treats Craft, Perform and Profession as class skills.  It shares all teamwork Feats that the master possesses, and can use Open/Close & Prestidigitation at will.  When delivering a harmless touch spell it can move both before and after delivering the spell as long as its total movement does not exceed its speed.  Last but not Least it gains Able Assistant (see below).  In addition to all this as you progress at 7th level it can also move before and after using the aid another action just like it does with touch spells.  At 13th level it can use aid another as a full-round action to aid up to 3 adjacent creatures bonuses from the action, each bonus may be for the same action or check or for different actions or checks.  For all this, you lose Alertness and scry on familiar and it cannot speak with animals of its own kind.  This is a very powerful option, but not one available for Improved Familiars.

School Familiars

All of these require the feat School Familiar to be taken, the School must match your chosen school of magic, and the familiar cannot have any other archetype.
        All School familiars are limited to delivering touch spells only of their matching school of magic, but they also gain one cantrip from that school as an at-will ability.  Once you develop powers from your school that work a limited number of rounds per day, your familiar can also activate them but at twice the cost in duration used.  This familiar type also can be improved at 10th level by taking the Greater School Familiar Feat which will open up an additional power for your familiar.

School: Abjuration:        This is a fairly solid choice for a school familiar.
                Lesser: Familiar is immune to magic missile, and gains Energy Resistance equal to ½ your wizard level (min 1), that it may share with any 1 ally within 5’.  You can change this energy type as a standard action.
                Greater: When the familiar strikes a creature with a natural attack the victim is also hit with a Dispel magic effect at the masters caster level.  If you can keep your familiar alive in combat this is an amazing way to get an edge through the fight even with the limit of 1x/24 hours per target.

Greater: When you cast a summon monster spell, if your familiar is in range it can inhabit the body of one creature summoned, keeping the familiar’s mental states but gaining the physical of the summoned creature.  The familiar is safely ejected when the spell ends or the summoned creature dies.
~This is a fairly strong power here for using summoned creatures intelligently.

Lesser: Familiar gains the benefits of the Forewarned school power, and the Master & Familiar can trade initiative results.

Greater: Master can Scry on Familiar as greater scrying at will.

Bloodline Familiars

At 1st level, anyone with access to a bloodline can choose to gain a bloodline familiar (of their bloodline).  They treat their class level as their wizard level for the purposes of this ability and the familiar gains an additional ability based on the master’s bloodline.

This will replace the 1st level bloodline power normally granted, and in addition, they will gain bonus spells from their bloodline one level later than normal.  Thus a sorcerer with the aberrant bloodline would not gain the acidic ray bloodline power, and they would gain their first bonus spell at 4th level, second at 6th level and so on.  Given the nature of this guide, I will assume that the bloodline familiar abilities will be used as written.

I feel the Familiar is more flexible to have (Especially once you get Improved Familiar by level 7) compared to any of the 1st level bloodline powers.

Familiar Feats

Familiar feats can be taken by characters who have familiars that meet the listed prerequisites. Wizards can take a familiar feat as a bonus feat, and witches can select a familiar feat in place of a hex. If you lose your familiar and gain a new familiar that doesn’t meet the listed prerequisites for a familiar feat you possess, your new familiar doesn’t gain the benefits of that feat. A new familiar that meets the prerequisites automatically gains the benefits of that feat.

When you gain a new level, if your current familiar does not meet the prerequisite of a familiar feat you possess, you can learn a new familiar feat in place of the one it qualifies for. In effect, you lose the old familiar feat in exchange for the new one. The feat lost can’t be a prerequisite for another feat you possess, and your familiar must meet the new feat’s prerequisites. You can exchange only one feat in this way each time you gain a level.

So what does all this Mean?

Effectively it means your familiar can do more than just stay in your pocket and hide in place.  With the right familiar and archetype combo, you can pull quite a few new tricks from your sleeves that many DMs will not expect.  One must remember though that just like with a PC, a familiar needs forethought and a bit of work to excel at what they do, and everyone has a weakness.

        The: “Pocket Familiar”: is the most common type of familiar that exists in all honesty.  You need a Tiny (or smaller) familiar and a Familiar Satchel to keep it.  This familiar is used primarily for the master’s bonus (often Initiative or a Saving throw boost), and can be made more useful by giving it a good archetype, either Protector for the shared HP & AC boost it provides, the Sage for the alternate take on knowledge checks, or the Valet for coming out in town to help craft items, and last but not least the Figment/Sage combo.

        To be marked as a Protector the familiar needed a +3 dex mod or higher & not be a “red” consideration.

The “Scout”: is the 2nd most common familiar type I have seen.  These familiars are often high in stealth and reasonably high in perception on their own, their masters ranks make them even better.  With Archetypes Pilferer or possibly Infiltrator it can scout ahead of the party and warn them of danger.  The empathic link you possess will  help you realize something is wrong without even a direct report returning to you.  Fliers have the advantage of mobility and the raven or thrush particularly have the advantage of speech at 1st level, but one must note that several ground-based familiars are far stealthier.
        To be marked as a Scout they need a +20 combined Stealth & Perception and have a movement speed of at least 30’.


        The “Combat” Familiar: There are several types of combat familiar each putting your familiar at a differing degree of risk, those are usually either the “Flanking Buddy” or “Bomber” roles. Fortunately sharing spells such as Mirror Image or Shield can provide the needed boost to keep your familiar alive.
        A “Flanking Buddy” is where your familiar gets into combat serving as a flanking aid for a much more dangerous ally, most frequently those that gain sneak attack due to the flank. By use of delayed and readied actions, the rogue can optimize his attack to when the familiar is in position, and if all goes well kill the opponent before it can do anything about it.  A familiar with Flyby attack or one with a master possessing lots of Acrobatics serves in this role quite well.  If the familiar is also a mauler or has been imbued with a touch spell they can dish out a bit of extra damage.  Stealthy familiars should not negate this option either since if done right the enemy won’t realize they are there until it’s too late[k][l].
        “Bombers” see the most use at lower levels when more enemies will fail the checks to avoid the effects of Alchemist Fire, Tanglefoot Bags, or Caltrops dropped from above by the familiar.  Flying familiars do best, but some other ground-based familiars (the ones with arms) can throw these devices into the fray as well. Another option on this vein is “Light the Way” having your familiar snap then immediately drop Sunrods, or pull a stone with light out of a sack and drop it. Thus illuminating areas up ahead with leaving your familiar as minimally exposed due to the light as possible.

“Extra Eyes”: If your familiar is highly perceptive, but not so stealthy keep it nearby, it can be an extra set of eyes to see things sneaking up on the party.  This role is great for those with blindsense, or tremorsense since they can detect things most people can’t.

If you want your familiar to fill this role, make sure it has a minimum +11 Perception.

So what does all this Mean (Continued)
        “Mover’s”  Flying and Climbing familiars see the most use here, but a swimming one can also help on rare occasions.  This usually involves taking a grappling hook (or even lighter grappling arrow) with silk rope attached either up or across some obstacle and leaving it where it can be helpful so the party can bypass the challenge with greater ease.

The “Packmule” tends to be used by Quadrupeds since they can carry the most by default.  With the addition of the Ant Haul Spell to triple their carrying capacity it can make up for the loss by size it tends to suffer, then slipping on a pair of Muleback Cords (1k) can enable it to carry as much or more than you.  This role rarely sees any use unless the party lacks other ways to carry lots of gear with them.

The “Mount” has a few angles of approach, all of which are rarely used.

The Constant Mount: Requires a small sized master with Undersized Mount Feat, and a Small sized familiar so they can ride his familiar at any and all times.  Adding Ant Haul and Muleback Cords let the familiar carry you with ease as you get around.  This will happen most likely with a   familiar go get one that can move faster than you can and you would be ok.  Warning enemies tend to shoot at mounts, and it’s easier to lose and replace a riding dog (or equivalent) than a familiar.

The Temporary Mount: This requires a Mauler Familiar, and the master being no more than medium-sized with the undersized mount feat or capable of riding medium sized mounts.  Often this is best served with a flying mount who can take a larger form and get you up where you need to go before its battle form runs out.

In Either case using your familiar as a mount may turn it into a target which is one reason it’s hardly used, but I have seen it used (even if not the most effectively) in PFS play so I feel it is worth including here.

Familiars: Improved

There are several ways to get “Improved” familiars, the most common of course is through the improved familiar feat.  Another option available Poppet Familiar (CL 3 required), or Craft Construct (another way to make a Poppet)

Improved familiar usually requires you to be within 1 step of their alignment on each part of the axis.  Thus an Imp (LE) will work for anyone who is LE, NE, LN, or True Neutral.

Improved familiars no longer give a bonus to their master like the base familiars do, on the other hand they have other unique abilities or attributes that make them popular.  The biggest draw is usually one that possesses both Hands, and the ability to speak, thus they can through sharing your skill ranks of Use Magic Device make use of items such as Wands or Scrolls.

Level 3 Improved Familiar options

At 3rd level if you take the Improved Familiar feat, you have the option of either 1) Summoning a new Base Familiar (listed above) with a Template.  Or instead summoning an improved familiar (listed below).

There are 5 templates you can choose between to add to a base familiar with the following abilities.

All templates listed below gain SR equal to its base CR +5 (so 5 for most familiars).
All except Fey Touched also gain Darkvision 60’ and a 1x/day Smite Attack that enables them to add their Cha mod as a bonus (if any) to hit, and their HD (equivalent to masters) to damage for the attack.

As you reach 5th & 11th level their resistances will improve by 5 points each (to 10 & 15 points respectively) and they gain DR as well.  The bonuses for the fey template is listed with its entry.

Celestial X:        Gain the default abilities as well as Resistance 5 to Cold, Acid & Electricity, and Smite Evil.

                At 5th level gain DR 5/Evil and increase it to 10/Evil at 11th.

Entropic X:        Gain the default abilities as well as Resistance 5 to Fire & Acid, and Smite Law.

At 5th level gain DR 5/Lawful and increase it to 10/Lawful at 11th.

Fiendish X:        Gain the default abilities as well as Resistance 5 to Fire & Cold, and Smite Good

                At 5th level gain DR 5/Good and increase it to 10/Good at 11th.

Resolute X:        Gain the default abilities as well as Resistance 5 to Fire, Cold & Acid, and Smite Chaos.

Fey Touched:        Gain Low-Light Vision, +2 bonus to Will saves, Woodland Stride as the druid ability & Change shape into a single fixed small or medium humanoid form (as per alter self).
