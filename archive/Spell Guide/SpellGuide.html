Introduction

This is a project I felt needed to be done. Every time I look at spells I feel like I am second guessing myself on what I want available for the spell lists that I am building. Sometimes I end up regretting my choices, realizing in the middle of the game that I should have had one spell instead of another. I am no expert on spells, even with the years of experience I have playing fantasy RPGs, but I hope to provide a resource that people can turn to when they are looking to select spells for their character. I also hope that people find this informative and helpful, and refer to it often in the course of the character’s career.

I do realize that not every build or class will find spells equally useful. Because of that I will try and address this in both the guide and ratings I give out to the spells. Even then, I know I will have missed something. Do try and take what you read here as my opinion and not fact. You might find that your character uses a spell I rated as bad quite frequently and in imaginative ways. On the other hand, you may discover that an excellently rated spell is one you never use.

How To Use This Guide

This document is meant to be viewed as a web page, not a word document. The problem I recently discovered was that if you have them, page breaks are going to be there no matter what I do. I will look into other options on where and how to post this later. For right now, I am sorry for the page breaks.

Because of the sheer size of this guide, I will be splitting it up into 10 different documents. Each spell level will have its own document. You can find links to these documents at the bottom of this introduction. I will be using the space below this intro later to cover various topics related to spells, so keep and eye on that space in the future.

If you are looking for a specific spell, the simplest thing to do is use ctrl+F and search for the name of the spell in the document that covers its spell level. If you are trying to build a spell list for your character from scratch, look at the Related Spread Sheet first and use the filter to show only your classes spells. This is done by finding your class column and pressing the filter button (on the right side of the toolbar, looks like a small triangle on a stick,) and then pressing deselect all on the window that pops up. From there, type in the spell levels you want and then press select all. You can also use the filter button to sort the spells however you want by using other columns.

I am still trying to figure out how to do the ratings on the spread sheet. I am open for suggestions, but currently if there are multiple ratings for a spell then the average is listed.

Color Code For Ratings

I mostly follow the standard of what is established in class guides, but added the + and -. This is because there are hundreds of spells per spell level, and just having four ratings isn’t enough to really distinguish the excellent from the almost excellent or the absolute worst from the mostly bad.

Excellent+          Spells of this rating are so good, they are nearly game breaking.

Excellent         Consider first for spell slots.

Good           Good second options for spell slots.

Average         Use these to fill holes in your spells known.

Bad                If you want these, use scrolls of magic items to cast them.

Bad-                 Why? Why would you ever consider using this spell?

How The Ratings Work

I am trying to rate spells by if you should have them known or prepared. I am not rating the spell on how good or bad it is, with the exception of the Excellent+ and the Bad- ratings. There are plenty of well designed and useful spells that you don’t want to have as known or prepared spells. An excellent spell, for example, is one you should consider first for spells to prepare or know. They are the spells that most builds will find useful and will cast frequently. Good spells are spells you could consider second, while average spells are spells you could use to fill in extra slots if you have them. A bad rating does not mean you shouldn’t use the spell at all, but they are mostly very situational and would probably be better from items like potions, scrolls or wands. There are bad spells you don’t need or wont even use because there are better options, and I will try to mention those options, but for the most part don’t ignore a spell just because it has a bad rating. If the name of a spell interests you, but the rating is bad, go ahead and read it. You never know what you will truly use until you study all your options.

What You Won’t Find

I am not rating the communal spells. The reason is simply to cut down the workload I have to do. I know there are not many of them, but I would rate them very close to the rating their base spells have. If you are looking for those spells, just go to spell they are based on.
