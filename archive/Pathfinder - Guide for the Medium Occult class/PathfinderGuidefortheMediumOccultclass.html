The Medium Occult class

(Pathfinder)

What is the concept of this class ?

The medium is a kind of mystic who channels spirits of heroes from the past, so that those spirits can help him in his adventures. Depending on his needs for the day, he can choose to channel the spirit of the right hero to get the help he needs most.

However, he is required to find the spirit before channeling it. To do so, he will find specific places such as altars, council rooms, forts, etc, and channel a spirit related to the place. Typically, in a city or town, it is intended that the medium can choose between several or often all of his spirit choices, whereas during travel in the middle of nowhere, he will have a hard time finding any spirit to channel at all.

At any time, even without any channeled spirit, the medium is also a spontaneous psychic 4th level spellcaster. But many of the spells on its spell list are available at a lower level than for other classes, so that he gains access to them as soon as a 6th level spellcaster would, or even, for a few of them, as soon as a 9th level spellcaster would.

The medium caster stat is charisma, with diplomacy, bluff, and intimidation as class skills, so he will likely be the one who talks.

So the concept of the class is a charismatic buffed 4th level spellcaster, who can choose to gain features of any of 6 other classes each day depending on the channeled spirit. The 6 kinds of spirits are :

  • The champion, adding features of a fighter
  • The archmage, adding features of a wizard
  • The hierophant, adding features of a cleric
  • The trickster, adding features of a rogue
  • The marshal, adding features of a bard
  • The guardian, adding features of a paladin or invulnerable rager barbarian

This makes the class really versatile : the medium has the potential to do almost any action available in the game. On the other hand, the medium doesn’t get all those options all the time, he has to choose one spirit each day, and some days, some spirits are not available.

Therefore, it is important not to focus on one specific spirit. First, because it would go against the concept of the class and remove a part of fun, and secondly because your favorite spirit will not always be available. (Note: If you still want to focus on one spirit, you can and it will be viable but only if you get a reliable way to channel it most of the time. However, this guide assumes that you will be routinely using several spirit categories.)

As you have virtually access to almost every spell in the game, and can make use of almost any feat in the game, this guide points out a selection of what I have found most useful for the medium.

Color Rating Code

In this guide, I will follow the usual color system to evaluate options.

Purple: Incredible. You have rarely seen something as good as this.
Blue: Excellent.  A great choice.  Much better than nearly any other option.
Green: Good.  A solid choice.  Perhaps not the best, but these will serve admirably.
Black: Average.  Functional.  A good baseline measurement.
Orange: Sub-par or situational.  Not actively harmful, but too narrow or niche.
Red: Poor.  A bad choice.  Broken, or else actively works against you.

Overview of class numbers and proficiencies

First, let’s have a look at the class stats.

  • Base skills per level : 4
  • Hit dice : d8
  • Basic attack bonus per level : 3/4
  • Only one good save : will
  • Proficient with simple weapons only
  • Proficient with medium armor

So overall, without any channeled spirit, it looks like an average non martial class.

Spirits

Now let’s have a more detailed look on the class main features : spirit channeling.

Mechanics

First you need to find a place where you can channel spirits. Each spirit category has a list of places where it can be channeled, called its favored locations.

Once you have found the place, you can take 1 hour to channel a spirit. From level 2, you will want to do that with your allies because they will benefit from the seance boon of the spirit for the next 24h. At the end of the channeling seance, you gain the benefits from the spirit and the spirit gains 1 point of influence over you. 

Some abilities will increase this influence the spirit has over you. When you reach 3 points of influence, the higher presence of the channeled spirit confuses you, so that you suffer a -2 penalty on initiative checks, and an additional penalty which depends on the chosen spirit category, but you gain a +4 bonus against possession effects and a +2 bonus on saving throws against mind-affecting effects that are not related to possession.

When you reach 5 points of influence, the spirit takes full control of your body and you become an NPC controlled by your GM until the end of the 24h after your seance! Try to avoid that, or at least discuss it with your party members.

The benefits from your channeled spirit are:

  • You gain a spirit bonus on a list of checks specific for each spirit category. The spirit bonus is +1 at 1st level, and increases by 1 at levels 4, 8, 12, 16, 20. Depending on your channeled spirit, this bonus can apply to 0, 1, or 2 saves so that the class has effectively 1, 2, or 3 good saves (at high levels, this bonus is exactly the same as the bonus provided by having a good save). This bonus also applies to one of the 6 basic ability scores, with some limitations.
  • You (and your allies, from level 2) gain the seance boon specific for each spirit category. This does not increase with levels, so it is especially powerful at low levels. In any case, for party members, it adds on top of numbers that are balanced without this bonus, so it will always be appreciated.
  • You gain special abilities called spirit powers specific for each spirit category. You gain spirit powers at levels 1, 6, 11, and 17. Those provide the main flavor of the spirit choice.
  • Once per round, you can gain 1 point of influence to use a spirit surge to add 1d6 (1d8 at level 10, 1d10 at level 20) to any d20 roll on which your spirit bonus applies. You do that after the roll has failed. Knowing the result of your roll may indicate whether you have failed by far or not, and you can decide to add 1d6 to make it succeed instead. This is very strong.
  • During your seance, you can choose to take a taboo, specific for each spirit category, to gain 2 free uses of spirit surge. Do it most of the time. Choose whichever you like; you can always find one which is easy to respect. Occasionally, you can violate it once or twice in the day, and you just get 1 point of influence each time.
  • You can also choose to forego a number of spirit powers (more powerful first) to gain 2 additional free uses of spirit surge, but you will not do it often since most of your spirit powers are very useful.

The Champion

You transform into a martial class.

Spirit Bonus applies on:

  • attack rolls, effectively promoting you to a full BAB class (+1 except at first level).
  • non-spell damage rolls, granting some damage as martial classes do.
  • Strength checks and Strength-based skill checks, effectively increasing your strength, except for your load capacity.
  • Fortitude saves.

Seance Boon: +2 bonus on all non-spell damage rolls. This is shared with your party members, so it is extremely good at low levels, and always very good against creatures with damage resistance. It will do best with multiple attacks, especially for yourself because it stacks with the spirit bonus.

Favored Locations: Arenas, battlefields, places of violence, practice yards. You will almost always have access to this one.

Influence Penalty: You are quick to violence and prefer a weapon to spells or contemplation. You take a penalty equal to your spirit bonus on Intelligence checks and Intelligence-based skill checks, and to your caster level (to a minimum of caster level 0); as usual, a reduced caster level may render you unable to cast spells. You can’t benefit from effects that increase your caster level.

Avoid reaching 3 points of influence before you have used all your best spell slots, losing them is bad. It can also make your character make poor decisions.

Taboos: Choose one: you are superstitious about arcane spellcasting, so you must not be the willing target of arcane spells or abilities and you must attempt a Will saving throw against even harmless arcane spells and abilities; you may not make an attack with any weapon except a specific manufactured weapon (such as “my father’s +2 falchion”) that you choose when you take the taboo, and you may not choose unarmed strike for this taboo; you must accept any challenge to prove your prowess in battle, including challenges to single combat—if you or an ally breaks the rules of the challenge, you break this taboo.

Spirit powers

Champion’s Prowess (Lesser, Su): You gain proficiency in all martial weapons and in one exotic weapon of your choice, Which you choose each time you channel a champion spirit.

You don’t want to invest in a weapon that you can use only with a champion spirit. Nevertheless, the following items can help you make use of this ability : 1/ the Estoc and Bastard Sword are Martial when two-handed but exotic one-handed, so if you choose one of them as your main weapon, this ability allows you to use it even with only one free hand ; 2/ the Bladed Belt magic item can transform at will into any masterwork slashing or piercing weapon, including an exotic/martial weapon or a simple weapon.

Sudden Attack (Intermediate, Su): Whenever you take a full-attack action, you gain one additional attack at your highest base attack bonus. This ability stacks with the extra attack from haste, but it doesn’t stack with special actions that grant extra attacks, such as flurry of blows or spell combat. Sudden attack works as normal with full-attack options such as two-weapon fighting. 

More attacks? That’s exactly what we need to optimize our spirit bonus and seance bonus! And at full BAB, so it can actually hit!

Fleet Charge (Greater, Su): You can take a swift action and a full-round action to move up to your speed and then make a full attack. This special action doesn’t combine with sudden attack, but works as normal with effects such as haste and two-weapon fighting. As soon as you begin moving, you are considered to have begun the full-round action, even if circumstances prevent you from attacking at the end of your movement.

While the full-round system in Pathfinder is annoying, this gives you a reliable means to full attack every round. And it is even better than pounce, because pounce has to be a charge.

Legendary Champion (Supreme, Su): You gain two combat feats of your choice, and you can choose different feats each time you channel a champion spirit. You can treat your base attack bonus from your medium levels as equal to your medium level for the purposes of qualifying for combat feats and determining their effects, and you can count your medium levels as levels of one other class whose base attack bonus equals its class level (such as fighter, barbarian, or slayer) for the purpose of qualifying for the two new combat feats.

The class lacks the combat feats of real martial classes, and this fixes this issue, but only at level 17...

The Hierophant

You transform into a cleric. Quite useless if there is a true cleric in the party. If not, you gain some of the benefits of having a cleric in the party by simply being able to channel a hierophant spirit, and most of them by actually channeling one.

Spirit Bonus applies on: 

  • Wisdom checks, Wisdom-based skill checks, Will saves, effectively increasing your wisdom.

Will is already your good save, so it would be better to increase Reflex or Fortitude checks.

Wisdom-based skills include Perception, which is very good.

Seance Boon: Your healing spells and abilities heal an additional 2 points of damage to each target. This does not affect healing conferred by magic items, nor does it add to fast healing or similar effects.

It is likely that it will not benefit your party members at all, but it is very good on yourself..

Favored Locations: Altars, churches, sacred groves, shrines. This spirit will often be available because pathfinder is a religious world.

Influence Penalty: Whether the spirit hopes to offer a chance for redemption or to sacrifice foes later on a dark altar, You must strike for nonlethal damage in combat whenever possible. You take a penalty equal to your spirit bonus on all Charisma checks and Charisma-based skill checks involving worshipers of faiths other than the spirit’s, except checks to convince others to convert to the spirit’s faith.

Decreasing your main stat is bad, even if it does not affect your spells. If you fight against constructs or other creatures immune to non-lethal damage, you won’t be able to damage them, but you can still support your party with spells and healing. Take a taboo and avoid reaching 3 points of influence.

Taboos: Choose one: you must strongly revere nature and may not wear metal armor or shields; you must follow a paladin or antipaladin code as appropriate for the spirit’s deity; you may not deliberately speak any lies, including bluffing, exaggerating, stating half-truths with intent to deceive, lies by omission, and so on, and if you know the answer to a question that someone asks you, you must give the answer.

Spirit powers

Divine Surge (Lesser, Su): This power functions as the archmage arcana spirit power, except that you add spells from the cleric/oracle list instead of the sorcerer/wizard list (these spells count as divine), and you need a divine focus if the spell requires one.

As with the archmage, gaining the spells per day of a 6th level spellcaster is extremely good because your medium spells are lower level than they would be for a true 6th level spell caster, which means that you are better than a true 6th level spellcaster.

On top of that, you have access to 1 spell of each level you can now cast. To exploit your seance boon, you will likely choose mainly cure spells until you get the next spirit power. So you can always heal your party of sicknesses, poisons, ability damage, resurrect them, etc, just wait a day to channel a hierophant spirit. Interesting spells are (non exhaustive) :

lvl 0 : detect poison
lvl 1 : remove fear, remove sickness, liberating command, obscuring mist, sanctuary, stunning barrier, comprehend languages, diagnose disease

lvl 2 : muffling sound, silence, admonishing ray (very useful under the influence penalty), remove paralysis (until you get placebo effect), lesser restoration, aid, consecrate, angelic aspect, delay disease, delay poison, dress corpse

lvl 3 : remove curse, remove disease, create food & water, dispel magic, symbol of healing, communal resist energy, searing light, see beyond, invisibility purge, water walk, Magic Circle against X

lvl 4 : restoration, sending, tongues, water walk communal, air walk, black spot, control water, dimensional anchor, freedom of movement
lvl 5 : raise dead, breath of life, scrying, plane shift, life bubble, break enchantment, commune, call spirit

Energy Font (Intermediate, Su): You can channel energy a number of times per day equal to 1 + your Charisma modifier. Choose whether you channel positive or negative energy each time you contact a hierophant spirit; this choice must match the spirit’s faith. If you choose positive energy, add cure spells of each level you can cast from the cleric list to your medium spell list and spells known. Otherwise, add inflict spells in the same way. These spells count as divine, as in the divine surge spirit power.

You gain the ability to channel as a true cleric, even better with your seance boon ! You have less base use of it than a cleric, but it is compensated by the fact that charisma is your main stat. You also gain automatic access to all cure spells, which frees much needed space for other cleric spells.

Overflowing Grace (Greater, Su): When you heal a creature to full hit points or a creature already at full hit points with your positive or negative energy, that creature gains a +1 sacred bonus on attack rolls, skill checks, ability checks, and saving throws for 1 round. The bonus is sacred if you use positive energy and profane if you use negative energy. If you destroy or kill one or more creatures with positive or negative energy, you gain a +1 bonus of the same type on attack rolls, skill checks, ability checks, and saving throws for 1 round.

It will trigger often when you channel on your allies. The bonus on killing creatures will likely not be very useful because once enemies are killed, the bonus is useless, and it triggers only on an ability which kills every enemy at the same rate. It is only a +1 bonus...

Legendary Hierophant (Supreme, Su): Once per day, You can request a minor miracle. This request must be in line with the options for miracle that don’t cost powdered diamond. As usual for a miracle, the deity or other being the spirit worships can choose whether or not to grant the request at its whim.

This provides you with abilities of a 9th level spellcaster, even limited, it is very good. Effects of miracles which cost powdered diamonds can be achieved with an archmage spirit.

The Marshal

You transform into a bard. Probably the best spirit by default when you are in a party. It is effective both in and out of combat.

Spirit Bonus applies on: 

  • Charisma checks and Charisma-based skill checks, effectively increasing your main stat, except that it does not affect you spellcasting. You were already a social expert, and this makes you even better.
  • Spirit surge rolls.
  • All your spirit powers, which are extremely good.

Important: You will want to boost your spirit bonus for this spirit (see the Spirit Focus feat, the spirit-bonded armor enchantment, and the Fiend Keeper archetype).

Seance Boon: Choose a seance boon from any of the other legends to benefit from. When using the shared seance class feature, each participant can choose a different boon. Best seance boon, obviously.

Favored Locations: Council rooms, stages, theaters, throne rooms. This spirit can be channeled in most cities and villages, but almost never outside.

Influence Penalty: The time you spend concentrating on your allies prevents you from attending to other important matters. You take a penalty equal to your spirit bonus on Wisdom checks and Wisdom-based skill checks. Additionally, if you are not at least nominally in charge of your present allies, you lose the marshal’s spirit bonus and seance boon.

Annoying for perception, but well, it is ok, which is great because you are going to need all the spirit surges you can get.

Taboos: Choose one: you may not allow yourself or your allies to prevent a fleeing enemy from escaping, since an at-large villain makes a better tale (this applies only to enemies seeking to escape, not to those retreating to alert others or fight again from a better position); you may never leave an ally behind or allow yourself or your allies to sacrifice another ally, even a summoned creature; you must embrace any opportunity to spread the legend of yourself and your allies, even when a low profile would be helpful.

Spirit powers

Marshal’s Order (Lesser, Su): You can use your spirit surge on attack rolls, saving throws, ability checks, concentration checks, and skill checks rolled by you or any allies who participated in your seance as long as you have line of sight and line of effect, and are within 30 feet of the chosen ally. You can still use spirit surge only once per round.

Yes, you can add 1d6+spirit bonus to almost any failed roll of any of your party members, at almost any time. This is truly amazing. Be sure to take a taboo and be at least nominally in charge of your present allies. And choose your spirit surges wisely, you can use only 5 spirit surges per day, but you can avoid a death on a failed saving throw, finish a bad fight by allowing a hit or a critical hit that would have missed on your big guy who has probably chosen the champion seance boon, save an important bluff, diplomacy, stealth, disable device, or escape artist check, etc.

Inspiring Call (Intermediate, Su): As a standard action, you can grant all allies who can see and hear you a competence bonus equal to your spirit bonus on either saving throws or attack and damage rolls. You choose which bonus to grant each time you use inspiring call. This ability lasts for 1 round. If you have the decisive strike greater spirit power, you can use inspiring call as a move action. If you have the legendary marshal supreme spirit power, you can use inspiring call as a swift action.

Well, if you work on increasing your marshal spirit bonus, you actually do your part of the job just by spamming this. Once again, those bonuses add on top of numbers that are balanced without them, and scale with your level ! If everyone in the party gets +4 on all their attack rolls, it can double the number of attacks that hits. If you also add +4 on all damage rolls, which makes +6 with the appropriate seance boon, you probably do much more than your part of the job if everyone is alive.

Decisive Strike (Greater, Su): You can allow the marshal to gain 1 point of influence over you as a swift action to allow an ally within 30 feet who can see and hear you to make a single melee or ranged attack during your turn. You can use this ability as a standard action to instead allow the ally to perform any standard action.

The previous spirit power is now a move action, which means that you can now take actions yourself, with all your bonuses. This almost adds a party member. Additionally, you can use your influence pool to provide more actions to the party, but be careful, this will rarely be as good as a spirit surge.

Legendary Marshal (Supreme, Su): You can use a lesser spirit surge without incurring influence. This lesser surge adds a maximum of 1d6 to the roll and does not apply your spirit bonus, regardless of other effects or abilities you have that alter your spirit surge. A lesser surge still counts as a spirit surge for the purpose of the limit of one spirit surge per round, but it does not consume any free uses of spirit surge you may have from effects such as taboo.

A spirit surge every round ! Don’t spam it blindly though, you only have one per round, so don’t risk to boost an unimportant roll and not be able to save the life of your ally later in the same round.

The Trickster

You transform into a rogue. Not so brilliant in combat, this spirit can be used to perform tasks that nobody in the party can do.

Spirit Bonus applies on: 

  • Dexterity checks, skill checks, and Reflex saves, effectively increasing your dexterity but not quite so because you miss armor, and maybe attack and damage. Remember that initiative is a dexterity check.
  • But wait, all skill checks, not only dexterity based ! This is very useful to perform all sorts of tasks.

Seance Boon: Choose one skill when you gain this seance boon. You gain a +1 bonus on skill checks using that skill, and that skill counts as a class skill for you.

Tell your party members about this ! If they take one rank in a skill which is not a class skill for them, they get a +4 untyped bonus on it with this seance boon. Each party member can choose a different skill, so everyone will benefit from it.

Favored Locations: Alleys, mazes, taverns, trap-filled locations. You will find it in any city or village, and in any trapped dungeon, which will happen quite often.

Influence Penalty: The trickster within you is a protean manipulator, and you begin to see the possibility that everyone around you might have ulterior motives as well. You never count as an ally for the purpose of gaining benefits from another creature’s abilities, nor do you count as a willing target for spells. Anyone attempting to target you with a touch range spell, even a beneficial spell, must succeed at a melee touch attack, though you need not attempt saving throws against harmless spells. You gain no benefit from another creature’s aid another attempts, as you are too busy making sure they aren’t secretly tricking or sabotaging you.

Annoying but not that bad compared to other spirits. Don’t be afraid of using your spirit surge on skill checks.

Taboos: Choose one: you can’t abide revealing your true identity, and you break this taboo when anyone pierces your disguise, even your own allies; you can never tell the truth; you can never pass up a more lucrative offer, even if it causes you to switch sides.

Spirit powers

Trickster’s Edge (Lesser, Su): Choose any two skills. These skills count as class skills for you, and you are treated as if you had an additional number of ranks in those skills equal to your medium level (to a maximum of your character level).

From a skill with no ranks, which is not even a class skill, you can bump it to fully trained, class skill, +1 from the seance boon, +spirit bonus, and a spirit surge if needed ! Any skill. So, any day, you can decide to be much better than an expert of your level at any skill. This can be very useful for getting out of a bad place with climb, swim, stealth, knowledge (geography), for crafting or repairing something, for some spying with stealth, bluff, etc.

Surprise Strike (Intermediate, Su): When you attack an opponent that’s denied its Dexterity bonus to AC, you deal 1d6 points of extra precision damage for every 3 medium levels. Your target counts as flat-footed against the first attack you make against that target in a day, regardless of abilities like uncanny dodge. After your first attack, that target is immune to being made flat-footed by your surprise strike for 24 hours.

A true rogue gets 1d6 every 2 levels. You cannot sneak attack by flanking... Again not so good in combat. Well, your target counts as flat-footed against your first attack, which compensate a bit. But your spirit bonus applies on initiative, you probably have enough initiative to have your first foe flat-footed anyway.

Transfer Magic (Greater, Su): You can allow the trickster to gain 1 point of influence over you to make a melee touch attack against another creature as a standard action. If the attack succeeds, you randomly steal one of the harmless spells of the highest spell level active on that creature, ending the effect for the original creature and gaining all remaining duration for yourself. This ability does not work on personal range or instantaneous- or permanent-duration spells.

Only occasionally will you encounter a foe who has a spell on him worth using this ability. It will likely be something like shield or haste.

Legendary Trickster (Supreme, Su): Once per day, instead of rolling for a skill check, you can choose your d20 result. Additionally, you gain the ability to change form at will as if using greater polymorph, except that you can mimic the form of a specific individual.

Huh, huh, greater polymorph at will, really ? Additionally, you can “mimic the form of a specific individual”, which means that with your high charisma score and a forced “20” on your disguise or bluff check, this mean you can basically impersonate anyone. A bit late, but still, insanely good in a social context.

The Archmage

You transform into a wizard. Well, a bad wizard actually. A true wizard will be able to cast your medium spells like you but with higher DC, and will be a true 9th level spellcaster.

The availability of the archmage can be very useful, but only on some specific occasions or for crafting purposes. You won’t channel this spirit by default.

Spirit Bonus applies on:

  • Intelligence checks, and Intelligence-based skill checks, effectively increasing your intelligence, except for skill points.
  • concentration checks

No save, you don’t make intelligence checks, you don’t have a lot of knowledges, it does not apply on caster level checks. Well, there is concentration, which is something

.

Seance Boon: Your damaging spells deal an additional 2 points of damage of the same type that they would normally deal to each target. Your party members will probably not benefit much from this, but it is good for yourself.

Favored Locations: Arcane redoubts, areas of unusual magic, libraries, schools. Wow, this is specific, villages won’t have such places, not all dungeons will have one.

Influence Penalty: Your body begins to respond as if you were a frail, aged scholar. You take a penalty equal to your spirit bonus on Strength checks, Strength-based skill checks, Constitution checks, attack rolls, and non-spell damage rolls.

A lot of penalties, none of which is negligible. Quite bad overall, but you will not always use a lot of influence, avoid getting 3 points of influence if possible.

Taboos: Choose one: You eschew all faith in the divine, so you must not be the willing target of divine spells or abilities and you must attempt a Will saving throw against even harmless divine spells and abilities; you must not pass up the opportunity to learn something new and significant (a nontrivial piece of information with a Knowledge check of DC 20 or higher) when that opportunity is directly present; You must use your own magical solution to a challenge if you can, even if a mundane solution that would require fewer resources is available.

Spirit powers

Archmage Arcana (Lesser, Su): Instead of your normal spells per day for your medium level, you use the spells per day from Table: Mesmerist. For each level of spell you can now cast (including level 0), each time you channel an archmage spirit, select a single spell of that level from the sorcerer/wizard spell list to add to your medium spell list and spells known until you lose contact with the archmage. When you cast these spells, they count as arcane (though not for the purpose of fulfilling prerequisites), and thus you must provide verbal and somatic components instead of thought and emotion components.

You gain potential access to most spells, which is nice.

As with the hierophant, gaining the spells per day of a 6th level spellcaster is extremely good because your medium spells are lower level than they would be for a true 6th level spell caster, which means that you are better than a true 6th level spellcaster. Except in the case of an archmage spirit, you don’t get anything else, so you are just worse than a wizard.

You will likely choose at least one damaging spell, such as:

lvl 0: jolt

lvl 1: snowball, shocking grasp

lvl 2: scorching ray, admonishing ray, electric thundercloud

On the other hand, if you use this spirit during downtime, you can make use of utility spells, or craft wands or scrolls. A few examples are:

lvl 1: crafter’s fortune, keep watch, infernal healing (to craft a wand)

lvl 2: locate object, misdirection, see invisibility, dress corpse, alter self

lvl 3: nondetection, invisibility purge, resist energy (communal), water breathing, seek thoughts, Magic Circle against X

lvl 4: locate creature, scrying (you can also use message through it !), detect scrying
lvl 5: Stoneskin (communal), Teleport, Secret Chest

Arcane Surge (Intermediate, Su): You can allow the archmage spirit to gain 1 point of influence over you in order to cast one of your medium spells known without expending a spell slot. When you do so, the caster level and DC of the spell increase by 1, and you can’t apply metamagic to the spell.

This is very good, but only at some specific levels. For example, at level 6, when you get this spirit power, scorching ray makes only one ray for 4d6 damage. But with this power, it makes two rays, doubling the effect of the spell. Again, at level 10, scorching ray gains a third ray one level early through this power. But that’s it, on levels with no such threshold, it does almost nothing.

Wild Arcana (Greater, Su): You can allow the archmage spirit to gain 1 point of influence over you in order to cast any sorcerer/wizard spell of a level you can cast. You must expend a spell slot of the appropriate level, and you can’t apply metamagic to the spell.

Any spell at any time ? That’s very handy. You don’t have enough influence for it if you plan to use Arcane Surge. Still, it is good if you don’t plan to use it, if you don’t plan to have a tough fight, and if you want the versatility.

Legendary Archmage (Supreme, Su): Once per day, you can cast any spell on the sorcerer/wizard spell list as if using the wild arcana ability, except the archmage doesn’t gain 1 point of influence over you, the spell doesn’t require a spell slot, and you can select a spell of any level.

Well, this was used to be reserved to 9th level spellcasters only. The possibility to cast any spell, including a wish, is very strong. After some point, you might want to channel an archmage spirit exclusively for this spirit power.

The Guardian

You transform into a kind of paladin, but focused only on defense. If you or your party need to survive some imminent threat of unknown nature, which will strike from nowhere by surprise, channel the guardian. But it is definitely not the spirit you will channel by default.

Spirit Bonus applies to: 

  • AC
  • Constitution checks, Fortitude saves, effectively increasing your constitution, except for HP
  • Reflex saves.

The two weak saves are boosted to good saves, armor is buffed, even constitution checks. It would only lack CMD to actually boost all defenses the class is not already good at.

Seance Boon: You gain a +1 bonus to CMD. Well, when I said it lacked CMD, I didn’t mean only +1. This is useful only when an enemy tries an important combat maneuver against a party member and would succeed exactly by 1. This almost never happens.

Favored Locations: City walls, forts, gates, keeps. You have such places in all cities, fortified villages, and some forts and keeps around. So this is kind of average.

Influence Penalty: You are incredibly cautious and guarded in all things, and your caution sometimes gets in the way. When attacking, you always fight defensively, and when casting a spell, you always cast defensively. Because of your focus on defense, you take a penalty on damage rolls equal to your spirit bonus.

Well, if you have channeled this spirit, you were willing to act like that anyway. The penalty on damage rolls is still unwelcome, but you were not focusing on damage today anyway.

Taboos: Choose one: you must always protect others from danger when you can (including defeated enemies, but not enemies that are an active threat to you and others); you must keep your body in fit physical condition, and you break this taboo every time you drop below half your maximum hit points; you must speak no words and use no abilities with the sonic descriptor, and you break this taboo if you become enraged, frightened, or panicked.

Spirit powers

Guardian’s Shield (Lesser, Su): You gain proficiency in heavy armors and shields (including tower shields).

In Pathfinder, shield proficiency is almost useless, because with mithral or darkwood, the armor check penalty is zero except for tower shields. And you won’t walk around with a tower shield, just in case you would channel a guardian spirit. You might have a shield with some special material other than mithral/darkwood just for the guardian, but that’s it.

You also get heavy armor proficiency, but you probably won’t have two sets of armor.

Absorb Blow (Intermediate, Su): You gain DR/— and resistance to acid, cold, electricity, fire, and sonic equal to 1/2 your medium level. Add paladin’s sacrifice to your medium spells known as a 2nd-level spell.

It fits the defensive idea perfectly. Half your medium level is quite low though. The spell “resist energy” gives energy resistance 20 at level 7. What is 5 points of damage at level 10 ? It is not like resistances could stack with other sources. At least you get a good spell.

Sudden Block (Greater, Su): After an enemy’s attack would hit you or an adjacent creature, you can use spirit surge to increase the victim’s AC, possibly causing the attack to fail, by rolling your spirit surge die and adding it to the chosen character’s AC. You must not be flat-footed, and you must be aware of the attack. If use of this ability causes the enemy’s attack to miss, you can make an attack at your highest base attack bonus against that enemy as an immediate action.

Very good, you can now use spirit surge to avoid attacks on any party members. In addition to most saves on yourself, it completes well the defensive build of the guardian spirit.

Legendary Guardian (Supreme, Su): Once per day as an immediate action, you can ignore the effects of a single attack against you, including enemy spells targeting you or whose area or effect includes you. The attack affects other creatures as normal.

You must not be taken by surprise (flat-footed) to be able to take an immediate action, which is a bit sad for this kind of ability. Then, I am not sure how this should work when you are not flat-footed: can you wait until you know the result of that attack against you or do you need to choose whether you use this ability beforehand ? In the first case, it is obviously much stronger.


Conclusion

Each spirit category has its own specificity and might shine on a specific day when you need it. Unlike other classes, you can have very different abilities each day, and you should make use of this choice.

By default, if you don’t have any specific need, the marshal and the hierophant spirits seem the best ones in a party. The champion is very good too and seems the best one if you are alone or with only one ally.

The archmage can really shine at specific levels, if you think you will have few but tough fights. Scorching rays provide 8d6+4 damage with a ranged touch attack at level 6, which means that you can shoot down an opponent of your level alone before he can even reach you.

The trickster is an expert at skills, and still has some fighting abilities.

The guardian looks more like a panic button to me. If you find yourself in jail or in some fort, it might be your only choice though.

Stats

Charisma: This is your main stat, it applies on:

  • your number of spell per day. You don’t have many without an archmage or hierophant spirit, and as a spontaneous spellcaster, they are always useful.
  • your spells DC. Your spells are lower level than usual, so the base DC will be low. It is useful to compensate for this by a high charisma score.
  • social skills. With high charisma, social skills as class skills, and even more charisma while channeling a marshal spirit, you will likely be the guy who talks. So, this is important.
  • “use magic device” skill. Also a class skill, and based on charisma, so if you want to use it you have all you need. It is especially useful in combination with Scribe Scroll or Craft Wand, because it lets you save your unused spells with a Wizard or Hierophant spirit for later use. For instance, you will sometimes need to channel a Hierophant spirit just to cast a couple of spells, and nothing else. In such cases, it might be beneficial to have written those spells as scrolls beforehand.
  • the number of times you can channel per day with the hierophant spirit. This is very useful.

Dexterity or Strength: Choose your weapon:

  • Finesse weapon implies only dexterity.
  • Normal melee weapon implies only strength.
  • Bow implies both dexterity and strength, so less charisma.
  • Crossbow implies only dexterity but no ability bonus to damage. Not encouraged.
  • Firearm implies only dexterity. I would advise taking one gunslinger level with the Mysterious stranger archetype in this case, to get the proficiency and a charisma bonus to damage.

Dexterity gives reflex, initiative, and armor. Strength only gives carrying capacity.

Using dexterity also allows you to use ray spells more effectively with archmage spirits.

In terms of damage, dexterity usually strikes more times with less damage, which fits better the per attack bonuses of the champion spirits, but it also requires more feats.

With a bow, prefer dexterity over strength.

With a focus on dexterity, you can dump strength.

With a focus on strength, don’t dump dexterity or you will have no reflex save at all, no armor, and poor initiative.

Constitution: As always, have some to avoid dying

Wisdom: As usual, it gives will and perception, which is good. Will is already your strong save, so you don’t need much wisdom. You can also take Steadfast personality to add charisma to most will saves.

Intelligence: Only useful for knowledge skills and bonus skill points.

Race

We are looking for a class with a charisma bonus. We have:

  • Halfling: You gain charisma and dexterity, and lose strength, this is perfect for a dexterity based medium. In addition, you can increase by 1/3 your seance boon for yourself, not allies. This might look small, but when you reach level 6 for example, it means +2 to all your damage rolls, or healing spells, or chosen crucial skill of the day. In comparison, at this level, feats like Power Attack give +4 on damage rolls and -2 on attack rolls. So it is actually on par with Power Attack which is a very good feat, and it gets better at higher levels.
  • Half-orc: You can gain 1/4 free spirit surge from taboos per level. This is especially useful when channeling a marshal spirit. It is only a few times per day though, so it is not as powerful as the halfling favored class bonus. You also gain proficiency with the falchion, which is very good for a strength based medium.
  • Human: Same favored class bonus as a half-orc. Solid choice, especially if you need the bonus feat.
  • Half-elf: Good choice as well. Unlocks the Paragon Surge spell.
  • Gnome: You gain charisma and constitution, and lose strength, which is good for a dexterity based medium. The DC of your illusion spells is increased by 1. But, the favored class bonus is quite bad.
  • Catfolk: You gain charisma and dexterity, very good for a dexterity based medium. In addition, you can increase by 1/3 the result of all spirit surge rolls. This is really good with a marshal spirit in particular, and to beat high skill DC with the trickster. You may also gain permanent natural attacks (2 claws), which can be combined with a Ring of the Rat Fangs (1 bite).
  • Dhampir: You cannot use hierophant spirits to heal yourself and your party members, unless they are all undead or dhampir themselves, in which case it is blue.
  • Drow: You gain charisma and dexterity, very good for a dexterity based medium. You also get the proficiency for the rapier which is a good finesse weapon.
  • Nagaji: You gain charisma and strength, very good for a strength based medium.

In addition, if outsiders are allowed and if you accept the concept of channeling spirits in your own soul which is also your body, you can look at fetchlings, aasimars, and sulis.

Archetype

The medium abilities from levels 3, 7, and 14 are quite useless in most campaigns. The medium abilities from levels 5, 13, 18 are not very good either. So it should be profitable to take an archetype.

Fiend Keeper medium

Replaces medium abilities from levels 3, 5, 7, and 13.

You gain access to many new abilities called dark powers, the most important of which are  :

  • 3 natural weapons, which is very good for the damage bonuses of champion spirits.
  • flying at level 7, always handy ! You will need to put a few ranks in the fly skill to use it properly.
  • increasing your spirit bonus, which is really good for marshal spirits in combat.

For each use of a dark power, you also gain temporary HP, which is always helpful in combat.

You even gain an improved version of the medium level 13 ability “Ask the spirit”, which is very good, and you have it from level 3.

All this gets much better at level 7 and again at level 13.

Make sure to max out your charisma score and buy a Circlet of Persuasion, so that using the dark powers do not incur influence points. You can also check out the Wicked Leader trait and see if your GM allows you to apply it to this check (I believe it should).

Building around natural weapons is a good option for both dexterity and strength based mediums, but it is not mandatory because if you don’t need it, you can get another good dark power instead.

You lose almost nothing in a non occult scenario but get many very good things from this archetype. A full round action to get a one minute buff means you will lose some time when activating your powers in combat though.

Spirit dancer

Replaces medium abilities from levels 2, 15, 18, and 20. Lose taboos.

Changes the class radically. You get much more versatility but your powers are limited to a number of rounds equal to 2*(medium level)+charisma+(4 for each influence point you sacrifice toward it), which goes up to about 18 at level 1.

The advantages are :

* no required location : access to every spirit every day !

* channels every day !

* condition removal at any time

* no need to channel a specific spirit one day long to be able to cast a single spell (like restoration)

The drawbacks are :

* No help from your spirits for tasks which take time

* You lose taboos, which is very painful to marshal spirits.

* You lose your spirit bonus and spirit surge when taken by surprise.


I think it would be interesting to try out. You may ask your GM to have it at the beginning and be allowed to drop it if you don’t like it.
About legendary influence, I would state that it costs one single point of influence and apply to all spirits. The archetype is balanced to provide any spirit at full power during a spirit dance, and the counterpart is limited rounds of spirit dance.

Wendo caller

Replaces medium abilities from levels 2, 3, 5, and 7.

Let’s assume you want to save your money. You lose one HP per level and lose shared seance, but get some useful spells, a domain ability, and become good at knowledges.

It doesn’t change the class much and it seems rather balanced.

Relic Channeler

Replaces medium abilities from levels 3, 5, and 7.

You don’t need to find an appropriate place to channel spirits, you always have one of the 6 spirits in your relics. You lose the versatility to make choices for a given spirit category, but you win the versatility to choose the spirit you like at any time. You also have stronger spirits, which kind of cope with the lack of choices.

If you want to use only two spells of each level with hierophant and archmage spirits, this might be for you. In a sense you are specializing each spirit to a more specific task, and you gain control over the choices at your disposal. Overall it is not bad, but I find it sad to give up on the access to the whole spell lists of clerics and wizards.

Reanimated medium

Replaces medium abilities from levels 3, 5, 7, and 20. Lose taboos.

By RAW, this archetype is broken because you almost always suffer your influence penalties. I therefore recommend the following change  “he still suffers the legend's influence penalty as normal at 3 or less points of influence”. With this archetype, you should be penalised when you have weak influence over your own body.

This archetype abilities earned at levels 3, 5, and 7 are quite poor, but not worse than what it replaces. Quite balanced at this point.

You lose a good level 20 ability to gain a poor one, but you might never reach level 20, so... ok.

Then, your influence pool goes from 6 to 0 instead of from 1 to 5. So your margin is increased by 2. It seems to compensate for the loss of taboos. However you still regain maximum 3 points of influence during your seance, as before (normally if you were at 4, the next day you would start at 1, so it decreases by a maximum of 3). This means that during good days, you will start at 6 and have 5 uses of influence or spirit surge as before with taboos, but on average, you only have 3 uses. This is a big loss, but you can mitigate it by alternating spirits with high influence consumption, with spirits with very little influence consumption. For example, you would burn 5 points of influence with a marshal spirit, then only 1 on the next day with a hierophant spirit, so that you start again at 6 the day after that.

The archetype provides 2 good points though:

  • You always have a channeled spirit, which is your own. If one day, you cannot channel, you don’t get your 3 points of influence, but you keep the bonuses of the spirit category you currently have. This is less painful than having no channeled spirit at all.
  • If you take this archetype after dying, you have a free resurrection. This is a one time bonus which might or might not have much effect depending on your scenario.

To add flavor to the game for players who don’t want to keep this archetype permanently, I would home rule it just a little bit. You would get it mainly after dying in this case. Dying is not nice for the player, so I would give its character the opportunity to channel his own spirit into his body with only 1 point of influence the day after his death. He would be weak at this point because of the influence penalty and an empty influence pool, which is expected from someone who was dead just a moment ago. The archetype would work as usual, but the player could choose to be resurrected (after all, he is dead), to lose the reanimated medium archetype, and, in terms of class features, get back what it had before dying. Modified like this, the archetype is strictly beneficial, but does not really provide more power to the class.

Storyteller

Replaces medium abilities from levels 2, 3, 7, 9, 15, 19, and 20. Lose taboos.

You start the day with 2 points of influence and lose propitiation. Just for that, don’t take it. The rest makes it even worse...

Feats

General feats

Spirit Focus: Increase your spirit bonus by 1 for one chosen spirit category ! This is excellent for champion and marshal spirits in particular. But take it with legendary influence rather than directly.

Legendary Influence: This feat is mandatory (still assuming you are going to routinely use several spirit categories). You start your day with 2 points of influence instead of one, but with one more feat which depends on which spirit you have channeled.

To have an effective hierophant spirit, you need Selective Channeling, but it is useless for other spirits. To optimize the marshal spirits, you need Spirit Focus(Marshal), but it is useless for other spirits. Those are your two best spirit choices in a party, so you want to have these feats. Then, archmage spirits have you drop your armor and shield, except if you have Arcane Armor Training, again, useless for other spirits. Why not pay the influence point and have all this in one feat ?

Improved legendary influence: Ok, you already have legendary influence and mandatory feats there, and you have already paid 1 point of influence. Paying one more begins to be costly. But you cannot take this one before you get propitiation, so if you take this, you already have a bit less influence. So, you may want Shatter Defense for trickster spirits (with Dazzling Display in Legendary Influence), Arcane Armor Mastery or some metamagic feat for archmage spirits, another channeling feat or Dreamed Secrets for hierophant spirits, and another combat feat for champion spirits.

Channel spirit: You choose one spirit category, and get an additional way of channeling it : no location constraint, no taboo, start at 0 influence (you do not lose the spirit at 0 influence), but you are possessed by the spirit at the end. If you take this feat when you are at level 8, it means that you can channel the spirit for the day and be possessed during the night, which is probably ok if you can lock your body in some place for example. So, it actually gives you a way to channel your favourite spirit at any time, even if you are jailed in the middle of nowhere. You can also pair it with Spiritual Training.

Steadfast personality: Use your high charisma instead of your weak wisdom for most will saves, why not ?

Toughness: More HP is always nice.

Expanded Arcana: As a spontaneous spellcaster, you want to have as many useful spells at your disposal as possible. Take this feat when you have enough medium levels to learn 2 more awesome 2nd and/or 3rd level spells from the medium spell list.

Subconscious Usurpation: As a psychic spellcaster, this feat allows you to act in otherwise catastrophic situations. Confusion is common enough, and possession or compulsion effects can be really nasty. While possessed, you can teleport, communicate through Sending or Audiovisual Hallucination, or try to free yourself from the possession with a Protection from Evil/Chaos/Good/Law. However, you need to have a base will save bonus of +2, which you might not want to pay for, and it is useful only in specific cases that you might never encounter.

Celestial Obedience (Arshea): The obedience needs to fit your role play, but it provides serious advantages (and fun !) : +4 sacred bonus on Charisma checks and Charisma-based skill checks when interacting with an intelligent creature who could be sexually attracted to you ; and Charisma to AC whenever you wear revealing clothing and no armor. However wearing no armor means that you won’t have the spirit bonded armor property.

See http://www.archivesofnethys.com/DeityDisplay.aspx?ItemName=Arshea

Spiritual Training: You gain knowledge about incorporeal beings, which fits your class well in terms of role play. But you also stay conscious while possessed, which may occur more often than with another class. And you even gain some control over your body while possessed. Depending on your build or if you plan to reach 5 influence, it can be very good.

Additional Traits: if you need some more traits.

Racial Heritage: If you want the class favored bonus from the halfling in a strength based build, take a human with this as your human bonus feat.

Craft feats

The medium can meet most prerequisites of crafting, has access to almost all spells, can become an expert at any craft skill, has spellcraft as a class skill, and everyone needs magical gear. So why not take a craft feat, and maybe retrain it later once you have saved some money with it.

Craft Wand: With your spells at lower level than usual, you can craft cheaper wands. Actually, since wands cannot be made for spell levels higher than 4, you can even make wands which usually cannot exist. You can easily use your wands later with any channeled spirit using your “use magic device” skill.

Brew Potion: With your spells at lower level than usual, you can craft cheaper potions. Actually, since potions cannot be made for spell levels higher than 3, you can even make potions which usually cannot exist.

Craft Wondrous Items: Put points in spellcraft, and make items whenever you can. You will get geared more quickly than by buying it. It will also help your party members if they need some specific item which is hard to find.

Scribe Scrolls: With your spells at lower level than usual, you can scribe cheaper scrolls. If you have a party member with a spell book, you can help him learn new spells. You can also use your scrolls later with any channeled spirit using your “use magic device” skill, but the DC will be higher than with wands.

Combat feats

First depending on your weapon, you will need the associated feats:

  • With a finesse melee weapon:
  • Weapon Finesse
  • one of Dervish dance/Slashing grace/Fencing grace or use natural attacks with an amulet of mighty fists with the “agile” property
  • Two weapon fighting (except if you use natural attacks)
  • Piranha Strike
  • Lunge
  • With a ranged weapon:
  • Point-Blank Shot
  • Precise Shot
  • Rapid Shot
  • Deadly aim
  • Manyshot if using a bow
  • With a normal melee weapon (probably two-handed):
  • Power Attack
  • Furious focus
  • Lunge

Then, with intimidation as a class skill and a high charisma score, you can choose Dazzling Display and Shatter defense. This is a simple way to get sneak attacks from a trickster spirit. In addition, making a target flat-footed is a solid way of consistently hitting it.

This will works better with a race which is as large as possible, maybe with the “enlarge person” spell, so it is especially appropriate with a strength based build, especially because you have more spare feats in this case.

Other interesting choices are :

  • Divine Fighting Technique (Way of the Shooting Star) : apply Charisma to attack and damage rolls, why not !?
  • Noble Scion (of war) : apply Charisma to initiative instead of Dexterity, very good if you don’t have high Dexterity.

Other strong feats with no prerequisite :

  • Improved Initiative : initiative is an ability check, so it will never go very high. Therefore, +4 is a big bonus. Playing before your foe, you won’t be flat-footed against him, he will be flat-footed against you, and you will hit him (and may be kill him) first. All this is very important in combat.
  • Blind-Fight : If you expect to fight invisible enemies, this is excellent. If not, well, don’t take it. And retrain it when you have Thoughtsense or True Seeing.

Spells

Keep in mind that you have access to most utility spells using your archmage and hierophant spirits. Don’t learn a spell on your medium spell list if it is something for which you can wait a day and have it by channeling a spirit.

Have at least one spell per spell level which you use very often, so that you can make use of your spell slots every day.

And remember that you can replace one spell every 3 levels after and including level 5. So you can take some spell at a lower level, and replace it later when you don’t need it anymore.

Level 0

  • Detect Magic: The party needs this, make sure someone has it. Having it twice is not necessarily overkill.
  • Guidance: Very good. All skill checks in your party virtually get a +1 competence bonus. Remember that it does not stack with other competence bonuses which are typically conferred by one’s equipment.
  • Light: The party needs this, make sure someone has it. If everybody has darkvision, forget about it.
  • Message: Very useful to keep contact with your scout or someone left behind to keep watch.
  • Stabilize: Don’t put the life of your allies (and defeated foes) at risk, stabilize them from a distance with no chance of failure !
  • Mage Hand: Thematically-appropriate utility.
  • Prestidigitation: Can be fun and has some use.
  • Read Magic: Quite useful if you want to use scrolls. You can easily have the spell on your spell list with the right spirit, or just use your “use magic device” skill.
  • Ghost Sound: No speech. In pathfinder, spells have manifestations so that people will see when you cast a spell, maybe even identify it. I find it hard to use.
  • Sift: Can be useful occasionally if you don’t have time to search normally or if you need touch information from a distance. But most of the time, the -5 penalty on your perception check makes this spell less efficient than just using perception. And it cannot detect creatures...
  • Resistance: Everyone in your party will have a cloak of resistance, so this gets useless pretty quickly.

Level 1

  • Enlarge person: Excellent buff for intimidation and strength based builds. You can also cast it on party members.
  • Reduce person: Excellent buff for dexterity based builds. You can also cast it on party members. Also useful to make an unconscious ally lighter to be able to carry him to a safer location, or simply to pass through small openings. If you become tiny, you use your dexterity instead of your strength for climbing, swimming, and to calculate your CMB.
  • Hermean Potential: Only occult classes and witches have it. Being able to roll twice on an important roll is very good. It even gets better at higher levels.
  • Ill Omen: Offensive version of Hermean Potential with no save. Very good.
  • Oneiric Horror: 2nd level spell for other classes, 3rd for 9th level casters. Does nothing if the target succeeds its save. But if it fails, it loses all its turns until it saves, after that it is fatigued. Useful in combat. Quite efficient to prevent someone from fleeing because the fatigued condition prevents from running.
  • Liberating command: Good spell to escape nasty grapples, this spell can save lives. Probably not yours though since it would require one immediate action to cast the spell, and another one to use its effect.
  • Calm spirit: 2nd level spell for other classes. Useful if you want to talk to a hostile ghost, or avoid fighting it, or just have it lose its turn in a fight.
  • Protection from Evil/Chaos/Good/Law: Good if you know your enemy. Evil is often a good choice.
  • Remove fear: Unusable on yourself. It is useful on allies but quite sporadically. It is a normal 1st level spell, and fear does not kill. Ok if you already have other spells that you use frequently.
  • Long arm: Reach allows you to cast your spells safely, do more full attacks, or have more than 0 reach if you are tiny.
  • Silent image: Be patient and take Audiovisual Hallucination instead.

Level 2

  • Haste: 3rd level spell for other classes. This one is overpowered, take it as soon as you can.
  • Placebo effect: Only available to occult classes. This spell has no emotion (S) component, which means it will be one of few spells that you can cast while under an emotion or fear effect like “shaken”. Moreover, in such a situation, this spell will actually free you from said effect and enable you to cast your other spells again. You can also use it to free yourself from being “paralyzed”, which can save your life. Finally, it can suppress many other annoying effects on you or your allies, which is very handy.
  • Heroism: 3rd level spell for other classes, except the bard. A solid buff lasting 10min/level. Good one.
  • Bestow insight: Gives an insight bonus for several minutes on an ally’s skill check. It stacks with Heroism, Hermean Potential, Grand Destiny, which helps you do what you do best : give your ally’s bonuses on top of bonuses on top of what they can normally do, so that your group is able to beat very high DC when it needs to.
  • Enchantment sight: See enchantments affecting creatures for a few hours. You also know if it is a charm, a compulsion or something else.
  • Detect Thoughts: Up to 1min/level, you sense the presence or absence of thinking creatures in a cone, even through some amount of material (most trees, screens, and inner walls). Therefore, it defeats most ambushes. Beware that you need to concentrate to maintain the spell.
  • Clairaudience-Clairvoyance: 3rd level spell for other classes. No line of sight. Long range. Safe scouting or spying. But the 10 min cast time prevents uses such as tracking someone who has just fled. Can also be very useful in combination with Dimensional Door or Mind Swap.
  • Slow: 3rd level spell for other classes. Saving negates but it targets multiple creatures at once, so some will be affected. Very good mass debuf spell.
  • Audiovisual Hallucination: 3rd level spell for other classes, which also means that your DC is one lower than usual. This is still a powerful illusion. If your target fails its save, you can have it do or believe things it would not have done or believed otherwise. Can also be used in combat.
  • Instigate Psychic Duel: Very complicated spell reserved to occult classes. It might allow you to kill your target with a single failed save. Read the rules for more detail.
  • Pack Empathy: 3rd level spell for other classes, except ranger. Allows you to form a day long limited communication link with your party members, so that you always know if someone is in danger. You can even define some code to transmit a few messages.
  • Inflict pain: Having your foe miss its attacks for at least one round, and maybe the whole fight is really good.
  • Invisibility: Always nice to have.
  • Analyse Aura: Good to diagnose poison, healness, harmful spells and conditions on allies. Or gather information about someone’s motivations and gain +2 on social skills against him. For the diagnostic part, it should be enough to have it on your Mnemonic Vestment. But having it for the social part can be very useful if you can observe someone before interacting with him.
  • Aura of the unremarkable: 3rd level spell for other classes, 4th for 9th level casters. Enables you to hide in plain sight what your party is doing. If too many people are around, some will notice but it can provide enough confusion for your purpose. Good only if your party often does illegal or suspicious stuff.
  • Sessile Spirit: 3rd, 4th or 5th level spell for other classes. Neutralizes spirits possessing or helping their host. Especially useful in an occult campaign.
  • Nondetection: 3rd level for other classes. Protects you from divination spells for 1h/level. If you think that you are spied, it is very nice to have. It costs 50 gp per use.
  • Bestow Curse: 3rd or 4th level spell for other classes. This spell is very strong and is permanent ! But... will negates entirely, so decreasing the DC by 1 or 2 is not very helpful. On top of that, most of the time, you want to beat or kill your opponent, not inflict him a very annoying curse and let it go. So in most fights, you are better off with Inflict pain, which is slightly weaker but always works for at least 1 round. That said, there might be situations where you want to force someone to do something. This could be the means to influence him: do what I want or keep your curse. Or you might need to beat an opponent too strong for you. This spell could weaken him little by little, one fight after the other, so that you can finally get the superiority. It is strong. I just cannot imagine using it more than a few times in practice.
  • Suggestion: Can be useful, but you could probably achieve the same effect with Audiovisual Hallucination in most cases.

Level 3

  • Dimensional Door: 4th level for other classes. Instantly teleport your party and loot out of harm ! No emotion component.
  • Greater Invisibility: 4th level for other classes. Strong spell, but it does not replace Invisibility because it lasts only 1 round per level. It allows sneak attacks with a trickster spirit.
  • Mind Swap: 4th or 5th level for other classes, 6th for 9th level casters ! Extremely powerful. Pin your body somewhere with your party, and take possession of someone  for the day, without even needing a line of sight. If it fails, just try again until it works. You get all the benefits of possession, but you also kidnap the target. It knows it is being possessed, and that you can suicide its body, it is helpless with your party member, so it should consider very seriously to cooperate with your party. You could get it with an archmage spirit when you need it, but you would have much less retry chances.
  • Fly: Very good spell, but don’t take it if you have other means to fly, such as the fiend keeper archetype, a better spell caster in your group, a wand, etc.
  • Retrocognition: 7th level for the wizard, so you cannot have this spell otherwise except once a day with an archmage spirit at level 17. It allows you to see the past in your current location. Not so useful before level 13.
  • Mind Probe: 4th level for other psychic classes, 5th for wizards. Very useful if you want to retrieve information from a prisoner. With Ill Omen, and repeated use, it should be quite effective. You can also get it with an archmage spirit.
  • Paragon Surge: Only for half-elves. Provides an extra feat of your choice which you can choose at the beginning of each day. You can either use this like the Legendary Influence feat, or with even more flexibility depending on what you plan to do on each day. However, it is a 3rd level spell like most other classes and you cannot spam it for every encounter.
  • Sending: 4th or 5th level for other classes. Infinite range communication is useful. If you use it often, take it. If you fear to be taken prisoner in a place where you cannot channel a hierophant spirit, take it. Else, you can get it when needed with a hierophant spirit or use your Mnemonic Vestment.
  • Burst of speed: Good escape spell with no emotion component and a casting time of one swift action. Have it at least on your Mnemonic Vestment.
  • Vampiric Touch: No save, heals as much as a cleric channel, while doing damage. Can save your life in a bad fight.
  • Dispel Magic: Someone in the party needs to have access to this. But you can have it through a hierophant or archmage spirit for out of combat use. You can still take it for in combat use.
  • Borrow Fortune: Can save your life. Few classes have access to this one. You probably don’t want to use it very often, so you can have it on your Mnemonic Vestment.
  • Magic circle against Evil/Chaos/Good/Law: Each one of these is a different spell. Evil is usually a good choice.
  • Dimensional Anchor: Someone in the party needs to have this if you are to encounter teleporting enemies. It is also useful against Blink. It might be enough to have it on your Mnemonic Vestment though. 
  • Complex hallucination : More powerful version of Audiovisual Hallucination
  • Scrying: 4th or 5th level for other classes. Infinite range spying and communication is useful. It is more powerful than Sending, but it allows a save, must overcome spell resistance, and has a very long casting time. You cannot target the same target twice in a day, so have it on your Mnemonic Vestment.
  • Glimpse of truth: Can win an otherwise desperate fight, sometimes, or reveal an important piece of information. But one turn is often not enough.
  • Gaseous form: You can still cast your spells while in this form. Can be useful, for example in combination with Mind Swap.
  • Call Spirit: 5th level for other classes. You have it for free since level 5 without any archetype. Gather information about the past, help someone in some quest with a dead friend, summon a dead enemy to have it talk. Take it only if you have an archetype and cannot wait to get it with a hierophant spirit.
  • Remove Curse: You can have it when needed with a hierophant spirit. No need to have it always.

Level 4

  • Thoughtsense: Strangely, Wizards get this as a 5th level spell, and it is otherwise reserved to occult classes. Actually, this one competes with True seeing because : it has the same duration ; it also pierces invisibility, displacement, blur, and darkness ; it covers the same surface area (in a sphere around you instead of a cone in front of you) ; it is personal only, and does not see through illusions nor inside the Ethereal Plane, but it can sense through some amount of material and does not cost money, which is a valid compromise. It is not clearly stated in the spell description how you can sense thoughts, but I assume that it is the same as Detect Thoughts. Therefore, 1 foot of stone, 1 inch of common metal, a thin sheet of lead, or 3 feet of wood or dirt blocks it.
  • Telepathic Bond: Communicate with your allies reliably, in silence, during a few hours. Very useful to avoid mistakes caused by poor coordination.
  • Grand Destiny: Great spell. Provides +4 competence bonus on 3 or 4 chosen rolls within the next few hours. Combined with Hermean Potential, it gives a reliable means to beat high DC. Notably, it can apply to caster level checks, which is quite rare, and +4 is a big bonus in this case, so it is also a good counter to spell resistance, and it can drastically improve your chance of breaking an enchantment, a curse, or dispelling magic.
  • Freedom of movement: You want to have this effect on your party members. Hopefully, you have a druid or cleric with better access to it, or maybe a ring of freedom of movement. Else, take this spell and have it at least on you (you can use Liberating command on your allies but not yourself).
  • Spirit-Bound blade: The duration is worth the spell slot. Enabling critical hits and sneak attacks on yourself or your big guy is really good. If you expect to fight a lot of incorporeal foes, learn it. Else, have it on your Mnemonic Vestment, in case you encounter a tough ghost.
  • Purify Body: Removes all physical ability damage, much faster than Restoration. It also heals like a cure spell and removes several other conditions. These other conditions are already covered by Placebo Effect, but the removal is permanent with this spell.
  • Mage’s Private Sanctum: Protects a place from scrying for one day. Take it if you need it.
  • Feeblemind: Excellent against casters.
  • Modify Memory: Can be very useful. You don’t have access to it by other means.
  • Inflict pain, Mass : Very solid debuff to all foes. The “mass” property and the fact that it applies for one round even on successful save makes it worth your time casting the spell.
  • Scripted Hallucination: You can take this instead of Audiovisual Hallucination. The DC is higher and the illusion more convincing.
  • True seeing: 5th, 6th, or 7th level for other classes. Very useful spell to pierce most things preventing you from noticing anything within 120 feet of sight. BUT, you still need to have a good perception to notice things hidden by conventional means, and you do not see through solid matter, AND it costs 250 gp per use. Because of the cost, take Thoughtsense, and have this one on your Mnemonic Vestment in case you need it.
  • Telepathy: Communicate reliably and silently with anyone (not only your group) within 100 ft.
  • Teleport: To travel, use the archmage to get it when you need it. To escape, Dimension door should be enough and uses a 3rd level spell slot. Have it on your Mnemonic Vestment.
  • Plane shift: 5th, 6th, or 7th level for other classes. A save or die spell you can use on your foes. But your DC is not very high and you don’t have many spell slots at this level. If you want to travel to another plane, you might just use a hierophant spirit and have a scroll to be able to come back any day you want.
  • Seeming: Change the appearance of your whole group in one action, for the next 12 hours. Or disguise someone unwilling you want to kidnap. Have it with an archmage spirit if you don’t need it every day.
  • Hold monster: Very good spell if it succeeds. The DC is higher than Oneiric Horror.
  • Persistent Image: Nice versatile spell. But it is not as useful as it could be if you have Audiovisual or Scripted Hallucination already.

Skills

Charisma

With high charisma and very high charisma with a marshal spirit, it would be a shame not to put ranks in charisma skills. You cannot have them all maxed out though, so you have to choose.

Diplomacy: Class skill, have at least one rank. With about 7 ranks, you will succeed most of your talks with creatures with an “indifferent” attitude, 12 ranks for “unfriendly” attitude, 17 for “hostile” attitude.

Bluff: Class skill, this is an opposed check against sense motive, so every point here counts.

Intimidate: Class skill. If you want to use it against creatures of your level, max it out, the DC to demoralize increases by one per opponent hit dice. This skill works better if you are larger than your target, so it works better with strength based mediums.

Use Magic Device: Class skill. If you want to use wands in combat, put about 9 ranks in it (+12+charisma should reach 20). This should actually be enough to be able to use most scrolls as well.

Disguise: If you want to use it, have enough points in it to beat others' perception. But you can also not use it at all.

Perform: Not useful, except for prerequisites. You can put a rank for role playing if you wish.

Handle Animal: Just use the trickster for this one, you probably won’t use it often.

Wisdom

You don’t have a lot of wisdom, so nothing really special here.

Perception: Class skill. Used very often. Detecting hidden enemies and treasures is very useful. So, I would max it out.

Sense Motive: Class skill. This is an opposed check, and creatures which tries to bluff you will likely have some ranks in Bluff. You can have one rank in it to get the class skill bonus, but I would not put much more. At higher levels, if you plan to use Mind Probe, max it out.

Heal: Class skill. Not so useful. You can put one rank for the class skill bonus.

Survival: You are not good at it.

Profession: Not useful, except for prerequisites. You can put a rank for role playing if you wish.

Intelligence

You don’t have a lot of intelligence, so nothing really special here.

Knowledge(arcana,religion,planes): Class skill. Have one rank for the class skill bonus and to be allowed a roll to identify things. I would not put more except if the scenario calls for one of those knowledges very often.

Spellcraft: Class skill. Put at least one rank to get the class skill bonus and be allowed a roll to identify spells and magic items. If you need it for crafting, get enough ranks for what you need. You can also put more because identifying offensive spells is useful to decide when to use a spirit surge.

Linguistics: Take it if you need to learn languages.

Craft: Class skill. To identify potions (alchemy) or craft specific non magic items, you can use the trickster. To craft magic items, it is probably better to use spellcraft.

Appraise: You are not good at it.

Dexterity/Strength

Fly: Class skill. Put enough ranks here when you can fly.

Stealth: This skill can be used very often. If you are a small dexterity based medium, you can get a decent score here. Else, you will not be good at it and it is an opposed check.

Acrobatics: Having 3 ranks here is always useful for the “total defense” action.

Disable Device: You can put one rank to be allowed to use it.

Ride: Have it if you plan to use “Mounted Combat”.

Escape Artist: It is an opposed check for which you will probably not have a great chance of success anyway. I would rather put 3 ranks in acrobatics to use “total defense” when in trouble while grappled, than trying to have a chance of success using escape artist.

Sleight of hand: It is an opposed check not to be noticed. You are not very good at it. It is also not used very often.

Climb/Swim: Can be useful, but probably not worth putting ranks in it. If you are a strength based medium, you will have enough already. If you are a dexterity based medium, you might be able to become tiny with “Reduce Person” and use your dexterity instead of your strength.

Equipment

Many pieces of equipment are good for anyone. You will easily find guides listing those. But as this is a guide for the Medium Occult class, here, I will only list items which have a synergy with this class.

Note: This list is not exhaustive, please leave a comment if you know of a good item to suggest.

Mnemonic vestment: Once per day, you can cast a spell from a written source (a scroll typically) without expending it. The spell must be on your spell list, must be psychic in our case, and you must use a spell slot of the appropriate level. In other words, it allows you to cast a spell that you haven’t learnt, as if you knew it. You have good spells on your list that you don’t need often, and this is a good way to have access to them.
It includes spells higher than your current maximum spell level. For instance, when you reach level 10, you learn a few 3rd level spells, but you can cast 4th level spells with an archmage or hierophant spirit. Therefore, you gain access to some spells like Teleport early, by using the Mnemonic Vestment. An extreme example is Mind Swap, which you can cast at level 7, much earlier than any other class. So, if you need such spells, buy this item early.

Good situational spells are Dimensional anchor, Sending, Scrying, Teleport, Scripted or Audiovisual Hallucination, Plane Shift, Borrow Fortune, Call Spirit. Spells which can be completely negated with a save are not very appropriate because you usually want to cast them again in case of failure, but Scrying can be tried on a given target at most once per day anyway.

Good full-day spells for this are Misdirection, Nondetection, Seeming.

Spirit-Bonded: This armor enchantment increases by one your spirit bonus ! Make sure to have a medium armor. A medium armor made of mithral is still medium for this purpose (see the official FAQ).

Circlet of Persuasion: +3 to all charisma-based skill checks and charisma checks, maybe also to concentration checks (ask your GM), for only 4.5 gp.

Rune of Power: You don’t have many spells per day, especially without channeling a caster spirit, and your spells are very useful. They are twice as expensive as Pearls of Power, but slightly easier to use, and your spells are often at a lower level than other classes. For example, a 2nd level Rune of Power is cheaper than a 3rd level Pearl of Power. 1st level are affordable. 2nd level already costs 8k, so you will probably want a few of them at some point, but make sure to improve your Headband of Charisma first, since it also increases your number of spells per day.

Spell Lattice: Provides additional known spells, like Pages of Spell Knowledge. By RAW, Pages of Spell Knowledge are limited to arcane and divine spells, but not Spell Lattices.

Rods

You have access to powerful spells, but at a lower spell level than usual. Therefore, lesser rods apply to most of your spells. You should take advantage of this.

Lesser rod of persistent spell: The target must succeed at its save twice instead of once. With spells like Mind Swap, it is almost like casting the spell twice in one turn, expanding a single spell slot. It is extremely powerful.

Lesser rod of extend spell: Doubles the duration of a spell. Very good with buff spells that you cast in advance (Heroism, Enlarge/Reduce Person, (Greater) Invisibility, and sometimes Haste). You don’t have many spells per day, so having to cast them again is expensive. Also doubles the effectiveness of Retrocognition.

Some examples

High damage

A 6th level halfling with a champion spirit and a one round preparation with the Fiend Keeper archetype (natural attacks) has 4 attacks at full BAB for 1d4+2(spirit bonus)+4(seance boon with halfling favoured class bonus)+4(dexterity with amulet of mighty fists agile)+4(piranha strike)+4(risky striker) damage, and all hit at +4(base attack bonus)+4(dexterity with weapon finesse)+2(spirit bonus)-2(piranha strike)+1(size).

Therefore it does 4x(1d4+18) damage with +9 to hit.

A 6th level ranged halfling with an archmage spirit shoots 2 scorching rays for 4d6+4(seance boon with halfling favoured class bonus)+1(point-blank shot) damage each, and hits at +4(base attack bonus)+4(dexterity)+1(point-blank shot)+1(size) on touch armor.

Therefore, it does 2x(4d6+5) damage with +10 to hit on touch armor (almost always hits), at range.

If this medium has a “maximized metamagic rod”, it is 2x29 damage, which kills an average CR9 monster in 2 rounds at range...

Party buff

An 8th level medium with a marshal spirit and a one round preparation with the Fiend Keeper archetype (spirit bonus) can have a spirit bonus of +3(base)+1(archetype)+1(spirit focus)+1(spirit bonded armor). Therefore, inspiring call gives +6 to attack and damage rolls, and the shared seance boon gives +2 to damage rolls. This might well double the damage of everyone else (works best with many attacks, not so well on spellcasters). And this is your baseline, you can then add some haste, heroism, and spirit surges on top of that !

At higher levels, your spirit bonus will be higher, so that the bonus to damage rolls will scale reasonably. But the bonus to attack rolls should normally not scale at all. But with inspiring call, it does. Therefore, the marshal spirit only gets better as you gain levels.
For example, a level 13th medium with a marshal spirit and a one round preparation with the Fiend Keeper archetype (spirit bonus) can have a spirit bonus of +4(base)+2(archetype)+1(spirit focus)+1(spirit bonded armor).
Therefore, inspiring call, which is now a move action, now gives +8 to attack and damage rolls, or saving throws, or both if you use two actions. With heroism and the seance boon, you can give +10 to attack and damage rolls and saving throws for all your party, including you. This is already insane. And you have spirit surges at 1d8+8 on top of that.
