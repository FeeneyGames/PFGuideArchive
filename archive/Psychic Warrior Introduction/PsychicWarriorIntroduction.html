Weapon of War

The Pathfinder Psychic Warrior (SRD)

By Jackissocool and Lucy Fur

“Some Warriors look fierce, but are mild. Some seem timid, but are vicious. Look beyond appearances; position yourself for the advantage.”                 ― Deng Ming-Dao

The Psychic Warrior was a psionic class introduced in the 3.5 Expanded Psionics Handbook, and updated for Pathfinder by Dreamscarred Press. Throughout this guide, we’ll be using the following rating system:

⧫ Amazing: A must have for an optimized build. It’s also used to denote strengths.

⧫ Good: Should be heavily considered for those who can use it.

⧫ Average:  Not bad or good, but there may be better things (generally option works only for certain builds or there is an opportunity cost).

⧫ Bad: A turkey; this is inferior and should be skipped. It’s also used to denote vulnerabilities.

⧫ GM Notes: Article notes and GM tricks, shows ways to counter psychic warriors, and what to forbid.

The psychic warrior mixes psionic powers with martial skills. Essentially a fighter, who self-buffs with psionics. Making them one of the best martial classes out there, with their armor and weapon proficiencies, bonus feats, path abilities and powers. They can be great offensively or defensively, in ranged-combat or melee, or all the above. They can be made to fill any martial role.

However, a psychic warrior is only a moderate manifester, gaining a limited power point pool, few powers know, and a slower maximum power level known which ends at 6th level. Their powers are mostly self buffs, not the best of blasters. However, they get more power points than Aegis and Marksmen, and far more powers know than Vitalists and Psychic Warriors. They can blast, and do use some of the tricks of other psionics classes, just not as often.

The psychic warrior is great for roleplaying anything with a warrior or monkish backstory. Someone specialized or hardened by conflict. They are versatile.

Laconic Condensing: Effort was put into having the most material in the least amount of pages. Thus, in parts, shorthand was used (esp. in the powers, build and NPC sections), which follows: w/=with, w/o=without, w/n=within, &=and, b/n=between, rnd=round, foe=target or subject, dmg=damage, Str/Dex/Con/Int/Wis/Cha=ability scores, and contractions saved space.

What a Psychic Warrior Does

The Psychic Warrior is flexible, it can be built to excel at just about anything, but there are things that come more easily to it.

Things a Psychic Warrior can do Easily:

⧫ Melee: You have good hit dice, Fortitude saves, BAB, bonus feats, and armor and weapon proficiency. Make sure to get inertial armor and either metaphysical weapon or claw. Focusing on getting powers that last longer, to conserve power points and combat time. The Ascetic, Dervish, Feral Warrior, Interceptor, Mind Knight, Survivor and Weaponmaster paths, and Thunderjarl archetype do this well.

⧫ Ranged: You get bonus feats and weapon proficiency. The BAB is lower than you want, reducing your attacks in a round. But some of the powers compensate for this (like Physical Acceleration, Battle Transformation, Barrage, etc.). The Archer, Assassin and Weaponmaster paths, and the Pathmaster archetype do this well.

⧫ Tank: Without the psionic body feat and buffing this becomes average. As your Hit Dice are only 1d8. However, with the feat and lots of psionic feats, plus all the pre-battle buffs a psychic warrior can do you can become a super tank. In many ways superior to an Aegis.

⧫ Buffing: You have the best self-buffs in psionics! With most lasting for hours, thus you can manifest them at the start of the day, long before battle. However, you are not good at buffing others.

⧫ Battlefield Control (Mundane): The moderate BAB limits most classes from being good at combat maneuvers. However, not with the Psychic Warrior. Martial weapon proficiency plus lots of bonus feats plus the right powers, can make them on par with a fighter.

Things a Psychic Warrior can do, with Difficulty:

⧫ Blast: There are a few blasting powers on their list. However, the low power points mean they can’t use them often.

⧫ Heal : With empathic transfer and hostile empathic transfer you can be a secondary healer. But the biggest problem is your low power points and lack of dispel psionics on your natural power list. Egotists, Vitalists and Tacticians (Battle Medic archetype) are far better.

⧫ Scout: Psychic Warriors don’t get a way to disable magic traps. They also get short changed on class skills, lacking disable device, most knowledges and stealth (except for Archer & Assassin paths).

Vital Statistics

⧫ d8/Medium BAB: Same as the Monk, Rogue, Cleric, etc. It’s a good chunk of health if you want to focus on manifesting and a good base for a decent melee build if you want to take it that way.

⧫ 4+Intelligence skills per level: You get most the class skills you would want for a martial character, plus bonus skills based on class. Further, you get bonuses to (a) path skill(s) of your choosing.

⧫ Good Fortitude: Great against the poisons you may encounter in melee, diseases from traps, and other physical maladies.

⧫ Bad Reflex and Will: The poor Reflex saves will be a constant problem for you, especially if you want to be in melee. Get a good Dexterity to compensate. However, the poor Will saves should be irrelevant, as you are a Wisdom-based manifester, thus your will saves should be good regardless.

⧫ Moderate PP Pool: You do not have too many power points to work with. Suggest getting Wisdom high for a few more.

⧫ Moderate Powers Known: Pathfinder improved this a little, as you get some bonus path powers. You have some great powers on your list. Thus you are not unknowledgeable on powers, just do not know as many as a psion.

Class Features

⧫ All Simple and Martial Weapons, All Armor and Shield (except tower) Proficiency: One of the best proficiency lists in psionics. Much like a fighter. This makes the Psychic Warrior adaptable, and able to use most weapon systems.

⧫ Bonus Feats: GET PSIONIC BODY! This will give you 2 HP per psionic feat you have, including itself and the psionic talent feat you got for free if you picked a psionic race. This is better than toughness. The Psychic Warrior gets a lot of bonus feats, thus can use feat combos easier.

⧫ Psionic Proficiency: Good for feat requirements, that is it.

⧫ Warrior’s Path, Expanded Path, Path Skill: These will be covered in the Warrior Paths section below.

⧫ Martial Power: Okay if you forgot to buff with a path power beforehand, or with some paths. But worthless with other paths.

⧫ Secondary Path: A second path is good (unless you are a pathmaster). However, you don’t get the bonuses from trace and maneuver until much later, and can’t have both paths’ traces at the same time.

⧫ Twisting Path: Switch your path as a swift action. This can be useful should the battle dynamically change.

⧫ Pathweaving: An awesome once per day ability. You can finally gain both traces at once.

⧫ Eternal Warrior: Very good capstone. Add Wisdom modifier to just about everything.

Ability Scores and Races

A Psychic Warrior can be built in a few different ways. Melee builds benefit more from a high Strength, while ranged should have a higher Dexterity. All Psychic Warriors benefit from high Wisdom. Constitution and Intelligence are good for the typical reasons. Charisma is nearly worthless.

⧫ Strength: For a melee or ranged Psychic Warrior with a composite bow, this is vital. Otherwise, keep the modifier positive.

⧫ Dexterity: For a ranged Psychic Warrior, this is especially important. And is good for all builds, as it modifies: AC, Reflex saves, initiative and many skills. If you’re getting heavy armor proficiency from somewhere, it becomes less important.

⧫ Constitution: Hit points and Fortitude saves. Even with a decent hit die, more hit points is always better than less.

⧫ Intelligence: You get many good class skills, but not many skill ranks. You can dump it if you only want to use a few skills in your career (Acrobatics, Perception and Stealth). But most players would want this in the 12-14 range. GMs who are just making single use NPCs can dump it.

⧫ Wisdom: You need this high for bonus power points! Psychic Warriors get so few power points that any extra point helps. This also modifies DC of some powers, but few Psychic Warrior powers have a save. This modifies a few other class abilities, and some skills.

⧫ Charisma: You can dump it, it only modifies a few social skills which a Psychic Warrior will rarely use anyway.

For a melee Psychic Warrior (or a ranged with a composite bow), your stat weights will probably look something like Wis>Str>Dex>Int>Con>Cha. For a ranged Psychic Warrior (except composite bow), flip Strength with Dexterity in importance. If you’ve got heavy armor proficiency, knock Dex to the bottom.

As far as races go, anything that gives you a bonus to Wisdom, Strength or Dexterity is at least good. Anything that doesn’t grant a bonus to one of those is almost always average, and anything that gives a penalty to Wisdom should be skipped immediately. Psionic races generally get bumped up a notch because they can get PP as their favored class bonus — which you need desperately.

Amazing Psychic Warrior Races: 

⧫ Duergar: +2 Con, +2 Wis, -2 Cha, Hardy (+2 to Saves vs. powers), darkvision and psi-like abilities! One of most durable races for a Psychic Warrior. The favored class bonus to use Expansion more often is very good. Better than the power points for a melee focused Psychic Warrior, as you won’t need to learn the power or expend focus to fully augment — it acts like it is fully augmented. Echos of the Abyss and Reborn Ancestor are good alternate racial traits — Echos is good vs. Cryptics. The others are not worth it. The light sensitivity is their only down-side. But most games are in a dark dungeon anyway. Additionally, treat all Dwarven weapons as martial, which most fancy weapons are Dwarven.

⧫ Half-Giant: +2 Str, -2 Dex, +2 Wis and powerful build. One of the best melee-weapon based Psychic Warriors. Powerful build alone increases all damage dice by one, and your maneuver offence and defence. The Liberty or Death alternate racial is generally better than fire or cold resistance. As mind-affecting effects are some of the worst. Stomp loses value after the first few levels (but is an okay area attack). But where this race shines is the favored class bonus, for certain path powers it can be fantastic.

⧫ Ophiduan: +2 Dex +2 Wis, -2 Cha, +1 natural armor (or +2 with an alternate racial trait), darkvision and a 1/day bite (which lasts 1 minute). One of better ranged or beastial Psychic Warriors. Most should take the Deformed and/or Metabolist alternate racials. Metabolist makes your manifester level one higher with psychometabolism powers, which can be a significant portion of your buffs. Poisonous Flesh is an okay poison for a ranged build. The favored class bonus is not worth it, put it into power points.

Good Psychic Warrior Races:

⧫ Elan: +2 to ability of your choice (Wisdom best), Resistance and Resilience. A good defensive race, with more ways to use power points. The favored class bonus can make it a min-max monster -- however, it reduces your overall power points (which are in short supply). Failed Transformation (Dromite, Duergar, Half-Orc and Halfling) are the only alternate racial traits that may be worth it. Though a base Elan with only Psionic Aptitude is generally better.

⧫ Human: +2 to ability of your choice (Wisdom best), bonus skill ranks, bonus feat. The bonus feat is what makes humans good.

⧫ Maenad: +2 to ability of your choice (Wisdom best), Outburst and 1/day Sonic Ray. The Outburst and Sonic Ray are good situationally. But strongly suggest the Crystalized Skin alternate racial. The favored class bonus is not bad if you want crazy good AC. However, generally more power points are better than epic AC. The flail proficiency is very nice.

⧫ Noral: -2 Dex, +2 Con, +2 Wis, Symbiotic Resistance and Surge, and 1/day Mind thrust. A good defensive race. It has a few once per day luck bonuses, and some good resistance vs. mind effects. The Resilient Symbiote alternate racial, is generally better than Mind Thrust, as it gives a massive amount of hit points for one battle. Symbiotic Sentry is also good.

Average Psychic Warrior Races:

⧫ Blues: -2 Str, +2 Dex, +2 Int, darkvision and small-sized. An average finesse or stealth Psychic Warrior. But lower damage dice, bad favored class bonus, and no bonuses to Wisdom. Avoid any alternate racial traits (base are better).

⧫ Dromites: -2 Str, +2 Dex, +2 Int, +1 natural armor, scent and small-sized. Same problems as Blues, but worse.

⧫ Xeph: -2 Str, +2 Dex, +2 Cha, darkvision, Innate Resistance (+1 to Saves vs. powers) and burst. The Strength bonus is bad for any build, even if the rest of their abilities are nice.

Hall of Shame: Forgeborn

Paths

At first level, you chose one Path, then a second at ninth level. You have 12 different standard paths to choose from, and a few good archetypes (Pathmaster and Thunderjarl are very good). This is what makes the Psychic Warrior class so specialized. Most of your character can be focused around your Path. You can even synergies paths at later levels, or specialize in one. They are rated on the basis that you are choosing them for the style they are best for.

According to Dreamscarred Press errata, all the skills a path receives bonuses to are class skills, not just the one. This will generally not change any path’s overall rating, but it could make a tiny difference in your decision making.

The Archer, Mind Knight and Survivor paths are the best ones for most people.

Ancestral Path (7th): Good knowledge and anti-incorporeal path. Even if not dealing with many incorporeal creatures, the knowledge and language boons may make it worth it for a scholarly Psychic Warrior. They also get to re-roll Will saves and skills.

⧫ Skills: Gaining Knowledge (History) as a class skill, not the worst knowledge skill, but still bad. Autohypnosis is already a class skill, and you only really need 4 ranks (for psionic meditation feat). Heal is more useful than you think.

⧫ Powers: Guidance of the Ancestors is a good way to increase your knowledge or gain temporary knowledge of a language. Especially useful for ancient, secret and/or obscure languages that you do not know. Ward the Mind is a good defensive buff with good augments.

⧫ Trance: Great against incorporeal, and a bonus to all knowledge skills. The manifester checks bonus is rarely useful.

⧫ Maneuver: For focus, can re-roll a skill check or Will save! Then take the better result! This alone makes this path good. Failing Will saves can be the worst, and that it applies to ALL skill checks is an added bonus.

Archer Path: The best path for a ranged Psychic Warrior. Good path combos are Ascetic or Survivor. Does NOT synergize well with Assassin, even if they have some similarities; Assassin's maneuver is melee only; Mind Knight is another bad combo.

⧫ Skills: Gaining Stealth as a class skill, which is good especially for sniping. Perception is one of the best skills in the game. Ride is worthless without a mount.

⧫ Powers: Force Screen is nice to have vs. magic missile or like. But inertial armor is far better. Situationally you may use it unaugmented to get your AC a little higher. Psychokinetic Cannon attack with ammo, without projectile (i.e., bow). And bonuses to the weapon it would normally use you have (Weapon Focus, this path's trance, etc.) apply to this.

⧫ Trance: A bonus to ranged attacks, which keeps your attack rolls as a full BAB class (except iteratives). Helps with Deadly Aim and Many Shot builds.

⧫ Maneuver: A ranged trip attack, that uses your Dex instead of Str. Also anything which modifies your attack also modifies your CMB. The possibilities if combined with Gladiator Path could be very good.

Ascetic Path: A monk-like Psychic Warrior which is good at dodging attacks. This is one of the better defensive paths. An Ascetic Mind Knight is a thing to fear.

⧫ Skills:Gain Knowledge (Religion) as a class skill, which is useful for common Undead enemies. Acrobatics is one of the best skills in the game, useful for avoiding attacks of opportunity or parkouring. Autohypnosis is already a class skill, and you only really need 4 ranks (for psionic meditation feat). Autohypnois is still a good skill, and put your path skill bonus into Acrobatics.

⧫ Powers: Defensive Precognition is a very good defensive battle buff, as it applies to AC, touch AC, and all saves. Offensive Precognition is great to breach a high AC enemy, but will be less used than defensive.

⧫ Trance: A competence bonus to AC (which is an uncommon boost that stacks with most things). It scales well.

⧫ Maneuver: For focus as an immediate action, gain a good dodge bonus. Stacked with your trance boost and defensive precognition, you will avoid just about any attack with this.

Assassin Path: A sneaky path with poison use and a sneak-attack-like maneuver. You deal good damage, but you're weaker against groups than most PsyWars. If you want a stealthy type, you need to take this path. It will work, but most PsyWar players are interested in battles, not backstabbing. Know what you're getting when you choose this path and you won't be disappointed. Otherwise you'll suffer rogue syndrome. Obviously for finesse builds. No good for archery or heavy armor types.

⧫ Skills: Gain Stealth as a class skill, which is good for sneaking. Acrobatics AND Perception are the two best skills in the game.

⧫ Powers: Distract is garbage. Prevenom/prevenom weapon is weak.

⧫ Trance: Damage bonus. This is alright, but it doesn't compare to an AC or attack boost on the same scale.         

⧫ Maneuver: d6s to damage. Reminiscent of sneak attack, this is basically half scaled. It applies under other conditions, specifically that your enemy has to be by themselves. Very assassin-y, but this much more and NPC than a PC power. Good against a boss man with no minions, I guess. That's about it.

Brawling Path: This path does grappling well. But grappling has lower return at higher levels, as monster CMD and CMB get out of reach. The problem is, this path doesn't give you bonuses to grapple, just to damage while grappling. Grappling right requires investment. This path will make it a little easier for you (but a fighter may be better).

⧫ Skills: Gain Escape Artist as a class skill, which is redundant as you are more likely going to use CMB to escape. Acrobatics is one of the best skills in the game. Autohypnosis is already a class skill, and you only really need 4 ranks (for psionic meditation feat). Autohypnois is still a good skill, and put your path skill bonus into Acrobatics.

⧫ Powers: Grip of Iron is good for grappling (the reason you picked this path in the first place). Hammer is not great, but you'll probably be unarmed or using natural weapons, so you can channel it through your punches/claws.

⧫ Trance: More damage while grappling. Grappling is not easy, but at least you do more damage when you succeed.

⧫ Maneuver: Losing focus to deal non-lethal damage is not worth it.

Dervish Path: So, you've got a Dex build, and you're swinging around two axes, and you're pouncing all around with psionic lion's charge. What completes this picture? The Dervish path. Boosting mobility and attack while you use two weapons, this is what you dream of. This is a good combo with Weaponmaster, Assassin, Ascetic, and, like every other path, Survivor.

⧫ Skills: Gain Bluff as a class skill, but with no sneak attack or other way to optimize it it is mostly used out of combat (but still nice for role play). Acrobatics is one of the best skills in the game. Perform is worthless.

⧫ Powers: Force screen is nice to have vs. magic missile or like. But inertial armor is far better. Situationally you may use it unaugmented to get your AC a little higher. Metaphysical Weapon is worse for you than usual because you need to use it twice.

⧫ Trance: Bonus to attack when you wield two weapons. This doesn't say you need to actually use both weapons, so this still applies to single attacks. Like all of the attack boosting trances, this is a great choice.

⧫ Maneuver: Can mix multiple 5’ steps with your attacks (i.e., flank, hit multiple enemies, step away to manifest). This doesn't stack with hustle.

Feral Warrior Path: The beast natural weapon build for a Psychic Warrior. It gives you natural weapons, and bonuses with them. Further, it gives a pseudo-pounce that eventually becomes a real pounce.Half-Giants and Ophiduans make good Feral Warriors.

⧫ Skills: Gain Survival as a class skill, which is a good environmental skill to have. Acrobatics AND Perception are the two best skills in the game.

⧫ Powers: Claws of the Beast is the backbone of this path. Make sure to get the metaphysical claw power, to manifest twice on both claws. Bite of the Wolf is not as good (due to duration and damage progression), but not a bad option.

⧫ Trance: Bonus to attack with natural weapons. Your to-hit almost keeps up with a full BAB class, and you don't suffer from lack of iterative         attacks because you don't use them anyways.

⧫ Maneuver: The maneuver seems like it should be awesome and the best thing you could ever get. But it isn't, and that's because of two powers: hustle and psionic lion's charge. The maneuver is worse than either of those and requires you to expend your focus. This is this path's weakness. And only becomes full pounce at 20th level.

Gladiator Path: This is a combat maneuver path. Combat maneuvers have the serious issue of monsters in the bestiary being huge with ridiculous strength scores. I will make the assumption that is what you're fighting. If you're against normal-sized enemies, you can definitely pull this off. Unlike the brawler, this has lots of different combat maneuvers to choose from, so it's a better choice. They could certainly combine well, though.

⧫ Skills: Gain Bluff as a class skill, but with no sneak attack or other way to optimize it it is mostly used out of combat (but still nice for role play). Acrobatics AND Perception are the two best skills in the game.

⧫ Powers: Grip of Iron is good for grappling (the reason you picked this path in the first place). Tactical Precognition is better than Hammer (but still a situational power).

⧫ Trance: A boost to your CMB and CMD. What makes this not automatically worse than attack bonuses is that it applies to D and B, while attack bonuses would just apply to B. Otherwise, you're still limited by combat maneuvers.

⧫ Maneuver: Basically free Improved [Combat Maneuver] for whatever you want whenever you want it. If you already have that feat, it's an additional +2 to that maneuver. The +2 is nothing to write home about, but avoiding AoO without needing a feat is great and really gives you a lot of versatility.

Infiltrator Path: Good for social infiltration or turning into a scary monster. It's a good choice for a more social Psychic Warrior. However, if you want to be a social character there are better classes than Psychic Warrior (Cryptic, Dread, Seer, Telepath, Vitalist or Wilder). This is the best social Psychic Warrior.

⧫ Skills: Gain Disguise as a class skill, this fits with an assassin build, but is useless in combat. Escape Artist is okay for a dexterity build. Intimidate mixed with a size increase from minor metamorphosis is a good combo (however, shaken has an opportunity cost in combat).

⧫ Powers: Chameleon is the power you would expect for a sneaky Psychic Warrior. Minor Metamorphosis is what makes this path shine. The menu of options available to you is phenomenal, and a reason many take this as a secondary path (if not primary).

⧫ Trance: Gain a bonus to disguise and damage rolls with natural weapons. This is good for any natural weapons from minor metamorphosis or to up your disguise.

⧫ Maneuver: For focus as a free action, can intimidate while metamorphosed (with a bonus to check at later levels). Being able to make enemies shaken is good. And it is questionable if it is worth focus. Make sure to refocus right after.

Interceptor Path: The ultimate bodyguard. Blocking enemies from your wards. It combines well with Infiltrator, for a Defensive-Intimidate build, scaring your foes away. Just be ready for enemies immune to fear and mind-affecting effects. This can work either as a heavily-armored tough guy or a quick and hard-to-hit speedster.

⧫ Skills: Gain Intimidate as a class skill, this allows you to debuff foes in combat without needing power points or focus. Acrobatics AND Perception are the two best skills in the game.

⧫ Powers: Burst is bad, not worth the power points unless you need to escape (which you should not be doing as a bodyguard). However, Expansion is one of the best powers for a Psychic Warrior. You get it for free which is awesome!

⧫ Trance: A bonus on attack and damage against enemies threatening your allies. Attack and damage is wonderful, but your ally has an enemy right next to them.

⧫ Maneuver: When an enemy attempts to attack an ally, you get a free attack or bull rush to try and interrupt them. They take a penalty on the attack if they can still make it, but your action gets resolved first! This can be well worth focus.

Mind Knight Path: You can summon a powerful random weapon, and psionic armor. With an overpowered trace and ways to empower their weapon, this is one of the best paths. However, the randomness of what weapon is summoned can become annoying. Suggest having the GM make a list of ten weapons, then rolling 1d10 -- otherwise it can slow down gameplay. Make sure to get the Detect Hostile Intent power, so you are always ready. This path also lets you into many prestige classes (Dark Tempest and some would argue Metaforge), and feats (Mind Blade Knight, Mind Knight's Arsenal and Soul Warrior) pathways not other Psychic Warrior would gain access to.

⧫ Skills: Gain Diplomacy as a class skill, which is useless in combat (but has functions out of combat). Autohypnosis is already a class skill, but is likely the best of the three to put your path bonus into. Ride is useless unless you have a mount.

⧫ Powers: A Psychic Warrior without Inertial Armor is poorly made. You get it for free, which is very good. Call Weaponry is the crux of what makes this path great. Your trace further empowers this. Randomness is the main issue.

⧫ Trance: The best trance of all, which gives bonuses to initiative, attack and damage. Wow! Remember, you can call any type of weapon. You're versatile. Reach, ranged, two-handed, lance, you name it, you've got it.

⧫ Maneuver: Can make multiple attacks and move, but your attacks all have to be against separate targets. Call a reach weapon and this is better. However, hustle/psionic lion's charge are generally better choices.

Shackler Path (7th): Good debuffing and anti-incorporeal path. Even if not dealing with many incorporeal creatures, the negative energy attacks, intimidate and shackling abilities may make it worth it for a debuffing Psychic Warrior. Bypasses fear immunity.

⧫ Skills: Gaining Knowledge (Religion) as a class skill, great for the common undead. Autohypnosis is already a class skill, and you only really need 4 ranks (for psionic meditation feat). Intimidate is modified by many path abilities.

⧫ Powers: Breath of Thanatos can be augmented to deal some negative energy damage. But it is a Fortitude to negate, undead auto-succeed on Fortitude saves, and the damage is minimal. Ward the Mind is a good defensive buff with good augments.

⧫ Trance: Intimidate as a free action any creature you hit! The way it is worded, this includes ranged attacks and powers.

⧫ Maneuver: For focus, can trap and debuff a target. Great against foes that might try to run away. Sadly the DC is Charisma-based. But a Will save instead of a physical skill check is very good.

Survivor Path: You die hard. This is the best tanky Psychic Warrior, and your best choice if you are new to Psychic Warriors. The damage reduction, bonus powers and Stalwart maneuver make you very durable. This is also a good healing Psychic Warrior.

⧫ Skills: Gain Survival as a class skill, which is a good environmental skill to have. Autohypnosis is already a class skill, but is perfect for this path. Heal is more useful than you think.

⧫ Powers: Catfall can be life saving when falling. And falling is not common in most games, it may happen every few months in real world time. But still good to have when you need it. Vigor with Empathic Transfer is a great combo, as is Vigor on any tanky build. For buffing with temporary power points before battle is more efficient than healing after.

⧫ Trance: Endure Elements and DR/―. Get some Warrior’s Bracers to make yourself like an Aegis.

⧫ Maneuver: As an immediate action, gain STALWART!!! Stalwart is Evasion for Will AND Fort saves. You can wait until you know if you succeed or fail at the save to use it. And then at 11, it's Improved Stalwart. You also get a bonus on Will save!

Weaponmaster Path: Weaponmasters are the switch hitters, the classic fighters. Don't combine it with Mind Knight, despite what you may think; there's just too much overlap. The trances are basically the same if you're using a called weapon, except this is worse. This path accomplishes exactly what it sets out to.

⧫ Skills: Gain Knowledge (Nobility) as a class skill, this sucks. Acrobatics is one of the best skills in the game. Craft is broken. Suggest houseruling that they gain Knowledge (Local or History) instead, it makes more thematic and mechanical sense.

⧫ Powers: Empty Mind is more useful in a psionic campaign, or when you really need to pass a Will save. Metaphysical Weapon unaugmented is garbage. But if you augment it by at least 4 power points it lasts hours per level. Becoming a very good power. As you get a nearly free +2 weapon which you can change each day. Buy many cheap weapons to make full use of this.

⧫ Trance: Attack bonus with all weapons except natural attacks. Great if you have a weapon for every situation.

⧫ Maneuver: For focus as an immediate action, can to retaliate then five-foot step. You get a bonus on attack and damage, but this doesn't stack with your trance, so it's really just to damage. Sadly, the foe’s attack gets resolved first (Interceptor’s is better).

Archetypes

BY PUBLICATION: Psionics Expanded (PE), Ultimate Psionics (UP), Psionics Augmented (PA), Seventh Path (7th)

⧫ Scaled Rider (Ophiduan) (PE): Gain a reptile companion mount (called a Karaan), instead of a warrior’s path. The Karaan is beefy, and so long as you remain within 5’ of it, you can share powers with it -- which is amazing. You get some good abilities with this mount, making it good for a mounted Psychic Warrior. But the loss of a warrior’s path will keep this average.

⧫ Thunderjarl (Half-Giant) (PE): If you want to hit something hard with a greatsword, this is the archetype for you. This gives you the Psionic Weapon and Greater Psionic Weapon feats as bonus feats, then makes the feats do extra damage. If you want to deal massive damage with a weapon, take this archetype. This also improves your stomp ability.

⧫ Pathmaster (UP): If you want to specialize in a path, this is the archetype for you. It starts making changes at 9th level, thus you can wait until then before making the decision. Specialize (with it) or synergize (by not taking it)? That is the question.

Powers

WARRIOR POWERS: Powers that buffs you for hours per level are generally the best. As you do not have many power points to use, and if you have to waste the first few rounds of combat you are doing it wrong. Psychic warriors should be able to attack first round pre-buffed.

  • 1st-Level: Absorb Weapon: Alright, I guess if you’re sneaking into the ball to stab the duke, this can have its uses. But you probably aren’t. You’re probably kicking the door open, killing his guards, and eviscerating him in front of the crowd in a spray of blood.
  • Astral Traveler: The person who’s casting the astral caravan should be able to cast astral traveler too.
  • Biofeedback: Damage reduction is seriously useful, and nothing overcomes this. A great defensive buff.
  • Bite of the Wolf: For a natural weapon build, this is a must. For any other build, it’s a free attack that doesn’t use your hands. The damage dice can be augmented, so this stays relevant. A solid offensive choice.
  • Blinding Flash: A swift action, but even that can save this power from the fact that dazzled is the worst condition there is.
  • Burst: It’s a swift action, which is nice, but compare this to expeditious retreat or skate. It’s not great.
  • Call Weaponry: Summon your sword to your side. This is a great power, and the core of a mind knight. The enhancement bonus scales at pace with metaphysical weapon, and you make whatever weapon you need at the time. Versatility plus power. This is a good deal.
  • Catfall: Listen, when you need it, you will be absolutely delighted you have it. But you won’t need it much.
  • Chameleon: Great for a stealth build, ok for anyone else.
  • Circumstance Shield: Good if you want to go first, as going first can mean winning. And there are no prizes for second place in a fight.
  • Claws of the Beast: Really long duration, switches on and off for free, and scaling damage dice. This is the signature power of a natural weapon build.
  • Compression: For a martial class, the uses are limited. Yes, you can get more dex for a finesse or archery build, but at two different penalties to damage. Not worth it usually, but occasionally it can serve its purpose well.
  • Corrosive Aura: Acid is not a very common resistance, and this doesn’t require you to do anything after manifestation to deal good damage for an battle, so you can keep whacking away with your big old club while enemies melt around you. I like this one a lot.
  • Dazzling Swordplay: See blinding flash.
  • Deaden Attack: Useful in situations where dealing nonlethal damage is important. Plus, it can be used on your enemies’ weapons. Easier to heal, even if it doesn’t really help you during the fight. This is alright, but there are better choices.
  • Dissipating Touch: Meh, blasting isn’t your job. This can do what it does just fine, but you’ll have better luck smacking someone with your hammer.
  • Elfsight: Can’t be augmented, not darkvision, only a small bonus to perception. There are better 1st level powers.
  • Empty Mind: Compare this to Defensive Precognition and you’ll see just how bad it is.
  • Expansion: This boosts damage, reach and size. Unlike its magic equivalent, can become huge by 7th level. A huge half-giant psychic warrior wields gigantic-sized weapons. Remember that when you want to smash your foes to atoms.
  • Force Screen: Unless you’re using a shield, which you probably aren’t, this is a substantial AC boost and makes you immune to the occasional magic missile.
  • Fortify: If all you want to do is boost saves, this is better than defensive precognition. But it is lacking the AC bonus of defensive precognition. Thus most should take defensive precognition instead.
  • Foxhole: Give yourself cover or knock an enemy prone. Could have some use, especially for a ranged build.
  • Grip of Iron: This is a good choice if you’ll be grappling, but you probably shouldn’t be grappling.
  • Hammer: Basically the same as dissipating touch.
  • Inertial Armor: One of the best buffs in the game. Gain free weightless light armor with no max Dexterity bonus or armor check penalty, that you can augment! Hell yes.
  • Inevitable Strike: Psionic true strike. Good in a pinch. But offensive precognition is generally more economic (as it lasts minutes instead of just one hit).
  • Metaphysical Claw/Metaphysical Weapon: This is a way to get free enhancement bonuses to weapons. Claw for natural bites and claws; weapon for manufactured weapons. It only affects one weapon (thus you may need to use it multiple times if you have multiple weapons). However, if you augment it at least once, it lasts for hours. This can save you money better spent on Wisdom headbands and Warrior’s Bracers. Does not stack with other enhancements.
  • Precognition, Defensive: Increases your AC and your saves. This is really good. Insight is an uncommon bonus, basically only from clairsentience, so this will always be relevant.
  •  Precognition, Offensive: Just like its defensive brother, but for attacks. Good against high AC opponents.
  • Precognition, Tactical: Good for a maneuver-based character. Otherwise, mostly useless.
  • Prescience, Offensive: There are better ways to increase damage (namely Metaphysical Claw/Weapon), and your average damage per round (DPR) is higher with a higher hit chance than damage generally.
  • Prevenom/Prevenom Weapon: The damage for these is terrible and doesn’t actually work like a poison.
  • Shield Wall: If you want a tower shield, essential. Otherwise useless.
  • Skate: Better than burst, with a duration, bigger boost to speed and better flavor.
  • Stomp: In melee, the short range isn’t a huge issue. Knocking enemies prone is a very useful control mechanic, especially if you’ve got reach and combat reflexes so you can eat up all those tasty AoOs. Plus, against most enemies, you can do a modest amount of damage.
  • Synesthete: It can negate blindness or deafness, which is really quite fantastic when you need it. Despite being rather situational, a low-level power with a good duration that completely negates some potentially crippling issues.
  • Thicken Skin: Better duration than defensive precognition, and stacks with most other armor (psionic, magic, inertial, force screen, etc.). Plus, it stacks with that power, and natural armor bonuses seem to weirdly stack with themselves. What natural armor bonuses do and don’t stack has gotten so confusing that I’ve just made NA the Dodge bonus of flat-footed AC. Talk to your GM and see what he says.
  • Vigor: Vigor gets a bad rap. But temporary HP can be very useful. Especially when everyone says avoiding damage is more important than healing it, this is a great damage avoider. It’s 5 HP per power point! At the same time, you could over or underestimate how much you need and see it go to waste, but usually that won’t happen.

Build Options

Most Psychic Warriors will dip in other classes or prestige classes to gain some versatility. However, a pure Psychic Warrior will advance in manifesting, trace and maneuvers quicker. There are some feats and equipment which can compensate for dipping.

BASE CLASSES: Base classes can be a great dip because they usually have good features first level. Melee wilders often multiclass for the martial or exotic weapon access. Manifester wilders should strongly consider one level of sorcerer.

⧫ Barbarian: A one to three level dip is tempting for rage. However, War Mind Prestige class is better because it advances manifesting and it does not have any of the problems that rage does (like loss of manifesting capacity when raging).

⧫ Fighter: If you need to dip for bonus feats this is the way.

⧫ Psion: An Egotist-Psychic Warrior combo may be good. Giving you more power points to work with, and more powers known. However, you are still limited by manifester level separately thus this is suboptimal at best, but more likely a turkey.

⧫ Soulknife: A decent way to up your weapon attacks. However, if you want to be a Psychic Warrior that specializes in weapons, take the Mind Knight or Weaponmaster paths. Or if you really like the idea, become a Dark Tempest.

⧫ Wilder: Potentially the only psionic class worthy of multi-classing in. As the wild surge compensates a little, and you do get many power points from it. Strongly suggest the Contemplative archetype of wilder, which gives Overchannel and Talented feats for free (which you can use on psychic warrior powers as well), you can change powers each day, and it is Wisdom-based.

On the Melding of Martial and Mental Mastery

What is a Psychic Warrior? The psychic warrior is exactly what it sounds like. It mixes psionic powers with martial skill. It's capable of filling just about any role that another martial class could cover. It's functional in light or heavy armor, melee or ranged, defensive or offensive, finesse or muscle. It has all the versatility of the fighter; arguably even more due to the addition of psionic powers. Their skill in combat comes from augmenting their martial skill with powerful self buffs. No one can self buff quite like a psychic warrior, and that's really the key to the class.

Hey, this class was lame in 3.5. What's so great about it now? Well, back in 3.5 the psychic warrior was just bonus feats + psionic powers. That was then. Now, thanks to Dreamscarred Press, the psychic warrior has the awesome path feature, a psychic warrior's means of choosing his focus. Paths give all sorts of nifty abilities, but we'll go into those later. The point is, DSP's psychic warrior has some truly unique features that make it much more than the fighter with medium BAB and psychic powers it was in 3.5.

Why should I play one? The psychic warrior is for those players who want to be a serious threat in combat but also want psionic powers. You should play one if you want to be a big bad buffed up monster. If you're looking to mix combat with blasting magic, well, go back to the magus. Although psionics is an effective system for blasting, it is not what the psychic warrior excels at. As stated several times already, his powers are for making himself stronger. His other big boost is the versatility offered by mixing psionics and martial skill. The average psychic warrior is a lot more versatile than the usual barbarian, paladin, or even fighter.

What is this “psionics” thing you keep talking about? Psionics is a wonderful magic system that has a long-running history in DnD and now, thanks to Dreamscarred Press, a welcome seat at the Pathfinder table. You may have heard that psionics is overpowered, or complicated, or just stupid. It is not the duty of this guide to justify psionics as a subsystem, but I will simply state I believe the current incarnation is well balanced, intuitive, and a blast to play.

Ok, well, how does it work? Instead of casting spells, you manifest powers. In many ways this is similar, but in other ways it is quite different. Like spells, powers can do a great deal of different, interesting things. They are categorized into disciplines, which are similar to schools. They provoke attacks of opportunity to manifest. There are powers from levels 0-9 (although the Psychic Warrior only goes up to 6). But you don't use spell slots. You have one pool of power points that you can spread around all of your powers of different levels, offering you a great deal of versatility. There are no components to powers, so you don't need to worry about being bound and gagged, spell failure, or having to buy expensive components. Instead you have manifestations, stylish things that happen, with no mechanical effect besides being noticed, whenever you manifest a power. They also don't scale automatically like spells, so you need to expend more power points to keep them relevant. At the same time, this means low-level powers stay useful throughout your character's life. Finally, you have psionic focus, a state of mental preparedness that confers a number of benefits when maintained and expended. For more info, you'll have to look into psionics yourself. I encourage you to give it a chance.

What's covered in this guide? First, I will be dealing with the content presented in DSP's Psionics Unleashed. All of that is available on the PFSRD, so it is easy to access for anyone. I will also be tackling the new options presented in Psionics Expanded, which, unfortunately, has barely been uploaded to the PFSRD so far. That stuff will be marked with an asterisk*. And finally, I'll be covering any primary Paizo material in the hardcover line. I will mostly be ignoring options from setting-specific material.

I will, of course, be using the standard Treantmonk Red, Orange, Green, Blue color coding system to help indicate good and bad options. If you see Light Blue, then you're looking at one of the absolute best options there is.

And finally, before we really begin, this is my first guide. Bear with me if you find I present bad options. I am more than happy to discuss and refine; criticism is welcome.

Now, on to the guiding!

Combat Style and Statistics

There are tons of different choices to go for with the Psychic Warrior, but I'll just deal with the three main arrays for you:

Two Hander/Natural Weapon: This is the classic melee brute build. Big old smacky weapon for slapping around the bad guys. This is the most effective melee choice, simply because two-handed is always the most effective melee choice in Pathfinder, like it or not. Alternatively, the same stats can be used to cover a psychic warrior focused on growing his own natural weapons, which is not quite as powerful but still a good choice.

Stats Priority:

Str>Wis/Con>Dex>Int>Cha

Basically the same stat set up as any melee class, but you also want good wisdom so you've got plenty of powerpoints and are able to manifest all your powers when you need to. You need the constitution to take the blows in melee, and dex for a little bit of AC and initiative. Intelligence and charisma are unneeded.

Finesse/Dual-wield: The finesse build is certainly a viable choice for a psychic warrior, but it just won't keep up with the two-hander for damage. Such is life. It is more on par with the natural weapon strength build if you decide to dual-wield, the superior finesse choice. You do have enough feats to pull off wielding two weapons at once, unlike a lot of classes. Plus, pounce is easily accessible for a PsyWar, greatly increasing the value of lots of attacks.

Stats Priority:

Dex>Wis>Con>Str>Int>Cha

Dex goes first for the purpose of attack bonus. Wisdom is important for the same reason it matters to the strength build. Con goes next so you don't die too easily, then Strength for some damage. If you've got some leftover points you can put them in Intelligence or Charisma, but neither really matters to you.

Archer: With all your bonus feats, bow proficiency, and the right path, you can make a great archer. Most of the powers for a Psychic Warrior serve melee more than ranged, but you've definitely got choices.

Stats Priority:

Dex>Wis>Str>Con>Int>Cha

Dex is king here for attack, and Wisdom matters again for your powers. Unlike the Finesse build, Strength goes before Constitution, because you'll be getting hit a lot less and can afford the boost to Strength for damage. As usual, no need for Intelligence or Charisma.

The Class Features

The Basics: You receive D8 hit dice and Medium BAB. You get proficiency in all martial weapons and armor and shields, except tower shields. You've got the needed proficiencies to wade into melee or wield a bow, but remember that, pre-buffed, you won't have the to-hit or health of a D10 full BAB class. For saves, your only good one is Fortitude, but your will should be decent due to your wisdom. You also get 4 + Int mod skills, so while you're no skill monkey, you can certainly cover a few bases. Knowledge (Psionics) could be useful if your campaign is psionics heavy, Autohypnosis is useful for all sorts of random little things, and you've got the best skill in the game, Perception. Spellcraft never hurts to have either. A mobile Dex build might make use of Acrobatics. Putting a rank in Climb and Swim can never hurt, but you can get powers to more than cover those bases. Finally, you've got Ride. Sadly, without a companion, a mounted psychic warrior is tough to pull off. Additionally, you'll get a bonus class skill from your path.

Manifesting: This is what makes you a viable combatant, the real backbone of the class. Your manifesting abilities allow you to pump up your stats, grow weapons, and give you all sorts of different boosts and buffs. You also get a few utility powers here and there. This will be covered more in detail in the Powers section.

Bonus Feats: You receive a bonus combat or psionic feat at levels 1, 2, and then every third level after. Bonus feats are always nice, so this is something to really enjoy. Psionic feats can be all over the spectrum in terms of what they do, so this can focus your combat skills or expand your options beyond just hitting things with a stick. The number of feats you get is second only to a fighter, so don't be afraid to try out a secondary focus for your character or one or two less needed feats.

Psionic Proficiency: When picking your bonus feats, you should be delighted to know that any psionic feats you want count your Psychic Warrior level as you BAB. This means you'll be picking a lot of psionic feats, because you can really take advantage of them more than anyone.

Warrior's Path: There are tons of these, and they'll be covered in their own section. Each path offers a few things. Two extra powers known, a class skill, and bonuses to a couple of skills. You also get a trance, which is a bonus you receive while you maintain psionic focus. Finally, there's a maneuver, which is a bonus you get by expending your psionic focus. The bonuses from trances and maneuvers are all over the place, but they're usually to AC, attack, or damage. You choose one path at level one, and then get a secondary path at level 9.

Martial Power: You expend your focus and get to manifest a power as part of an attack. This can be used on a touch attack, in which case it's like a magus's spell combat. Otherwise, you basically just manifest it as a free action.

Twisting Path: This allows you to switch between your two path trances as a swift action, so you can actually make use of both of your paths.

Pathweaving: This is a better version of twisting path, where you can get access to both of your trances for 5 minutes. You get very few uses of this per day, but getting both paths simultaneously can be pretty great.

Eternal Warrior: The psychic warrior capstone is a temporary mega trance. You get to add your wisdom modifier to just about everything for five minutes and your speed increases by 5 x your wisdom score. That's a huge speed boost (like, 100+ feet), but this whole capstone is a little underwhelming. It does stack with your other trances, but overall this isn't quite what a capstone should be.
