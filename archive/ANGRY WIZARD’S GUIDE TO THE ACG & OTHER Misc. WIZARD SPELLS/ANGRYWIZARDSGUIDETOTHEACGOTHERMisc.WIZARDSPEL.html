ANGRY WIZARD’S GUIDE TO THE ACG & OTHER Misc. WIZARD SPELLS

This guide is dedicated to Treantmonk, Professor Q, Brewer, N. Jolly, and everyone else who was written a pathfinder guide.

Also a thanks to Ipslore the Red, without whom I would still be getting angry at trying to share the URL for this guide.

I love wizards. While there are many classes that I enjoy, such as clerics, paladins, bards, and druids just to name a few, above all others the wizard speaks the most to me. Bearded old men with staves, immortal elven archmages, high stone towers, and the books upon books upon enormous, dusty old books all make my mind quiver with eldritch delight. When I was newb way back, my girlfriend brought me into her Pathfinder group. She handed me the book and I flipped it to wizard. I asked her to show me how to make one and since then pathfinder has consumed my life… in a positive way.

The first guide I read for wizardry was by the immortal Treantmonk, whose dry humor and love of wizardry left a lasting impact on my gaming style. Since then dozens of guides have come out for many different classes, but all of them have been dedicated to the rating style and format Treantmonk. So I would like to continue the tradition of guide writing by writing a little guide of my own.

This guide will be focusing on the Advanced Class Guide spells for wizards, as well as wizard spells from miscellaneous other sources that I have yet to see guides or ratings for. I was inspired to do so because I have never seen a guide for any of these spells in any wizard guides anywhere, and as a sat with my beer and pretzels in front of my computer screen I thought to myself “Man, someone needs to write a guide for these spells… *munchmunch*…..Why not me?”. So here we are. Three things about me before we begin.

1.    I’m human. Perhaps I’ve misunderstood a spell somewhere along the line. Perhaps you’ve thought of a use for a spell that I have not. If you do, post it on the Paizo forum this guide will be posted on. If you convince me, I’ll edit it. Overall these are just my initial opinions on these spells, all of which a subject to change.

2.    I like evocation spells more than summoning spells. Now while you sharpen your pitchforks to run me out of Wizard College as a heretic, let me explain. I find it more fun to blast things than to summon things to blast for me. Is evocation underpowered compared to conjuration? Probably. Is it more fun for me than summoning? Hell yeah! The reason I share this is because I admit to having a bias towards evocation spells, so I think about spells like an evoker more often than I do for any other school of magic. These are MY opinions. If you don’t agree with my rankings for certain spells, talk with me on the forum page or exit stage left.

3.    I make terrible jokes sometimes. You have been warned!

With that all said, let’s finally go over all these spells from the ACG and other Misc. sources.

 

 Color Rating Guide

Blue: Excellent Spell. Pick up whenever possible, it’s that good.

Green: Good Spell. This is a strong option for your character to take.

Orange: Okay Spell. There are better choices out there, but it’s not horrible.

Red: Bad Spell. Either too situational or just plain terrible, avoid these spells.

Brown: Shit Spell. DO NOT USE THIS SPELL. NO POSSIBLE WAY TO USE WELL.

Black: Unrated Spell. This spell, for whatever reason, cannot be rated properly.

1st Level Wizard Spells

Abjuration

Invisibility Alarm: (ACG) Even more situational than alarm, with an even shorter duration. The only real use this can get is with a permanency spell to place on an entryway, but this is a scroll at best if you ever need it.

Line in the Sand: (ACG) Decent enough for magi and bloodragers who are more dex based, terrible for the wizard’s lowly BAB.

Stunning Barrier: (ACG) The short duration makes this hard to recommend for buffing and unless you know you’re about to get hit, the stun isn’t likely to happen, nor would you want to be near melee attackers in the first place. Also doesn’t even scale.

Sunder Breaker: (ACG) Hey, if you’ve got someone who likes to sunder, it really speeds up the process. However, like most adventurers, you WANT the demon’s +3 flaming bastard sword either for its battle use or to sell for profit and it’s unlikely you have someone who specializes in repairing magic items. It’s a highly specific spell and it only last for one hit. Someone might want to use it, but not me.

Wave Shield: (ACG) What a fascinating spell. It’s an immediate action DR/- or fire resistance vs one incoming attack. However the damage it blocks is weak, only protects against ONE attack and chews through spell slots like no one’s business. Might have a little more use with spontaneous caster, but wizards have better spells to be preparing.

Conjuration

Adhesive Spittle: (ACG) It’s a decent spell that does exactly what a tanglefoot bag does. In fact it’s better than a normal tanglefoot bag as it’s DC scales with your level unlike normal tanglefoot bags, and instead of making an attack roll with you ½ BAB the opponent must make a reflex save. Depending on what kind of enemy you’re facing (fliers), this can be a great spell. It’s also cheaper than a tanglefoot bag on a wand or even a scroll. If you can find uses with a tanglefoot bag, you’ll find uses for this spell.

Glue Seal: (ACG) It’s grease’s evil twin from a parallel dimension! No but seriously, this is anti-grease, though it does exactly what it does: battlefield control. It keeps the baddies from going anywhere while the fighter whacks at them. Like grease it also has alternate uses. The thought of gluing the anti-paladin’s sword to his sheathe before initiative rolls is a hilarious thought, and it brings a smile to my face to think of covering a wizard in glue before the tetori monk gives him a big old bear hug. Sadly the area is only a 5-foot square, but I still like it regardless.

Divination

Cultural Adaptation: (HoG) Ummm…. Situational at best?

Detect Metal: (PR) Well I never thought I would find a use for this spell. This spell is not blocked by the usual dense materials that stop detect spells (Stone, steel, lead, your mum) and can be used to sense if something wearing metal armor or carrying metal weapons is nearby. Still, like most detect spells that aren't at-will or used by summons, it’s really only useful as a consumable and not one of your precious spell slots.

Discern Next of Kin: (ACG) For all you lawyers and government employees who’re having a hard time tracking down next of kin, this one’s for you! Unless you’re going evil plot hunting in political campaigns, avoid.

Heightened Awareness: (ACG) What an interesting spell. No not the knowledge check bonus, take 10 already, I’m talking about the nice initiative bonus if you walk into combat with this on. If you suspect incoming encounters or know an enemy is behind the door, this could make an excellent pre-battle buff. The spell is decent as a memorized spell, but excellent as a scroll or potion, and a wand of this spell would be godly for those struggling with initiative.

Technomancy: (TG) Are you playing Iron Gods or some other advanced tech campaign? It’s alright, a combined “detect magic” and “identify” spell for tech, though sadly at first level spell slot. In any other campaign, would this spell even exist?

Enchantment

Keep Watch: (KoIS) Well… it’s useful if you manage not have an encounter the whole night. It’s a campsite buff spell and not even a very good one in fact, because if you have a just one campsite encounter, then it must be cast on you AGAIN, wasting spell slots or scrolls. HOWEVER, check with your GM and see if item crafting would fall under their definition of “vigorous activity”. If they don’t or there isn’t a ruling that proves me otherwise, you could potentially spend your 8 hours crafting magic items like scrolls or wondrous items while staying at a safe location, and still prepare spells and adventure in the morning. If not, then it’s just trash.

Memorize Page: (ACG) Situational at best. Would be interesting to see in an espionage campaign. Of course, so would a lot of spells.

Evocation

Gentle Breeze: (ACG) Another situational spell. Gases can be deadly, but they aren’t that common in the majority of campaigns and some are usually only for a round. Also, if you’re concerned about heat, endure elements.

Thunderstomp: (ACG) SUMO SLAM!!! Fun spell if you’ve ever wanted to trip as a wizard. Use with honor.

Illusion

Alter Musical Instrument: (ACG) If I was a bard, I might want to change my flute to sound like a Van Halen guitar solo for shits and giggles, but for wizards this is worth close to nothing.

Blurred Movement: (ACG) Just wait for Blur at the next spell level if you really want it.

Clarion Call: (KoIS) HEAR YE, HEAR YE!!! THIS SPELL HAS SOME FUN FLUFF AND RP USES, BUT MECHANICALLY IT DOES NOT MUCH ELSE!!!

Disguise Weapon: (ACG) Aha! You thought I was wielding a greatsword, when really you’ve been getting the crap beat out of you by a quarterstaff! … yeah no.

Necromancy

Bed of Iron: (KoIS) Well, if you’re expecting to be attacked in the middle of the night, might as well make sure the BSF sleeps comfortably in his full plate. A scroll spell for the paranoid adventurers at best.

Phantom Blood: (ACG) Would be nice to slap this on the barbarian, but unfortunately it’s a self spell only. Lame.

Repair Undead: (ACG) For the budding necromancer to keep his little pals “alive”. Niche use, but good for what it does, though you might want to get this as a wand. For Dhampir wizards this is especially blue, as you now have a reliable source of healing with going to a cleric for help.

Transmutation

Body Capacitance: (ACG) It’s like Shocking Grasp, but even worse.

Bouncy Body: (MC: Goblin) Look goblins, I know you don’t read, but please do at least read this. This spell is easily outclassed by grease and feather fall. Unless you really want to bounce when you fall off a cliff, please skip.

Blood Money: (RoRLAE) Okay, so after going over some forums for this spell and listening to all the rulings, arguments, and combinations for this spell I only have this to say… This spell is a munchkin’s wet dream. I don’t want to fill up an entire page on what this monstrosity can do, but I will give you 4 words to so you’ll have an idea: Diamond Free Wish Spell. If your GM allows this spell, he is a fool. If you are allowed this spell, TAKE IT!!! Hunt the Paizo forums for more information on this horror of a spell.

Emblazon Crest: (KoIS) So situational it hurts. Plus those commoners work hard to craft those coats of arms on shields and armors, and you just want to take that business away from them? Shame on you.

Endothermic Touch: (MC: Lizardfolk) Wow, another niche spell but if you can land this on a dragon it could really ruin their Sunday afternoon. Sadly it’s useless vs any creature but reptiles.

Long Arm: (ACG) Using your familiar or Spectral Hand are just better options who want to use melee touch attack at a range. Other classes might get a use out of it, but not you.

Mirror Polish: (ACG) This has some synergy with Mirror Hideaway (see below), where you can use it on a steel shield or a breastplate, as well as creatures with susceptability to mirrors (like vampires). However, I would rather just carry some armor polish and save myself the slot/scroll.

Monkey Fish: (ACG) Very useful utility spell as a wand or some scrolls to get past many low level obstacles. As an everyday prepared spell, only if you know for sure you’d be encountering this spell. Even then, just get a wand or scroll. Slowly goes down in rating as spells like Fly and other such spells are obtained, but it never becomes useless.

Poisoned Egg: (ISG) I find usually poison to be rather lame when used by PCs unless it’s a really good poison. This is not one of those.

Refine Improvised Weapon: (ACG) …. No. Well okay if you play around with improvised weapons then sure, but for the most part, no.

Sundering Shards: (ACG) Even if your BSF specializes in sundering, this is terrible.

Swift Girding: (KoIS) Well, if you’re expecting to be attacked in camp, this is a good way to get your paladin’s armor on and ready. Like Bed of Iron, it’s a scroll spell for the paranoid adventurers at best.

Transfer Tattoo: (ISM) Total munchkin abuse material. With a tattoo sorcerer or a wizard who took craft tattoo you can give the fighter some Enlarge Person tattoos and save some spell slots, among other interesting options. Talk with your GM first before attempting. But unless you’re going down that route, it’s trash.

2nd Level Wizard Spells

Abjuration

Bullet Ward: (ACG) A relatively expensive and niche spell, but man is that mental picture hilarious. You’re shooting down bullets with other goddamn bullets! Usefulness is solely dependent on the number of guns you face.

Crimson Confession: (ACG) You’ve been caught red handed! Not really useful unless you’re doing some sort of game of intrigue or something along those lines, then its usefulness increases.

Conjuration

Cloud of Seasickness: (AP:WWM) It’s Lesser Stinking Cloud. If you like cloud spells, you’ll be pleased by this.

Euphoric Cloud: (ACG) Hey, another cloud spell! This one can also be nice to use. I like to imagine casting this right before a Fireball and denying them a reflex save.

Mud Buddy: (MC: Goblins) Goblins rejoice, you’ve found a friend filthier than you are. It’s Unseen Servant, but seen. It can also do things Unseen Servant can’t, like trip your opponent.

River Whip: (ACG) Water Tribe! Sorry, focusing. Not the best spell honestly. I’m sure there are uses for it for the creative player, but not enough to warrant a better average rating.

Stone Discus: (ACG) Man, another spell that conjures images of the Last Airbender. Anyways, interesting spell. It’s like Scorching Ray, although it’s not vs touch AC. It’s above meh, but not entirely good either. Somewhere inbetween.

Whip of Spiders: (ACG) Another whip spell? Okay, so unless you have Weapon Finesse, you’re using your most likely dumped Str score and your awful BAB to hit, although the target being touch AC helps against this liability. A good majority of monsters are immune to poison, though the distraction effect and the ability to shift it into a swarm are nice. However, the use of this as a weapon still carries a serious problem of being in melee/charging distance of a creature, which goes against a wizard’s natural instinct to not get hit.

Divination

Carrion Compass: (USH) Now that’s a bizarre mental image. Unless you’re trying to find the town’s mystery necromancer, pass.

Determine Depth: (DBH) Engineer: “Finally, the spell for me!” Not great, might have a use somewhere, but not here.

Embrace Destiny: (LK) Umm… Could be useful if your luck is high, but is overall potential waste of slots.

Twisted Futures: (ACG) Niche use, but useful against those enemies who rely on luck to save them.

Enchantment

Arcane Disruption: (ACO) Very strong use against casters. Most enemies you fight don’t have good buffs on their concentration checks.

Investigative Mind: (ACG) A useful spell on days off, when you’re studying, crafting, or identifying, but not a spell for everyday use.

Touch of Mercy: (CoP) While the non-lethal damage is inconsequential apart for it getting healed doubled by healing magic,the real draw of this spell is causing said opponent to take a -4 on weapon attack rolls, which makes it a nice debuff for this spell level. Obviously not as effective against casters or anything immune to compulsion.

Evocation

Aggressive Thundercloud: (ACG) Hey look, it’s Flaming Sphere, but with lightning! It’s slower than flaming sphere, but it has the advantage of flight. If you like using Flaming Sphere, you’ll like this.

Burst of Radiance: (CoP) This is an excellent spell. It’s the Evocation cousin of Glitterdust. It blinds/dazzles on reflex saves and doesn’t allow for repeat saves to end it early. If you’re an evoker, you can use this instead of Glitterdust and apply your Spell Focus: Evocation feats to the DC. I’d love to see a metamagic evoker work their magic on this. The damage it deals is meh, but it’s something and also not the reason everybody should pick this spell.

Contingent Action: (ACG) Hmm, Lesser Contingency. Useful if you like to play around with stuff like that. Only last a minute per round though, so more useful as a buff than anything.

Ice Slick: (MC: Frost Giants) Hey I’m always happy to see more elemental spells to help flesh out those who want to focus on one element, and this one is pretty decent. It’s one round Grease with some damage, perfect for those evokers out there who want more damage spells with utility. Sadly reserved for frost giants, but see if you can convince you GM to let it slide. (Ha, puns. I’m so funny)

Sonic Scream: (ACG) Oooh sonic damage, that’s rare. Wait, what’s that at the bottom…  LORD HAVE MERCY!!! No spells with verbal components until all three uses of the spell have been used!? Well if you really want to do sonic damage, here you go. Just be sure you don’t have to cast anything else for 3 rounds.

Illusion

Silent Table: (ACG) A situational spell for it’s intended use, however this spell can be used to increase stealth checks. just cast this on your rogue’s armor and watch him sneak around the enemy base with silent delight as the DCs to hearing him jump up by 20. Nicely paired with invisibility for absolute sneaky, but without invisibility, it’s worth a little less than it. Even greater with Greater Invisibility, making a flying, invisible, muffed Wizard a force to be reckoned with.

Twilight Haze: (ACG) Does the same thing as Fog Cloud, but is unaffected by wind and being underwater. Useful in those situations, but I’d rather just cast Fog Cloud for the most part.

Necromancy

Blood in the Water: (MC: Sahaugin) Useful in an aquatic campaign if you have a shark on your team, but wouldn’t that cause the shark to come after you, not your enemies? Useless in other campaigns. Plus how likely are you to be a Sahaugin?

Companion Life Link: (ACG) Temporary Bracelet of Friendship, though you have no way of retrieving them.

Life Pact: (ACG) This could be so much more useful if only you could donate more than one friggin’ HP per creature.

Stricken Heart: (ACG) Melee touch attack spells make me sad. The staggered effect however is nice because there is no save. But if you fail the touch then you’re for a world of hurt. Nicer to use with a familiar.

Touch of Bloodletting: (ISG) Booo, bleed damage!

Transmutation

Adhesive Blood: (ACG) Hahaha, what!? Man if they fail the reflex save, that could make for a memorable moment in any campaign…. Of course this means getting into melee range, which is terrible for wizards.

Air Step: (ACG) Horizontal Levitate spell. Not as useful in combat per say, but plenty of utility.

Amplify Stench: (MC: Troglodyte) Listen, I know we smell, but wizards simply don’t have time to bathe when we’re busy warping all of reality- oh wait this is for troglodytes. Unless you’re a troglodytes, pass.

Angelic Aspect, Lesser: (CoP) Wow, two energy resistances AND protection from evil all in one spell? SOLD!

Blood Armor: (ACG) ….. Or just cast Mage Armor!

Carry Companion: (KoIS) I choose you, Charizard! Convince your party ranger to help you make friends with a bunch of animals, cast, and then use when needed. I can think of a lot of uses for having 6 horses in my pocket. Use this to help the Cavalier take his mount anywhere and make him LOVE you.

Eldritch Conduit: (ISM) It’s hard to rate this spell, as its potential is entirely based on how an individual uses it.

Extreme Flexibility: (ACG) For a wizard, this stinks. If you want +X bonuses to escape grapples, cast grease on yourself. You’re unlikely to grapple anything else and a +1 dodge bonus from a level 2 spell is just lame.

Fleshcurdle: (ISM) Gross, and it only allows for one of those debuffs.

Mirror Hideaway: (ACG) Neat, an alternate Rope Trick. A bit more difficult to hide the spell’s focus (a mirror) in a dungeon, but if you can find a place to stick it where it won’t look out of place or find a good sized mirror already around, this has just as many utilities as Rope Trick. (I wonder if you can use a BSFs steel shield for this?)

Molten Orb: (ACG) Ouch, molten metal all over you. Unfortunately this doesn’t seem to have the oomph we need in an attack spell.

Scale Spikes: (MC: Lizardfolk) Are you a dragon, reptile, or a creature with a natural armor bonus? No? Then this is pointless (haha, pun). Even if you were any of those, this still sucks.

Snow Shape: (HoG) Do you wanna build a snowman~? Unless you’re playing an arctic campaign, this is not likely to get much milage. If you are, then please do consider.

Steal Size: (GHH) Well, if you were a melee fighter, this would be pretty cool. But, you’re not.

Tattoo Potion: (ISM) OR JUST DRINK THE POTION NORMALLY!!!

Telekinetic Volley: (RTT) *Sigh* if you’re honestly that desperate….

Time Shudder: (ACG) Man, talk about a gamble. Potential haste or slow for anyone around you every round. Too big a gamble for my tastes.

Tremor Blast: (ISG) Wait, didn’t I already cover Thunderstomp at a lower level?

3rd Level Wizard Spells

Abjuration

Blade Snare: (ISG) Neat spell, but once again it’s one that requires you to be butt-clenching close to the opposing melee fighter.

Shield Companion: (ACG) If you use you familiar as a touch spell delivery, this can help improve its survivability so you don’t have to go through the expensive replacement process (and of course the emotional trauma… maybe). Otherwise, don’t bother scribing it into your spellbook.

Stunning Barrier, Greater: (ACG) Same as its previous version, only a little better for the stun duration, but at this point you will most likely have rings of protect, cloaks of resistance, and other such items to give you those protection bonuses.

Conjuration

Force Anchor: (USH) Interesting spell if you want to slow a retreating enemy down, but not fantastic otherwise.

Ice Spears: (ISM) Decent spell, okay damage and a trip effect. Plus it’s another spell for those who specialize in Conjuration to get use out of the Spell Focus Conjuration feat. Becomes blue if you’re a fan of metamagic and Dazing Spell to make a nasty SR-proof dazing AoE.

Nauseating Trail: (ACG) Nice spell, but answer me this before you proceed.……Are you farting during this spell?

Silver Darts: (ACG) You can do better than this.

Storm Step: (BoE) Just cast Lightning Bolt, unless you really need to be over there now.

Summon Totem Creature: (HoG) Nothing great, more can be accomplished with Summon Monster III.

Sundered Serpent Coil: (MC: Serpentfolk) Single target Black Tentacles. Decent, but limited to serpentfolk unless you beg your GM for it.

Swarm of Fangs: (MC Troglodytes) Cool swarm spell, but limited to Troglodytes. Ask GM.

Divination

Aura Sight: (ACG) If you really need to now everyone’s alignment in the room all at once.

Discern Value: (DHB) Okay so, like Identify, this is a downtime spell. If you’re sitting on a dead dragon’s hoard and you have no immediate need to leave, take some time and find the most valuable items to stuff into your bag of holding. Outside of downtime or if you have some sucker who’s investing high skill points into appraise, this spell is lame as hell.

Pierce Disguise: (ACG) True Seeing for magical disguises and polymorph spells. Cool, but niche.

Enchantment

Burdened Thoughts: (GHH) “Let go your earthly tether…” Sorry, the references are getting too easy. This is a pretty nice spell to pull out on fliers, as many BSF classes will not have thought to bring a ranged option. This makes it easier for them to do what they do best.

Charitable Impulse: (CotR) Ahaha! Lord have mercy, I know this is a save or suck, but if they fail their save they’re screwed! Drop this on an enemy non-caster and the battle is as good as over for them.

Mindlocked Messenger: (ACG) Now, this isn’t a spell you’re going to use often, but I’ll be damned if this couldn’t have some use in a political intrigue campaign or something.

Pugwampi’s Grace: (ISM) An interesting debuff that can really ruin someone’s day if it lands. Sadly inferior to Accursed Glare which does the almost the same thing, but with a longer duration and no creature limitation.

Evocation

Air Geyser: (ACG) So if I’m reading this right, then in say a 30 ft high room I cast this and deal 2d6 damage, then they go sailing up and hit the ceiling for 3d6 (1d6 per 10 ft) and then fall back down and hit the ground for another 3d6? That’s some Looney Tunes style combat right there. If nothing else, it’s a hilarious way to make an enemy prone.

Thunderstomp, Greater: (ACG) See Thunderstomp above.

Trial of Fire and Acid: (MC: Troll) Ironically a spell used exclusively by trolls is also a spell that could ruin their day if used by adventurers. Granted the same effect on trolls could also be accomplished by a fireball, and it should since this spell bites.

Vengeful Comets: (ISM) A nice spell to have. If you are hit by an enemy’s spell, these comets with strike them back with a ranged touch attack, no saving throw. Now if only these also retaliated against non-magical attacks, then they would be even better.

Illusion

Adjustable Disguise: (ACG) Disguise Self, but change be changed to a new disguise as a swift action. If you find yourself needing Disguise Self often, then you may find a use for this.

Isolate: (MC: Bugbear) I suppose this could be useful against an enemy bard, but in my experience enemy bards aren’t that frequent. It’s also only available to Bugbears.

Wall of Nausea: (ACG) Wow, this is a nice wall. I mean it’s not going to prevent creatures from coming through, but if they do they may become vulnerable.

Necromancy

Accursed Glare: (BotM) An interesting debuff that can really ruin someone’s day if it lands. Superior to Pugwampi’s Grace, as it lasts a day per level, has no creature limitation and an ally can help you overcome SR through an intimidate check. The 1 round casting time hurts it’s rating though. Still, a really nasty debuff if it lands.

Aura of Cannibalism: (ACG) Not bad. It’s basically an aura of Vampiric Touch that also bestows additional buffs the more HP you drain. Although the 20 ft. brings you pretty close to the combat.

Barrow Haze: (ACG) You know, I was about to question why a spell for hexes was on the wizard spell list, but then I found the spirit whisperer archtype. This spell is superb for that archtype and witches, but to the “average” wizard this spell is useless.

Hydrophobia: (MM) …Sorry what? I was too busy chuckling at the thought of casting this on an Aboleth and watching it rocket up to the surface to beach itself. Situational spell, but I’ll be damned if this isn’t a fun one for aquatic campaigns.

Unliving Rage: (ACG) http://paizo.com/threads/rzs2rcio?Advanced-Class-Guide-Potential-Errors#19

*The above link confirms that the undead’s bonus to Con is actually converted to Cha for the purpose of determining HP and Fort saves.* This is an excellent spell for the budding necromancer to make their undead minions into much stronger BSFs. Useless for anyone else not using undead buddies.

Transmutation

Air Breathing: (MC: Sahuagin) Unless you’re a Sahuagin invading the land or your GM allows a non-Sahuagin to cast it to let a fish to explore the wonders of the land, skip.

Anchored Step: (ACG) So situational it hurts.

Serren’s Armor Lock: (KoIS) Fun debuff to land on those heavy tanks. Anything lighter, not as much.

Blast Barrier: (ISM) An okay but short-lived wall that ends with a bang. Literally. However the damage is so-so and only hits those adjacent to the wall.

Blood Sentinel: (ACG) For those wizards without familiars that want a scout or touch spell buddy, although the Int drain on you when it dies seriously outweighs its benefits in my book. Only ok in my book due to the steep risk.

Control Vermin: (MC: Duegar) Blegh. If you ever wanted to tame giant spiders… better to let the Duegar keep this spell.

Disable Construct: (ACG) Save or suck vs constructs. It’s also a melee touch attack, which is gross. I will admit it’s brutal enough that when used against it’s intended foe it warrants a green or even blue rating, but I try not to rate niche spells very highly. Get a scroll if you need it.

Heart of the Metal: (ACG) Decent buff if you’re fighting creatures weak to those metals and you don’t want to spend the money or deal with the drawbacks of weapons made of those materials. Loses use as you level up and get +3 weapons that overcome that anyways.

Hollow Blades: (GHH) An okay debuff to land on the enemy BSF. There are better debuffs though.

Imbue With Addiction: (MM) How to be a jerk 101: cast this spell on some guy. More of a bad guy NPC spell than anything, like bleed.

Monstrous Extremities: (ACG) Yikes, One minute casting time for a debuff? No thank you.

Polymorph Familiar: (ACG) I feel my raven familiar would do much better right now as a fish while we swim to the aboleth’s lair. It’s alright, it’s rating is dependent on how much you use your familiar and how creative you are with it.

Scale Spikes, Greater: (ACG) Like its predecessor, it’s just not that great. However if you got someone that meets the specific requirements and they’re going down the grappling route, it’s just a tiny smidgen better. Although they may just invest in armor spikes anyways.

4th Level Wizard Spells

Abjuration

Enchantment Foil: (ACG) A nice spell if you’re going up against Enchantypants McGnomington, but probably won’t see too much use.

Spellcrash, Lesser: (ACG) Terrible, just absolute trash. It targets will saves for a spell designed to be used on CASTERS and then gives them a choice of losing a spell of a lower level than the one you’re casting. It has a rating of brown for what it’s worth.

Suppress Primal Magic: (ISM) Too rare a situation to be considered more than a scroll at best.

Ward Shield: (KoIS) A nice buff to cast on your BSF with a shield. The SR is alright, but the real bread winner is that +5 to reflex saves against spells like fireball.

Conjuration

Conjure Deadfall: (DHB) Oooh, so close. If only you could do half damage on successful save. Alas, this awesome sounding spell out of a Mario game is a save or suck, and not even a very good one.

Enchantment

Antithetical Constraint: (CoB) Well, if you know the exact alignment of what you’re fighting and no one is the exact opposite of it, this is an excellent spell. Although you must account for the variables of your party alignment composition and what you’re fighting against.

Symbol of Laughter: (ACG) Symbols? Ha! Don’t make me laugh!

Triggered Suggestion: (ACG) The KGB strikes again! Your mind is already reeling from the possibilities of this spell in your campaign; it will be useful in your campaign. Although it probably won’t get to much use in others.

Evocation

Aggressive Thundercloud, Greater: (ACG) See Aggressive Thundercloud above.

Contingent Scroll: (ACG) ….Well if you really can’t wait for Contingency I guess it’s alright. My only beef with it is that it’s duration is only an hour per level. But if you can work around that, go right ahead.

Creeping Ice: (ACG) The only real use I can think of for this is to create temporary bridges across rivers. Your GM may rule that it has an effect in combat, but overall it just doesn’t seem that useful to me.

Flaming Sphere, Greater: (ACG) Well if anyone was sad to see their precious Flaming Sphere spell drop off in use, here’s more of the same spell you love.

Unbearable Brightness: (ACG) A nice counter for magical darkness and blinding enemies with light sensitivity. Be careful when casting this around your own teammates though.

Shadow Barbs: (ISM) Well if you ever wanted a wizard with a spiked chain, here you go. But you already know my disdain for being in melee range. Possibilities with other classes, not with this one.

Necromancy

Aggravate Affliction: (AP82) Welcome back to How to be a Jerk 101: Cause the little boy to die of cancer sooner.

Bloatbomb: (ACG) Disgusting…. And lame.

Bloody Arrows: (RTT) Booo, bleed damage. Well alright this one is cumulative, but even then bleed isn’t the most effective way to make something dead. Plus you need an archer on the team.

Sadomasochism: (ISG) Kinky~. Sadly you don’t have the HP nor the personal combat prowess to make this effective. Plus it goes against the natural wizard instinct to NOT GET HIT.

Wall of Blindness/Deafness: (ACG) A nice wall that has the potential to permanently blind or deafen someone who dares to pass its border. I mean yes, it’s a save or suck, but if you have to choose it’s a way better wall spell at this level than Wall of Fire in my opinion. The blindness version loses out terribly once Wall of Light comes into the club though.

Transmutation

Abyssal Vermin: (AP78) How often do you fight good creatures in most campaigns? It’s alright if you can get it on a decent swarm and you’re fighting good, but unlikely to see decent use in most campaigns.

Adjustable Polymorph: (ACG) Like Adjustable Disguise, but with Alter Self. If you can find plenty of uses for Alter Self, you should be pleased with this spell for your particular campaign.

Conversing Wind: (RTT) Now you can share battle plans mid-combat without it being considered metagaming.

Create Holds: (DHB) Excellent for its purpose, but don’t you already have Fly from last spell level though?

Curse of Burning Sleep: (ACG) Wow, this is a hilarious spell for bad guys to cast. Hell, if you know they can take it, cast it on an annoying teammate. May be useful in some sort of campaign, but as a combat spell, pass.

Eyes of the Void: (ACG) Ultimate Darkvision; for when you really just want to give Deeper Darkness the finger.

Mirror Transport: (ACG) Might be fun for someone in a funhouse of mirrors, but otherwise highly situational.

5th Level Wizard Spells

Abjuration

Calm Air: (RTT) An okay cloud spell disperser. Not bad, not great, just okay.

Siphon Magic: (ISM) I am conflicted. This is a melee touch spell, which I usually hate, but it has no save and no SR, so the thought of going up to an enemy caster and swiping his mage armor is quite tempting. If you use a familiar to deliver touch attacks and you’re able to ID the buffs on an enemy, this is good. Otherwise it’s okay, but kind of risky.

Spell Absorption: (ISM) I think this will get more use for an Arcanist, since they are much better counterspellers than wizards. Even then it’s just not that great unless you know you’re going to be spending an entire combat session counterspelling an enemy caster.

Spellsteal: (MC: Serpentfolk) Are you a serpentfolk? No? Move along. If your GM allows non-serpentfolk to cast it, it’s very bad, for the same reason Spellcrash is bad: Will save, opponent’s choice of spell, and only targets lower level spells. Welcome to the brown layer.

Conjuration

Summon Lesser Psychopomp: (AP80) These are okay little critters to flood the battle field, but I’d rather just summon a bralani at this level.

Whip of Centipedes: (ACG) See Whip of Spiders.

Evocation

Augmenting Wall: (RTT) Huh, odd little spell. Tiny elemental damage boost for ranged attackers. Okay at best vs creatures with elemental vulnerabilities and you have a ranged attacker, but you have way better spells to be casting at this level.

Runic Overload: (GHH) Ugh. You would need a lot of set up to use its secondary use to only so-so damage at best, while it’s primary use against  the rare rune giant is okay, but limited to those and other runed creatures. If you’re playing Rise of the Runelords and are an evoker, maybe you’ll get SOMETHING out of it.

Wall of Light: (PM:TDD) Wow, a wall that blinds anything that passes through with no save, just SR? Hot damn, I’m in love!

Necromancy

Blood Boil: (MM) Ew. Also ew, it’s a melee touch spell. I will admit though if you land this it does a harsh amount of damage.

Feast on Fear: (ACG) A lot of creatures are immune to fear though, and I’d rather cast Aura of Cannibalism for a better benefit at a lower spell level.

Ghoul Army: (ISM) I like this spell. Summons multiple critters that can potentially paralyze your foe with having to pay for a Create Undead spell. Not amazing per say, but nice. Loses effectiveness quickly as you level up though.

Repair Undead, Mass: (ACG) Like Repair Undead before it, this spell is excellent for your undead army healthy and strong. If you don’t use undead then this is a big fat avoid.

Vampiric Shadow Shield: (ACG) You know by now how loathe I am to enter melee range. However, this spell is a nice buff against the proper melee opponent. It deals damage for you with no save and heals you a little bit. Beware undead and reach weapons with this though.

Transmutation

Angelic Aspect: (CoP) It’s so beautiful! You get Darkvision, Resist Energy x2, damage reduction, and flight! That’s five buffs in one spell!

Caustic Blood: (ISG) Gross, another spell that requires you to get hit to work. Nice if you’re going up against something with acid vulnerability, but again, it requires you to get hit.

Geniekind: (ISM) Damn, this is a nice buff spell! You get AC, HP, and a resistance and movement speed of your choice. The rest is just icing on the cake.

Gift of the Deep: (MC: Sahuagin) Unless you’re a Sahuagin, why would you even want to use this? Maybe if you have a bunch of them as minions, that’s not likely to happen in most campaigns. If it does, it’s great.

Hungry Earth: (MC: Ghoul) Wow, beware ghoul spellcasters from now on. This is basically alternate Black Tentacles. If you like that spell (I certainly do) and you convince your GM to let you have this, you’ll like this.

Lighten Object, Mass: (FoB) Lame. Maybe if you wanted to carry a lot of heavy items, but we have bags of holding and Ant Haul for that.

Transplant Visage: (ISG) Definitely has some uses for impersonating specific people, perhaps in a political campaign or something. Won’t see too much use outside of that though.

6th Level Wizard Spells

Abjuration

Dust Ward: (MC: Duegar) Once more a spell more useful in the hands of bad guys than good guys, this one being exclusive to Duegar. It might have been more useful to beg your GM for, if it were not for the magic item CL limit.

Spellcrash: (ACG) Still shit.

Conjuration

Chains of Light: (CoP) Land this on an opponent, then have the BSF Coup de Grace them. No SR and only a reflex save with a built in dimensional anchor makes this spell godly.

Summon Vanth: (AP80) It’s alright, but I’d rather prepare Summon Monster VI and have the versatility on what I summon.

Whip of Ants: (ACG) See Whip of Spiders.

Enchantment

Unconscious Agenda: (RoRLAE) Can’t you just cast Dominate Person instead? Yet another spell that might have good uses in this political subterfuge campaign I keep on wistfully dreaming about.

Evocation

Elemental Assessor: (CotR) Wow, I hope you evokers out there are looking at this. Here’s a spell that deals 2d6 damage each of fire, lightning, cold, and acid all at once with a ranged touch attack AND whichever element deals the most damage deals additional 4d6 damage for 1d4 rounds afterwards. Not as great for those not focusing on Evocation spells, but damn, this is a fine ass spell for evokers if you’re unsure of an enemy’s elemental resistances or just want to seriously screw with something with elemental vulnerabilities for several rounds after..

Necromancy

Banshee Blast: (ACG) Meh, better for necromancers who want a blast spell for whatever reason. Even then it’s two saving throws with SR on top of that.

Transmutation

Eldritch Conduit, Greater: (ISM) See Eldritch Conduit.

Impart Mind: (ISM) If you really want to pay out the nose for a temporarily intelligent magic item, go ahead. I’m not.

Sabotage Construct: (PR) Ugh, another construct targeted spell that requires you to go against its good save and hit it with a melee touch attack. Not to mention it has spell resistance and the majority of constructs have immunity to magic.

Sonic Form: (ACG) Oh boy, you can be incorporeal, but you can’t pass through solid objects…. There are better self buffs than this.

7th Level Wizard Spells

Abjuration

Magnetic Field: (PR) …. Why would you do this?

Conjuration

Dimensional Bounce: (ACG) This is a good spell if you’re in need of escape routes. Just teleport to someone’s side when you’re being attacked and let them protect you.

Evocation

Archon's Trumpet: (CoP) Excellent debuff, though being save or suck prevents it from being as good as it could be. Still better than Power Word Stun. It’s also better as an evoker.

Hungry Darkness: (ISM) Ouch, continual Con damage in a 60 ft. radius with no saving throws. Deeper Darkness thrown in is just the icing on the cake.

Necromancy

Create Variant Mummy: (AP81) I guess if you’re in an Egyptian themed campaign? It’s too bad Create Undead is still pretty risky to use and this is at a higher level than it with less options.

8th Level Wizard Spells

Abjuration

Spell Absorption, Greater: (ISM) See Spell Absorption.

Spellscar: (ISM) The unpredictability of this spell make it close to impossible to rate properly. It could give bonuses to casters, it could give penalties, but it’s entirely random so it’s not very useful as a dependable debuff on others or a buff on yourself. If you feel lady luck is on your side, go right ahead.

Spellcrash, Greater : (ACG) Congratulations Spellcrash, every single version of you has earned the right to be called one of the worst spells in Pathfinder. Good job, bravo, I wish you the best and while you’re at it, sod off.

Conjuration

Rain of Arrows: (RTT) “Then we will fight in the shade!” Fun spell. Not fantastic, but it’s something to cast on groups of lower level mooks.

Transmutation

Angelic Aspect, Greater: (CoP) *Ode to Joy* I may have found one of my favorite buff spell lines ever while doing this guide. Darkvision, DR, two elemental immunities plus immunity to petrifaction, flight, and a combined +4 Magic Circle Against Evil and Lesser Globe of Invulnerability. The rest is just butter-cream icing on this glorious angel-food cake. Neon blue for its glory and greatness!

9th Level Wizard Spells

Abjuration

Spellbane: (ACG) …Dang, this is a nice spell to end on. It’s a selective Antimagic Field that can stop any magic short of artifacts and gods. Granted you won’t be able to cast the spells you select as well, the spell is extremely potent with the right planning. It’s very difficult not to recommend this.
