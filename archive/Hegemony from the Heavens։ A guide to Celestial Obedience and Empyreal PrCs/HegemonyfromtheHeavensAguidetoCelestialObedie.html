        The Pathfinder tabletop adventure game has a lot of deities to follow, but one of the largest groups of faithful figures to follow are the Empyreal Lords. These all-good gals, guys and otherwise are at the forefront of the eternal battle against evil.

        For a lot of players, playing as good characters is a pretty common path, and often more preferable than neutral or evil aligned characters. Naturally, good characters might want to pick a good deity to follow, and what better group to follow than that of the biggest holy pantheon in the setting?

        Unfortunately, some sacrosanct selections… suck. There are a fair bit of trap options, whether they be underpowered or just flat-out weird to worship. This is why this guide was created, to assist other players in making educated decisions for divine and divine-influenced characters. I’ll also be taking a look at the six empyreal lord inspired prestige classes in order to assess their abilities.

        I’ll be using a simple star rating system for my grading scale. The reason why I’m not using the popular color based scale that Treantmonk used is because some people might be colorblind and I don’t want to exclude them due to crap genetics.

The guide is as follows:

(*)= Total crap. If you can avoid it, do so.

(**)= Mediocre, or useful, but in very specific circumstances.

(***)= Good to have. Something might do a better job, but it’s still nice regardless.

(****)= The best of the best. This can definitely help you no matter what you build your character as.

Mystery Cultist

        The Mystery Cultist is prestige class that was released through the Pathfinder campaign setting sourcebook, “Chronicle of the Righteous”, in 2013. In order to qualify for the prestige class, you need to possess the following:

  • Any Good Alignment
  • The Celestial Obedience feat, which requires 3 ranks in Knowledge (Planes) to take.
  • 7 ranks in Knowledge (Religion)
  • Worship an empyreal lord
  • Be either personally saved from death by a celestial of CR 5, or gain a beneficial buff or effect from a celestial of at least CR 10.

        So you need to be at least 7th level to access the prestige class. It takes a while to be able to take that class, but in taking it, you gain the following abilities:

  • D8 hit die (**): The standard hit die for divine classes. This makes you more squishy on the frontlines, but it’s still better than the d6.
  • 2 skill points per level (*): The worst base skill points you can have. If you dumped intelligence, expect getting the minimum of one skill per level.
  • Class Skills (**): You barely get any, but most of them are good. You do get several face skills (Diplomacy, Intimidation, Sense Motive) and a few good knowledge skills (Planes, Religion, Spellcraft). Heal is the one skill on the list that you can safely skip.
  • 9/10 spellcaster (***-****): While you do not need to be a spellcaster to gain entry to this prestige class, the fact that it is there helps plenty of casters greatly. The one lost caster level kind of hurts, though. The real benefit is in the details; Mystery Cultists, while having the divine theme, aren’t relegated to divine casters. This means that arcane classes such as Wizard, Sorceror, or Witch can benefit from the prestige class.
  • 3/4 BAB (**-***): As mentioned above, the prestige class is more attuned to casters. Full BAB classes like Barbarians and Fighters are still capable of taking the class with the right skills, but the loss in BAB will hurt. Arcane casters, on the other hand, have ½ BAB and will benefit from the increase in BAB.
  • Simple Weapon and Favored Weapon Proficiency (**-****): Probably already covered by many classes, but some, such as the Wizard and the Druid, markedly do not possess complete simple weapon proficiency. The real thing to keep track of is the favored weapon proficiency. Each diety possess a “favored weapon” that their followers are proficient with. While many of them are proficient with simple weapons, some offer proficiency with martial or even exotic weapons, allowing for more martial flexibility.
  • Bad Reflex and Fortitude Saves, Good Will Saves (*-**): There’s a popular saying about the saves: “Failing reflex hurts you. Failing fortitude kills you. Failing will kills the whole party.” While not making two saves sucks, being safe against the third is nice, especially since missing that save can result in even more worse things than just simply killing you. Spellcasters usually have good will saves, though.
  • Blessed (**): You have to die for it to work, but if you are resurrected, you can come back from the afterlife with a few answered questions a la the Contact Other Plane spell, but better. Good for RP, not so much in practicality.
  • Obedience Boons (****): This is probably why you took the prestige class. While Celestial Obedience can work just fine without the class, you have to wait until 12th, 16th, and 20th level for the first, second, and third boons respectively. By taking the class, however, you can gain the boons as early as 10th, 13th, and 16th level. In regards to the third boon, you might be able to access 9th level spells, depending on which lord you worship.
  • Glorious Aura (Su) (**): At 2nd level, you gain an aura that, upon activation, boosts your charisma and frightens enemies within 10 feet of you. Unfortunately, while the charisma bonus is a decent size (+4), only certain characters can make good use of it, mainly classes that key off of charisma in the first place. The frightening effect also requires a good charisma for the DC, which some characters might not even make use of, even with the charisma buff. The low number of uses per day, based on Mystery Cultist levels, is also aggravating.
  • Brand of Healing (Sp) (****): At 4th level you get Heal as an SLA. Nice for when somebody needs it.
  • Summon Celestial (Sp) (****): You can summon celestial creatures once per day at 4th level, and the strength of the monsters scales with your level. Summoning is always considered a powerful effect, but it can mess with the flow of battle. Always check with your teammates and GM before you summon to see if they’re cool with it.
  • Fervor (Su) (****): At 5th level, you can enter a sort of holy rage that boosts your attack and damage rolls, caster levels in regards to certain effects, gives you DR 5/Evil, and boosts your move speed for rounds per character level. Very nice to have on the battlefield, but there’s a catch: while you’re in fervor or once you’ve come out of it, you take a hit to charisma and charisma based skill checks. As long as you aren’t pulling face duties after a battle, you’ll be fine.
  • Incorruptible (Su) (**): At 7th level, you gain bonuses on saves against disease and poison. While situational, both effects can be potentially nasty to suffer from. The real kicker here is the fact that your body can now be resurrected at any time and can’t be turned into an undead creature. Which is nice and all, but this ability is more of a contingency than anything.
  • Sacred Haven (Su) (**): As a capstone, this works more thematically than statistically. It’s a glorified Mage’s Magnificent Mansion with built in Portable Holes in every room… but it’s also in a very good place to be (Heaven, Nirvana, or Elysium, based on your deity’s alignment).

        Verdict: The Mystery Cultist is a good all-rounder, but especially for casters. There are a lot of thematic abilities that don’t offer much gameplay wise, such as Blessed and Sacred Haven, but the early access to empyreal lord boons and divine SLAs is a great tool to have.

        

Empyreal Prestige Classes        

        On top of the above PrC, the Pathfinder Player Companion, “Paths of the Righteous” released five more prestige classes related to the empyreal lords. This time, however, they are each tied to a specific lord, which grants them unique and thematic bonuses.

Ashavic Dancer

Requirements:

  • Neutral Good or Chaotic Good alignment
  • You must worship Ashava
  • 3 ranks in Knowledge (Religion) and 6 ranks in Perform (Dance)
  • Possess the Ghost Whisperer feat, which requires the ability to cast 3rd level spells
  • The ability to cast 2nd level spells

Abilities:

  • 3/4 BAB (**-***)
  • 9/10 Spellcasting (***)
  • 4 Skill Points per Level (**)
  • Class Skills: This list is all over the place. You get Diplomacy and Sense Motive, which can make you a good face. You also get Knowledge (Religion and Planes) for librarian needs. Perception is always useful, and Acrobatics can get you out of a lot of situations. Survival and Fly are meh. Perform (Dance) seems like a wash at first glance, but proves to be useful later on.
  • Good Reflex and Will saves, Bad Fortitude (**)
  • Ashavic Dance (***): This works like the Bard’s Bardic Performance ability. While it takes a move action to start up, this gets lowered to a swift action at 7th level. Through this ability, you can utilize several abilities that hinder undead enemies, although this does place strategic limitations. The real benefit is how this stacks with the Bard’s Bardic Performance abilities. Note that all of these abilities DCs are based off of charisma, so classes that utilize that stat will make the most out of this ability.
  • Deathwalking Dance (*): Gained at 1st level. Deals damage to haunts and undead equal to 1d6 + your charisma modifier. Will save negates the damage. This is not the best way to spend your performance rounds.
  • Untethering Steps (*): Gained at 3rd level. By making a successful Perform (Dance) check against their CMD, you can make one or more undead sickened for one round. If the check exceeds their CMD by 10 or more, they become staggered for one round as well. This is a great way to eat up your performance rounds and enemy CMDs only get stronger with each level. Mediocre at best.
  • Soothing Steps (**): Gained at 6th level. Same as the Bard’s ability. Mass Cure Serious Wounds and removes minor annoying status effects. Could be useful in a pinch, but it eats up 4 rounds of performance.
  • Exorcist’s Waltz (***): Gained at 7th level, this allows you to end any nearby compulsion or possession effects, acting as Break Enchantment for compulsions and granting a new saving throw to the victim for possessions. If the possession effect ends, the victim is immune to further possession effects from the original source for 24 hours. Mind-Affecting effects can get people killed, so this is a good defense against them.
  • True Spark’s Guidance (***): At 10th level, you can target one undead and deal 10 damage per Ashavic Dancer level you have. They get a will save to negate the damage, but if they pass, they still have all of their move speeds reduced. It’s a lot of damage, but other than that, it’s okay.
  • Ashavic Rites (****): At 2nd level, an Ashavic Dancer’s performances automatically get past the undead’s immunity to mind-affecting effects. That’s not why I’m giving this ability four stars. If you spend a round of bardic performance, the immunity bypass also applies to any spell that you cast. This is huge for spellcasters.
  • Peerless Dancer (****): At 2nd level, you gain bardic versatility with the Perform (Dance) skill which allows you to put ranks into Acrobatics and Fly on a 2-for-1 deal. It gets better at 6th, 8th and 10th level, since you can add more skills to the Perform (Dance) skill. Namely, these are all the face skills (Bluff, Diplomacy, Intimidate, and Sense Motive), which makes you an excellent face. All in all, a minimum of 5 skills for one rank is a great deal.
  • Ghostly Grace (****): At 4th level, all of your weapon attacks and spells can affect incorporeal foes without fail. This ability extends to your allies at 9th level. This essentially means you can skimp out on spending a metamagic feat or weapon enhancement for little personal cost!

Verdict:

While the Ashavic Dances are underwhelming, this is a decent PrC to enter if you are in undead heavy campaigns. This is great for team players as well, since abilities like Peerless Dancer and Ghostly Grace makes them excellent skill monkeys and buffers. Any class with the bardic performance ability can use this, not just bards.

Crimson Templar

Requirements:

  • Lawful Good
  • Must worship Ragathiel
  • +6 BAB
  • Bastard Sword proficiency
  • Possess the Power Attack feat, which requires 13 Strength and +1 BAB
  • Possess the Vital Strike feat, which requires +6 BAB

Abilities

  • D10 Hit Dice (***): Standard for front-line fighters.
  • 2 Skill Points Per Level (*): Lowest skills per level possible.
  • Skill List (**-***): Surprisingly, you have most of the face skills, only missing Diplomacy. You have access to Knowledge (Religion and Planes) as well as spellcraft, which can be helpful in a fight. Perception is always good. Fly, Heal, and Survival are meh, but Disguise can be useful for a face. You’re missing Stealth for your sneak attack, though.
  • Good Reflex and Fortitude Saves, Bad Will Save (***): While bad will saves are dangerous for a frontline fighter, good reflex and fortitude saves are great for avoiding damage.
  • Full BAB (****): As it should be for a martial class!
  • No Caster Levels (*): Also to be expected of a martial class, unfortunately.
  • Fiendish Studies (****): You can study an evil outsider as a move action, gaining a bonus to skill checks, attack and damage rolls and Crimson Templar ability DCs against that outsider. The activating action can be reduced to a swift action at 2nd level. At 5th and 10th level, you can study another target. Since the bonuses scale, this is a great ability. It also stacks with the Slayer’s Studied Target ability.
  • Obedience (*-****): You get Deific or Celestial Obedience as a bonus feat at first level. If you already have it, you get a bonus combat feat. Free feats are always nice. Ragathiel’s daily obedience still sucks, though.
  • Shield of Wings (****): This starts out at 1st level as fire resistance 5 which increases in strength at 3rd, 6th and 9th level, ultimately reaching full fire immunity. Since fire is really common, this is great to have. But then you can voluntarily lose the fire resistance as a swift action to turn it into a flight speed. You lose the fire resistance for a few minutes afterwards as well, but flight is always nice to have.
  • Ruthlessness (****): At 2nd level, when you coup de grace with a bastard sword, it only takes a standard action instead of a full-round action. This can free up your action economy a lot.
  • Sneak Attack (***): It’s a little late to gain this, and you don’t gain a lot to begin with, but extra damage is always nice. Since this stacks with other sources of sneak attack damage, consider classes such as the Rogue or Slayer that also gain sneak attack.
  • Bonus Combat Feats (****): You gain them at 3rd, 6th and 9th level. Again, free feats are always nice to have.
  • Obedience Boons (****): While you can enter this PrC earlier than Mystery Cultist, the boons are received a level later than when the MC would get them as well. This means you effectively get them at the same time as MCs do. Ragathiel still has a lot of great boons.
  • Heavenly Fire (**-***): When you sneak attack an enemy you’ve studied at 4th level, you deal extra damage based on your Crimson Templar level. This damage is half fire, which is commonly and easily resisted, and half holy, which is unblockable. The enemy does get a fortitude save (a commonly good enemy save) to halve the damage, and the save DC is based on your charisma, which you might have dumped. Extra damage is extra damage, but there are a ton of caveats here.
  • Immolate (***): Whenever you reduce a foe to negative hit points through sneak attacks or coup de grace at 7th level, the enemy must make a fort save or get disintegrated. It’s a little overkill, but it can prevent resurrection. Note that this doesn’t work on non-evil characters.

Verdict:

        Crimson Templar is a great martial class, especially in evil outsider heavy adventures. While the ability DCs are based on what is commonly considered a dump stat for pure martials, it can assist you with your skills and possibly help you be a good face. The templar’s abilities are remarkably similar to the slayer’s, so this is a good recommended class to build off of.

Runeguard

Requirements:

  • Any good alignment
  • Must worship Soralyon
  • 5 ranks in Linguistics and Spellcraft
  • Must possess the Scribe Scroll feat
  • Must speak Thassillonian
  • Must be able to cast 3rd level spells

Abilities:

  • D6 Hit Die (*): Lowest hit die possible. Brace for the squishiness.
  • 2 Skill Points per Level (*): Lowest skill points possible.
  • Skill List: You have all knowledge skills taken individually, plus Spellcrafting and Use Magic Device, which is natural for a lot of spellcasters. Appraise, Heal, and Linguistics are rather weak, although Heal gets a boost from the Kindness rune.
  • Bad Reflex and Fortitude Saves, Good Will Saves (**): Since you’re not a frontline fighter with this PrC and spellcasters usually have good will saves, nothing has changed. Traps, poisons and relevant spells are particularly dangerous to you.
  • 1/2 BAB (*): You’re a caster. Stick to the back rows and leave the melee work to everyone else.
  • 9/10 Spellcasting (***-****): While losing a caster level is always weak, you can easily regain this with the Prestigious Spellcaster feat. It’s up to you if you want to burn two feats to get it, though.
  • Rune Magic (*-****): While the Glyph and Rune type of spells tend to be on a lot of spell lists, the fact that you get them for free is still nice. This goes doubly for spontaneous casters or classes that don’t get Glyph or Rune spells. Since you get them at 1st level, these might be good for a dip. They are expensive as all hell, though.
  • Virtuous Runes (***-****): Don’t worry about wondering which ones you should get, you learn all of the runes over time as you gain more runeguard levels. You can use any rune you want, but only for a number of times per day equal to your runeguard level.
  • Charity (****): You can give an ally the effects of any abjuration spell you know or have prepared, a la Imbue with Spell Ability without the HD limit. Considering that there are a lot of good abjuration spells that affect only you or are centered around you, this has a lot of applications. Note that you can only grant spells with levels equal to your Runeguard level -1, so you can’t grant allies your best defense spells right off the bat.
  • Kindness (***): This bumps the strength of the Heal skill in regards to treating deadly wounds: You don’t expend any uses from a healer’s kit, nor does this count against your daily uses of that skill. Plus, you heal twice as many hit points.
  • Generosity (****): When you use the Aid Another ability, you can sacrifice a spell to grant an ally an insight bonus equal to the level of the sacrificed spell. This bonus can be applied to attack rolls, skill checks or AC and lasts for a number of rounds equal to your Runeguard level. This can be an amazing buff due to how versatile the buff can be. The duration requires a lot of investment in Runeguard, though.
  • Humility (****): This is exactly the same as Generosity, only centered on you.
  • Love (****): By sacrificing a spell, you and a number of allies equal to the spell you sacrificed gain knowledge on each other’s well being a la Status. If you sacrificed a spell of 5th level or higher, you and your allies also gain an undispellable Telepathic Bond. Everyone also gets a +2 to saves against charm and compulsion effects. These effects have no range limit as long as you are all on the same plane. This lasts until you prepare spells again. This is absolutely amazing, especially if you are worried about the party being involuntarily split up.
  • Temperance (***): Immunity to negative energy is always nice. Immunity to negative effects from negative energy is also nice since you can avoid negative levels, too, but being staggered sucks a little.
  • Zeal (****): As a swift action, you gain a bonus on concentration effects equal to your Runeguard level. You can also apply any metamagic feat that increases the level by +1 to any spell that he casts that round. There are a lot of feats that up the spell level by +1, and there are a lot of spells that take a round or less to cast. Do the math.
  • Rune Focus (***): At 3rd level, your Glyph and Rune spells become harder to resist, along with all of your language-dependent spells. This stacks with your Spell Focus feats and grows even stronger 7th level!
  • Sihedron Rune Mastery (****): Permanency as an SLA, although you can only have one permanent effect at a time. Still, you can save a ton of money using this. Plus, the ability to swap permanent effects out can allow for not only a bunch of flexibility, but can allow for creative applications.

Verdict:

        Runeguard is definitely a spellcaster’s Prestige Class. Tons of the runeguard’s abilities work off of the ability to cast and sacrifice spells. With that in mind, the PrC’s base stats are almost exactly similar to the wizard (D6 hit die, low skills per level, similar skill list). Considering how many spells they can cast/sacrifice per day, wizards make excellent runeguards.

Scar Seeker

Author’s Note: As the title implies, this PrC deals heavily with self-harm, especially due to its affiliation with Vildeis.

Requirements:

  • +5 BAB
  • Lawful Good or Neutral Good alignment
  • Must worship Vildeis
  • Must have 3 ranks in Heal, Intimidate and Knowledge (Religion)
  • Must possess the Toughness Feat

Abilities:

  • D10 Hit Die (***): Standard for a front line character
  • 2 Skill Ranks per Level (*): Your list was crappy, anyways.
  • Skill List (*): Possibly the worst list out of all 5 of the specific PrCs. You only get two face skills (Intimidate and Sense Motive), two knowledge skills (Knowledge (Religion) and Spellcraft) and Heal. That’s it.
  • Good Fortitude and Will Saves, Bad Reflex Save (***): You’re pretty much a defender here, resisting death and mind control attempts for those that can’t. Since Toughness is a prerequisite for you, you can definitely survive a few failed reflex saves.
  • Full BAB (****): Since you’re a tank, you might as well wade deep into the field and hit stuff.
  • 5/10 Spellcasting Levels (**): Losing caster levels always sucks, but this is ludicrous. For classes that only get 4 levels of spells, such as paladins or bloodragers, the loss isn’t so bad since the spells are just a convenient way to self buff.
  • Enduring Scar (*-****): While there are a lot of scars you can pick, you can’t pick up all of them; you only get 5 scars. What may further limit you is that you can pick certain scars multiple times to increase their effects. While this PrC has a little bit of customization, you can only go so far. Be careful with your choices.
  • Blood (***): You can apply the Vicious weapon ability to your weapon. You can select this up to three times to increase the damage die size, although this also increases the damage received as well. It’s a bit high risk, high reward, especially since Vicious deals a ton of damage to enemies anyways.
  • Healing (***): You can heal twice as much damage when using your lay on hands ability on someone else. However, you take half of the damage you healed. I can see this being used in case of emergencies, but just be careful of your own health.
  • Martyr (**-***): When you get knocked below 0 hp, you can use up all of your lay on hands to simultaneously heal allies and damage enemies around you. Enemies gain a will save to halve the damage, but since its holy damage, it can’t be reduced. I usually don’t like abilities that require you to be in harm’s way, but this is a fairly nice contingency.
  • Mercy (**-****): When using your lay on hands ability, you can use a mercy you don’t have but qualify for. This is a nice way to become a flexible healer, but a paladin oath, Oath of Charity, does the same thing. Check with your DM to see if that oath still applies to this PrC.
  • Sacrifice (**): It’s essentially Shield Other with a shorter duration. This will eat through your scar uses per day, but it works well for you since you’re a tank.
  • Suffering (***): You get untyped DR and a small bonus on saves versus emotion and fear effects. Selecting this a second time increases both bonuses to half your scar seeker level. While the duration sucks, irresistible DR and a bonus on saves are great for tank characters.
  • Tenacity (*): When you get crit on or suffer a sneak attack, you can get a small morale bonus to attack rolls, skill checks, ability checks, and saving throws. If you already have a morale bonus for these checks, then you get a +1 bonus on top of it. The bonuses may be nice, but relying on random chance to even activate it, on top of the possibility of dying before you even get to use it is way too much for me.
  • Painful Purification (***): Another way to activate your scars, which is taking a point of damage per each hit die you have. This damage can’t be reduced, and can’t be healed by anything other than a full night’s rest. This is more of a last resort than anything else, but extra scar useage is always nice.
  • Lay On Hands (***): Healing this way at 2nd level is nice, although you can’t apply mercies- the real big draw of LOH- unless you have either the Mercy scar or you already had the mercy ability. If you already had this ability, this stacks, too!
  • Smite Evil (****): Hitting stuff better and harder is always nice, and since a lot of enemies you face are going to be evil, you will make a lot of good use of this.  It stacks with previous levels, too, which is good; you get this at 4th level, giving you a -3 level penalty compared to other paladins.
  • Sanguine Sacrifice (****): Using Painful Purification now gives you a minor morale bonus to the same checks that you get from the Tenacity scar. On top of getting extra uses of your scar ability, you get a bonus for hurting yourself! It’s a little bit of risk/reward that can help every now and then.
  • True Martyr (***): This is pretty much Total Party Kill insurance. Super strong dragon breath attack? It only affects you a lot. If it kills you, it summons a rather powerful angel that fights for you for a minute. As a 10th level capstone, I can bet that other players would have access to resurrection spells. This heroic sacrifice can only happen once per week.

Verdict:

        Scar seeker is a very tanky PrC, to the point that it has so much health points, it can readily sacrifice some to fuel its own abilities. Since many of the abilities key off of charisma, half-caster classes such as paladin and bloodrager are strongly recommended. The self-harm aspect might set a lot of players off, though.

Stargazer

Requirements:

  • Must be one step within Chaotic Good
  • Must worship Pulara
  • 5 ranks in Knowledge (Geography and Planes), 3 ranks in Survival
  • Must be able to cast 3rd level spells

Abilities:

  • D6 Hit Die (*): Bog standard for arcane casters, but a step down for divine casters. Gag.
  • 4 Skill Ranks per Level (**): This is actually a step up for most casters, especially for intelligence based ones who are expected to gain skills through their ability scores.
  • Skill List (****): Access to all knowledge skills is great, along with Spellcraft and Perception. Diplomacy can help get out of a tough spot. Acrobatics, Climb, and Swim help you get around. Survival can be replaced via magic, but serves a thematic purpose. Perform is there only due to its connection to the Thrush arcana.
  • Good Reflex and Will Saves, Bad Fortitude Saves (***): Casters have naturally good will saves, but rarely anything else. If anything, a good reflex save might save your hide in a game of rocket tag.
  • 3/4 BAB (***): Arcane casters taking this PrC can benefit from this for the sake of ranged attacks. For divine casters, this is usually par for the course.
  • 10/10 Spellcasting Levels (****): This is the only one of the five PrCs that doesn’t sacrifice a spellcaster level at any time! Now you don’t have to worry about spending +2 feats to recover the levels!
  • Guiding Light (****): Familiars are always good for a lot of reasons, such as scouts and other labor.
  • Mystery Magic (****): You gain signature abilities from several casting classes, such as witches, shamans, clerics and oracles.
  • At 1st and 9th level, you can gain a hex, not just from the witch list, but from the shaman’s list and their heaven spirit as well.
  • At 3rd level, you gain access to the Stars subdomain; this can be an addition to the domains you already have as a cleric, but even if you are not a cleric, you still get the subdomain’s spells and its “Stars Are Right” ability.
  • At 5th and 7th level, you get the “Coat of Many Stars” and “Star Chart” revelations from the oracle’s heaven mystery. The former is a bonus to AC, later granting DR, while the latter is essentially Commune as an SLA. Both of these are nice. What makes this even better is that not only do previous oracle levels stack with this ability, so do cleric levels!
  • Sidereal Arcana (*-****): While the large number of options and limited slots seems intimidating at first, the capstone gets rid of that fear of picking a bad option entirely. Most of these are minor but versatile abilities with a strong bonus effect that works specifically against demons.
  • Bridge (*-***): The cold resistance is fairly minor, but darkvision can help out a lot of characters. Races that already have darkvision can skip this. Being able to see past demonic darkness effects is situational at best.
  • Daughter (**): A bonus against fear effects and becoming harder to intimidate are both narrow bonuses. Immunity to fear from demonic sources can be a life saver, though, especially if one of them has Phantasmal Killer.
  • Follower (**): While a bonus against death effects seems narrow, immunity to demonic death effects and negative energy/level effects is great.
  • Lantern Bearer (***): The size of any magical light sources you create increases by 10 feet and the spell is buffed by +2 caster levels. Not bad, especially since a fair bit of spells can create light as a secondary effect.
  • Mother (***): Certain healing spells heal double the amount of damage. Also, if you were a cleric before taking this PrC, your stargazer levels count as cleric levels for the sake of channeling positive energy. If you were utilizing abilities such as variant channel energy, this is an excellent way to keep it relevant. Being a healer is almost always appreciated.
  • Newlyweds (*): Charm and language dependent effects become harder to resist for targets that can be attracted to you. Also, you can lie to and diplomatize with them a little easier. Blegh! On the other hand, demonic charm effects can become easier to resist. This might be useful against the occasional succubus.
  • Pack (*-****): A bonus to a skill you don’t have and frankly isn’t too useful is weak, but summoning another animal whenever you summon multiple animals can be really nice battlefield control.
  • Patriarch (*): You get skill bonuses on avoiding getting lost, but spells can easily beat the purpose of that. Immunity to maze effects can help, but only against certain BBEGs.
  • Rider (****): Phantom Steed as an SLA with a built in Sanctuary effect? That uses your character level for the caster level? Very nice! The fact that it shines like a torch is a double-edged sword; it helps you see at night, but it can hinder you if you’re trying to stay stealthy.
  • Stargazer (****): A bonus to initiative is always nice, as is not being considered flat-footed.
  • Stranger (**): Cultural Adaptation three times per day as an SLA, with a built in transformation effect to help you blend into a certain demographic. This is very nice for infiltration and social missions.
  • Thrush (**-***): A bonus to diplomacy and perform (sing) checks equal to half your level. Diplomacy I can get, but unless you’re a bard, I doubt you’ll get much out of perform.
  • Wagon (****): A 10-foot bonus to move speed and you can take 5-foot steps in difficult terrain. Plus you can cast Freedom of Movement three times per day as a swift action, but only against demons and it only lasts for one round. Still, this is a nice way to get out of a grapple.
  • Stars’ Dance (****): And now you can swap your sidereal arcana! Granted, it’s once per day and you can only change one, but considering how versatile the arcana can be, this is great!

Verdict:

        Stargazer is an excellent caster’s PrC for any type of caster, but especially arcane casters since they don’t lose anything compared to divine casters. Granted, certain divine casters can make use of the Mystery Magic ability, but especially clerics.

The Empyreal Lords

Who are the Empyreal Lords?

The Empyreal Lords are large group of powerful demigods that fight for the side of good. Their ranks are both numerous and various, as they include lawful good Archons, neutral good Agathions, chaotic good Azatas, and the good-of-any-type, but usually neutral, Angels. Together, they assist the mortal plane live in peace by teaching them the ways of good and combating the forces of evil.

Because the list of possible deities is so long and only so many of them grant Celestial Obedience boons, I’ve listed them here for posterity. I have also listed the domains they offer as well as their favored weapon to help with consideration. In the domains section, if something is in (parenthesis) like this, it means that it is a subdomain. Please note that some gods offer subdomains without offering the relevant domain as well.

For the sake of brevity, all bonuses granted by performing the obedience section is considered to be +4, unless noted otherwise.

Andoletta

Alignment: Lawful Good

Areas of Concern: Consolation, Respect, Security

Character: She’s specifically described as grandmotherly, so wise, elderly characters would do well to worship her. She’s also friends with Erastil and works with lawful institutions.

Domains: Good (Archon), Knowledge (Education, Memory), Law (Archon), Protection (Defense, Purity)

Favored Weapon (*): Quarterstaff. Simple double weapon. 

Obedience (**): Hope you took Craft (Basketweaving) and memorized a book! Other than that, giving to the poor isn’t bad at all. You get a bonus on saving throws against spells that deal non-energy hit point damage. A very big, but very narrow bonus.

Boons:

  1. (***) SLAs that can easily be found on the Cleric list, but are still nice to have otherwise. Sanctuary and Magic Circle against Chaos or Evil are good ways to protect yourself while Magic Weapon can get past the DR of Evil or Chaotic foes.
  2. (***) Silenced Dictum as an SLA, which is also nice, albeit dependant on your enemies’ hit dice. The silence effect is redundant, though, since SLAs require no verbal components to begin with.
  3. (****) A great capstone: you get darkvision (or an extention if you already have it), and the ability to buff yourself and your allies’ AC, saving throws and give them SR for 1 round per HD. The duration is a bit weak, but by the time you get it, you can have an ample amount to throw around if you conserve it.

Verdict: While the obedience reward is rather weak, the actual act is very easy to do. The boons given are also nice if you aren’t already a divine caster.

Arqueros

Alignment: Lawful Good

Areas of Concern: Bodyguards, Protection, Watchfulness

Character: A vanguard against all that is evil, he protects both morals and divines alike with his heavy shield and longspear. Supported Ragathiel’s ascension into empyreal-lorddom.

Domains: Good (Archon), Law (Archon), Protection (Defense, Fortification), War (Tactics), (Cooperation)

Favored Weapon (**): Longspear. Simple two-handed reach weapon.

Obedience (**): Hold a heavy shield over your head for an hour and pray. Gain a bonus on strength and strength-related checks. Good god to follow if you need to lift something.

Boons:

  1. (***) The SLA’s given here help you and your allies with avoiding and mitigating damage. Shield Other provides a strong but risky buff that fits the theme of this deity nicely. The other two provide bonus to AC, with Magic Vestment being more indirect about it.
  2. (**) Bull rushing and charging enemies always take attacks of opportunity from you. You also gain a +10 bonus against bull rush maneuvers. Situational, but nice to have regardless.
  3. (****) You get Iron Body as a once-per-day SLA, but with the addition of a ton of SR against the spells of evil creatures. Nice excellent buff with a new improvement.

Verdict: Arqueros is great for any tank or melee character, even if you aren’t taking the PrC.

Arshea

Alignment: Neutral Good

Areas of Concern: Freedom, Physical Beauty, Sexuality

Character: Arshea is a free spirit in many senses of the phrase, being genderfluid and assisting with artists.

Domains: Charm (Love, Lust), Good (Agathion), Liberation (Freedom, Self-Realization), Strength (Self-Realization)

Favored Weapon (***): Flail. Martial one-handed weapon with the trip and disarm qualities.

Obedience (*): OKAY, this one is riddled with NSFW details. You have to have sex with a partner or by yourself while proclaiming the best qualities of either yourself or your partner, then pray to Arshea. In return, you get a narrow bonus on charisma and charisma-based checks against intelligent creatures that can be attracted to you. Check with your GM and party before you do this so you don’t squick anyone out or make anyone uncomfortable.

Boons:

  1. (***) You get a diverse medley of SLAs: Mirror Image makes it hard for enemies to attack you directly, while Charm Monster can help you remove a threat from the field entirely. Disguise Self is good for RP purposes for a variety of reasons.
  2. (***) Gain a bonus to your AC while wearing no armor and revealing clothing. If you are a Sorcerer, this is practically gold.
  3. (**) Freedom as a once per day SLA. Meh.

Verdict: Arshea can be a very divisive deity to talk about if handled poorly enough. While Pathfinder does have a very strong LGBT+ fandom, the openness of the obedience can put off a lot of people. The boons themselves are not as strong as one would hope either, so Arshea gets a low grade.

Ashava

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Dancers, Lonely Spirits, Moonlight

Character: A dancer at heart, Ashava assists lonely souls, both literally and figuratively, find peace and safety in the night. She also has a good reputation with werewolves, of all things.

Domains: Chaos (Azata, Revelry), Darkness (Moon), Good (Azata), Repose (Ancestors)

Favored Weapon (****): Bladed Scarf. A two-handed exotic weapon with the disarm and trip qualities. It also deals 1d6 (or 1d4 if small) slashing damage on an opponent that successfully grapples the wearer.

Obedience (****): Dance alone under the moon. Otherwise, pray for the dead and light a lantern in a dark place. Gain a bonus on saves against the spells and abilities of the undead. Undead have plenty of nasty tricks up their fetid sleeves, so this can be nice to have.

Boons:

  1. (****) Another medley of SLAs. Cat’s Grace can boost your dexterity, which helps with a lot of things. Faerie Fire can help you track enemies in the dark, especially if they can go invisible. Daylight also creates light, but with more strength in terms of brightness and no effect on invisibility or concealment. The last one also has a unique twist in that it shows up as “Moonlight” rather than “Sunlight”, although that is only the visible effect, so the actual effects might be the same.
  2. (***) Enlarged Sunbeam as a once-per-day SLA. Sunbeam always had an iffy reception in regards to its rules, but it does good damage to undead creatures and can cause permanent blindness. The visual effect of it looking like moonlight is only a cosmetic change, so that is a nice touch.
  3. (***) Irresistible Dance as a once-per-day with a twist: if you cast this on a willing person, they gain a bonus to dexterity, dexterity skill checks, and reflex saves for the next 24 hours. Not a bad buff duration at all.

Verdict: While Ashava doesn’t have a tight focus, she brings a lot to the table: buffs to dexterity are always nice and anti-undead tricks can help you out in a pinch.

Bharnarol

Alignment: Neutral Good

Areas of Concern: Creativity, Invention, Persistence

Character: Bharnarol is a god of invention, but unlike other inventor gods, he is strictly against mad science. If he deems an invention too dangerous, he tries to convince its creator to change or stop the invention. If that fails, he takes the invention and hides it in his domain in Nirvana.

Domains: Artifice (Construct), Community (Cooperation, Education), Good (Agathion), Knowledge (Education, Thought)

Favored Weapon (*): Sickle. A simple one handed weapon with the trip quality.

Obedience (**): Go through the motions of creating something with Bharnarol’s teachings in your mind. Gain a bonus to AC against construct attacks and a bonus to saves against their spells and abilities.

Boons:

  1. (***) Grease can provide some nice crowd control or prevent people from grabbing something. Fox’s Cunning can boost intelligence for skill checks. Secret Page is a bit of a stinker, being situational and only allowing one use per day.
  2. (****) You can improve the qualities of a magical item that is consumed on use or requires charges a la metamagic feats. You can use it three times per day, which is nice, and you can use this power in multiple ways too.
  3. (****) Extended Polymorph Any Object once per day. Incredibly versatile and can help you out of a lot of sticky situations.

Verdict: Bharnarol is a creator’s god. He has access to the construct subdomain that gives you the spells to make constructs of your own, and grants the ability to enhance your magical items. If you ever face constructs, you will have some protection, too.

Black Butterfly

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Distance, Silence, Space

Character: Born from the shadow of Desna, Black Butterfly despises the masters of the Dark Tapestry, namely the Outer Gods and Great Old Ones.

She also has a history with Shelyn and Zon-Kuthon: When Dou-Bral (Zon-Kuthon’s original name) got lost in the Dark Tapestry, Black Butterfly found him and returned him to his family. Unfortunately, Dou-Bral became Zon-Kuthon, torturing his father into becoming his herald and becoming one of the most monstrous gods in the setting. Black Butterfly has regretted bringing him back ever since.

Domains: Chaos (Azata), Good (Azata), Liberation (Freedom, Revolution), Void (Stars)

Favored Weapon (**): Starknife. Martial light weapon that can be thrown like a dagger.

Obedience (**): Be nice to someone without seeing them or being seen. If you are, pray for an hour blindfolded and give tithe to a good god. A little weird, but nothing too out of the ordinary. You get a bonus against gaze and sonic attacks, which are unfortunately not too common.

Boons:

  1. (****) All excellent options for a stealthy character. Obscuring Mist can provide some concealment. Silence makes stealthy movement a breeze even for heavy armored players. Deeper Darkness helps skirt around enemies with light sources and even darkvision.
  2. (***) Quickened Blindness/Deafness as a once-per-day SLA. Blindness is downright nasty for anybody, although deafness has its uses against spellcasters. The boon also limits what can dispel the effect as well.
  3. (****) A bonus to stealth checks, minor concealment when not fighting blind-fighters or in daylight, and an at will fly speed? I’d say this is an excellent capstone boon.

Verdict: Black Butterfly’s backstory isn’t just barebones and BB’s boons are basically brilliant.

Chadali

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Fate, Safety, Serendipity

Character: She was born under an aurora, a lunar eclipse, and a meteor shower all happening at the same time. As such, she is a big believer in fate, but not to the point of it overbearing you.

Domains: Chaos (Azata), Good (Azata), Luck (Fate), Protection (Defense, Purity)

Favored Weapon (***): Net. An exotic ranged weapon that entangles foes with a successful touch attack.

Obedience (*): Now this is a recipe for disaster. Either you deliberately get lost in a place you’ve never been in or you walk 200 steps blindfolded. You could trip down a flight of stairs, walk through an open window, or even bump into an unfriendly foe. For your troubles, you get a bonus to AC whenever you trigger AoEs by casting spells or SLAs.

Boons:

  1. (*) Not a good start: the SLA’s you get aren’t very helpful on a consistent basis outside of Aid. While Protection From Energy can help you against specific foes, Pass Without Trace only helps you if you are being followed.
  2. (***) You can knock projectiles away three times per day as if you had Deflect Arrows. If you actually do have Deflect Arrows, then you can do it an unlimited amount of times per day, even against attacks you aren’t aware of!
  3. (**) Once per day, whenever you hit 0 HP or less, you can choose to deduct 10 points of damage from whatever hurt you. While this can possibly put your health back above 0 HP, there are plenty of other things that can put you down that don’t rely on HP damage. Ultimately,  as an “insurance” tactic, it doesn’t work well.

Verdict: While I do like the second boon Chadali grants, there isn’t a lot going for her at all. Her obedience requires a bit of risk to pull off and even more so to make use of. Her boons are incredibly unorganized, with little to tie them together in terms of theme or utility.

Chucaro

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Dreams, Hallucinations, Mist

Character: Born from the dreams of an angel, Chucaro helps the mentally ill and drug-addled. She helps the latter group specifically, finding hard drug use to be heinous and tries to lead users to find enlightenment from within.

Domains: Chaos (Azata, Revelry, Whimsy), Good (Azata), Madness (Insanity), Magic

Favored Weapon (*): Morningstar. Simple one-handed weapon that deals both bludgeoning and piercing damage.

Obedience: Wear gauze, eat shrooms and meditate on the teachings of Chucaro. You get a bonus on saves against poison. Just don’t get caught eating where its illegal.

Boons:

  1. (*) The first two SLAs hurt later on due to their hard HD limits, but Color Spray and Hypnotic Pattern can still work wonders against low level enemies. Gaseous Form is good as an escape plan.
  2. (***) DR 5/bludgeoning. A bit flat, but DR never hurts to have.
  3. (***) You get Weird as a once-per-day SLA. A great save-or-die spell, since it can target multiple foes and even if it fails to kill, it still deals damage and stuns them.

Verdict: Chucaro might seem underwhelming, but her effects are still potent. She’s also one of the few good gods to have the Madness domain, so there is that detail as well.

Damerrich

Alignment: Lawful Good

Areas of Concern: Executions, Judiciousness, Responsibility

Character: Damerrich is a grim, towering juggernaut who does what is needed for the side of good. His job is simple; kill any evil things that stands in his way. He does not do it out of sadistic glee, however; he kills evil because it must be done. He is often seen working alongside Olheon, executing despots and tyrants that perform unlawful executions.

Domains: Death, Glory (Honor, Heroism), Good (Archon), Law (Archon, Judgment)

Favored Weapon (****): Greataxe. Two-handed martial weapon.

Obedience (***): Ruminate on those you have killed and your actions in the name of law. By doing this, you gain a bonus to your AC against critical hit confirmations.

Boons:

  1. (***) This is definetly an SLA list for front-line fighters. As stated before, Sheild of Faith boosts your AC. Keen Edge, on the other hand, doubles the crit range of your weapon, which can raise your damage output substantially depending on what weapon you use. My only problem with this boon is that it also has Death Knell, which is a spell that kills a dying foe and gives you bonuses for it. That sounds bad enough, but it also has the [Evil] descriptor on it. Make of it what you will, but ask your GM if using the spell would be against your alignment.
  2. (****) You ignore the first 5 points of an enemy’s DR, no matter what type of weapon you are using. Again, for fighters, this is a godsend.
  3. (**) You get Power Word Kill once-per-day. While it doesn’t include a saving throw, making it unblockable, the spell requires you to know exactly how much HP an enemy has. Because of that last detail, it becomes a hard spell to use efficiently.

        Vertict: Dammerich is definitely a god for a front-line fighter. His obedience is easy to do and lowers the chance of receiving a critical hit, and his boons also help in a battle as well.

Eritrice

Alignment: Neutral Good

Areas of Concern: Debate, Opinions, Truth

Character: Surprisingly for an agathion, Eritrice’s story begins in the pits of hell, where the archdevil Geryon filled a pit with thousands of lies. From those lies came a sole truth that took the form of Eritrice and fled pit into the upper planes. She helps others learn information, specifically through debate, and is a friend to many felines.

Domains: Charm, Good (Agathion), Knowledge (Education, Memory, Thought), Nobility (Leadership)

Favored Weapon (*): Dagger. Simple light weapon that can be thrown up to 10 feet.

Obedience (***): Write a truth on a scrap of paper, slather it in honey and place it in your mouth until the honey dissolves. Kinda bizzare, but I get the symbolism. You gain a bonus on saves versus illusions, which is nice considering how annoying illusions can get.

Boons:

  1. (***) If you are the party’s face, this is the SLA list for you. Comprehend Languages and Tongues can let you understand any language whatsoever, whether it be written or spoken. Enthrall can help you out if you need to work a crowd, although both you and the crowd will be out of action for an hour.
  2. (****) You can cast silenced True Seeing three times per day as an SLA. This spell allows you to get past a lot of annoying things such as concealment or illusions. It’s also heightened to 7th level, which helps out with the duration. The silenced bit is redundant, though; on top of being an SLA, which doesn’t require components, when does looking at things make a sound?
  3. (***) You essentially get Holy Word as a 60 foot cone that also deals 2d6 sonic damage. That’s 20 additional feet of range and extra damage from a rare element, so it can be considered a straight upgrade. Just be careful around enemies immune to sonic effects, as the spell will be wasted on them.

Verdict: If you ever have worries or doubts about illusions in your area, Eritrice has you covered. Fittingly for a god based around truth, she can help nullify plenty of illusions while also helping you with other people on the side.

Falayna

Alignment: Lawful Good

Areas of Concern: Femininity, Martial Training, Rings

Character: An archon who was once the wife of another empyreal lord. When he fell in combat versus the arch devil Moloch, Falayna took up his sword, both figuratively and literally, and ascended into empyreal lordship. She is a female goddess that embodies feminine beauty, with most of her followers being female, although there are male followers as well.

Domains: Good (Archon), Law (Archon), Liberation (Resolve, Revolution, Self-Realization), Strength (Resolve, Self-Realization)

Favored Weapon (****): Longsword. Martial one-handed weapon.

Obedience (****): Punch a fighting dummy while wearing rings until you leave imprints on your hands. You gain a bonus to both grapple checks and CMD, which is incredibly nice for martial characters.

Boons:

  1. (****) All three SLA options here are great for martial characters. Divine Favor three times a day gives you some nice buffs to your hit and damage rolls. Spiritual Weapon can let you form a longsword to not only hit incorporeal foes, but deals force damage and buffs your to-hit with your wisdom modifier, if you have one. Greater Magic Weapon grants your weapon a scaling enhancement bonus and allows you to bypass DR/Magic.
  2. (****) Increase the damage dice of your weapon by one step for a number of rounds per day equal to your hit dice. The rounds don’t have to be consecutive, which is nice for rationing them throughout the day.
  3. (***) You get a particularly blingy Crushing Hand as a once-per-day SLA. This is a nice, versatile effect that can really help with battlefield control.

Verdict: Falayna is a goddess of war and fashion, and both her obedience and boons prove it. She is a solid choice for martial characters, especially for grappler types. Tetori monks love her.

Ghenshau

Alignment: Lawful Good

Areas of Concern: Ignorance, Placidity, Simplicity

Character: This archon once tried tried to develop an equation that would form perfect order and goodness. However, developing the equation took its toll on him and he abandoned his work, preferring a life of tranquility. He now spends his time appreciating the little things in life and taking it all one step at a time.

Domains: Community (Home), Good (Archon), Law (Archon, Loyalty), Repose (Ancestors)

Favored Weapon (****): Handaxe. Martial light weapon with a crit multiplier of 3.

Obedience (***): Plant something you’ve never read in fertile soil and plant a seed above it. This might be a big waste of money, but a bonus to saves against transmutation effects is nice, too.

Boons:

  1. (**) A weird medley of SLAs. Erase really doesn’t do much for you outside of rare occasions. Deep Slumber has a hard HD limit of 10 HD, but putting a foe or two to sleep is a good way to take them out of combat. Calm Emotions can get rid of enemy buffs or nerfs on your allies, and it might have some application outside of combat as well.
  2. (**) Three times per day, you can damage an enemy’s wisdom by  1d6 points of damage plus half your HD to a maximum of +5. This can total out to the potential penalty of -11 wisdom. A fort save halves the damage. An okay amount of uses per day helps a mediocre stat nerf, but you could probably use your standard action on something better.
  3. (****) Permanent Mind Blank spell, and if anyone tries to read you, they must pass a will save or take intelligence damage. Very nice.

Verdict: Ghenshau is great for characters that want to play it a bit loose by lawful standards, but the boons are hit and miss while the obedience can potentially cost you a lot of money.

Halcamora

Alignment: Neutral Good

Areas of Concern: Gardens, Orchards, Wine

Character: Halcamora is a god of harvest, specifically in the field of winemaking. She is a social god that is specifically stated to get along well with other gods of nature. She hates fiends that carry insects and disease. She doesn’t have a lot of backstory, unfortunately.

Domains: Good (Agathion), Luck, Plant (Growth), Weather (Seasons), (Revelry)

Favored Weapon (*): Club. Simple one-handed weapon.

Obedience (**): Pour half a glass of wine onto fertile ground. Drink the rest. You gain a bonus against bleed and disease effects.

Boons:

  1. (**) Plenty of Druid-exclusive SLAs here. Goodberry isn’t much health unless you forgot to pack rations. Tree Shape is good if you need a disguise in the woods, plus it gives a huge bonus to AC and immunity to critical hits, although your dexterity is reduced to 0 and you can’t move. Plant Growth provides a ton of crowd control as it slows down foes and possibly buff other plant-based spells.
  2. (**) Once per day, you can touch someone and deal 1d6 damage to all of their mental stats.Fortitude halves the damage. As nice as damaging all of their stats sounds, there are more potent ways to hurt stats.
  3. (**) You can cast Control Plants three time per day. Plant monsters are pretty rare encounters, but there are a nice number of uses. Being heightened to 8th level doesn’t do anything since it already was an 8th level spell.

Verdict: Halcamora really doesn’t offer much. Her favored weapon is rather plain, her boons are mediocre and she doesn’t have a lot to her character. The only good thing I like about her is that you can pour one out to your homies in-universe and it wouldn’t be out of character.

Immonhiel

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Herbs, Medicine, Toads

Character: A healer in the war of good and evil, Immonhiel provides respite to those injured and mercy to the mortally wounded. She has been hosted by almost every empyreal lord and several mortals, often in the guise of a sagely old woman for the latter.

Domains: Chaos (Azata), Good (Azata), Healing (Medicine, Restoration, Resurrection), Plant (Growth)

Favored Weapon (****): Handaxe. Martial light weapon with a critical multiplier of 3.

Obedience (*): Fill your mouth with herbs, have a bath with a toad and think of Immonhiel’s teachings. Bizzare, and only nets you a bonus against disease. Bleh.

Boons:

  1. (****) An interesting all-around medly of SLAs, and certainly fits Immonhiel’s theme of nature and healing. Cure Light Wounds three times a day can save a lot of characters a lot of grief. Barkskin provides a boost to your AC, which a lot of characters can also appreciate. Lily Pad Stride allows you to walk on water, along with some allies if they can consistently pass their acrobatics checks.
  2. (***) Empowered Heal once per day. Very nice method of healing, especially with the Empower metamagic effect boosting the heal by 1.5.
  3. (****) You can summon a Chaotic Good Viper Vine to help you for one round per HD you possess. It never hurts to have another ally, especially since the plant template has a lot of defenses.

Verdict: Immonhiel is a surprisingly good character to worship. Everyone appreciates a healer and while the obedience is downright weird, the boons she offers are very nice to have.

Irez

Alignment: Neutral Good

Areas of Concern: Cards, Scribes, Spells

Character: Emerging from the reeds of Nirvana’s shores, Irez is a goddess of magic and luck. She understands how runes interact with magic and so is worshipped by scholars in many fields, from historians to calligraphers to harrow readers. Surprisingly, she is also worshipped by gamblers.

Domains: Good (Agathion), Luck (Fate), Magic, Runes (Language, Wards)

Favored Weapon (*): Dart. Simple ranged weapon.

Obedience (**-***): Draw a pair of cards from a deck. If the suits match, you gain a bonus against two schools of magic of your choice. If they don’t, you gain a bonus on spells from scrolls. It’s an interesting obedience to say the least; it relies on chance and can provide you with a superior or inferior bonus depending on the circumstances. I prefer the match bonus, as the schools can protect you from spells cast from scrolls as well.

Boons:

  1. (***) Another all-around set of SLAs that fit Irez’s theme, this time around magic and runes. Divine Favor is a nice buff for attacking with. Augury can do a lot of things, especially with exploration and RPs. Glyph of Warding can be a nice trap to set for others, although since it’s an SLA, you probably cannot use the spell rune function.
  2. (***) Three times per day, when you use a scroll to cast a spell that deals hit point damage, you can change half the damage type to holy. This is nice since holy damage is unblockable, but the kicker is that you can also use an improved form of Use Magic Device by adding half your HD and your charisma modifier instead of your usual UMD skill. It’s an option, and you can use whatever is higher.
  3. (***) You summon 2d6 cards that follow you around and absorb 1 die of sneak attack or critical hit damage per card. This is a nice defense to mitigate damage and can stack with other defenses like the fortification armor ability.

Verdict: Irez has a bunch of weird but unique boons to her obedience, which sets her out as one of the most unique empyreal lords to follow. Her character is a bit flat, but the amount of variety in the gameplay she presents is definitely something to consider. Players looking for a straightforward god with few moving parts ought to look elsewhere.

Jaidz

Alignment: Neutral Good

Areas of Concern: Cowards, The Untested, Youth

Character: Jaidz believes in assisting the cowardly rise up to the occasion. He also assists the young in preparing for adulthood.

Domains: Glory (Hubris), Good (Agathion), Protection (Defense, Purity), Travel (Exploration)

Favored Weapon (****): Short sword. Light martial weapon.

Obedience (*-**): Draw some spirals on your skin with chalk and trace the chalk lines with a tiger’s fang. Don’t break the skin. You get a bonus to AC against attacks of opportunity from natural weapons. Incredibly situational, but if you’re stuck in the middle of a jungle, I guess this would be useful to have.

Boons:

  1. (****) A nice selection of utility SLAs. Remove Fear can help you or an ally get rid of an annoying or even debilitating status effect. Darkvision is always nice to have. Heroism is an excellent buff to have for any occasion.
  2. (****) You can cast Greater Heroism three times per day. Also an excellent buff, but with the added benefit of fear immunity and temporary hit points. This can be a life saver in a tight spot. Heightening it to 7th level also improves its duration, albeit by only a little.
  3. (****) You can cast Symbol of Fear wherever you want for free. It has its casting time reduced to a minute and you can attune 10 enemies to it without increasing the time it takes to cast. With all of the improvements over the vanilla spell, I would say this is a decent capstone.

Verdict: Jaidz’s character is bare bones, but you can certainly appreciate his boons, as they work well with any sort of character.

Jalaijatali

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Natural Music, Rapids, Waterfalls

Character: Jalaijatili is essentially a hippie music teacher. She loves to teach mortals the marvels of natural music (despite her holy symbol being a pipe flooding with water), and hates any fiends that attempts to pollute water sources.

Domains: Chaos (Azata, Revelry), Good (Azata), Scalykind, Water (Flotsam, Rivers)

Favored Weapon (***): Whip. One-handed exotic weapon with 15 ft. reach and the trip, disarm, and nonlethal qualities. Enemies with more than +1 armor or +3 natural armor are not harmed by whip attacks unless the user has the “Whip Mastery” feat.

Obedience (*-****): Pour water over your head and sing. You gain a bonus against the spells, SLAs and Supernatural abilities of creatures with either the aquatic or water subtype. Useful in an aquatic setting, but not much else.

Boons:

  1. (***) Some solid water-themed SLAs. Hydraulic Push bull rushes enemies using your charisma as a bonus to the maneuver. Slipstream gives you either a bonus to movement speed which can increase or decrease depending on the slope you’re standing on, or a swim speed. Water Breathing is pretty self explanatory.
  2. (***) You get a +3 bonus to your natural armor. Now you’re immune to your own deity’s favored weapon!
  3. (***) You and a number of willing companions equal to half your HD can turn into water and travel across flowing bodies of water at 50 miles per hour. While the duration on this is great, going at 1 hour per HD you possess, I don’t like how it limits the effect: you specifically have to use a flowing body of water such as a river or creek. Oceans may be out of your league. However, there are some fun tricks you can use with it, such as riding the crest of a Tsunami spell.

Verdict: With water definitely on her mind, Jalaijatali is a rather restricted deity. Her capstone boon is admittedly rather weak, being limited to flowing waters, and her obedience will only show strong use in aquatic themed campaigns. That said, her first two boons have plenty of promise.

Korada

Alignment: Neutral Good

Areas of Concern: Foresight, Forgiveness, Peace

Character: Korada is a deity of peace and nonviolence. Those that have been subject to violence or have committed violent acts are welcomed into Korada’s fold, as he forgives and protects all. He also assists diplomats and those faced with difficult decisions. He is a talented user of divination, but he does not act on what he predicts.

Domains: Good (Agathion), Healing (Medicine, Restoration), Magic (Divine), Protection (Defense)

Favored Weapon (****): Unarmed Strike. You gain the Improved Unarmed Strike feat, which allows you to do lethal and nonlethal damage with your bare fists. The feat is also a requirement for several other feats, which is useful to keep in mind.

Obedience (***): Exactly at dawn, meditate on those who have harmed either you or your loved ones and try to forgive them. Depending on your backstory, this will either be easy or impossible. You get a bonus on saves against effects with the Lawful or Chaotic descriptors.

Boons:

  1. (***) Being able to use Bless three times a day is a nice, if meager, buff for the group. Augury can help decide with group decisions without going too meta with the GM, and as an SLA, it doesn’t cost 25 GP per casting. Mad Monkeys can cause plenty of havoc by deafening, nauseating and stealing the gear of targets. Do note that stolen items take damage during its time in the swarm.
  2. (***) Three times per day as a swift action, you can up the damage of your weapon by one step, but the damage is nonlethal. You do not take the usual penalties for making nonlethal attacks. This effect goes on for an hour, so it can be useful for multiple battles. This can pair well with feats or classes that utilize non-lethal damage, such as the Enforcer feat or the Swashbuckler’s Whirling Dervish archetype. Not useful against the undead.
  3. (****) You are affected by a permanent Sanctuary spell, with foes requiring a will save to attack you. Outsiders have to roll a second, slightly stronger will save if they make an attack, as they get banished back to their home dimensions a la Dismissal. If you attack, this effect is dismissed for 1d4+1 rounds. This is especially nice for casters that prefer to do battlefield control, but it sort of defeats the purpose of the previous boon.

Verdict: Korada is a nice god to worship. He’s peaceful and loving so there are a lot of reasons to like him personally. He grants a feat to his worshippers that is a common prerequisite and his boons are varied enough to be worthwhile for any build.

Also of note is that Korada has a unique magic trait known as “Transmuter of Korada” which not only grants all transmutation spells a caster level bonus, but treats one of the six ability score boosting spells with Extend Magic once per day for free! What is especially nice about the trait is that you don’t have to actually worship Korada; the trait’s text says you learned from one of his followers.

Lalaci

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Rainbows, Relaxation, Shade

Character: He is a weird sun god. He particularly likes characters with some internal conflict, like those with a balanced mix of humors. He is mainly worshipped by glassblowers, illusionists and desert-dwellers.

Domains: Chaos (Azata, Whimsy), Good (Azata), Luck (Fate), Sun (Light, Thirst)

Favored Weapon (*): Sling. Simple ranged weapon. You can use ordinary rocks as ammo for a penalty, thus have infinite ammo.

Obedience (***): Paint your forehead with a rainbow and hang suncatchers everywhere you can. You get a bonus against fire effects. Considering how common fire can be, this is useful to have.

Boons:

  1. (*) You get a bunch of HD based SLAs. All of the options can take enemies out of a fight, but since they all have a HD limit, it becomes hard to use them in later levels.
  2. (**) +4 bonus against blinding effects, and whenever you successfully save against a spell with the light descriptor, you can either ignore it or redirect it at someone. Nice, if a bit selective.
  3. (***) You get Prismatic Sphere as a once per day SLA. An incredible defense option, but the blind effect is also capped by an HD limit.

Verdict: Lalaci is sort of an odd one. He offers a bunch of bonuses against several different effects such as fire and light, but his SLAs are capped by HD. Characters with angst are drawn to him, but whether this is a good or bad thing depends on how well the character is played. All in all, meh.

Lymnieris

Alignment: Lawful Good

Areas of Concern: Prostitution, Rites of Passage, Virginity

Character: Lymnieris is another sex-positive god in the empyreal pantheon. He assists virgins from being forcefully deflowered against their will. He also assists sex workers that love their jobs and obey the law. He is friends with Arshea, the other sex-positive god in the pantheon.

Domains: Community, Good (Archon, Friendship), Law (Archon), Protection (Purity), (Lust)

Favored Weapon (****): Longsword. One-handed martial weapon.

Obedience (*-***): As with Arshea, Lymnieris’s obedience is VERY NSFW. You have to lie naked on a hard flat surface and orgasm without touching yourself. The key aspects of the phrase is “not touching yourself.” Not only is this obedience lewd, but it is also difficult. There are some real life methods on how to do this, but for obvious reasons, I am not linking them here.

For your reward, healing spells you cast heal one more point of damage per die. Healing is always nice, just not in combat. Just be sure to have a positive constitution modifier before you do this obedience.

Boons:

  1. (***) These SLAs can help you avoid getting hurt in combat or possibly prevent combat from starting. Eagle’s Splendor boosts your charisma score, which helps you talk to people. Sanctuary makes it so that enemies don’t want to hit you. Archon’s Aura makes enemies that want to hurt you either pass a will save or take a penalty to attacks and AC until they actually hit you.
  2. (****) Permanent Greater Polymorph! This spell is an incredibly versatile spell, being able to change any willing party member into several versatile forms. The fact that this is permanent until it is used again is icing on the cake.
  3. (****) Once per day, you can make a touch attack on a target and cause them to become younger. They lose mental stats, don’t gain any physical stats and gain the simple child template. This can be done on the same target as well, causing them to suffer more mental stat debuffs as well as a further loss in size. As a gameplay mechanic, this is actually very potent, with some unintended benefits on the side. Since there is no listed duration, this means that the effect can be considered permanent. Becoming a child forever practically means you and your party have gained immortality. Symbolism wise, I get that this ability is supposed to give people with bad childhoods a second chance, but since Lymnieris also doubles as a goddess of sexuality, this can be interpreted in another unsavory fashion.

Verdict: Lymnieris is definitely a progressive god to follow, but I can’t help but think that this god could have been handled a bit better. The obedience is bound to set some people off. Also the third boon, while powerful, can definitely be interpreted as a boon made for depraved characters such as sex criminals. Thank goodness Lymnieris is a Lawful Good deity. If you can get past those two details, Lymnieris is an exceptionally powerful god.

Olheon

Alignment: Lawful Good

Areas of Concern: Deservedness, Nobility, Rulership

Character: She is the final word in rightful rulers. Knowing the names of every leader, whether it be through inheritance or revolution, she ensures that their rule is just and merciful. Olheon shows no mercy to tyrants, especially when she works with her empyreal colleague, Dammerich. Was once a friend of Tabris, the exiled Scholar-Warrior who wrote the Book of the Damned.

Domains: Good (Archon), Law (Archon), Nobility (Hubris, Leadership, Martyr), Protection (Defense)

Favored Weapon (****): Halberd. Two-handed martial weapon that can inflict piercing or slashing damage. Has the trip and brace qualities.

Obedience (****): Pour hot wax on your forehead and mark it with the sigil ring of a noble royal family. Ouch. As long as you have that mark on your forehead, you gain a bonus against compulsion and charm effects.

Boons:

  1. (***) These SLAs are all based around bossing people around, although given that they are enchantment spells, creatures immune to mind-affecting abilities don’t have to worry about them. Enthrall makes you the center of everyone’s attention. Command allows you to control people by giving them simple commands to follow. Suggestion allows you to plant an idea into someone’s mind.
  2. (*) You can cast a modified Mark of Justice that has a casting time of 1 round and penalizes the target by -6 wisdom and charisma. However, the target is prohibitively exact; it must be placed on someone that has shirked their duty or acted in a way unbecoming of an official. With the last detail in mind, it becomes hard to use this ability.
  3. (****) You gain the Leadership feat. This is already powerful enough, but if you already have the feat, you gain a +2 bonus to your leadership score. You also gain an archon cohort whose HD scales with yours to be -2 to your level.

Verdict: If your character is related to royalty in any manner, whether it be by blood or binding law, then you cannot go wrong with Olheon. Her boons definitely make her a people person, allowing her worshippers to command with ease while resisting the manipulations of others.

Picoperi

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Pranks, Jokes, Surprises

Character: A prankster at heart, Picoperi is full of surprises. He assists alchemists in the jungle by surprising them with new discoveries. On the battlefield, he surprises his enemies with well placed ambushes. His trickery is well known among the planes, too; the demon lord Urxehl once laughed at his jokes. Urxehl hasn’t forgiven him since.

Domains: Chaos (Azata, Revelry, Whimsy), Charm, Good (Azata), Trickery (Deception)

Favored Weapon (*): Blowgun. A simple ranged weapon with low damage by itself, but can deliver poisons.

Obedience (**): Prank someone. If nobody’s around, write an original joke and leave it for someone to find. You get a bonus to AC during the surprise round, no matter whether you act in it or not.

Boons:

  1. (***) Another all-around set of SLAs. Invisibility twice a day is incredibly useful for a lot of reasons. Lesser Confusion deals out a useful-if-it-works debuff, but only for one round. Suggestion can be useful with the right applications.
  2. (****) Enlarged Mislead three times per day, heightened to 7th level. Two great spells for the price of one! And it is metamagic’d to have a larger range! And it has a higher level, meaning it can go even further on top of that! Very nice, especially if you’re a trickster type.
  3. (****) Whenever you or an ally rolls initiative within 30 feet of you, you and your allies can take one person’s roll instead of theirs. They still add their own modifiers to the roll instead of the person they took the roll from. Initiative can make or break a game, so being able to go first ahead of every enemy is ludicrously useful.

Verdict: I like Picoperi a lot; he’s fun, he’s good, he pissed off a demon lord, and his boons and obedience are both incredibly useful.

Pulura

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Constellations, Homesickness, Northern Lights

Character: Appearing as a female Tian dancer, this empyreal lord was worshipped in the lost nation of Sarkoris alongside the Demon Lord Kostchtchie as dualistic deities of cold. She assists those lost in the cold wilderness and attacks her foes by using starlight as sling bullets.

Domains: Air (Clouds), Chaos (Azata), Good (Azata), Weather (Seasons), (Stars)

Favored Weapon (*): Sling. Simple ranged weapon. You can use ordinary rocks as ammo for a penalty, thus have infinite ammo.

Obedience (***): Study the stars or draw the constellations on a wall. The  strength of any magical light source you hold or conjure increases by 10 ft. If you are playing a race that doesn’t have any special vision, this can help a lot.

Boons:

  1. (***) These SLAs help you find your way and prevent you from becoming lost, or at least from being ambushed. Color Spray is great at low levels, but a hard HD cap diminishes its effectiveness at later levels. Continual Flame acts like a permanent Light cantrip, shedding light like a torch. Since it’s an SLA, it bypasses a minor material cost, but also might diminish its permanence. Check with your GM. Guiding Star helps you find an area again after you left it. Situational, but can be great for RPs.
  2. (***) You can make a number of sling bullets equal to your HD into +2 Brilliant Energy bullets three times per day. Since Brilliant Energy is a +4 magical effect, this means you can effectively upgrade your bullets by +6. Brilliant Energy also allows you to get past AC. Doesn’t help you with certain enemies like undead or constructs, though, so be careful. They also shed light, so it benefits from your obedience.
  3. (*-**) You can cast Interplanetary Teleport once per day. Since most campaigns are stuck on one planet, this will probably see little use. Unless you worship Pulara in Starfinder somehow, then this will probably be a helluva capstone.

Verdict: Thematic, but underpowered in some respects. If you are a sling user, the second boon will probably be great for you.

Ragathiel

Alignment: Lawful Good

Areas of Concern: Chivalry, Duty, Vengeance

Character: Ragathiel is the son of Dispater, one of the archdevils of hell, and a minor fire deity named Feronia. He takes more after his mom since he adopted her favored weapon and also since Dispater tore off one of his six wings. He swore to fight against his father and join the heavens, but had trouble gaining their trust on account of his heritage and the rage in his heart. To this end, he headed off into the Maelstrom for 16 years to strangle the life out of puberty one particularly nasty protean. He later threw down the infernal duke Deumus and slew Typhon, the first ruler of the first layer of Hell, although the latter was probably was because Asmodeus wanted to teach Typhon a lesson. After all of this, he was finally accepted into the ranks of the empyreal lords. He was assisted by Iomedae’s herald at one point before the goddess ascended through the Starstone.

In other words, Lawful Good Barbarian + overwrought backstory = RAEGTHIEL, THE ANGRIEST ANGEL EVER.

Domains: Destruction (RAGE), Good (Archon), Law (Archon, Judgment), Nobility (Martyr)

Favored Weapon (****): Bastard Sword. Exotic one-handed martial weapon. Works similarly to a regular longsword but with an upgraded damage die. Can be wielded as a martial weapon if used with two hands.

Obedience (*-****): Definitely my least favorite obedience in terms of practicality. The obedience requires you to KILL ONE GUILTY EVIL PERSON PER DAY. Unless your character is a legal executioner with a constantly full schedule, this is going to be almost next to impossible. You cannot just kill things that perform evil actions such as a fiendish animal. They have to be “Proven” to be a wrongdoer. On the bright side, this also means that you can’t be mistaken for killing an innocent, by your own error or otherwise. For this arduous task, you gain a bonus against any evil creatures spells and effects. Considering how often you’ll be fighting evil things, this is great, but MAN, the obedience makes you fight for it!

Boons:

  1. (***) These SLAs are offensively minded, and are especially useful on the front lines. Bless Weapon three times per day can get past enemy DR, strike evil incorporeal creatures and automatically confirms crits on evil targets. Litany of Righteousness can make quick work of evil foes, but only if you have a good aura. Magic Vestment enhances your armor, making you harder to hit.
  2. (***) If anything hits you in battle, you gain a bonus to damage with slashing weapons equal to half your HD. DR issues aside, half your hit die as damage can be pretty solid depending on when you get the boon.
  3. (****) You can cast Holy Aura once per day. On top of the already great buffs it grants you, you get DR 10/Good and Cold Iron and and treat your weapon as a +5 Holy weapon. It may only apply to you, but that’s still good. The duration may suck depending on when you get it, though.

Verdict: Ragathiel is a bit of a base-breaker for a lot of reasons; his “I Hate You, Archdevil Dad” backstory coupled with his large personal accomplishments can cause people to either paint him as a “Gary Stu”. His obedience of killing one bad guy a day also doesn’t win him any favor. But he’s still ferociously Lawful Good, and the fact that he was born evil and took the hard effort to become good in the first place can appeal to a lot of players. His actual boons and what you get from the obedience are definitely strong effects.

Rowdrosh

Alignment: Neutral Good

Areas of Concern: Herd Animals, Husbandry, Shepards

Character: He shows up in a lot of Kellid myths. Essentially, he helped herd animals such as sheep and caribou actually gather in herds. Hates predatory beasts such as schirs and worgs.

Domains: Animal (Fur), Good (Agathion), Protection (Defense), Travel (Trade)

Favored Weapon (*): Quarterstaff. Simple two handed weapon with the monk and double weapon qualities.

Obedience (*): Scatter and re-gather a pile of grain. For this tedious task, you gain a bonus to AC against attacks of opportunity from moving out of a threatened space. Narrow bonus, mundane task, not a very interesting obedience.

Boons:

  1. (*) Lots of animal based SLAs here, which heavily reduces their general usefulness. Calm Animals is self explanatory, Animal Trance fascinates them, and Dominate Animal controls them.
  2. (***) If ewe gotta move, you gotta move! You can cast Summon Stampede three times a day, and its heightened to 7th level. This is a great way to perform crowd-control, especially considering how large the stampede is and how far the effects can reach.
  3. (***) You can cast something similar to Ethereal Jaunt and include one ally per HD you have. You can also communicate telepathically with each other. These effects persist as long as you are all 30 feet of one another. What makes this expecially powerful is that there isn’t a set in stone duration, so it can be implied that this can be an at-will effect. You all have to jaunt as a bunch of herd animals, though, so that can be weird.

Verdict: His obedience is weak, and the boons don’t have a clear focus outside of the animal theme. Still, they are nice to have regardless, even if the SLAs from the first boon are incredibly narrow in application.

Seramaydiel

Alignment: Neutral Good

Areas of Concern: Communication, Inspiration, Music

Character: A lover of instrumental music, she leads souls stolen away from Astradaemons back to Nirvana. Although she is a diplomat at heart, she is not above using trickery in combat, being able to lull foes to sleep and slaying them.

Domains: Air (Wind), Charm, Good (Agathion, Friendship), Nobility (Leadership)

Favored Weapon (*): Blowgun. A simple ranged weapon with low damage by itself, but can deliver poisons.

Obedience (**): Play an instrument in public and have people hear you. If nobody is around, compose an original poem based on Seramaydiel. You get a bonus on saving throws against sonic effects.

Boons:

  1. (***) These SLAs fit Seramaydiel’s character perfectly. Comprehend Languages allows you to be the perfect diplomat and understand any language. Whispering Wind lets you send short messages over several miles. Sculpt Sound can help you pull off a ton of tricks and has a ton of applications.
  2. (***) Silenced Brilliant Inspiration. Being able to roll twice and take the higher is always a powerful effect, especially when it applies to a lot of rolls. Silenced would have removed the only component it had left, but since it’s an SLA, this is redundant.
  3. (****) Once per day, you can affect everything within 30 feet of you as if you cast Hold Monster. Paralysis is a great status effect, but I hope you have a good Perform skill: that’s the DC.

Verdict: Seramaydiel is an interesting lord to follow, with some nice boons and a flexibly interpretable character.

Sinashakti

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Journeys, Joy, Messengers

Character: He is the embodiment of joy; people pray to him so that they can receive some form of joyous occasion in their lives, such as friendly reunions or safe shelter during long travels. He also acts as a messenger for the empyreal lords. He was rumored to have traveled to Abaddon to personally sent a declaration of war to the Div, although he denies this rumor.

Domains: Chaos (Azata), Good (Azata, Friendship), Luck (Fate), Travel (Exploration)

Favored Weapon (****): Shortbow. Martial ranged weapon with a range of 60 feet.

Obedience (***): Sit crosslegged. Light a candle in front of your face and blow it out with your laughter. Then stand up and put your right foot first. For completing this bizzare ritual, you gain a bonus on saves against spells and effects that would hinder your movement speed.

Boons:

  1. (****) A couple of mobility based SLAs and a rather good buff. Longstrider increased your base speed, while Forest Friend removes some narrow movement penalties regarding forested terrain. Good Hope, as mentioned before, is an excellent buff.
  2. (*-****) Mind-affecting spells that you cast are harder to resist, even more so if they are the same race as you are. Pretty good for spellcasters, useless for martials.
  3. (***) You can cast Word of Recall once per day and can bring up to six people with you. Can be nice as a contingency or as a way to get around.

Verdict: Sinashakti can be nice for characters that move around a lot, such as rogues and melee martial characters.

Soralyon

Alignment: Neutral Good

Areas of Concern: Guardians, Magic, Monuments

Character: Soralyon is a protector of monuments and historical sites. He grants blessings to particularly moral archaeologists, although he does try to dissuade them if they come close to finding evil artifacts. This puts him at odds with Bharnarol and other divine scholarly types.

Domains: Artifice (Construct), Earth (Caves, Petrification), Good, Protection (Defense, Purity)

Favored Weapon (****): Heavy Pick. One handed martial weapon with a x4 crit multiplier.

Obedience (***): Keep a 5-pound marble statuette on you at all times. Trace the shape of it with your fingers and treat it like a teddy bear when you go to sleep. Let the teachings of Soralyon be your lullabies. You get a bonus on saving throws versus magical aging, slowness, and anything that can cause ability score damage. Kinda narrow and a little miscellaneous, but fits with the theme and is easy to do.

Boons:

  1. (***) Pretty bog-standard utility SLAs. Identify works as an improved Detect Magic, Locate Object can help you find stuff, and Glyph of Warding can be a nice trap to set for others, although since it’s an SLA, you probably cannot use the spell rune function.
  2. (****) You get permanent Analyze Dweomer vision, but only for objects. Cursed magical objects can be extremely nasty, so instantly identifying them can be a life saver.
  3. (***) Once-per-day Flesh to Stone is a nasty save-or-die spell. Being able to transform the victim into a stone golem with HD equal to your level for a couple minutes is just salt on the wounds. Sure, it deletes the entire spell after the 1d4 minutes and the golem is limited to protecting a single specific object, but if you need to protect something from getting into the wrong hands, this can work just fine.

Verdict: Soralyon seems a bit bland to me in terms of effectiveness. His obedience grants incredibly narrow bonuses and his first two boons fall into “Boring, but Practical”. Still, there are a lot of evil magical objects in the world, so his character can fit well in a lot of campaigns. Also, the third boon can be really fun, if only just to mess with people.

Tanagaar

Alignment: Lawful Good

Areas of Concern: Night, Owls, Watchfulness

Character: Tanagaar is mainly worshipped by people in positions that require observation, such as guards or lookouts. He keeps tabs on the borders of the multiverse. He is constantly assisted by his archons. Does not like poachers.

Domains: Animal (Feather), Darkness (Moon, Night), Good (Archon), Law (Archon)

Favored Weapon (****): Kukri. Light martial weapon that crits on 18-20.

Obedience (**): Watch a mouse for 100 breaths from a distance of at least 30 feet, then catch and release the mouse in an owl’s hunting ground. For this task, you gain a bonus to saves against anything that would impact your sight or hearing.

Boons:

  1. (***) These SLAs are actually pretty good for sneaking around. Calm Animals can prevent them from giving away your location. Eagle Eye is a great way to learn about your surroundings, especially in urban or forested locations. Deeper Darkness can keep you hidden, even from people with darkvision.
  2. (****) You gain 60 foot darkvision. If you already have it, your darkvision grows by another 60 feet. Darkvision is always great to have, and extensions are always welcome.
  3. (****) You get a sneak attack of +3d6. If you already have a sneak attack, it stacks. If the sneak attack does piercing damage, for every sneak attack damage die you have, it does an additional 2 points of damage. This is incredibly great for martial classes and absolutely gold for rogues.

Verdict: Tanagaar is an excellent deity to worship, especially for characters that like to sneak around. His capstone is an excellent counterpoint to Shax’s demonic obedience, which is the same, only replace “piercing” with “slashing”.

Tolc

Alignment: Chaotic Good

Areas of Concern: Cold, Frozen Wilds, Survival

Character: Tolc helps people survive the frozen wastelands of the world. He is a sworn enemy of the Demon Lord Kostchtchie, and his followers regularly do battle against frost giants and white dragons. Every now and then, he chooses a champion that gains strength and tactical knowledge that helps the champion’s people.

Domains: Chaos (Azata), Good (Azata), Travel (Exploration), Water (Ice), (Storms)

Favored Weapon (*): Javelin. Simple ranged weapon with a range of 30 feet. If used as a melee weapon, you take a -4 penalty on attack rolls.

Obedience (***): Kneel outside at night while naked and hold some lukewarm water above your head until it evaporates. You get a bonus on saves against cold effects.

Boons:

  1. (***) There’s only one consistently good SLA I can recommend here and that is Mount, which you can use three times per day. Even though it’s only limited to caribou, extra mobility options and another meat shield by your side is always nice. Chill Metal can cause some miniscule cold damage to anyone holding an item that can be easily resisted if attended to. Protection from Energy grants you limited immunity to one type of energy, but it’s only once per day.
  2. (**) You get a breath attack that does cold and slashing damage, both of which can easily be resisted through energy or damage resistance. Worse off, enemies can pass a reflex save and halve the damage. At least it’s a 15 foot cone and you can use it three times per day, meaning you can use it on multiple enemies.
  3. (***) You can cast Polar Midnight once per day. The cold damage isn’t the big deal here; it’s the big amount of dexterity damage and the fact that you can freeze enemies alive. This an excellent capstone spell.
