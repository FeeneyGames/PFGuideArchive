Archmage Variel’s Guide to the Vampire Hunter: How Not to Suck

Now that you’ve dealt with my terrible puns, on to the guide. The Vampire Hunter is a little known class produced by paizo from the book Pathfinder Roleplaying Game: The World of Vampire Hunter D. The Vampire Hunter is a sort of hybrid of four Pathfinder classes: the Hunter, Inquisitor, Paladin, and Slayer. The Vampire Hunter utilizes the spell list of the Inquisitor, and the spells per day of the Paladin, while also being a spontaneous caster like the hunter. This makes the Vampire Hunter a 4th-level divine caster. Like all other 4th-level divine casters, the Vampire Hunter has a full base attack bonus, making them a capable martial combatant.

Throughout the advancement of the class, you get several major class abilities. These abilities tend to have the effect of doing either one of two things, helping you kill vampires and the undead, or helping you kill everyone.

In this class guide, I will attempt to explain why you may want to consider playing a Vampire Hunter, review the various class abilities of the Vampire Hunter, and outline how to get the most out of playing a Vampire Hunter.

I’ve been playing tabletop roleplaying games since I could first open a book, and have frequently found guides similar to this to be useful when learning to play a new class. This is my second completed class guide (after Archmage Variel’s Guide to the Shifter), and I hope you find it useful.

Color Coding

In this guide I will be color coding the options and abilities available to you, using the rating system coined by Treantmonk. I will also have a number of stars corresponding to the option’s usefulness, as I am colorblind and it is just as much for maintaining my own sanity and ability to read my own guide as it is for your own use.

Red (*): This is a poor option.

Orange (**): This is an ok option.

Green (***): I recommend this option.

Blue (****): The best option possible.

Black: This option is unranked or contains a very wide range of ranks depending on a variety of factors.

What is a Vampire Hunter?

(And Why Would I Want to Play a Vampire Hunter?)

The Vampire Hunter is a full base attack bonus 4th-level spontaneous caster who, as the name would suggest, is very good at hunting down and killing undead (particularly vampires). The Vampire Hunter possesses vampiric foci. These are supernatural abilities, much like inquisitor judgements, which a Vampire Hunter can use to gain some of the abilities of their chosen prey. While the Vampire Hunter is quite skilled at combating the undead, the class is still a capable melee and ranged combatant against any form of enemy, and optimizing the combat ability of this class against any enemy shall be the focus of this guide. A Vampire Hunter, through study or training, uses the powers of vampirekind to hunt the monsters who stalk the night. Playing a Vampire Hunter, you become a dark-cloaked bringer of death who instills fear in both the living and the dead. Your dedication to the hunt reaches an almost religious devotion, instilling in you a degree of divine power. If you want to play a skilled shadowy crusader and hunter of dangerous supernatural prey, the Vampire Hunter may be the class for you.

Like many full base attack bonus classes, a major concern of your class is dealing damage. The two most optimal approaches to achieving this are two-handed weapons (***) and archery (***). Other builds which are possible to utilize, yet less optimal, include two-weapon fighting (**) and crossbows (*). Further detail concerning these builds can be found below:

Two-Handed Weapon (***): Two-handed weapon builds enable the Vampire Hunter to deal lots of damage to singular targets in close range. Two-handed builds are far less feat intensive than many other builds, however, they require that you be up close and personal with your target.

Archery (***): Archery builds enable the Vampire Hunter to deal a lot of damage to multiple targets at range, as well as make attacks of opportunity at a distance. While archery has a lot of benefits, it is important to note that archery is exceptionally feat intensive.

Two-Weapon Fighting (**): Two-weapon fighting builds do less damage than two handed builds, but are relatively less feat intensive. Much like two-handed weapon builds, two-weapon fighting builds require you to remain within melee distance of your enemy. However, unlike two-handed weapon builds, two-weapon fighting builds utilize multiple attacks per round.

Crossbow (*): The damage output of crossbow builds is very low, however, it requires relatively few feats compared to the other archetypes. As low as the damage is with crossbows, sometimes you just want to play the stereotypical crossbow wielding vampire killer. If you really need to play this build, multiclassing and taking 5 levels in the Gunslinger class with the Bolt Ace archetype will give you a great boost to your damage, and would raise this build to green (***).

The Vampire Hunter Class Abilities

Hit Points (**): d8 hit points is’t much for a martial fighter, but is easier to bare when you’re at a distance.

Base Attack Bonus (****): A full base attack bonus is the best possible.

Saves (***): Good Reflex and Will saves.

Proficiencies (***): All simple and martial weapons, as well as light armor, medium armor, and shields (except tower shields). This is a good range of options.

Skills (***): 6+ skill ranks and a lot of useful skills.

Detect Undead (Sp) (*): This is a very situational ability, but it’s very useful if you’re looking for the undead.

Technique Feat (****): At 1st, 3rd and every three levels after 3rd, you get to choose between either a bonus combat feat or a bonus technique feat. Many of the technique feats are lacking in their usefulness, but any recommended feats can be found in the Feat section later in this guide.

Track (Ex) (*): Very situational.

Vampiric Focus (Su) (***): A few times per day, you can give yourself a buff which gives you access to various abilities and stat increases. I will go into more detail about the choices in this ability in a later section.

Relentless (Ex) (*): Very situational.

Stake (Su) (*): Very situational.

Spellcasting (**): Very few spells per day from a limited spell list. However, the spells you do have access to complement this class exceptionally well.

Relentless Band (Ex) (*): Like it’s parent class ability, very situational.

Vampire Tracker (Ex) (*): This is somehow more situational than track, but if you’re playing a Vampire Hunter, you may find this useful for hunting vampires.

Swift Tracker (Ex) (*): Very situational.

Vampire Bane (Su) (*): This ability is exceptional when confronting vampires. It serves no use against anyone else.

Remove Vampirism (Su) (*): This ability is exceptional when you have been fighting vampires. It has no use at any other time.

Quarry (Ex) (**): This would be great if it didn't require you to target an undead enemy, and specifically an undead enemy that you are aware is undead.

Critical Reflexes (Su) (**): This is an interesting ability, and with the use of magical items this could have some interesting applications, however it is limited to use against the undead.

Improved Quarry (Ex) (***): Dramatic improvement to Quarry, but you still need to use it on an undead enemy.

Master Vampire Hunter (Ex) (****): This is an exceptionally powerful ability against undead enemies, with the added benefit of giving to the constant effects of one of your vampiric foci.

Ability Scores

How you prioritize your ability scores depends on what type of fighting you plan to use. Because of this, the stats will have four ratings: archery (Bow), two-handed weapon (2h), two-weapon fighting (TWF), and crossbow (Xbow).

Strength (Bow (***)/2h (****)/TWF (*)/Xbow (*)): Strength is used for martial weapons and adds to the damage of composite bows. This is an important source of damage for both archery and two-handed weapon builds. Two-weapon fighting and crossbow builds don’t get any damage or accuracy from this stat, but may not want to dump it if their GM requires they keep track of carrying capacity.

Dexterity (Bow (****)/2h (**)/TWF (****)/Xbow (****)): Dexterity impacts the accuracy and survivability of archery, two-weapon fighting, and crossbow builds. This makes it a crucial stat for all three builds. For two-handed builds, Dexterity is slightly less useful, but has some impact on the individuals armor class.

Constitution (Bow (**)/2h (**)/TWF (**)/Xbow (**)): For all Vampire Hunters, Constitution is nice for gaining extra hit points, and is useful considering your low base Fortitude saving bonus.

Intelligence (Bow (**)/2h (**)/TWF (**)/Xbow (**)): A higher Intelligence gives you extra skill ranks. Extra skill ranks allow you to do better with your skills. This can be useful.

Wisdom (Bow (***)/2h (***)/TWF (***)/Xbow (***)): All Vampire Hunters use Wisdom as their casting stat, and will need a Wisdom score of at least 14 in order to cast spells at all their available spell levels. Having a Wisdom score past 14 should not be your top priority.

Charisma (Bow (*)/2h (*)/TWF (*)/Xbow (*)): Unless you really need to be the parties face (at which point you really need to be considering a different class) Charisma is a dump stat for you. It is helpful with increasing your social skills, and impacts vampire bane, which is an incredibly situational ability.

Races

I will overview the standard races, however there are many other races within the Pathfinder RPG for you to choose from, many of which would make exceptional Vampire Hunters.

Dwarf (***): The dwarves ability score racial traits are good for any build. You get darkvision and some very nice defensive bonuses. The dwarf is an overall nice option for a Vampire Hunter.

Elf (**): The elves ability score racial traits are ok, but not great, so long as you’re not playing a two-handed weapon build, for which this is ranked red (*). The immunities, bonus to perception, and low light vision are all quite useful, however weapon familiarity is not as useful for you. You should consider the Long-Limbed and Fey-Sighted alternate racial traits, or the Crossbow Training alternate racial trait if you plan to play with a crossbow build.

Gnome (*): The gnomes ability score racial traits are terrible, and very little of the rest of this race is of use to you. I would recommend choosing another race.

Half-Elf (***): A flexible ability score bonus is great for any build, as is Skill Focus, Elven Immunities, Keen Senses, and Low-Light Vision. Multitalented’s usefulness really depends on whether you decide to multiclass. You should consider the Reflexive Improvisation alternate racial trait.

Half-Orc (****): A flexible ability score bonus is great for any build. Intimidating, Orc Ferocity, and Darkvision are all nice abilities. Weapon Familiarity is an especially great trait for archery builds, as this gives you proficiency with the Orc Hornbow. You should consider the Sacred Tattoo alternate racial trait.

Halfling (**):  The halflings ability score racial traits are ok, so long as you’re not playing a two-handed weapon build, for which this is ranked red (*). If you are playing a two weapon fighting or crossbow build, this race is instead ranked green (***). Fearless, Halfling Luck, Keen Senses, and Sure Footed are all useful to you, however weapon familiarity is not as useful for you. You should consider the Skulker alternate racial trait.

Human (****): The flexible ability score bonus and bonus feat are amazing, and an extra skill point every level is great too. You should consider the Military Tradition alternate racial trait.

Feats

As a Vampire Hunter, you gain access to bonus feats from your class in addition to any other feats you may possess. The bonus feats available to a Vampire Hunter must be chosen from either combat feats or vampire hunting technique feats. Such feats will be marked with a ☆ to denote that they may be chosen for your technique feat class ability. Your choice of feats is critical to your your ability to succeed in combat as a Vampire Hunter. Below are some of the most notable feats available to Vampire Hunters.

Clustered Shot ☆ (****): This is a must have for ranged builds as it allows you to negate a lot of damage reduction.

Critical Focus ☆ (****)/Staggering Critical ☆ (**)/Stunning Critical ☆ (**): +4 bonus to critical confirmation rolls. This is useful for builds with a lot of attacks. Staggering Critical and Stunning Critical add buffs to your critical hits.

Crossbow Mastery ☆ (***): This feat allows you to full attack with your chosen crossbow. You also no longer provoke attacks of opportunity while reloading. This is very useful for crossbow builds.

Deadly Aim ☆ (****): Sacrifice accuracy for damage. The equivalent of power attack for ranged attacks. This is a crucial feat for any ranged build.

Exotic Weapon Proficiency (***): Many of the best weapons available require you take this feat for proficiency. That makes this an good feat to have for increasing your damage on many builds.

Extend Spell (**): Make a spell last twice as long in exchange for taking one more level to cast. This is useful for any type of build.

Extended Vampiric Focus ☆ (**): Add your Wisdom modifier to the number of minutes per day that you can use your vampiric focus ability. This is useful for any type of build.

Great Fortitude (**)/ Improved Great Fortitude (**): Vampire Hunter’s have a low base Fortitude saving bonus. This feat chain helps mitigate that, and is useful for any type of build.

Heavy Armor Proficiency ☆ (**): Allows you to wear heavy armor without penalty. Any build that heavily focuses on dexterity will want to stay away from this feat, but it could be useful for two-handed weapon builds.

Improved Critical ☆ (***): You can double the crit range of your weapon. This is especially useful for builds that use weapons with already large crit ranges.

Improved Initiative ☆ (**): This gives you a +4 bonus to your Initiative checks. It is not as critical for you to have a high Initiative as some other classes, but it is still helpful.

Manyshot ☆ (****): When making a full-attack action with a bow, your first attack fires two arrows. This increases your overall damage, and so is a great feat for archery builds to take.

Martial Focus ☆ (**)/Burrowing Shot ☆ (***): Martial Focus gives you a +1 bonus on damage rolls with a fighter weapon group, and enables you to take weapon mastery feats for that fighter weapon group. Burrowing Shot gives you a debuff to be applied by your ranged attacks. These are good feats for ranged builds.

Overwatch Style ☆ (**)/Overwatch Tactician ☆ (**): This style allows you to ready two ranged attacks with your chosen weapon as a full round action. Overwatch Tactician changes this to a standard action. This can be useful for ranged builds.

Painful Blow ☆ (**): Good for disrupting casters. An ok feat to take for two-handed weapon builds.

Pinpoint Targeting ☆ (**): This would be a much more interesting feat if it was available sooner than level 16. A feat to consider for ranged builds.

Piranha Strike ☆ (****): Sacrifice accuracy for damage. The equivalent of power attack for Weapon Finesse attacks. This is a crucial feat for two-weapon fighting builds.

Point Blank Shot ☆ (**): In of itself, this feat is not incredibly useful, however, it is required for many of your best ranged build feats.

Power Attack ☆ (****)/Furious Focus ☆ (**): Power Attack sacrifice accuracy for damage. This is a crucial feat for two-handed weapon builds. Furious Focus makes you not suffer power attack penalty on the first attack with a two-handed weapon. This increases your chance to hit, which is always helpful.

Precise Shot ☆ (****)/Improved Precise Shot ☆ (****): Precise Shot allows you make ranged attacks on your opponent who is engaged in melee without taking the standard –4 penalty on your attack roll. With Improved Precise shot, your ranged attacks ignore the AC bonus granted to targets by anything less than total cover, and the miss chance granted to targets by anything less than total concealment. These are both great feats for any ranged build.

Rapid Reload ☆ (**): This reduces the amount of time required for you to reload your chosen weapon (in this case likely a crossbow). This is a required feat for crossbow builds.

Rapid Shot ☆ (****): Make an extra ranged attack at your full base attack bonus while full attacking. All of your attack rolls take a –2 penalty when using Rapid Shot. This is great for increasing the damage of ranged attack builds.

Snap Shot ☆ (**)/Improved Snap Shot ☆ (**)/Greater Snap Shot ☆ (***): This feat chain enables you to make attacks of opportunity with ranged weapons. This is a good feat chain to consider for ranged builds.

Step Up ☆ (**)/Following Step ☆ (***)/Step Up and Strike ☆ (**): This feat chain allows you to remain within melee distance of your intended target. Good for any melee build to consider.

Toughness (**): Additional hit points are useful, especially for builds that remain in melee combat.

Two-Weapon Fighting ☆ (***)/Improved Two-Weapon Fighting ☆ (****)/Greater Two-Weapon Fighting ☆ (**): Two-Weapon fighting, put simply, allows you to make attacks with both your primary and off-hand weapons at a reduced penalty. Improved Two-Weapon Fighting allows you to make an extra attack with your off-hand at a -5 penalty. Greater Two-Weapon Fighting allows you to make an third attack with your off-hand at a -10 penalty. You are less likely to hit with this attack, making it not as crucial as the previous two feats. This feat chain is critical for succeeding in a two-weapon fighting build.

Vital Strike ☆ (**)/Improved Vital Strike ☆ (**)/Greater Vital Strike ☆ (**)/Devastating Strike ☆ (**): Vital Strike lets you double your weapon damage as a standard action. Improved Vital Strike changes this to triple your weapon damage, and Greater Vital Strike changes this to quadruple your weapon damage. These feats are nice for two-handed weapon builds, but not for much else.

Weapon Finesse ☆ (**): Allows you to use your dexterity modifier in place of your Strength modifier for making attack rolls. Crucial for two-weapon fighting builds.

Weapon Focus ☆ (***): Increases your accuracy with a single weapon. Great for any Vampire Hunter Build

Skills

Your 6 + Int modifier skill points are relatively good, and you have quite a few great class skills for you to invest points into. You may want to consider investing ranks in non-class skills, however I have listed only the class skills for this guide.

Bluff (Cha) (***): This is an important social skill to have, though you won’t likely be as good at it as someone with a high Charisma score.

Climb (Str) (**): Situational. Occasionally used depending on the location of the campaign.

Craft (Int) (*): I can’t think of many reasons you would want to take this.

Handle Animal (Cha) (*): Unless you’re planning on getting an animal companion, this is very situational.

Heal (Wis) (***): This is a great way to help keep your party alive outside of combat.

Intimidate (Cha) (***): This is an important social skill to have, though you won’t likely be as good at it as someone with a high Charisma score.

Knowledge (arcana) (Int) (****): Gives you information on ancient mysteries, magic traditions, arcane symbols, constructs, dragons, and magical beasts. This is incredible useful.

Knowledge (geography) (Int) (**): Situational. Gives you information on lands, terrain, climate, and people.

Knowledge (local) (Int) (***): Gives you information on legends, personalities, inhabitants, laws, customs, traditions, and humanoids. This is a useful skill to have ranks in.

Knowledge (religion) (Int) (****): Gives you information on gods and goddesses, mythic history, ecclesiastic tradition, holy symbols, and undead. This is incredible useful.

Perception (Wis) (****): This is the most used skill in the game, and you should have a pretty good Wisdom score on top of that. Put ranks in it when you can.

Profession (Wis) (*): Very situational. If you’re looking to spend the game running a business, you’re probably playing the wrong game.

Ride (Dex) (*): You’re not really built for mounted combat. Very little reason to put any ranks in this.

Sense Motive (Wis) (***): This is an important social skill to have, though you won’t likely be as good at it as someone with a high Wisdom score.

Spellcraft (Int) (***): Allows you to identify spells. This is a helpful skill to have, though it becomes less necessary if you have another caster in the party who would be better at this skill.

Stealth (Dex) (***): You likely have a decent Dexterity score. You’re no rogue, but you could definitely serve as a scout if you need to.

Survival (Wis) (***): It’s very situational, but your high wisdom score and tracking class abilities can make this a nice skill to have if your GM ever has you running wilderness encounters.

Swim (Str) (**): Situational. Occasionally used depending on the location of the campaign.

Traits

Here are some of the most useful traits for Vampire Hunters.

Adopted (Social) (****): Pick a trait from another race.

Deadeye Bowman (Religion) (****): While using a longbow, and only a single creature is providing soft cover to your target, your target does not receive the +4 bonus to AC. This is a great trait to have for archery builds.

Dirty Fighter (Combat) (****): Deal +1 damage on every attack you make against an enemy you are flanking. This is a great trait to have for two-handed weapon and two-weapon fighting builds.

Elven Reflexes (Race, Half-Elf) (****): +2 trait bonus to Initiative checks. A higher initiative bonus is good for any build. Remember that trait bonuses don’t stack.

Fate’s Favored (Faith) (****): Luck bonuses you get are increased by +1, which you are likely to get some use out of when using divine favor.

Indomitable Faith (Faith) (***): Gain a +1 bonus on Will saves.

Reactionary (Combat) (****): Another +2 trait bonus to Initiative checks. A higher initiative bonus is good for any build. Remember that trait bonuses don’t stack.

Resilient (Combat) (***): Gain a +1 bonus on Fortitude saves.

Warrior of Old (Race, Elf) (****): Still another way to get +2 trait bonus to Initiative checks. A higher initiative bonus is good for any build. Remember that trait bonuses don’t stack.

Equipment

Equipment, including magic item choices are a critical part of any build. Below is a list of useful equipment for the Vampire Hunter.

Armor

Hide Armor (***): Good starting armor for you until you can afford better.

Celestial Armor (****): This is a great armor later in your adventuring career for builds which have a higher Dexterity score, like archery, crossbow, and two-weapon fighting builds.

Mithral Breastplate (****): This is a great armor later in your adventuring career for builds which have a lower Dexterity score, like two-handed weapon builds.

Mithral Buckler (***): This can be a useful and relatively inexpensive item for an archery or crossbow build to pick up.

Magic Armor Special Properties

Bolstering (***): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus. Can be applied to medium armor, heavy armor, light shields, heavy shields, and tower shields. Get a competence bonus on saving throws against a creature you damage for a turn after damaging them.

Buoyant (**): This armor or shield property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. It is relatively cheap at later levels, and negates the armor check penalty for Swim checks.

Creeping (***): This armor property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. It is relatively cheap at later levels. The armor check penalty does not apply to Stealth checks, and the wearer can get the armor’s enhancement bonus to their Stealth checks for one minute once per day.

Deathless (***): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus. Armor exclusive. Gain resistance against positive energy or negative energy. Chance to ignore negative levels from any attack.

Determination (****): This armor or shield property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. This can be relatively expensive, but the property can be literally life saving. Once per day when the wearer reaches zero or fewer hit points, this item casts the breath of life spell.

Etherealness (****): This armor property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. This can be relatively expensive, but the ability it gives is very useful. The wearer can become ethereal once per day for as long as they desire.

Expeditious (***): This armor property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. It is relatively cheap at later levels. Three times per day as a swift action, the wearer gains a bonus to the distance one can move using any mode of movement for a round.

Ghost Touch (***): Costs a +3 enhancement bonus. It is expensive, but critical when fighting incorporeal opponents.

Glamered (***): This armor property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. It is relatively cheap at later levels. The armor can change its shape and appearance to assume the form of a normal set of clothing.

Mental Focus (****): This armor property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus, and can only be applied to medium armor. It is relatively cheap at later levels. Grants its wearer an enhancement bonus on concentration checks.

Restful (****): This armor property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. It is relatively cheap at later levels. The wearer only needs two hours of sleep, and does not become fatigued while wearing this armor.

Righteous/Unbound/Unrighteous/Vigilant (****): These armor properties costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. These can be relatively expensive, but the ability they give is very useful. Once per day, the wearer can activate an effect similar to the righteous might spell, lasting 10 rounds. You can use multiple versions of these properties on the same armor, so long as your alignment isn’t opposed to the special property in question.

Sensing (****): Costs a +3 enhancement bonus. Armor exclusive. When the wearer is blinded or in magical darkness, they gain blindsense and blindsight. Not as useful for ranged builds due to the range.

Shadow/Shadow, Improved/Shadow, Greater (***): These armor properties costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. The cost depends on which of the properties you take. Grants the wearer a competence bonus on Stealth checks.

Spell Storing (***): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus. Armor exclusive. Store a single touch spell of up to 3rd level in the armor, and then cast it on a target when that target hits you with a melee attack or melee touch attack.

Titanic (****): Costs a +3 enhancement bonus. Armor exclusive. Among several other bonuses, this armor give its wearer a once per day enlarge person effect for 1 minute.

Weapons

Butchering Axe (****): A great exotic two-handed melee weapon. Great for two-handed weapon builds. Taking a level in the Vampire Hunter class does not give you proficiency with this weapon, so you much get proficiency by another means.

(Composite) Orc Hornbow (****): A great exotic ranged weapon. Great for archery builds. Has a lower range increment than that of a longbow, but deals more damage in turn. Taking a level in the Vampire Hunter class does not give you proficiency with this weapon, so you much get proficiency by another means.

Falcata (****): A great exotic one-handed melee weapon. Combine with the effortless lace to dual wield them on two-weapon fighting builds. Taking a level in the Vampire Hunter class does not give you proficiency with this weapon, so you much get proficiency by another means.

Kukri (***): Use this as your off hand weapon for two-weapon fighting builds while you are trying to get your hands on an effortless lace to wield a second falcata.

Heavy Crossbow (**): Not an exceptional weapon, but necessary for a crossbow build.

Magic Weapon Special Properties

Adaptive (****): Bow exclusive. This weapon property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. An archery build should have this on their composite bow.

Agile (****): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus, melee exclusive. Use your Dexterity modifier when calculating damage with a melee weapons usable with weapon finesse

Anarchic/Axiomatic/Holy/Unholy (***): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus. Deal additional damage against creatures of an alignment opposed to the weapon’s. Also helps to get through damage reduction respective to the weapon.

Bane (***): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus. This enhancement bonus is great if you can reliably predict what types of enemies you are going to face.

Conserving (***): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus, ranged exclusive. If you miss with a projectile attack, the projectile isn’t consumed.

Corrosive (***)/Flaming (**)/Frost (**)/Shock (***): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus. Deals extra elemental damage on a successful hit.

Corrosive Burst (***)/Flaming Burst (**)/Frost Burst (**)/Shock Burst (***): Costs a +2 enhancement bonus. Functions as a corrosive/flaming/frost/shock weapon, but with additional elemental damage dealt on a critical hit.

Distance (***): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus, ranged exclusive. Doubles the range increment of the weapon.

Ghost Touch (***): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus, melee and ammunition exclusive. Deals damage normally against incorporeal creatures.

Glamered (***): This weapon property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. The weapon can change its shape and appearance to assume the form of another object of similar size.

Greater Designating (****): Costs a +4 enhancement bonus, ranged and ammunition exclusive. An improved version of lesser designating. Allies gain a morale bonus on melee attack rolls and melee damage rolls against the designated target for 1 round after the target is hit by this weapon.

Impact (****): Costs a +2 enhancement bonus, exclusive to melee weapons which are not light weapons. Among other things, this weapon deals damage as if it were one size category larger than it is.

Impervious (***): This weapon property costs gold, not an enhancement bonus. You should put this on all your magic weapons to ensure that they don’t simply break.

Keen (****): Costs a +1 enhancement bonus, piercing or slashing melee exclusive. Increases the critical threat range of the affected weapon.

Vampiric Foci

Vampiric focus enables the Vampire Hunter to select vampire foci. Each vampire focus can be used a number of minutes per day equal to your level. The vampire hunter can have only one vampiric focus active at a time. You may select one vampire foci at 1st, 8th, and 16th levels. At level 20, each day when you regains your spells per day, you choose one vampiric focus to be active on yourself for the entire day. This focus is in addition to uses of your vampiric focus class ability. Below I have rated and detailed the vampire foci available for you to select.

Vampiric Agility (**): This increases your movement speed and later gives you evasion and improved evasion. Not a crucial foci for you to take.

Vampiric Call (****): Provides you with temporary hit points and, later, a deflection bonus. This is a great focus for a Vampire Hunter, especially one engaged in melee combat. It’s important to note that you can drop out of a vampiric focus as a free action, and enter as a swift, allowing you to utilize this ability to soak up a surprisingly large amount of damage overall.

Vampiric Cunning (***): Gain bonuses to Perception on Stealth. These can be helpful to you as a Vampire Hunter.

Vampiric Heart (***): Energy resistance and several other life saving abilities. This is helpful for staying alive.

Vampiric Might (***): This is an incredibly useful stat bonus for any Vampire Hunter who makes use of their Strength score. Not as necessary if you plan to have a belt which increases your Strength ability score.

Vampiric Momentum (****): Gives you a range of useful movement related abilities.

Vampiric Presence (***): While the stat bonus this gives you isn’t incredibly useful to Vampire Hunters, the spell-like abilities this focus give you access to are great. A nice vampiric focus to consider.

Vampiric Resilience (**): You gain protection against energy drain. This can be slightly situational vampiric focus.

Vampiric Resolve (*): Gain a bonus to save against mind-affecting effects. Situational.

Vampiric Sight (***): You gain several sight related abilities which can be incredibly useful to you.
