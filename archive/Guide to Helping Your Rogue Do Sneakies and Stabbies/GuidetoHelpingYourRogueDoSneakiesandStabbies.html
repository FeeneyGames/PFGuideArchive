Guide to Helping Your Rogue Do Sneakies and Stabbies

Hello Friendly Party Member,

We rogues try to be as self-sufficient as possible, but the truth is no matter how min-maxed we might be, a lot of our success in combat depends on how much or how little our friends help us. On our own, we’re basically bad, squishy fighters. With cooperative friends, we can be the biggest damage-dealers on the field. We know you have many demands on your limited resources, from fighting to battlefield control to buffing everyone who’s asking for it, and helping your sneaky buddy is probably less important than all of that, but if you find yourself able to assist in the quest for kidney-plucking, these pages will teach you how to help best.

Effects that Allow for Sneak Attack

Anything that denies dexterity bonus to our enemies or allows for flanking makes sneak attack possible. Here are several of the conditions that achieve these ends.

·         Rogue is invisible

·         Rogue is using stealth (see below)

·         Enemy is flat-footed

·         Enemy is “balancing” (causes flat-footed)

·         Enemy is flanked

·         Enemy is blind

·         Enemy is feinted

·         Enemy is helpless

·         Enemy is cowering

·         Enemy is stunned

·         Enemy is paralyzed (causes helpless)

·         Enemy is unconscious (causes helpless)

·         Enemy is pinned (causes flat-footed)

·         Enemy is climbing

·         Enemy is running

·         Enemy is squeezing

Ways to induce these conditions

Flanking

If allies can flank an enemy with the rogue, she will be able to sneak attack. Positioning yourself for a flank while holding a melee weapon or casting Summon Monster (CRB) and positioning your creatures to do so are gold. Combat maneuvers that reposition enemies like Bull Rush, Reposition, or Grapple (and move) will help, as well as spells with similar effects like Hydraulic Push (CRB), Battering Blast (DoG), or Hostile Juxtaposition (UC).

Invisibility

Casting Vanish (APG), Invisibility, or Invisibility Sphere (CRB) on your rogue will let her use sneak attack on her first attack against all enemies who can’t see invisible creatures. Casting Greater Invisibility (CRB) will do so for the duration of the spell.

Blind

Blind enemies lose their dexterity bonus to AC. Spells which inflict blindness include Glitterdust, Pyrotechnics, Color Spray, Blindness/Deafness, Holy Smite, (CRB), Blinding Ray, Mudball (ARG), Burst of Radiance (CoP), Spear of Purity (UM), Fear the Sun, and Touch of Blindness (BoS). Executing a Dirty Trick combat maneuver, or having a Chain of Perdition (UC) or an intelligent summon to whom you can give verbal instructions do so can also blind an enemy for one round.

Helpless (Unconscious and Paralyzed)

Spells which render the enemy unconscious, paralyzed, or otherwise helpless allow for sneak attack. Such spells include Sleep, Color Spray, Deep Slumber, Hold Person, Hold Animal, Hold Monster, and Ghoul Touch (CRB).

Stunned

A monk’s Stunning Fist ability, as well as spells such as Color Spray, Sound Burst, (CRB) and Stunning Barrier (ACG) can stun enemies and deprive them of their dexterity bonuses.

Balancing

Spells and effects which require enemies to make Acrobatics checks to move through a square without falling will deny them their dexterity bonus to AC. Such spells include Grease (CRB) and Ice Slick (MC), as well as spells that can alter the terrain like Stone Shape (CRB). Forcing an enemy onto a naturally uneven, slippery, or narrow surface works too.

Cowering and Running

Spells and techniques that scare enemies can provoke them into cowering or running, both of which deny them their dexterity bonuses to AC. Fear effects are cumulative, so while any given scare tactic may not be enough, several used together can be. Shaken creatures suffer several penalties but will not flee or cower. Frightened creatures will flee (and may or may not run) but will not cower. Panicked creatures will flee (run) and/or cower, and thus this condition is ideal. Spells which induce fear include Cause Fear (frightened/shaken), Doom (shaken), Fear (panicked/shaken) (CRB), Feast on Fear (panicked) (ACG), Haunting Mists (shaken), and Vision of Hell (shaken) (UM). The Intimidate skill can also be used to demoralize foes, making them shaken. The Turn Undead ability renders undead panicked.

Pinned

Following a successful roll to maintain a grapple, an enemy can be pinned, denying their dexterity bonus to AC. Barbarians, fighters, monks, and other characters skilled at combat maneuvers can achieve this. All spells I’m aware of that can grapple do not allow pinning, but summoned monsters can. Monsters which are exceptional at grappling include Giant Frog (SM2), Crocodile (SM3), Lion (SM4), Owlbear (SNA4), and Dire Lion (SM5).

Stealth

Though it makes intuitive sense, there is no line in any rulebook I’m aware of that explicitly states that attacking from stealth denies enemies their dexterity bonuses. Page 179 of the Core Rulebook reads “If you can’t react to a blow, you can’t use your dexterity bonus to AC.” Page 106 reads “Creatures that fail to beat your Stealth check are not aware of you.” Jason Bulmahn, lead designer at Paizo, indicated “It was our intent that if you are unaware of a threat, you cannot react to a blow…but space in the Stealth description was extraordinarily tight and every word was at a premium.”* This would suggest that if a character successfully uses stealth, their target would be unaware of them, denying their dexterity bonus and allowing sneak attack on the first attack. Provided your game master agrees with this interpretation, spells which allow your rogue to use stealth (ie. provide cover or concealment) will also help with sneak attack.

Keep in mind that concealment also prevents sneak attack (though only total concealment stops Unchained Rogues), so the ideal spell would be one that hides your rogue but not your enemies. Hide From Undead (CRB) does this beautifully with undead enemies. If your rogue has darkvision, Darkness (CRB) will work. Deeper Darkness (CRB) can provide concealment but blocks darkvision. Obscuring Mist, Pyrotechnics, Fog Cloud (CRB), or lighting a Smokestick all fit the bill too but may obscure your enemies. If this is a regular strategy you mean to use, encourage your rogue to pick up a Goz Mask or Fogcutting Lenses.

Your sneaky friends thank you.
